# INV-0015

# The Academic Classification of Yiguandao — How Religious Studies Classifies Yiguandao in Relation to Daoism and to Chinese Salvationist Religions, on What Basis, at What Grade, and with What Consensus or Dissent

## Draft Investigation Record

> Authored using **TPL-0003**. Fifteenth research workflow (RQ-0015). **Opened as a scaffold**; to be executed and closed through the full adversarial circuit (OPS-0003: Research Specialist → Critical Reviewer → Knowledge Architect; Vision Steward/owner closes). The opening/closing split is structural (STD-0006 §7.6 independence principle): the session that authored this scaffold does not itself generate and certify the findings that fill it. The circuit runs under a separate brief after owner review.

> **Reading C — the object under test is the CLASSIFICATION and its evidential basis.** This is not a broad "Yiguandao study" and not a tracing of Yiguandao's Daoist strand. The owner's external comparative thesis (~12,200 words) is held at **arm's length: it is the HYPOTHESIS UNDER TEST, not evidence, not citable, not a premise.** Its headline finding — that academic religious studies classifies Yiguandao as a Chinese salvationist religion, not a school of Daoism — is precisely what this investigation independently evaluates. The thesis may suggest where sources live; it grounds nothing.

> **Verification & reliance note (STD-0006 §7.5 analog).** Religious-studies investigation — **not health/high-stakes, not child-facing** — no health gate. It is, however, **externally consequential** (adjacent to the owner's comparative-religion thesis) **and verification-light-expected**: the investigation leans on scholars' *arguments* (classification criteria), which are largely paywalled and unreachable by the live-retrieval channel. This is a known structural posture, recorded plainly at opening, not a defect to engineer around. §7.5 therefore governs: the Specialist and the Critical Reviewer **must each disclose their verification strength**, and **the findings are NOT cleared for external reliance (thesis-supporting or public use) regardless of closure** — genuinely independent re-verification is the gate-lift.

> **Thin-base authorization (owner-ratified).** English-language Yiguandao scholarship is limited and much primary material is Chinese-language. **"UNSOURCED / insufficient base to grade" is a legitimate, recordable finding, not a failure to paper over. Do not thin-source to manufacture a grade.** Anti-fabrication (KOS-0003 §12.1) is absolute.

> **Scale discipline.** Every on-record confidence rating — frontmatter and every grading field — is native **`Level N (Label)`** (KOS-0003 §8), the only authorized on-record confidence vocabulary. No H-band in any frontmatter or grading field; no ★-glyphs anywhere in any Knowledge Object.

# 1. Research Question

**Primary (authoritative wording, owner-ratified; revised by owner ruling at v0.2 to the symmetric, non-prejudging form):**

> How does academic religious studies classify Yiguandao (I-Kuan Tao) in relation to Daoism and to the category of Chinese salvationist religions, on what evidential basis, at what grade, and with what degree of scholarly consensus or dissent — and how does that classification relate to Yiguandao's own self-understanding?

**Decomposition mandate (owner-ratified): six discrete, separately gradable claims** (identifiers assigned at execution, not here):

1. **Historical origin and formation** of Yiguandao (19th–20th c. sectarian milieu; the datable facts of its emergence and lineage claims).
2. **Doctrinal and organizational structure** — its syncretic composition (Confucian, Buddhist, Daoist, folk, and other strands) and how those strands are proportioned and integrated.
3. **THE CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA THEMSELVES (load-bearing claim):** the specific criteria by which religious-studies scholarship assigns the "Chinese salvationist religion" category, and whether Yiguandao meets them. **This claim is where the RQ is actually answered.**
4. **Daoist elements present in Yiguandao** — real and identifiable, assessed as **INCORPORATED STRANDS versus LINEAGE MEMBERSHIP** (this is where Reading C touches the Daoist-strand question without becoming it).
5. **Yiguandao's self-understanding** regarding its relationship to Daoism — as a fact about the movement (the §2.1 dual-track rule applies in full).
6. **Scholarly dissent**, if any, from the majority classification — steelmanned at its strongest.

**The three mandated elements — answered explicitly and separably for EACH claim** (a lesson encoded from INV-0011 criterion #2: "treated in prose" is not "answered separably"):

- **(a) SOURCE & BASE:** the strongest available sourcing, its language and accessibility (English/Chinese; open/paywalled), and — where the base is thin — an explicit statement of what is and is not establishable from it.
- **(b) CLASSIFICATION BEARING:** what this claim establishes for or against the salvationist-vs-Daoist classification, and what it does not reach.
- **(c) RIVAL/COUNTER-READING (steelmanned):** the strongest opposing reading — for most claims, the Yiguandao-self-understanding or Daoist-lineage reading at full strength — then whether the claim survives it and at what cost to confidence.

# 2. Scope & Disambiguation

## 2.1 The dual-track steelman discipline (operative rule)

Unlike INV-0011 and INV-0014, whose poles were both scholarly, one "pole" here is a religious movement's **self-understanding**. The rule:

- Yiguandao's self-description (including any claim to Daoist lineage) is evidence about **THE MOVEMENT'S SELF-UNDERSTANDING** — a fact — and is steelmanned at its strongest **in that register**.
- It is **NOT** evidence about whether the academic classification is correct; that is the scholarly question, answered on scholarly grounds.
- **Both slides are guarded:** do not let self-understanding covertly settle the classification (**confessional drift**), and do not dismiss self-understanding as mere error (**debunking drift**). Both tracks are recorded at their honest weight.

## 2.2 The consensus-asymmetry rule (operative rule; added by owner ruling at v0.2)

> Symmetric framing does not require symmetric findings. If the scholarly basis for one classification is stronger than for another, the investigation reports that asymmetry at its actual weight. Representing a lopsided consensus as balanced would be as much a distortion as prejudging the question — neither the RQ's neutrality nor the dual-track rule licenses false balance.

## 2.3 Thin-base authorization

**UNSOURCED / insufficient base to grade is a recordable outcome.** Where the accessible base cannot support a grade, the record says so; no claim is thin-sourced into a grade it has not earned (see the opening banner; owner-ratified).

## 2.4 Register

Neutral **religious-studies** register throughout. No confessional framing in any direction — neither for the movement's self-account nor against it.

## 2.5 The thesis is hypothesis-under-test

The owner's external comparative thesis is **not evidence, not citable, not a premise**. No claim, source evaluation, or finding may cite it, paraphrase it as support, or treat its conclusions as established. It may suggest where sources live; it grounds nothing. The investigation evaluates its headline finding independently, on the evidence the circuit itself assembles.

## 2.6 Boundary with prior religion work

**INV-0011** (Zoroastrian eschatology) and the **Iranian-family standing source base (SRC-0104…SRC-0123)** are separate lines of work: **no overlap, no draw**. No INV-0015 object cites, extends, or edges into that base. Prior investigations (including INV-0001 and INV-0014) are likewise not drawn on — this investigation stands on its own source base, assembled at execution.

## 2.7 Language boundary

Chinese-language primary material is **in scope for the self-understanding track where reachable in scholarly translation**. Untranslated material that cannot be verified is **recorded as a gap, not paraphrased from model knowledge** (anti-fabrication, KOS-0003 §12.1).

## 2.8 Reliance posture

**Verification-light expected, known at the outset.** The classification-criteria literature (claim 3's base) is largely paywalled and unreachable by the live-retrieval channel; the investigation is structurally destined to close verification-light, with findings **gated from external reliance from the opening onward** (see the banner and §7). This is recorded plainly as posture, not treated as a defect.

## 2.9 Terminology

"Yiguandao" (一貫道; also romanized I-Kuan Tao) is used throughout; "Chinese salvationist religion" refers to the religious-studies category (and its cognates in the scholarly literature) whose criteria claim 3 examines; "school of Daoism" refers to lineage membership in the Daoist tradition as the scholarly literature bounds it. **What each category requires is itself part of what the circuit establishes — the definitions are not pre-settled here.**
