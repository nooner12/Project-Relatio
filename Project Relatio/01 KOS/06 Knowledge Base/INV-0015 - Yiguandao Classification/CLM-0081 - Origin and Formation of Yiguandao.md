# CLM-0081

# Yiguandao Emerged from the Late-Qing Sectarian Milieu as a Xiantiandao-Descended Movement, Named in 1905 and Refashioned into a Mass Movement by Zhang Tianran in the 1930s — a Datable Formation History Located in the Sectarian Tradition, Not in Institutional Daoist Lineage

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Yiguandao's formation is datable and scholarly-established in outline: it descends from the **Xiantiandao** ("Way of Former Heaven") sectarian tradition; Wang Jueyi (1832–1886?), counted the fifteenth patriarch in that line, formed a group c. 1875; Liu Qingxu gave the name "Yiguandao" in 1905; Zhang Tianran (with Sun Suzhen) assumed the eighteenth patriarchate in 1930 and from 1934 refashioned the movement into the mass organization that spread through Republican China, was suppressed on the mainland and in Taiwan, grew into Taiwan's largest sect, and was legalized there in 1987. **The formation lineage scholarship traces runs through the late-imperial sectarian milieu — not through the ordination or institutional lineages of Daoism.**

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — a dated-formation claim about a modern movement, resting on the scholarly literature's convergent account.

---

# Element (a) — Source & Base
- **Strongest available sourcing:** SRC-0134 (Irons, WRSP profile) — **the only fully live-read source in this base** — carries the entire dated spine used above (c. 1875 formation; 1905 naming; 1919 Lu Zhongyi; 1930 joint patriarchate; 1934 refashioning; 1987 legalization; 810,000 Taiwan members / 3.5% by 2005). SRC-0132 (Lu 2008, the first dedicated anglophone monograph) corroborates the trajectory (suppression → largest sect in Taiwan → 1987 legal status) at the review-verified level; SRC-0130 (Goossaert & Palmer) corroborates the Republican-era redemptive-societies formation context at the placement-verified level.
- **Language & accessibility:** all English; SRC-0134 open access (content-verified); SRC-0132/0130 paywalled monographs (bibliographically verified, interiors unread — disclosed).
- **What is and is not establishable from this base:** the dated outline is establishable at the reference-work level with monographic corroboration at one remove; **fine-grained formation history (e.g., the precise relation of Wang Jueyi's group to prior Xiantiandao branches, or intra-lineage disputes) is NOT establishable from the accessible base** and is not asserted. The self-presentation's divergent lineage dates (SRC-0135) are recorded in that record, not adjudicated here.

# Element (b) — Classification Bearing
- **What this claim establishes:** the *formation-historical* premise the field's classification rests on — Yiguandao's documented descent line runs through the late-imperial **sectarian** milieu (Xiantiandao), and its founders are counted as patriarchs of that line, not as clerics or lineage-holders of institutional Daoism. Formation history is the first thing the salvationist/redemptive-society framing organizes.
- **What it does not reach:** formation history alone does not *settle* the classification (a movement could in principle descend from a sectarian milieu and still be argued into another category); the criteria question is CLM-0083's, per INV-0015 §3.1.

# Element (c) — Rival / Counter-Reading (Steelmanned)
**The lineage-antiquity reading at full strength:** Yiguandao's own account (SRC-0135) does not date itself from 1875 or 1905 — it claims a patriarchal transmission from remote antiquity (Fuxi), with the 19th–20th-century figures as late links in an unbroken chain; on this reading the "formation" scholarship dates is merely the modern organizational phase of something ancient, and privileging the scholarly dates begs the question against the movement.
**Why the claim survives:** the two accounts answer different questions (INV-0015 §2.1). The claim grades the *historically documentable* emergence of the organization — group-formation, naming, mass expansion — which is what historical method can reach; the antiquity claim is a **lineage theology**, graded in CLM-0085 as self-understanding at its own weight, not dismissed and not competing on the same evidential field. No documentary evidence for the pre-19th-century organizational existence of *this* movement is offered by any source in the base.
**Cost to confidence:** minimal for the dated organizational outline; real for any statement about what the lineage "really" is beyond documentation — which this claim therefore does not make.

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- The reference-profile genre reliably transmits the monographic consensus for dated-outline facts (checked against no contrary datum in the surveyed base).
- The dual-track bracket (INV-0015 §2.1): the movement's antiquity self-account is not evidence against the documentary dates, and vice versa.

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** Convergent dated testimony across three independent scholarly surfaces, unopposed within the surveyed base → the outline is established at moderate confidence, capped by the verification posture. **Risks checked:** *single-thread dependence* (the spine's full text rests on one live-read source — capped at Level 3, not 4, for exactly this reason); *confessional/debunking drift* (the antiquity self-account neither adopted nor dismissed — routed to CLM-0085).

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 3 (Moderate)** — dated outline convergently attested and uncontested in the surveyed base; **ceiling stated:** only one source's full content was independently read; monograph corroboration stands at one remove (INV-0015 §2.3/§2.8). A verification-strong read of SRC-0132's interior would likely support Level 4; the accessible base does not.

# Limitations
- Outline only: no fine-grained formation history; intra-Xiantiandao branch relations not graded.
- The 810,000/3.5% scale figure is carried at reference-work strength as of 2005 — a snapshot, not a current census.

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Lineage-antiquity reading** — element (c): a different register (theology of lineage), graded in CLM-0085; does not contest the documentary dates.
2. **Earlier-origin historiography** (the movement as a direct continuation of Ming-Qing Eternal-Mother sects generally, beyond Xiantiandao specifically) — compatible with the claim; the base supports the Xiantiandao line specifically and the broader milieu generally; not adjudicated finer than that.
