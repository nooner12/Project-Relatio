# CLM-0083

# Anglophone Religious Studies Places Yiguandao in the Salvationist / Redemptive-Society / Sectarian Family with Effective Unanimity Across the Surveyed Base — MODERATE for the Placement and Its Consensus; While the Field's Specific Criteria-Mechanics, Paywalled Beyond This Session's Reach, Are Graded LOW

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **(i) Placement and consensus — Level 3 (Moderate):** the anglophone religious-studies literature classifies Yiguandao within the **salvationist religion / redemptive-society / syncretic-sectarian** family — as a Xiantiandao-descended member of the Republican-era redemptive-societies wave — with **effective unanimity across every source surveyed in this base**; no surveyed source classifies it as a school of Daoism. **(ii) Criteria-mechanics — Level 2 (Low):** the field's *specific* criteria for the category (articulated most explicitly in Palmer 2011) and the point-by-point demonstration that Yiguandao meets them are carried here only at the level the accessible surfaces support — the article's framework distinguishes recurring salvationist movements from the Republican wave and lists Yiguandao among the wave's exemplars — because **the criteria text itself is paywalled beyond this session's independent reach**; the mechanics are not reconstructed from model memory (INV-0015 §2.3 thin-base rule).

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / interpretive** — a claim about the state of a scholarly field (its category assignments and their distribution), with an interpretive component (what the category's criteria are).

---

# Element (a) — Source & Base
- **Strongest available sourcing:** SRC-0129 (Palmer 2011) is the category's most explicit conceptual statement — bibliographically live-verified with its framing and Yiguandao-exemplar placement confirmed from open surfaces, **interior unread (paywalled/403)**. SRC-0130 (the standard survey) verifies the survey-level placement (ch. 4, redemptive societies, Yiguandao named). SRC-0131 verifies current-scholarship usage live ("one of the major redemptive societies of Republican China," OUP description). SRC-0134 verifies specialist reference usage live ("Chinese syncretic religion" / NRM with salvationist-millenarian dimensions).
- **Language & accessibility:** all English; the criteria-bearing article and both monographs **paywalled**; the two live-read confirmations are the OUP description and the WRSP profile. The Chinese-language scholarship on Yiguandao's classification is **out of the surveyed base entirely** — a recorded gap (INV-0015 §2.7), not an assumed concurrence.
- **What is and is not establishable:** the placement and its distribution across the surveyed base are establishable and shown; **the criteria-mechanics — the enumerated marks of the category and their point-by-point application — are NOT establishable at strength from the accessible base.** This is the investigation's central, expected thin point, recorded per the thin-base authorization rather than papered over.

# Element (b) — Classification Bearing
- **What this claim establishes:** this is where the revised RQ is answered, under the consensus-asymmetry rule (INV-0015 §2.2): **the surveyed field's classification is lopsided to the point of unanimity** — salvationist/redemptive-society/sectarian family placement in every surveyed source, school-of-Daoism placement in none. Reporting that asymmetry at weight is required; representing the question as scholarly-balanced would be false balance.
- **What it does not reach:** (1) the *criteria-mechanics* component stands at Level 2 — the unanimity of placement is better evidenced than the argued basis of placement; (2) unanimity **within the surveyed base** is not unanimity of the whole field — the base is anglophone, small, and partly at one verification remove (the bound carried by CLM-0086); (3) per §3.1, claims 4 and 5 inform but do not settle this claim, and this claim asserts nothing about what Yiguandao *essentially is* — only how the field classifies and on what accessible basis.

# Element (c) — Rival / Counter-Reading (Steelmanned)
**The category-artifact reading at full strength (the strongest available counter, since no Daoist-school classification was located to steelman):** classification vocabularies in Chinese sectarian studies have historically encoded outsiders' and persecutors' taxonomies (SRC-0136's autonym/label critique); "salvationist religion"/"redemptive societies" is itself a scholars' construction — Palmer's own title asks whether it is "historical phenomenon or sociological category" — and Yiguandao's placement in it may reflect the category's convenience rather than the movement's nature; the movement's own register (SRC-0135) uses none of this vocabulary.
**Why the claim survives:** the claim is *about* the field's classification, so the artifact reading does not contradict it — it qualifies what the classification *is* (a scholarly category assignment, reflexively debated within the field, not a metaphysical identification). The claim's wording already carries this: it grades the placement and its consensus, not the category's ontological adequacy. What the artifact reading defeats is any inflation of (i) into "Yiguandao *is* essentially a salvationist religion and the field has proven it" — an inflation this record does not make.
**Cost to confidence:** it is one reason component (ii) sits at Level 2 (the criteria's justificatory force is exactly what could not be independently read), and it feeds CLM-0086's honest statement of where the field's real debate lies.

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 for placement (four independent scholarly surfaces, two live-read); 2 for criteria-mechanics (interior unread).
- Relevance: 5 — this claim is the RQ's load-bearing answer.
- Independence: 3 — Palmer appears twice in the base (SRC-0129, SRC-0130 co-author); the census discounts for it (the placement still stands on Billioud, Irons, and the survey's co-author Goossaert — a Daoism specialist).
- Quality: 3 for placement; 2 for mechanics.
- Limitations: anglophone-only; small base; criteria interior unread; consensus measured over the surveyed base, not the whole field.

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0129 high authority, paywalled (the disclosed gap); SRC-0130 high; SRC-0131 high, description live-read; SRC-0134 moderate-to-high, fully live-read. The Palmer double-appearance is disclosed and discounted in the independence score.

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- The surveyed base is representative of the anglophone field's classification usage (checked against no contrary hit in any search this session; still an assumption — bound stated in element (b)).
- The classification-evidence rule (INV-0015 §3.1): this claim answers on scholarly-criteria grounds and does not import CLM-0084/0085 as deciders.

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive census with a disclosed gap.** Every surveyed classification surface assigns the salvationist-family placement; none assigns Daoist-school placement; the criteria-mechanics behind the placement are attested but unread → placement Moderate, mechanics Low. **Risks checked:** *false balance* (refused — the asymmetry is reported as found, per §2.2); *prejudgment* (the mirror risk — the claim asserts field-placement, not essence); *fabricated criteria* (the strongest risk for this claim: reconstructing Palmer's criteria list from model memory would be undisclosed fabrication — **refused**; the mechanics component is graded down instead, per the thin-base authorization).

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Component (i) — placement and consensus: Level 3 (Moderate).** Unanimous across the surveyed base with two live-read confirmations; capped below High by the base's size, anglophone bound, and partial verification remove.
- **Component (ii) — criteria-mechanics: Level 2 (Low).** The criteria text exists, is identified, and frames the field's usage, but was not independently readable; nothing finer is asserted.
- **Split deliberately not averaged.**

# Limitations
- Anglophone base only; Chinese-language scholarship a recorded gap.
- "Effective unanimity" is a property of the surveyed base (8 sources, 4 bearing directly on classification), not a whole-field census.
- The criteria-mechanics gap is the claim's defining limitation, held at Level 2.

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Category-artifact reading** — element (c): absorbed as a qualification; defeats only an inflation the claim does not make.
2. **Daoist-school classification** — *no located instance in the surveyed anglophone base*; recorded as an absence (see CLM-0086), not as a refuted position.
3. **"New religious movement" / "sect" as competing labels** — not competitors: the surveyed sources use these interchangeably with the salvationist-family framing at different levels of abstraction; the distribution is label-plural but family-consistent.
