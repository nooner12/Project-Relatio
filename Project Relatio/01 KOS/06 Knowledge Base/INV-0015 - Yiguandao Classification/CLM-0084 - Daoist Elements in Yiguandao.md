# CLM-0084

# Daoist Elements in Yiguandao Are Real and Identifiable — Including Quanzhen-Derived Teachings in Its Xiantiandao Inheritance and Dao-Centered Cosmological Vocabulary — and the Surveyed Scholarship Treats Them as INCORPORATED STRANDS Within a Sectarian Synthesis, Not as LINEAGE MEMBERSHIP in Institutional Daoism

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Yiguandao carries **real, identifiable Daoist elements**: the surveyed scholarship's account of its Xiantiandao inheritance includes teachings **"derived from Quanzhen Daoism"** (SRC-0134, live-read), and its name, goal-concept, and cosmological vocabulary center on the **Dao**. The same scholarship treats these as **incorporated strands** within the Eternal-Mother sectarian synthesis — materials the sect family absorbed and reworked — **not as lineage membership**: no surveyed source describes Yiguandao as standing in the ordination, liturgical, or institutional lineages of Daoism (Quanzhen, Zhengyi, or other), and none classifies it as a Daoist school on the strength of these elements.

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / interpretive** — descriptive on the elements' presence; interpretive on the strand-vs-membership distinction, which is consumed from the scholarship's own treatment.

---

# Element (a) — Source & Base
- **Strongest available sourcing:** SRC-0134 (live-read) states the Quanzhen-derived datum directly: Wang Jueyi "added a strong neo-Confucian interpretation on top of existing teachings derived from Quanzhen Daoism" — i.e., the profile's own account both *affirms* a Daoist-derived stratum and *frames* it as material subsequently reinterpreted within the sectarian line. SRC-0133 (review-verified) documents the milieu mechanism: Taiwan's spirit-writing sectarian family absorbs and recombines materials across traditions as its normal mode.
- **Language & accessibility:** English; SRC-0134 open (content-verified); SRC-0133 paywalled (review-verified). The specialist literature that would inventory Daoist elements item-by-item (largely Chinese-language, or in paywalled monographs) is **not in the accessible base — recorded as a gap**: this claim grades the presence and the field's treatment, not an inventory.
- **What is and is not establishable:** presence of Daoist-derived elements — establishable (directly attested, live-read); their *treatment as strands* — establishable across the surveyed base (it is how every surveyed source frames them); an *itemized inventory or proportion* — **not establishable from the accessible base** and not asserted.
- **Single-source specificity disclosure (Critical Review – RQ-0015 F-2, adopted):** the *general* presence of Daoist elements and vocabulary is uncontested across the surveyed base; the *specific* genetic attribution "derived from **Quanzhen** Daoism" is a **single-reference-work attribution** (one sentence of SRC-0134), uncorroborated elsewhere in the accessible base, and the precise Daoist ancestry of the Xiantiandao line is contested in the wider literature. The claim's Level 3 covers the presence-plus-treatment content; the Quanzhen-specificity is carried at the lower evidential weight this disclosure states, not at the claim's headline grade.

# Element (b) — Classification Bearing
- **What this claim establishes:** the distinction the revised RQ turns on when it asks about Yiguandao "in relation to Daoism": the relation the surveyed field describes is **genetic incorporation** (Daoist-derived materials inside a sectarian synthesis), not **taxonomic membership** (being a school *of* Daoism). Reading C touches the Daoist-strand question here exactly and goes no further.
- **What it does not reach (per §3.1, stated as the claim's own discipline):** the presence of Daoist elements — however real — **does not settle the classification in either direction**: strands do not make membership, and the absence of membership does not erase the strands. This claim informs CLM-0083; it does not decide it.

# Element (c) — Rival / Counter-Reading (Steelmanned)
**The lineage-membership reading at full strength:** the Xiantiandao line itself descends (per the movement's own account, SRC-0135, via ninth patriarch Huang Dehui) from a milieu saturated with internal-alchemy Daoism; if the transmission chain runs through Daoist-derived teachers and Quanzhen-derived teachings, then "incorporated strands" understates a genuine descent relation — the movement would be a *popular continuation* of a Daoist lineage, and the institutional test (ordination, canon, clergy) begs the question by defining Daoism as its elite institutions.
**Why the claim survives:** even at full strength, the reading establishes **descent through Daoist-influenced sectarian intermediaries** — which is compatible with, indeed part of, the claim's own content (the Quanzhen-derived stratum *is* the descent trace). What it cannot produce from the surveyed base is a scholarly voice drawing the conclusion: no surveyed source, including the movement's own English self-presentation (which claims the trans-religion Dao, not Daoist-school identity — SRC-0135/CLM-0085), classifies Yiguandao as Daoism. The definitional protest ("Daoism ≠ its institutions") is a real methodological question, but it belongs to the field's category debate (CLM-0086), and no surveyed participant in that debate has run it to a Daoist-school classification of Yiguandao.
**Cost to confidence:** it sharpens the claim's bound — the strand-vs-membership framing is the *surveyed field's* treatment, held at the base's verification level, not an adjudicated metaphysics of lineage; the claim is worded accordingly.

Type: **Historical (secondary).**

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 — the load-bearing datum is live-read in a specialist profile; milieu corroboration at one remove.
- Relevance: 5 — directly the Daoist-strand question.
- Independence: 2–3 — two sources, one carrying most weight; the thinnest evidential spread in this investigation apart from CLM-0086.
- Quality: 3 — the datum is specific and uncontested in the surveyed base.
- Limitations: no item-level inventory; proportion ungraded; two-source spread.

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0134 moderate-to-high authority, open, fully read; SRC-0133 high authority, paywalled, review-verified with an explicit scope restriction (no Yiguandao-specific chapter content asserted).

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- The strand/membership distinction is coherent and is the one the surveyed literature in fact uses (checked: every surveyed source is consistent with it; none asserts membership).
- Dual-track bracket: the movement's lineage self-account bears on CLM-0085, not on this claim's scholarly-treatment content.

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive, with the bound stated.** Direct live-read attestation of a Daoist-derived stratum + uniform strand-treatment across the surveyed base + zero located membership-classification → the claim as worded. **Risks checked:** *strand-counting slide* (the §3.1 rule is restated inside element (b) — presence informs, never settles); *definitional prejudgment* (the institutional test is reported as the field's usage, and the protest against it is steelmanned, not suppressed); *inventory overreach* (refused — gap recorded).

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 3 (Moderate)** — the presence datum is live-read and specific; the strand-treatment is uniform across the surveyed base; **ceiling stated:** two-source spread, no item inventory, and the membership question's definitional edge held open at CLM-0086 (INV-0015 §2.3/§2.8).

# Limitations
- No itemized inventory of Daoist elements; no proportion estimate.
- The claim describes the surveyed field's treatment; it does not adjudicate what "counts as Daoism" — that definitional question is CLM-0086 territory.

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Lineage-membership reading** — element (c): survives as a descent observation, fails as a located classification; bounds the claim.
2. **Minimal-Daoism reading** (the Daoist elements as superficial vocabulary only) — rejected as understating the live-read Quanzhen-derived datum; the stratum is genetic, not decorative, in the surveyed account.
