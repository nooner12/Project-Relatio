# CLM-0086

# The Located Scholarly Dissent Concerns the CATEGORY'S CONSTRUCTION, Not Yiguandao's Membership in It — No Anglophone Source Was Located That Classifies Yiguandao as a School of Daoism — Graded LOW as a Bounded Negative Over a Small Surveyed Base

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Within the surveyed anglophone base, **the real scholarly dissent in this literature is about the categories themselves, not about Yiguandao's placement**: ter Haar's foundational critique shows that sectarian-studies labels have historically encoded outsiders' and persecutors' taxonomies (autonym vs. label), and Palmer's own category statement is reflexively titled as a question ("historical phenomenon or sociological category?") — the field debates *what its categories are*, while continuing to place Yiguandao inside the salvationist/sectarian family under every label variant. **No anglophone scholarly source classifying Yiguandao as a school of Daoism was located this session.** This is a **bounded negative**: absence of located dissent in a small, anglophone, partly-paywalled surveyed base — graded Low accordingly — and not a demonstration that no such dissent exists anywhere.

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive (state-of-the-field), with a negative-existential component** — the kind of claim most exposed to search-boundedness, and graded with that exposure priced in.

---

# Element (a) — Source & Base
- **Strongest available sourcing:** SRC-0136 (ter Haar — review-verified thesis) anchors the *actual* dissent tradition: category-critique. SRC-0129's reflexive title (verified) documents that the category-debate runs *through* the category's own chief architect. The negative component rests on the totality of this session's searches (every classification surface returned salvationist/redemptive-society/sectarian/NRM framings; none returned a Daoist-school classification).
- **Language & accessibility:** English only — **the bound is structural**: Chinese-language scholarship, where a dissenting classification would most plausibly live if it exists, was not surveyed (recorded gap, INV-0015 §2.7); the anglophone paywalled interiors could also harbor nuanced positions not visible from open surfaces.
- **What is and is not establishable:** that the located dissent is category-level — establishable and shown; that the surveyed base contains no Daoist-school classification — establishable (it is a property of the surveyed base); **that no such classification exists in the field at large — NOT establishable and not claimed.** This is the investigation's second designed thin point (with CLM-0083-ii), recorded per the thin-base authorization.

# Element (b) — Classification Bearing
- **What this claim establishes:** the consensus/dissent distribution the revised RQ asks for, reported under the consensus-asymmetry rule (INV-0015 §2.2) at its actual weight: **lopsided**. The live debate is not "salvationist religion vs. school of Daoism" — that contest was not located anywhere in the base — but "are these categories sound instruments at all," a debate *internal* to the classifying field which does not, in any located instance, relocate Yiguandao to Daoism.
- **What it does not reach:** the negative's bound (element (a)); and per §3.1 this distribution claim informs the finding's consensus statement without adding evidential weight to the placement itself beyond what CLM-0083 already carries.

# Element (c) — Rival / Counter-Reading (Steelmanned)
**The hidden-dissent reading at full strength:** absence of located dissent is an artifact of the survey's limits — an anglophone, open-surface, single-session sweep over a literature whose classification arguments are paywalled and much of whose scholarship is Chinese; moreover, category-critique *is* dissent in the strongest available sense (if the categories are artifacts, the "unanimous placement" is unanimity in an artifact), so reporting "no dissent from placement" understates how deep the located critique cuts; and adjacent classification practice (e.g., legal registration of Yiguandao under Daoist associations in some jurisdictions, if it occurs) would constitute institutional dissent invisible to this survey.
**Why the claim survives:** the claim *asserts* the bound the first arm presses (it is graded Low for exactly that reason, and the negative is worded as a property of the surveyed base). The second arm is absorbed rather than resisted: the claim's own content is that the real dissent is category-level, and CLM-0083's element (c) already routes the artifact-qualification into the placement grade. The third arm (registration practice) is a **located hypothesis with no source in the accessible base** — recorded inside the gap, not graded; it would in any case evidence administrative accommodation, a different register from scholarly classification.
**Cost to confidence:** constitutive — it is why this claim carries the investigation's lowest grade rather than riding the placement-census's Level 3.

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive over a bounded census, plus disclosed negative reasoning.** Located dissent sorts entirely into the category-critique register; the placement-dissent cell of the census is empty; the emptiness is reported *as bounded*. **Risks checked:** *false balance* (refused — the asymmetry rule forbids inflating category-critique into a placement controversy that was not located); *asymmetry overstatement* (the mirror risk — the negative is bounded and graded Low, not paraded as proof); *absence-of-evidence fallacy* (the claim's own wording blocks the slide from "not located" to "not existing").

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

# Limitations

- Chinese-language scholarship and paywalled interiors unsurveyed — the two places hidden dissent would most plausibly live.
- Administrative/legal classification practice (registration categories) is outside this claim's register and unsurveyed.
