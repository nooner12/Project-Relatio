# FND-0015

# How Religious Studies Classifies Yiguandao — a Layered Verdict at Verification-Light Strength

## Draft Finding Record

---

# 1. Statement
> **The surveyed anglophone religious-studies literature classifies Yiguandao within the salvationist / redemptive-society / syncretic-sectarian family — as a Xiantiandao-descended member of the Republican-era wave — with effective unanimity and under plural but family-consistent labels; no surveyed source classifies it as a school of Daoism (Level 3, Moderate, for the placement and its consensus).** The evidential basis accessible to this investigation supports that placement-census far better than it supports the field's **criteria-mechanics**, which are paywalled beyond independent reach and therefore graded **Level 2 (Low)** rather than reconstructed from model memory. The **Daoist elements** in Yiguandao are real — including a live-read Quanzhen-derived stratum in its Xiantiandao inheritance — and are treated by the surveyed field as **incorporated strands, not lineage membership (Level 3)**. The movement's **self-understanding**, graded strictly as a fact about the movement, claims the **primordial trans-religion Dao** through a Fuxi-anchored patriarchal lineage — notably *not* a school-of-Daoism self-claim in the read self-presentation — so classification and self-understanding **diverge on register more than they collide on propositions (Level 3, scope-bounded)**. The **consensus/dissent distribution is lopsided**: located dissent concerns the categories' construction (autonym-vs-label critique; the category architect's own reflexive question), not Yiguandao's placement; no Daoist-school classification was located, a **bounded negative graded Level 2**. Per the consensus-asymmetry rule, that lopsidedness is reported at its actual weight — and per the thin-base authorization, the two Level-2 components are recorded as honest thinness, not graded up.

# 2. Supporting Claims
- **CLM-0081 (Level 3)** — the datable formation history (Xiantiandao descent; 1905 naming; 1930s mass movement; 1987 Taiwan legalization): the formation-historical premise of the family placement.
- **CLM-0082 (Level 3)** — the doctrinal architecture (Eternal Mother; three ages; multi-tradition incorporation; Confucian public dominance): the composition premise; strand proportions recorded as a gap.
- **CLM-0083 (split: Level 3 / Level 2)** — the load-bearing answer: placement + consensus Moderate; criteria-mechanics Low (paywalled interior; anti-fabrication honored over grade-manufacture).
- **CLM-0084 (Level 3)** — Daoist elements real, treated as incorporated strands, not lineage membership; strand-counting expressly does not settle the classification (§3.1).
- **CLM-0085 (Level 3, scope-bounded)** — self-understanding as primordial-Dao lineage theology, no school-of-Daoism self-claim in the read voice; dual-track discipline held both directions.
- **CLM-0086 (Level 2)** — dissent is category-level; the placement-dissent cell is empty in the surveyed base; bounded negative.

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Layered — deliberately not averaged (the split verdicts of FND-0010…FND-0014 are the model):**
- **Level 3 (Moderate):** the placement-census (how the surveyed field classifies, and that it does so near-unanimously under family-consistent labels); the formation and composition premises; the strands-not-membership treatment; the read self-understanding.
- **Level 2 (Low):** the criteria-mechanics (paywalled base); the negative on placement-dissent (search-bounded).
- **No component reaches Level 4:** the verification posture forbids it — only two sources' full contents were independently read this session; the monographic and criteria interiors stand at one remove (each claim states its ceiling). **No Level 5 anywhere.**
- Finding-level confidence is bounded by its weakest load-bearing component where that component is load-bearing — the *answer to "on what basis"* inherits Level 2's caution even while the *answer to "how does the field classify"* holds Level 3.

# 4. Scope & Limitations
- **Scope:** the anglophone scholarly classification of Yiguandao, its accessible evidential basis, its consensus/dissent distribution, and its relation to the movement's (read, English-language) self-understanding. Reading C throughout: the classification is the object; this finding asserts nothing about what Yiguandao *essentially is*, and nothing about the truth of any of its teachings.
- **The thin points are the finding, not a defect of it:** (1) criteria-mechanics unreachable (paywalled) — Level 2; (2) placement-dissent negative search-bounded — Level 2; (3) Chinese-language scholarship and internal literature unsurveyed — recorded gaps (INV-0015 §2.7). An honest "insufficient base to grade higher" is this investigation's designed outcome class (INV-0015 §2.3).
- **Dual-track discipline (INV-0015 §2.1):** self-understanding graded as fact-about-the-movement at full steelman strength; it settles nothing about the classification and is dismissed nowhere.
- **Consensus asymmetry (INV-0015 §2.2):** the lopsidedness of the surveyed field is reported at weight; the symmetric RQ framing did not manufacture balance.
- **Verification & reliance (§7.5-analog):** **verification-light overall** — two open sources fully live-read (SRC-0134, SRC-0135) plus one publisher description (SRC-0131); five works bibliographically live-verified with interiors unread; no page-level citation asserted anywhere. **These findings are NOT cleared for external reliance (thesis-supporting or public use) regardless of closure**; genuinely independent re-verification — ideally by a reader with access to Palmer 2011, Billioud 2020, and Lu 2008 in full, and to the Chinese-language literature — is the gate-lift.
- **Relation to the owner's thesis:** evaluated independently; the thesis was not cited, consulted, or used as a premise (INV-0015 §2.5). Where this finding lands relative to the thesis's headline is for the owner to assess outside this record.
