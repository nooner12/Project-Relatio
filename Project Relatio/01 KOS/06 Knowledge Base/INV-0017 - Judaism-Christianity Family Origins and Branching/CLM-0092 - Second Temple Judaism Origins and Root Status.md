# CLM-0092

# Second Temple Judaism Origins and Root Status

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **Second Temple Judaism** is the **root tradition** of this family: the shared Jewish matrix — centred on the Second Temple, the Torah, and the covenant God of Israel — from which **both** Christianity **and** Rabbinic Judaism descend. It is bounded by the **Second Temple period** (roughly the completion of the rebuilt Temple, c. 516 BCE, through the Temple's destruction in 70 CE), reported at grade with contested bounds carried as contested. It is modelled as **one tradition with internal parties** (Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, apocalyptic and Hellenistic currents), not as several distinct traditions — a **reasoned finding against the §2.3 distinctness threshold**, not an assumption. Its **own upstream descent** — from Israelite/Yahwistic religion and the Canaanite substrate — is **deferred to a later "family 2-deep" investigation and is NOT asserted here**; accordingly it carries **no `branches_from` edge**. This claim is the hub warrant for the fork; it takes **no supersessionist or polemical position** on either branch.

---

# Element (i) — Emergence Dating
- **Period bounds (live-verified).** The Second Temple period runs from the completion of the rebuilt Jerusalem Temple (c. **516 BCE**, following the Persian-era return under Cyrus's edict) to the Temple's destruction by Rome in **70 CE**. These period bounds are secure and were live-verified this session against standard reference scholarship; SRC-0137 (Sanders) works within a narrower late-period frame (63 BCE–66 CE) and SRC-0138 (Cohen) spans "from the Maccabees to the Mishnah," so the catalogued base supports the framing from its own titular scope.
- **"As a tradition," the emergence is contested.** *When* the configuration properly called "Second Temple Judaism" (as distinct from pre-exilic Israelite religion) crystallises is genuinely debated: candidates range from the Persian-period reconstitution and the centralisation of Torah (Ezra–Nehemiah) to the later, better-documented Hellenistic and Hasmonean phases that SRC-0137/0138 treat most fully. The claim reports the **period bounds** securely and the **tradition-emergence point** as contested — no false precision.
- **Source posture (bounds the grade).** SRC-0137 and SRC-0138 are **live-verified bibliographically but PARAMETRIC in their interiors** (unread this session); no page-level claim is drawn from them. The specific emergence dating is therefore graded **Moderate**: the period frame is firm, but the tradition-emergence question is contested and the interiors are unread.
- **Disposition:** `display_range` on ENT-0012 reads "c. 516 BCE – 70 CE (Second Temple period)" as a render-only summary; the sourced, graded content and its contestedness live here. The end bound is 70 CE because Second Temple Judaism **in its Temple-centred form** does not continue past the destruction — its heirs are the two branches, which is exactly the fork.

# Element (ii) — Root Position (single-tradition justification vs §2.3)
- **Root for this family — NO `branches_from` edge.** Second Temple Judaism is the family root as bounded by INV-0017. Its **own upstream descent** — from Israelite/Yahwistic religion and the Canaanite substrate — lies **outside this investigation's scope** and is **deferred to a later "family 2-deep" investigation (§2.5)**; it is noted in prose only, **not** asserted and **not** edged. Placing no edge on the hub is deliberate and load-bearing (§2.2): the fork must not read as "Second Temple Judaism became Christianity."
- **Single-tradition status is a REASONED FINDING against the §2.3 threshold, not an assumption.** The internal groups — Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, the apocalyptic milieu, Hellenistic Judaism — are modelled as **parties/currents *within* one tradition**, not as distinct traditions, because they **share a Second Temple, a Torah, and the covenant God of Israel**, and their differences are **interpretive** (halakhic, sectarian, eschatological) rather than differences of a distinct religion with its own separate transmission lineage claiming to be a different faith. This is the substance of Sanders's "**common Judaism**" (SRC-0137): a broadly shared practice and belief beneath the sectarian plurality. On the §2.3 test, none of the internal parties independently clears the bar of a *named distinct tradition with its own descent* — they are sub-streams, and the tradition is one.
- **Door stays open (entities-on-demand).** No internal sub-stream is forced into an entity; equally, none is suppressed. If a later investigation shows a Second-Temple sub-stream that genuinely clears the §2.3 threshold (its own distinct tradition + descent), that is a **future-investigation / Anchor Fit candidate**, flagged not forced. (The Essenes/Qumran community is the nearest candidate — a distinguishable community with its own literature — but it is extinct, left no continuing tradition, and the sources treat it as a Second-Temple *sect*, not a branching tradition; it does not clear the bar here.)
- **`tradition_type` on ENT-0012 is `emergent`.** Second Temple Judaism emerged from the post-exilic reconstitution; it is not founder-attributed *as this tradition* (its foundational Mosaic/Sinai narrative is a different, earlier layer, and asserting Moses as historical founder would breach the metaphysical bracket). **Anchor Fit note:** a `founded` reading (via the Mosaic covenant) is conceivable but historically and neutrally weaker; `emergent` chosen as least-wrong, tension recorded (routed to Anchor Fit).

# Element (iii) — Rival Reading (steelmanned)
- **Strongest rival — "Judaisms," not one Judaism (Neusner-type plural reading).** On this reading "Second Temple Judaism" names not a single tradition but a **period containing several distinct Judaisms** (Pharisaic, Sadducean, Essene, apocalyptic, Hellenistic), each with its own system; to speak of one "tradition" flattens a real plurality and smuggles in a false unity. **Survives, partially:** the internal plurality is genuine and well-attested, and it is the reason the single-tradition status is graded **Moderate, not higher** — the model is a defensible simplification, not a demonstrated unity. **Cost / why it does not overturn the root model:** even on the plural reading, the several Judaisms share the Temple, the Torah, and the God of Israel and are recognisably *variants of one thing* (Sanders's common substrate); and for **this family's** purpose the two heirs (Christianity, Rabbinic Judaism) both descend from that shared Second-Temple matrix, whichever internal party they trace to. The plural reading refines the internal picture; it does not supply a *different root* for the fork. The residual force of the rival is encoded in the Moderate grade.
- **Second rival — periodisation quibble (start-date).** Some place the tradition's start at 538 BCE (Cyrus's edict / return) rather than 516 BCE (Temple completion), or locate the decisive formation in the Hellenistic period. Handled as **contested bounds, not a defeater**: the render-only `range_start_year` uses the Temple-completion anchor while the claim records the spread.

**Descriptive / historical** — about the emergence, bounds, and genealogical position of a tradition; the tradition's own truth claims are bracketed.

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- SRC-0137 (Sanders, *Judaism: Practice and Belief*): the "common Judaism" characterisation and the late-period (63 BCE–66 CE) frame (parametric interior; live-verified bibliographically — weak content-verification, disclosed).

- Live-verified this session: the Second Temple period bounds (c. 516 BCE – 70 CE) against standard reference scholarship (strong, general-reference level — not a page-level source claim).

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** — the period bounds are secure; the "one tradition vs. several Judaisms" question is a live scholarly debate, and the tradition-emergence point is contested.
- Consensus strength: **High** on the period bounds and on the reality of internal plurality; **Moderate** on modelling the whole as a single tradition (the "common Judaism" thesis is influential but itself contested, per SRC-0137 §3).

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Family-boundary assumption:** the deeper Israelite/Yahwistic root is treated as *outside* this family, so Second Temple Judaism functions as the root; this is a scoping decision, recorded as such (§2.5).
- **Single-tradition modelling assumption:** the internal parties are treated as currents within one tradition per the §2.3 threshold and the common-Judaism substrate — a reasoned finding, with the plural-Judaisms rival costed into the Moderate grade.
- **Metaphysical bracket:** the tradition's truth claims are set aside; only historical emergence and genealogical position are at issue; no supersessionist or polemical framing.

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **emergence_dating — Level 3 (Moderate):** period bounds firm (live-verified); tradition-emergence point contested; source interiors parametric. **No Level 4/5** — verification-light posture, R0.
- **root_and_single_tradition_status — Level 3 (Moderate):** secure as the shared root of both branches; the single-tradition modelling is defensible via the common-Judaism substrate but is bounded by the live plural-Judaisms debate (element (iii)). **No Level 4/5.** R0.

# Limitations
- Establishes the root position, the period bounds, and a reasoned single-tradition modelling; does **not** fix a precise tradition-emergence date, does **not** adjudicate the "one vs. several Judaisms" debate by fiat, and does **not** assert or characterise the deeper Israelite/Yahwistic root (deferred).
