# FND-0017

# Origins and Branching of the Judaism-Christianity Family

## Draft Finding Record

---

# 1. Finding (the honest shape)

The Judaism–Christianity family, as the catalogued base and the inherited closed records support it, has the following origins-and-branching shape — **one fork, layered by tradition, thin where the evidence or the vocabulary is thin**:

- **Second Temple Judaism is the family root** (Moderate). It is the shared Jewish matrix — Temple, Torah, covenant God of Israel — bounded by the Second Temple period (c. 516 BCE – 70 CE; period bounds live-verified, tradition-emergence point contested). It is modelled as **one tradition with internal parties** (Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, apocalyptic, Hellenistic), a reasoned finding against the §2.3 distinctness threshold via the common-Judaism substrate — with the plural-Judaisms rival steelmanned and costed into the Moderate grade. It carries **no `branches_from` edge**: its own upstream descent from Israelite/Yahwistic religion is **deferred to a later "family 2-deep" investigation**, not asserted. It ends as a Temple-centred configuration at 70 CE — its heirs are the two branches, which is the fork.

- **Christianity is a first-century-CE branch of Second Temple Judaism** (emergence + descent High; schism-qualifier fit Moderate). The earliest Jesus movement was a Second-Temple Jewish sect that inherited its whole apocalyptic frame from Second Temple Judaism (seed warrant CLM-0058, High), anchored by the closed first-century results (Jesus's existence/execution c. 30 CE, CLM-0075/0076; the undisputed Pauline letters c. 50–60 CE, CLM-0068 — all inherited, not re-derived). It `branches_from` Second Temple Judaism, qualifier **`schism`** — the least-wrong value for "separated to become distinct," **with a load-bearing caveat that `schism` must not be read as a sharp datable break**: the "parting of the ways" was gradual, late, uneven, and locally variable (Boyarin, Becker & Reed, against Dunn's earlier-partings pole).

- **Rabbinic Judaism is the post-70-CE CONTINUATION / main line of Second Temple Judaism** (emergence + descent Moderate; continuation-qualifier fit Low). It consolidated after the Temple's destruction, reconstituting Jewish life around Torah study and halakhah (Yavneh the conventional early locus; Mishnah c. 200 CE the standard anchor, SRC-0142), most directly continuing the Pharisaic stream. It `branches_from` Second Temple Judaism, qualifier **`reform`** — **the anchor stress-test: the controlled list contains no right word for main-line continuation.** `reform` is least-wrong but understates the least-departed line; the `reform_qualifier_fit` is graded **Low to encode the vocabulary strain, not doubt about the descent**, and a recommendation for a new `continuation`/`main-line` qualifier is routed to Anchor Fit.

**Net:** the family resolves into **one root (Second Temple Judaism)** and **a genuine fork — two branches from one parent**: Christianity (`schism`, with a sharpness caveat) and Rabbinic Judaism (`reform`, a strained stand-in for main-line continuation). The Christianity emergence/descent spine is the family's firmest result (High, inherited); everything else sits at Moderate or below, and the Rabbinic-continuation qualifier is the program's sharpest vocabulary strain to date. **Neither branch is the normatively "true" continuation; the register is neutral throughout — neither supersessionist nor polemical in any direction.**

# 3. What this finding does NOT establish (brackets)
- It does **not** fix a precise tradition-emergence date for Second Temple Judaism, nor for the separation of Christianity, nor for the rabbinic consolidation (all reported as contested where contested).
- It does **not** adjudicate the "one Judaism vs. several Judaisms" debate, the early-sharp vs. late-gradual parting debate, or the continuity-vs-rupture debate — each is steelmanned and costed into the grades, not resolved by fiat.
- It does **not** assert or characterise the deeper Israelite/Yahwistic root of Second Temple Judaism (deferred to a later family 2-deep) — **no edge on the hub**.
- It does **not** endorse `reform` as an adequate qualifier for Rabbinic Judaism — it flags the vocabulary gap (Anchor Fit).
- **Metaphysical bracket (acute):** no tradition's truth claims are affirmed or denied; **no supersessionist frame** (Christianity is not the fulfilment/replacement of Judaism; Rabbinic Judaism is not a residue) and **no polemical frame** in any direction.
- **No cross-family edges:** later Hellenistic/Greco-Roman inputs to Christianity, and the deferred Israelite root, are noted in prose, not modelled as edges.

# 4. Anchor Fit Assessment (ADR-GOV-0009 §7(a) — RECOMMENDATIONS, never self-applied)

Per **ADR-GOV-0007 §3**, every finding here is a **recommendation routed to GB-2026-041 / the review queue** — this investigation amends **no** standard, template, or tool. This family is the program's sharpest anchor test to date; the four reserved questions:

**(1) Qualifier-list fit — THE CENTERPIECE (continuation qualifier).**
The controlled `branches_from` qualifier list — `schism` / `reform` / `syncretic-descent` / `heterodox-offshoot` / `disputed` — **contains no right word for MAIN-LINE CONTINUATION**, which is exactly Rabbinic Judaism's case. Each listed value is wrong for it: `schism` (a break away — but Rabbinic Judaism is the line that stayed), `heterodox-offshoot` (a deviant current — but it is the mainline, and the label is mildly polemical), `syncretic-descent` (multi-parent blending — but it is single-line), `disputed` (uncertain descent — but the descent is the *least* uncertain thing; only the degree of continuity is contested). **`reform` is the least-wrong** (Rabbinic Judaism reconstituted the tradition around Torah and halakhah) **but understates the least-departed line as a departure-with-change.** The rupture and continuity rivals squeeze `reform` off-centre in *opposite* directions — the clearest sign the list, not the case, is at fault.
- **RECOMMENDATION (routed):** add a **`continuation`** (or `main-line`) qualifier to STD-0004 §7.2 for the case where a branch is the parent tradition's own line carried forward after a transforming event, rather than a departure from it. Until then, `reform` stands as least-wrong with the tension recorded and `reform_qualifier_fit` graded Low.
- **Christianity's `schism` fit (secondary):** `schism` is the least-wrong value for "separated to become distinct," but it connotes a sharp datable break, whereas the parting was gradual/late/uneven. **RECOMMENDATION (routed):** consider whether the qualifier vocabulary (or an edge annotation) should distinguish a *sharp* schism from a *gradual/protracted* separation; for now `schism` stands with the sharpness caveat recorded and `christianity_schism_qualifier` graded Moderate.

**(2) Edge semantics — the fork.**
`branches_from` is ENT→ENT, asymmetric, warrant-by-graded-claim. The fork (one parent, **two** edges — both on the children, none on the hub) represents the history honestly: **it correctly prevents the false reading that Second Temple Judaism "became" Christianity**, because the parent also has the Rabbinic edge, and the parent's `range_end_year: 70` shows the Temple-centred configuration superseded by *both* heirs. **However**, `branches_from` semantically reads as "derives from / departs from," which **misdescribes a main-line continuation** (Rabbinic Judaism does not "depart from" Second Temple Judaism so much as *carry it on*). The two-edge fork is structurally sound; the *label on the continuation edge* is the strain (see (1)).
- **RECOMMENDATION (routed):** with the `continuation` qualifier (1), `branches_from` can represent a fork-with-a-main-line honestly; no change to the ENT→ENT fork structure is needed.

**(3) Distinctness threshold (§2.3) — the hub.**
The tradition-vs-period criterion was applied to "Second Temple Judaism as one tradition vs. a period of several Judaisms." It **discriminated, but under strain**: the internal parties (Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, apocalyptic, Hellenistic) do **not** clear the bar as distinct traditions (no separate transmission lineage claiming to be a different religion; the common-Judaism substrate holds them as one), so the hub is modelled as **one internally-plural tradition**. This is a defensible simplification, not a demonstrated unity (hence Moderate). The nearest threshold-pressuring sub-stream is the **Essene/Qumran community** (a distinguishable community with its own literature) — but it is extinct, left no continuing tradition, and the sources treat it as a Second-Temple *sect*, so it does **not** clear the bar here.
- **RECOMMENDATION (routed):** the §2.3 threshold handles an internally-plural root adequately *for branching purposes* (both heirs descend from the shared matrix whichever party they trace to); flag that a root "internally plural" is a recurring pattern worth a note in the threshold guidance. No structural change proposed.

**(4) `tradition_type` observations.**
- Second Temple Judaism `emergent` (founded-via-Moses tension flagged).
- Christianity `emergent` (founded-via-Jesus/Paul tension flagged; `emergent` chosen for neutrality).
- Rabbinic Judaism `reform` — and note the **double-`reform` coincidence** (§2.8): the `tradition_type` value `reform` and the `branches_from` qualifier `reform` land on the same word but are different vocabularies with the same understatement problem.
- **RECOMMENDATION (routed):** the `tradition_type` vocabulary (`founded`/`emergent`/`reform`/`syncretic`) likewise lacks a clean "continuation" value; if a `continuation` qualifier is added (1), consider whether `tradition_type` needs a parallel refinement. No change made.
