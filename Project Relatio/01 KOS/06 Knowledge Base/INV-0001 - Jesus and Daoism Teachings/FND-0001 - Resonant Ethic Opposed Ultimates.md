# FND-0001

# Resonant Ethic, Opposed Ultimates

## Draft Finding Record

> First object created under the new Finding type (KOS-0012 §5.2). It extracts the synthesized answer that the pressure test had to fold into INV-0001 §5 because no Finding object then existed (closes Pressure Test Report F-2).

---

# 1. Statement

> The teachings attributed to Jesus in the canonical Gospels and those of philosophical Daoism (*Daodejing*, *Zhuangzi*) **resonate strikingly in an ethic of non-striving**, yet this shared surface **rests on opposed conceptions of ultimate reality** (a personal God vs. an impersonal Dao). The resemblance therefore illuminates each tradition by contrast as much as by likeness: the metaphysical divergence is precisely what prevents the practical convergence from amounting to sameness.

---

# 2. Supporting Claims

- **CLM-0001 (Non-Striving Convergence)** — establishes the genuine but bounded practical resonance (non-forcing, yielding, non-contention, nature imagery).
- **CLM-0002 (Ultimate-Reality Divergence)** — establishes the fundamental ontological contrast (personal/relational/moral God vs. impersonal/ineffable/non-agentive Dao).

The finding is the *integration* of the two: CLM-0002 qualifies CLM-0001.

---

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 3 — Moderate.** The two supporting claims are individually stronger (CLM-0001 ≈ Level 3; CLM-0002 ≈ Level 4), but the *integrated interpretive thesis* — that the divergence bounds the convergence — is a defensible reading rather than a fact, so the finding is held at the lower Level 3. A synthesis is no more confident than its weakest necessary link.

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations

- Textual, not historical (compares teachings as the corpora present them; historicity bracketed).
- Applies to the core corpora; apophatic Christian theology and quasi-religious readings of the Dao soften the edges.
- Cross-linguistic (Koine Greek / Classical Chinese via English) — lossy.
