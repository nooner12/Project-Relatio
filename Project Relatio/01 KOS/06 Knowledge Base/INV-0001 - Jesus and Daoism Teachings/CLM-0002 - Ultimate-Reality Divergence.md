# CLM-0002

# Ultimate-Reality Divergence

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim

> The teachings **diverge fundamentally on the nature of ultimate reality**: the canonical Gospels present a **personal, relational, moral** God (addressed as Father, inaugurating a Kingdom), whereas philosophical Daoism presents an **impersonal, ineffable, non-agentive** Dao. This ontological contrast propagates into their respective practices and goals.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)

**Metaphysical** (primary — it concerns the nature of fundamental reality as each tradition frames it) + **interpretive**. Metaphysical claims require philosophical analysis, assumptions, explanatory power, and competing models (KOS-0003 §3.6). Note: the claim is about *what each tradition asserts about* ultimate reality, not an adjudication of which is true (KOS-0004 ontological humility).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)

Type: **Historical/textual** + **philosophical**.

Gospels (SRC-0001):
- God as *Father*/*Abba* addressed in prayer (Matt 6:9); a *will* to be done (Matt 6:10); love, judgment, forgiveness; the coming *Kingdom* (*basileia*). Ultimate reality is personal, relational, moral, purposive.

Philosophical Daoism (SRC-0002):
- "The Dao that can be told is not the eternal Dao" (*DDJ* 1) — ineffable, unnameable source.
- "Heaven and earth are not *ren* [humane/benevolent]; they treat the ten thousand things as straw dogs" (*DDJ* 5) — non-anthropomorphic, non-moral-agent.
- The Dao generates spontaneously (*ziran*) without intention or personality.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)

- **Reliability: 5** — these are central, repeatedly attested framings in each corpus.
- **Relevance: 5** — directly on the nature of ultimate reality.
- **Independence: 4** — traditions historically unconnected; contrast is genuine, not constructed.
- **Quality: 4** — well-supported textually and corroborated by mainstream comparative philosophy of religion.
- **Limitations:** Some Daoist and Christian mystical strands complicate a hard binary (apophatic Christian theology also stresses divine ineffability; some read the Dao quasi-religiously). The contrast is strong at the level of the *core teaching corpora*, softer at the mystical margins.

---

# Source Evaluation

Per SRC-0001, SRC-0002. Corroborated externally by standard comparative-philosophy-of-religion treatments of the personal/impersonal-ultimate distinction.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)

- That "personal" vs "impersonal" is a valid axis for cross-tradition ontology (this axis maps onto KOS-0004's theism vs. immanent/non-dual models).
- That the *core* corpora (not later mystical commentary) define each tradition's baseline teaching.
- That translation preserves the personal/impersonal distinction (well-supported; less lossy than CLM-0001's posture comparison).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)

**Deductive/abductive.** Given the texts' explicit characterizations, the personal/impersonal contrast follows directly (deductive from the passages). Abductively, this contrast is the *best explanation* for the traditions' divergent practices (petitionary prayer and obedience vs. meditative attunement and accord).

**Reasoning risks checked:** false dichotomy (mitigated by acknowledging apophatic/mystical overlap at the margins); category error (mitigated by mapping to KOS-0004's ontological model-types rather than importing one tradition's categories into the other).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 4 — High.** Strong textual support, external corroboration, few unresolved issues at the level of the core corpora. Not Level 5 because the mystical margins genuinely complicate a strict binary.

---

# Limitations

- Applies to the core teaching corpora; apophatic Christian theology and religious-Daoist/quasi-theistic readings soften the edges.
- Concerns each tradition's *framing* of ultimate reality, not a verdict on which framing is correct (out of scope; KOS-0004 humility).

---

# Alternative Interpretations

1. **Convergent-mystical:** at the apophatic limit, "personal God beyond names" and "ineffable Dao" approach each other. (Acknowledged at the margins; rejected as the *baseline* reading.)
2. **Hard-incommensurability:** the frameworks share nothing and cannot be compared at all. (Rejected — the personal/impersonal axis is a coherent shared comparand.)
3. **Adopted:** a fundamental baseline divergence with softening only at the mystical margins.
