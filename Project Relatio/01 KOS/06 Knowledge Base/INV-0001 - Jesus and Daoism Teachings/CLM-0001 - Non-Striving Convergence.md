# CLM-0001

# Non-Striving Convergence

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim

> The teachings attributed to Jesus in the canonical Gospels and the teachings of philosophical Daoism **converge in counseling a posture of non-striving, yielding, and non-contention**, each expressed through nature imagery — but this practical resonance rests on divergent metaphysical grounding and should not be read as identity.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)

**Interpretive** (primary) + **comparative**. It asserts a similarity of *meaning/posture* across two textual traditions, not a historical or causal fact. Interpretive claims require framework identification, assumptions, and competing interpretations (KOS-0003 §3.3).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)

Type: **Historical/textual** (primary-text evidence from SRC-0001, SRC-0002).

Gospels (SRC-0001):
- "Do not be anxious about your life… consider the lilies… they neither toil nor spin" (Matt 6:25–34).
- "Do not resist the one who is evil… turn the other cheek" (Matt 5:39).
- "Blessed are the meek" (Matt 5:5).
- The seed grows *of itself* — *automatē* (Mark 4:26–29).

Philosophical Daoism (SRC-0002):
- *Wu wei* (無為, non-forcing), *ziran* (自然, self-so), *bu zheng* (不爭, non-contention).
- "The highest good is like water" (*DDJ* 8); "the softest overcomes the hardest" (*DDJ* 43); water overcomes the hard (*DDJ* 78).
- *Zhuangzi*: Cook Ding carving the ox by effortless accord with nature.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)

- **Reliability: 4** — passages are securely present in the corpora.
- **Relevance: 4** — directly on the theme of non-striving.
- **Independence: 4** — the two traditions are historically unconnected, so parallel themes are genuinely independent (not borrowing).
- **Quality: 3** — evidence is strong for *presence* of the theme; weaker for *equivalence* of meaning, which is interpretive.
- **Limitations:** Establishes that both corpora contain non-striving teachings; does **not** establish that they *mean the same thing*.

---

# Source Evaluation

Per SRC-0001 and SRC-0002. Both are transformative-intent primary texts read in translation; both contain counter-currents (selection risk).

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)

- That English renderings of *wu wei*/*ziran* and of Matthew's Greek preserve enough of the posture to compare (linguistic).
- That thematically selected passages fairly represent each corpus (methodological) — contested by counter-texts (Matt 10:34; strategic *wu wei*).
- That "posture/ethic" is a valid cross-tradition comparand (ontological/category).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)

**Analogical.** "Are the similarities sufficient to justify the comparison?" The analogy holds at the level of *practical orientation* (non-forcing, yielding, nature imagery). It weakens at the level of *grounding and telos* — which is why the claim is explicitly qualified rather than asserting identity.

**Reasoning risks checked:** confirmation bias (perennialist pull toward "all mysticism is one"); false equivalence (treating analogy as identity). Both are actively guarded against by the qualification and by CLM-0002.

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 3 — Moderate.** The *presence* of resonant non-striving teachings is well-supported (nearer Level 4); the *interpretive thesis that they meaningfully converge* is a defensible reading, not a fact — meaningful uncertainty remains. Overall Level 3.

---

# Limitations

- The convergence is at the practical surface; it does not extend to grounding (see CLM-0002).
- Both corpora contain assertive/strategic counter-currents that the claim does not accommodate in full.
- Cross-linguistic comparison is lossy.

---

# Alternative Interpretations

1. **Perennialist:** the traditions express one underlying mystical insight. (Rejected as overstatement — ignores CLM-0002.)
2. **Deflationary:** the resemblance is a translation artifact / coincidence of imagery with no real kinship. (Rejected as understatement — the thematic parallel is substantive and independent.)
3. **Adopted (middle):** a genuine but bounded practical resonance, qualified by metaphysical divergence.
