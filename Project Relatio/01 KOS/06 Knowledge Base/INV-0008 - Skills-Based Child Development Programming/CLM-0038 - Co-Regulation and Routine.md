# CLM-0038

# Predictable Routine, Warm Skilled-Adult Presence, and Graduated Skills Coaching (Co-Regulation) Support Children's Self-Regulation — as Universal Developmental Supports

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The developmental-science literature supports that **predictable routine, a warm and responsive skilled adult, felt safety, and graduated skills coaching** — together the process of **co-regulation** — support the development of children's **self-regulation**. These are best understood as **universal design features**, not interventions for identified (clinical) populations; where the underlying evidence comes from clinical/at-risk populations, that provenance limits general application.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal (developmental mechanism)**, resting on a **synthesis of developmental research** rather than a single trial; effect magnitudes are not quantified in the anchor.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical/synthesised** (federal practice brief synthesising developmental literature).
- **SRC-0051 (Rosanbalm & Murray 2017, OPRE 2017-79):** self-regulation develops through **co-regulation** — three caregiver supports: (1) warm responsive relationship, (2) structured/predictable environment, (3) skills instruction/coaching — working through **social cues, predictability, and a relationship experienced as safe**.
- **Design/population:** predominantly **caregiver–child (parenting)** developmental research, spanning birth to young adulthood; some strands draw on at-risk/clinical populations.
- **Effect direction/magnitude:** direction positive; **no effect sizes** (synthesis brief, not meta-analysis).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — authoritative federal/academic synthesis; but a brief, not primary trial evidence, and no pooled magnitudes.
- **Relevance: 3** — the design features (routine, felt safety, skilled adult, graduated coaching) map directly onto the proposed program; but the *setting* studied is caregiving, not community programs.
- **Independence: 3** — synthesises many sources; single catalogued anchor here.
- **Quality: 3** — strong conceptual grounding; weak on quantified, program-level causal proof.
- **Limitations:** establishes co-regulation as a **developmental support**; does **not** quantify effects, does **not** establish that a *community program's* co-regulatory features produce measurable self-regulation gains, and inherits **clinical provenance** where the underlying strands are clinical.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** — strong theoretical/developmental consensus; thin on quantified program-level outcomes.
- Consensus strength: **High** that co-regulation matters developmentally; **Low** on magnitude in non-caregiving settings.
- Relationship: supported as mechanism; unproven as a program lever.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0051: moderate–high authority (OPRE/FPG), transparent, program-neutral. It is a synthesis brief; treat conclusions as *well-grounded developmental consensus*, not measured effects.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Ontological:** self-regulation is a coherent, trainable capacity — well supported.
- **Methodological:** caregiver co-regulation findings transfer to non-parental skilled adults in programs — plausible, the key untested extension.
- **Provenance:** universal-design framing holds even where evidence is clinical — partly; flagged.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive/mechanistic.** **Risks checked:** *provenance laundering* (named — clinical-population evidence must not be silently upgraded to universal claims); *setting transfer* (named — parenting ≠ community program); *absence of magnitude* (named — no effect sizes, so no strong quantitative claim made).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8) — DUAL GRADE
- **Underlying-evidence confidence: Level 3 (Moderate)** — strong developmental consensus that co-regulation supports self-regulation, bounded by the synthesis-level evidence, absent magnitudes, and partial clinical provenance.
- **Application confidence (generic community heritage-skills program): Level 2 (Low)** — the design features are directly translatable in principle, but no located evidence shows a *community skills program's* predictability/felt-safety features produce measured self-regulation gains in children; the caregiver-to-program transfer is untested.

---

# Limitations
- No quantified effects; synthesis-level evidence.
- Caregiving-context evidence; program transfer untested.
- Universal-design framing partly rests on clinically-sourced strands.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"These features are inseparable from a good caregiver relationship and won't port to a weekly program."** A serious limit; accepted as bounding the application grade, not refuting the developmental claim.
2. **"Trauma-informed design only matters for traumatized children."** Rejected as framed — the co-regulation literature is developmental and universal; but the reverse caution (don't over-claim clinical benefit for general populations) is retained.
