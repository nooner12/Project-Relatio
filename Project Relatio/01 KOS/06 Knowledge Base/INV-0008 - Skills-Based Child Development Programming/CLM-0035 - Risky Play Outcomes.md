# CLM-0035

# Risky / Graduated-Challenge Outdoor Play Is Associated with More Physical Activity and Better Social Health, with Predominantly Minor Injuries; Self-Regulation/Anxiety Benefits Are Weakly Evidenced

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Access to **risky and graduated-challenge outdoor play** is associated with **increased physical activity and improved social health/behaviour**, and with **predominantly minor (not serious) injuries**. Claims that it builds **self-regulation, resilience, or reduces anxiety** are **plausible but weakly evidenced** — the field's enthusiasm exceeds the graded evidence.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive/associational** with a causal-leaning interpretation for the physical-activity strand; **mechanistic-hypothesis** status for the regulation/anxiety strand.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (systematic review of 21 mostly observational studies, GRADE-appraised).
- **SRC-0046 (Brussoni et al. 2015):** overall positive associations with health indicators, **most consistently physical activity**, plus social health and reduced aggression; exploration/"disappear-get-lost" play linked to higher activity in 7 of 8 relevant papers; injuries predominantly minor.
- **Design/population:** children; observational designs dominate; GRADE quality **low–moderate**.
- **Effect direction/magnitude:** positive direction; **magnitudes not pooled** (review does not report a single effect size); no clean numbers carried.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** (PA/social) / **2** (regulation/anxiety) — the latter rests on fewer, weaker studies.
- **Relevance: 4** — graduated physical challenge is a core ingredient of the proposed program.
- **Independence: 3** — single review; topic carries advocacy energy.
- **Quality: 3** — GRADE low–moderate; observational.
- **Limitations:** supports **PA and social** associations and a **minor-injury** profile; does **not** establish self-regulation/anxiety benefits, causal direction, or serious-injury safety as unconditional.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** (PA/social) / **Low** (regulation/anxiety).
- Consensus strength: **Moderate** that risky play supports activity and social development; **contested/thin** on regulation and anxiety.
- Relationship: supported for the physical/social strand; the regulation claim is more advocacy than evidence.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0046: high-authority GRADE review, transparent about weak evidence and its own promotional intent — which is exactly why the regulation/anxiety strand is held down.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** observational associations approximate causal effects — only partly safe.
- **Cultural:** "risk" is culturally framed; injury tolerance varies — bracketed.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** **Risks checked:** *enthusiasm/advocacy bias* (named — split the grade to isolate the over-claimed strand); *safety over-generalisation* (named — "minor injuries" is not "no serious risk"); *confounding* (active children may seek risky play, not vice versa).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8) — DUAL GRADE
- **Underlying-evidence confidence: Level 3 (Moderate)** for the **physical-activity/social-health** strand and the **minor-injury** profile; **Level 2 (Low)** for the **self-regulation/resilience/anxiety** strand.
- **Application confidence (generic community heritage-skills program): Level 2 (Low)** — graduated challenge is directly relevant, but the studied outcomes (free-play physical activity) are not the program's headline claims, and the regulation/anxiety benefits the program would most want are the least evidenced.

---

# Limitations
- Observational; no pooled effect sizes.
- "Minor injuries" finding must not be read as endorsing unsupervised or unbounded risk.
- Regulation/anxiety outcomes are mechanistic hypotheses, not demonstrated.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"Reverse causation — active/regulated kids choose risky play."** A real confound; lowers the causal reading, not the association. Carried forward.
2. **"Risk competence transfers to emotional regulation."** The field's steelman; plausible mechanism, but the review does not evidence it, so it stays a hypothesis (feeds the application-grade ceiling).
