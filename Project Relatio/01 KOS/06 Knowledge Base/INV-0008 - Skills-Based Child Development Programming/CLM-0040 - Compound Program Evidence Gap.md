# CLM-0040

# No Located Study Evaluates the Compound Program as a Whole; Its Benefit Is an Evidence-Informed Hypothesis, Not a Demonstrated Outcome

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **No located study evaluates the specific compound intervention** — heritage/craft skills + outdoor/nature setting + non-parental adult mentoring + predictable routine/felt safety + family-ritual/intergenerational framing — **as a single, integrated program.** The component literatures support the *ingredients* to varying degrees, but the claim "a community heritage-skills program improves child development outcomes" is an **evidence-informed hypothesis assembled by analogy**, not a demonstrated result. This is why every domain's **application grade is bounded to Level 1–2** regardless of underlying-evidence strength.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — a claim about the *state of the evidence base* (a demonstrable absence), plus a **normative/methodological** implication for how confidently the compound may be asserted.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Meta-evidential** (absence established by the structure of the located literature).
- Each catalogued source (SRC-0046…SRC-0052) studies **one** ingredient in **one** setting (a greenspace program, a mentoring match, an academic self-efficacy sample, caregiver co-regulation, family rituals). **None** combines them, and none studies the heritage-skills bundle.
- Established program models nearest the application (e.g., BBBS friendship mentoring; forest school; greenspace NBIs) each differ from the compound on **content, dose, or delivery**.
- No effect size can be assigned to the compound because **no trial of it exists** — and none was invented (KOS-0003 §12.1).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 4** — the absence is robust: a structured search across six literatures surfaced no compound evaluation.
- **Relevance: 5** — directly governs how the whole finding may be used.
- **Independence: 4** — the gap is visible across all six independent literatures.
- **Quality: 4** — an absence-of-evidence claim, appropriately bounded (absence of evidence ≠ evidence of absence of benefit).
- **Limitations:** establishes that the compound is **untested**, not that it is **ineffective**; a well-designed program could still work — it simply is not *demonstrated* to.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High** that no integrated evaluation exists.
- Consensus strength: n/a (a structural observation, not a contested claim).
- Relationship: this claim **caps** the application grades of CLM-0034…CLM-0039.

---

# Source Evaluation
Rests on the absence observed across SRC-0046…SRC-0052 and the general child-development program literature searched this session; the compound-search is representative, not exhaustive — a targeted evaluation could exist and have been missed (flagged).

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Epistemological:** ingredient evidence does not automatically transfer to a bundle (interactions, dose, fidelity can change effects) — well supported methodologically.
- **Methodological:** the session's search is broad enough to license "no located study" — probable but not certain; stated as *located*, not *existent*.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive.** Best explanation of the literature's structure: the compound is a **plausible synthesis of separately-studied parts**, not itself a studied object. **Risks checked:** *absence-of-evidence fallacy* (named — untested ≠ ineffective); *search incompleteness* (named — "located," not "exists"); *composition fallacy* (named — the parts working separately does not entail the whole works, or works additively).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8) — DUAL GRADE
- **Underlying-evidence confidence: Level 4 (High)** — that no located study evaluates the compound and that ingredient evidence does not license a compound claim.
- **Application confidence: n/a** — this claim *is* the application caveat; it sets the Level 1–2 ceiling on the other domains' application grades rather than making a positive application claim.

---

# Limitations
- "No located study" is bounded by this session's search; a niche evaluation could exist.
- Establishes untested-ness, not ineffectiveness.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"Enough strong ingredients justify asserting the compound works."** Rejected — the composition fallacy and the small/conditional nature of the best-evidenced ingredients (mentoring d ≈ 0.21) make the leap unsafe.
2. **"Absence of a trial means we should assume no benefit."** Rejected symmetrically — absence of evidence is not evidence of absence; the honest position is *undemonstrated*, not *disproven*.
