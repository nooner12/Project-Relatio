# CLM-0036

# Mastery (Enactive) Experiences Are the Strongest Source of Self-Efficacy, Which Is Moderately Associated with Persistence and Achievement

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> In Bandura's social-cognitive theory, **mastery (enactive) experiences are the most influential source of self-efficacy**, and **self-efficacy is moderately and reliably associated with academic persistence and performance**. A skills-mastery activity plausibly builds the self-efficacy this pathway describes — though the *program-level* effect is an applied inference, weaker than the underlying association.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal (theoretical mechanism) + descriptive (meta-analytic association).** Mastery → self-efficacy is a validated theoretical mechanism; self-efficacy ↔ achievement is a measured (reciprocal) association.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (meta-analysis) + **theoretical** (Bandura).
- **SRC-0053 (Bandura 1997, *Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control*; orig. 1977):** the theoretical anchor for the "strongest source" half — self-efficacy is built from four sources (enactive mastery, vicarious experience, verbal persuasion, physiological/affective states), of which **enactive mastery experience is the most influential.** This is the mechanism a skills-mastery program targets.
- **SRC-0050 (Multon, Brown & Lent 1991):** the empirical anchor for the association half — meta-analysis finding self-efficacy positively and significantly related to **academic performance and persistence** across subjects/designs; self-efficacy accounted for **~14% of variance in performance** (r ≈ .38, reported as approximate).
- **Division of evidentiary labour (RQ-0008 remediation):** SRC-0053 carries "mastery = strongest source"; SRC-0050 carries "self-efficacy ↔ achievement." Neither source alone supports both halves — the claim rests on the pair.
- **Design/population:** students in academic settings; **correlational and reciprocal** (achievement also feeds efficacy).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 4** — foundational, heavily replicated meta-analysis; Bandura's framework among the best-validated in psychology.
- **Relevance: 3** — the mechanism (mastery builds efficacy) maps well onto skills programming; the *measured outcomes* are academic, not craft/heritage.
- **Independence: 4** — many independent studies pooled.
- **Quality: 4** — high for the association; but correlational and reciprocal, not experimental proof that raising mastery raises outcomes.
- **Limitations:** establishes the **association and the mechanism**; does **not** prove a *program* raising mastery experiences will causally raise achievement/persistence, nor that craft mastery transfers to academic or life outcomes.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High** for the self-efficacy–achievement association and for mastery as its primary source.
- Consensus strength: **High** — mainstream, textbook-level.
- Relationship: strongly supported; the debate is about causal direction/size, not existence.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0053 (Bandura): very-high-authority foundational theory; anchors "mastery = strongest source" (four-source hierarchy confirmed via secondary summaries this session; no figure drawn from it). SRC-0050 (Multon): high authority, transparent, independent; anchors the self-efficacy↔achievement association. The exact per-outcome correlations were not re-derived to the decimal this session; the ~14%/r ≈ .38 figures are reported as approximate.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Epistemological:** self-report efficacy measures index a real capability belief — well supported.
- **Methodological:** correlational association licenses an intervention inference — this is the weak link, quarantined into the application grade.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive/inductive** — the best explanation of the robust efficacy–achievement link plus the mastery-source theory is that mastery experiences build efficacy that supports persistence. **Risks checked:** *reverse causation* (named — reciprocal; prior achievement raises efficacy too); *domain-transfer* (named — academic efficacy ≠ craft efficacy ≠ global self-worth); *false precision* (per-outcome decimals flagged approximate).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8) — DUAL GRADE
- **Underlying-evidence confidence: Level 4 (High)** — that mastery is the strongest efficacy source and that self-efficacy is moderately, reliably associated with persistence/achievement. Not Level 5: correlational/reciprocal, and one anchor meta-analysis carried.
- **Application confidence (generic community heritage-skills program): Level 2 (Low)** — the mechanism is highly plausible for a skills program, but no located study shows that *heritage-skills mastery in a community program* produces measured self-efficacy or downstream outcomes; the causal-from-correlational and domain-transfer gaps both bite.

---

# Limitations
- Correlational/reciprocal; not proof that programs move the outcome.
- Outcomes measured are academic; transfer to craft/heritage/life domains unestablished.
- Single meta-analytic anchor; exact correlations approximate.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"Achievement drives efficacy, not the reverse."** Genuine (reciprocal) — refines to bidirectional, does not remove the mechanism's relevance.
2. **"Self-efficacy is domain-specific and won't generalise from craft to school/life."** A strong limit; accepted as bounding the application grade rather than refuting the underlying claim.
