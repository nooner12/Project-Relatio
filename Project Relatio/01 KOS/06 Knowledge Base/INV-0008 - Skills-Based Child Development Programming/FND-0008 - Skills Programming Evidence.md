# FND-0008

# The Ingredients of Structured, Adult-Mentored, Skills-Based Programming Are Individually Supported to Varying Degrees; the Compound Program Is a Reasonable, Evidence-Informed Hypothesis, Not a Demonstrated Intervention

## Draft Finding Record

> **Health/high-stakes gate (STD-0006 §7.5):** Child-facing programming is health/high-stakes class. All findings in this investigation are gated from any client-facing or external reliance until independently re-verified outside Project Relatio's internal circuit (different model and/or qualified human review). Until then they inform internal design work only.

---

# 1. Statement
> **(a) The ingredients are individually supported — unevenly.** Across six literatures, the components of structured, adult-mentored, skills-based programming have **real but mostly modest and largely associational** support. The **best-evidenced pathways** are **skill mastery → self-efficacy** (mastery is the strongest source of self-efficacy; self-efficacy is moderately, reliably associated with persistence and achievement — CLM-0036, underlying **Level 4**) and **structured non-parental mentoring** (small but real average benefits, d ≈ 0.21, conditional on relationship quality and program structure; corroborated by a true RCT — CLM-0037, underlying **Level 4**). **Nature exposure** (short-term attention/inattention benefits — CLM-0034), **risky/graduated play** (physical-activity and social benefits with predominantly minor injuries — CLM-0035), and **co-regulation / felt-safety design** (predictable routine + warm skilled adult supports self-regulation — CLM-0038) are each **Moderate** underlying but thinner or shorter-term. **Family routines/rituals** are the **weakest** strand — a confounded, cross-sectional correlation (CLM-0039, underlying **Level 2**).
>
> **(b) The compound program as a whole is untested.** No located study evaluates the specific bundle — heritage/craft skills + nature setting + mentoring + predictable routine/felt safety + family-ritual framing — as one integrated intervention (CLM-0040, **Level 4** that the gap exists). The claim "a community heritage-skills program improves child development outcomes" is therefore an **evidence-informed hypothesis assembled by analogy**, not a demonstrated result. This caps every domain's **application confidence at Level 1–2** regardless of how strong its underlying science is.
>
> **(c) Honest bottom line.** The design is **plausible and, in two of its pathways, well-grounded**; it is not reckless to build on this evidence. But the *specific recipe* has **not been shown to work as a whole**, the effect sizes that do exist are **small** (mentoring), the benefits a program most wants (self-regulation, resilience, anxiety reduction) are the **least evidenced**, durability is largely **unstudied**, and nothing in the located evidence isolates **heritage/craft content** as adding value over any structured skill. The right posture is **build-and-evaluate**, not **build-and-assume.**

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Compound-program application: Level 2 (Low).** The finding's *application* verdict is capped at its **weakest necessary link** and at the integrative gap (CLM-0040): because no study tests the bundle and ecological transfer from each studied intervention to community heritage-skills programming is weak, the application claim cannot exceed **Low** — with mentoring the one domain reaching toward **Low–Moderate**.

**Underlying-evidence picture: mixed, Level 2–4 by domain.** Two pathways (mastery/self-efficacy; mentoring) are genuinely **High**; three are **Moderate**; one (family ritual) is **Low**. The finding does **not** collapse these into a single number — the per-domain table is the honest representation.

**No claim or synthesis reaches Level 5** (reserved; cross-domain analogical transfer does not warrant it). The synthesis is a **convergence of separately-studied ingredients**, which is genuine but interpretive and — critically — **not a validation of the compound**.

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations (what is NOT supported)
- **NOT supported:** that "a community heritage-skills program improves child development outcomes" as a compound — this is untested (CLM-0040).
- **NOT supported:** large effects. The best-quantified pathway (mentoring) is **small** (d ≈ 0.21); BBBS RCT effects were small.
- **NOT supported:** the outcomes a program most wants — self-regulation, resilience, anxiety reduction — as *demonstrated* program results; these are the least-evidenced strands (CLM-0035 regulation strand; CLM-0038 lacks magnitudes).
- **NOT supported:** any *specific* value of **heritage/craft content** over generic structured skill — no located evidence isolates it.
- **NOT supported:** durability — most evidence is short-term or single-follow-up.
- **NOT addressed at all:** any individualized child/clinical claim (out of scope and gated).
- **Provenance limit:** the co-regulation/felt-safety features rest partly on **clinical/at-risk-population** research; the universal-design framing is reasonable but must not import clinical-population effect claims.
- **Motivated-reasoning guard:** the culturally attractive conclusion ("a wholesome outdoor mentored skills program is good for kids") **partly survives** (mastery + mentoring pathways) but the *compound* claim does **not**; application grades were held at Level 1–2 deliberately, not softened.

# 5. Implication (offered — not an endorsement, and gated)
At the literature level, the evidence supports a **build-and-evaluate** posture: a program that (i) centres **genuine skill mastery** with graduated challenge, (ii) secures **durable, high-quality, well-matched non-parental mentoring relationships** with adults trained in program best practices, and (iii) provides **predictable routine and felt safety** (co-regulation) is aligning with the **strongest** available pathways. This is a **design orientation associated with the better-evidenced ingredients — not a demonstrated intervention, not individualized advice, and gated (STD-0006 §7.5) from external reliance** pending independent re-verification. Establishing whether any particular program actually delivers these features, and with what effect, is an **empirical evaluation task outside this finding's scope.**
