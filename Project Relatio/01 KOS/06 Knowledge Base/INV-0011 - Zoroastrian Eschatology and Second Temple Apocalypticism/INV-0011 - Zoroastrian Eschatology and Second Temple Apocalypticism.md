# 1. Research Question

**Primary (authoritative wording — do not improve it):**

> To what extent, and through what evidenced pathways, did Zoroastrian eschatology shape Second Temple Jewish apocalypticism — the stream the earliest Jesus movement (the Nazarenes) inherited?

**Decomposition mandate.** Break the question into discrete, separately gradable claims across the candidate doctrines: **resurrection of the dead; individual/final judgment; cosmic dualism and periodized history; angelology/demonology; and a savior figure (Saoshyant parallel).** For each doctrine, three sub-questions must be answered explicitly:

- **(i) The dating problem (load-bearing).** Is the *Zoroastrian* doctrine itself datable to **before** the Jewish attestation? This is where the Avesta's oral-transmission history and the lateness of the Pahlavi books (9th c. CE) bite: the systematic Zoroastrian eschatological corpus is attested late, while the Jewish apocalyptic corpus (Daniel, 1 Enoch, Qumran) is datable and earlier. If the Zoroastrian doctrine cannot be shown to predate the Jewish text it allegedly influenced, the influence thesis is not established for that doctrine.
- **(ii) The contact pathway.** Is there an *evidenced* channel of transmission — Achaemenid administration of Yehud/Judea, the exilic and post-exilic Persian period, Magi/priestly contact — as opposed to mere parallel?
- **(iii) The alternative.** Does the influence thesis survive the strongest rival explanation, **independent internal Jewish development** (inner-biblical growth, Hellenistic-era crisis, prophetic-tradition trajectories)?

**Chain terminus (mandated).** At least one claim must sit on the **Nazarene / earliest-church** end of the chain — what the earliest Jesus movement's apocalyptic frame demonstrably *received from Second Temple apocalypticism* — so the terminus of the influence chain is in view, not merely its Persian origin.

**Register.** Neutral, scholarly, comparative-historical. **No confessional framing in any direction.** The live dispute — **influence vs. independent development** — must be represented at its strongest on both sides; a genuinely split verdict at honest confidence *is* an acceptable and expected answer (§6, Non-Negotiable 7 of OPS-0003).

# 2. Scope & Disambiguation

## 2.1 What "Zoroastrian eschatology" denotes here
The cluster of end-time and afterlife doctrines associated with the Zoroastrian tradition: bodily **resurrection** (*ristāxēz*), **individual judgment** at the Chinvat Bridge and a **final/general judgment**, the **frashokereti** (the "making wonderful," cosmic renovation), **cosmic dualism** (Ahura Mazda vs. Angra Mainyu / Ohrmazd vs. Ahriman) organized as a **periodized world-history** running to a final resolution, developed **angelology/demonology** (Amesha Spentas, yazatas; daevas), and the **Saoshyant**, a future benefactor/savior. The investigation must keep separate the **Old Avestan (Gathic) layer**, the **Younger Avestan** layer, and the **Pahlavi (Middle Persian) books** (e.g., Bundahišn, Dēnkard, Zand ī Wahman Yasn), because they are attested at very different dates — the crux of sub-question (i).

## 2.2 What "Second Temple Jewish apocalypticism" denotes here
The apocalyptic stream of Second Temple Judaism (roughly 3rd c. BCE–1st c. CE): **Daniel** (esp. 7–12), the **Enochic** literature (1 Enoch — Book of the Watchers, Book of Dreams, etc.), **Qumran/Dead Sea Scrolls** dualism (e.g., the Two Spirits treatise in the Community Rule, the War Scroll), and related texts, carrying resurrection, judgment, angelology/demonology, dualistic periodized history, and messianic/eschatological deliverer expectation.

## 2.3 The Nazarene terminus
"Nazarenes" = the earliest Jewish followers of Jesus as a Second-Temple Jewish sect, whose eschatological frame (resurrection of the dead, imminent judgment, two-age periodization, angel/demon cosmology, a coming deliverer) is inherited from Second Temple apocalypticism. The claim on this end is about **what the sect demonstrably received from the Jewish apocalyptic stream** — not a fresh Persia→Christianity claim, and not a theological claim about Jesus.

## 2.4 Excluded by construction
- **Not comparative teaching/ethics.** This is a historical-influence and dating question, not a comparison of doctrines' *content* for resonance (that would overlap INV-0001; it does not).
- **No theological adjudication** of any tradition's truth.
- **No ranking of doctrines by importance/magnitude** absent a stated metric.
- **Manichaeism, Zurvanism, later Islamic-era developments** are out of scope except where a source uses them to date an earlier layer.
