# CLM-0056

# The Evidential Asymmetry Between the Late-Attested Zoroastrian Corpus and the Firmly-Datable Jewish Apocalyptic Corpus Is the Load-Bearing Constraint on Every Influence Claim — the Systematic Zoroastrian Eschatology Is Written Down Centuries *After* the Jewish Texts It Is Compared With, So Directional Priority Must Be Argued by Reconstruction, Not Read Off Manuscripts

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The single most important methodological fact governing this investigation is an **evidence-class asymmetry**: the Zoroastrian primary corpus was transmitted **orally** for roughly a millennium and written down only under the Sasanians (Avesta, c. 6th–7th c. CE) and later (the systematic Pahlavi eschatology, 9th c. CE), whereas the Jewish apocalyptic texts it is compared with (Book of the Watchers ~3rd c. BCE; Daniel ~165 BCE; Qumran 2nd–1st c. BCE) are **firmly and independently datable primary manuscripts**. Therefore any *directional-priority* claim (Zoroastrian doctrine X predates Jewish doctrine Y) rests on **linguistic reconstruction (H-e)** of the Avestan layers and/or on **later tradition attributing content backward (H-d)** — never on a contemporaneous Zoroastrian manuscript. This asymmetry caps the confidence of every influence claim in the investigation.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / methodological** — a statement about the state and class of the evidence, not itself an influence verdict.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (philology of transmission).
- **SRC-0073 (Skjærvø):** the Avesta was composed 2nd–early 1st millennium BCE, transmitted orally for ~a millennium-plus (with editorial standardization and later scribal reshaping), and written down only under the Sasanians in a purpose-made script; the Old vs Younger Avestan layers are distinguished by comparative philology (reconstruction).
- **SRC-0074 (de Jong):** models the source-critical discipline; warns against retrojecting late systematic doctrine into early periods.
- **SRC-0076 (Zoroastrian corpus):** the systematic eschatology (resurrection sequence, three Saoshyants, periodized ages) is in the 9th-c.-CE Pahlavi books (Bundahišn, Zand ī Wahman Yasn), which *claim* to preserve lost Avestan nasks (H-d).
- **SRC-0071 (Collins):** supplies the firm Jewish-side dates.

**Dual-coding (prose only):** this claim *is* the H-band machinery. Zoroastrian-side early-dating evidence is systematically **H-e (reconstruction)** and **H-d (later tradition)**; Jewish-side attestation is systematically **H-a (datable primary texts)**. The asymmetry (H-e/H-d vs H-a) is the finding's spine.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 5 — the transmission history and the Jewish dating are both mainstream, well-established scholarship.
- Relevance: 5 — it governs every other claim.
- Independence: 5 — it is technical philology/dating, not a position in the influence dispute; it constrains all parties equally.
- Quality: 5 — this is the securest claim in the investigation.
- Limitations: it establishes the *evidence class*, not any doctrine's actual history; it neither proves nor disproves influence — it sets the burden of proof.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High**.
- Consensus strength: **High** — the oral-transmission/late-fixation history of the Avesta and the dating of the Jewish corpus are not seriously contested; even pro-influence scholars (Boyce, Hultgård) accept the transmission facts and argue *around* them by asserting faithful preservation.

---

# Source Evaluation
The dating/transmission sources (SRC-0073, SRC-0074) are technical and independent of the influence dispute; this is why the claim can carry High confidence while the influence claims cannot.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that comparative philology reliably orders the Avestan layers (accepted; itself reconstruction but robust).
- **Epistemological:** that a doctrine's *written attestation date* is the appropriate default anchor for priority, absent a demonstrated faithful-transmission argument — this is the disciplined default (and the point of contention with maximalists).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Deductive/descriptive.** **Risks checked:** *treating oral antiquity as if it were attestation* (named — the core error the claim guards against); *over-skepticism* (named — the claim does **not** assert the doctrines are *late in origin*, only that their *early dating is not attested by manuscript*; genuine antiquity is possible and is what pro-influence scholars argue).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 4 (High)** — that this evidence-class asymmetry holds and is load-bearing for every influence claim. Not Level 5: Level 5 is reserved; and the precise absolute dating of the Avestan layers (and thus the exact size of the asymmetry per doctrine) is itself reconstruction, which introduces the small residual uncertainty appropriate to High rather than Very High.

---

# Limitations
- Establishes the burden of proof and the H-band asymmetry; does **not** by itself settle any doctrine's influence verdict.
- Does not claim the Zoroastrian doctrines are *late in origin* — only that their *early priority is reconstructed, not attested*.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Faithful-oral-transmission maximalism (Boyce/Hultgård).** Steelmanned: a conservative priesthood may have preserved archaic doctrine verbatim, so late attestation need not mean late doctrine. **Partly accepted** — it keeps genuine antiquity live — but it is an *argument*, not attestation, and does not dissolve the asymmetry; it is precisely the move that must be *demonstrated* per claim, not assumed.
2. **Radical skepticism (nothing early can be known).** Rejected as over-reach — the relative layering is robust; the Gathic material is genuinely archaic even if undatable absolutely.
