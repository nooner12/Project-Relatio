# CLM-0055

# Zoroastrian Influence on Second Temple Jewish Savior/Messianic Expectation (via the Saoshyant) Is the Weakest Doctrine Case — the Parallel Is Generic, the Distinctive Saoshyant Features Are Late-Attested (Pahlavi, H-d) and Do Not Map onto the Davidic Messiah, and Jewish Messianism Has Deep Independent Internal Roots

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The Second Temple **eschatological-deliverer / messianic** expectation was **most likely not** meaningfully shaped by the Zoroastrian **Saoshyant**. This is the **weakest of the five doctrine cases**: the parallel is generic ("a future deliverer"), the Saoshyant's *distinctive* features (miraculous conception from Zoroaster's preserved seed; three successive world-saviors; the agent who effects the universal resurrection) are systematically attested only in the late Pahlavi corpus (H-d) and do **not** map onto the Jewish Davidic-royal messiah, and Jewish messianism has robust independent roots (the Davidic covenant, 2 Samuel 7; the anointed king; Isaiah's royal oracles).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Interpretive / causal (directional-influence)** — here reaching a *negative-leaning* verdict (influence not supported).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (textual attestation + comparative typology).
- **SRC-0076 (Zoroastrian primary corpus):** the Saoshyant is *named* in the Younger Avesta (Yasht 13.129; described Yasht 19.88–96) but the developed **three-saviors** scheme and the savior's role in the resurrection are systematic only in the Pahlavi Bundahišn (9th c. CE) — later than the Jewish material and attributing backward (H-d).
- **SRC-0072 (Hultgård):** documents the Iranian savior figure but frames Iranian influence generally as *stimulus*, not source; does not press a strong Saoshyant→messiah derivation.
- **SRC-0067 (Barr):** the messianic parallel is a textbook case of a generic resemblance mistaken for a channel.
- **SRC-0071 (Collins):** Jewish messianism develops from internal royal/Davidic ideology and priestly/prophetic expectation, datable within the Second Temple corpus (H-a).
- **Jewish side:** the Davidic messiah (Psalms of Solomon 17; Qumran messianic texts; the "anointed one") derives transparently from 2 Samuel 7 and royal ideology, not from a resurrection-effecting cosmic savior.

**Dual-coding (prose only):**
- **Zoroastrian-side H-band for early dating: H-e** (Younger Avestan *naming* of the Saoshyant, reconstructed date) → **H-d** for the distinctive systematic scheme (Pahlavi). Both the dating *and* the specificity are weak.
- **Jewish-side H-band: H-a** (Davidic-messianic texts) — with a strong internal Hebrew Bible antecedent (2 Samuel 7), also H-a.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 — attestations real; the linkage is weak and the distinctive features late.
- Relevance: 4 — on the savior/messiah question.
- Independence: 3 — poles represented; even the pro-influence pole is muted here.
- Quality: 2 — generic parallel; distinctive Saoshyant features are late and non-mapping.
- Limitations: establishes only a generic "future deliverer" resemblance; the specific Saoshyant profile is late-attested and does not correspond to the Jewish messiah.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Low** for any directional influence.
- Consensus strength: broad agreement that Jewish messianism is largely **internally derived** (Davidic ideology); the Saoshyant parallel is generally treated as weak precisely because of the dating problem and the non-correspondence of features.

---

# Source Evaluation
This is the case where even the pro-influence sources are cautious and the measured/skeptical sources are firm — the convergence runs *against* strong influence. SRC-0076's own layering shows the distinctive savior scheme is late.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Cultural/linguistic:** that "Saoshyant" (benefactor who effects the renovation) and "messiah" (anointed Davidic king) name a comparable role — largely false at the level of distinctive features; true only at the generic level.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive**, reaching a negative-leaning verdict. **Risks checked:** *generic-parallel inflation* (named and decisive — "both expect a deliverer" is too thin to carry a channel); *late-attestation smuggling* (named — the Pahlavi three-saviors scheme must not be retrojected to the Achaemenid period to manufacture priority); *convenient-derivation bias* (named — a Persia→messiah→Christ pipeline is a popular narrative and is resisted here). The internal Davidic source is steelmanned as sufficient.

---

# Sub-Question Answers (INV-0011 §1)

> Explicit, separable answers to the three sub-questions INV-0011 §1 mandates for every doctrine claim (acceptance criterion #2). Authored at v0.2 from the existing source base (SRC-0067…SRC-0076) only; these answers **restructure and make discrete** the analysis already carried in this record — they change no evidence and no confidence level.

## (i) Dating — is the Zoroastrian doctrine datable *before* the Jewish attestation?

**No — the name is early-by-reconstruction only, and everything distinctive is late.** The Saoshyant is *named* in the Younger Avesta (Yasht 13.129; described Yasht 19.88–96; SRC-0076), with that layer's pre-Jewish date resting on linguistic reconstruction (H-e). The **distinctive** Saoshyant profile — miraculous conception from Zoroaster's preserved seed, the three successive world-saviors, the savior as agent of the universal resurrection — is systematically attested only in the 9th-c.-CE Pahlavi Bundahišn (H-d), centuries *after* the datable Jewish messianic texts (Psalms of Solomon 17, Qumran messianic texts — H-a), and the Jewish stream's own taproot (2 Samuel 7) is far earlier. **For the features that would make the parallel meaningful, documented attestation runs Jewish-side-first; no Zoroastrian priority is established.**

## (ii) Contact pathway — is there an evidenced doctrine-specific transmission channel?

**No. This claim inherits CLM-0057's general verdict (attested opportunity, unattested channel); no doctrine-specific channel evidence exists for the savior figure.** No source in the base evidences transmission of the Saoshyant expectation to Jewish circles — and here even the pro-influence pole declines to assert one (SRC-0072 Hultgård frames Iranian influence as diffuse stimulus, not a savior-doctrine handover). The explicit negative stands.

## (iii) Independent development — does the influence thesis survive the strongest rival explanation?

**The strongest rival is independent development from the Davidic-royal tradition, and the influence thesis does not survive it.** Steelmanned: Jewish messianism derives transparently from the Davidic covenant (2 Samuel 7), the royal psalms, and prophetic restoration hope, individualized into the "anointed one" of Second Temple expectation (SRC-0071 Collins — datable, internal, sufficient); the resulting figure (an anointed Davidic king restoring Israel) shares with the Saoshyant only the generic feature "future deliverer," while the Saoshyant's distinctive features are late-attested (item (i)) and non-mapping (a resurrection-effecting cosmic benefactor, not a royal restorer). The rival explains everything the Jewish evidence contains without remainder; influence adds nothing the internal account needs (SRC-0067 Barr's generic-resemblance diagnosis). **What survives is only an unexcludable shared "future deliverer" horizon — which is why the claim sits at Level 2 (Low) as a verdict *against* meaningful influence rather than lower, and why the popular Persia→messiah→Christ pipeline is explicitly resisted.**

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 2 (Low)** — and the Low is a verdict *against* meaningful influence: the evidence does not support a Saoshyant→Jewish-messiah influence, though a generic eschatological-deliverer resemblance exists. Held at Low rather than Level 1/0 because a diffuse shared "future savior" horizon is not nothing and cannot be positively excluded; but the distinctive features and dating both fail, so no higher level is warranted, and the honest reading leans negative.

---

# Limitations
- Licenses "influence not supported; only a generic resemblance," **not** any positive derivation claim, and **not** the popular "Persian savior → Jewish messiah → Christ" pipeline.
- Says nothing theological about Jesus (out of scope by construction, §2.3).

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Internal Davidic/royal development.** Steelmanned and *preferred*: 2 Samuel 7, royal psalms, and prophetic restoration hope produce Jewish messianism without Persia. This is the main reason the case is weak.
2. **Generic shared eschatological horizon.** A future-deliverer expectation is widespread; the resemblance may be convergent. Held open at Low.
3. **Late cross-fertilization the other way.** The systematic Saoshyant scheme is late enough that, if anything, some directionality could run toward Iran from the wider apocalyptic milieu — not asserted, but the dating does not permit the opposite to be assumed.
