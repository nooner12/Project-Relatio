# CLM-0053

# Zoroastrian Influence on Second Temple Cosmic Dualism Is the Single Strongest Doctrine Case — the Qumran "Two Spirits" Treatise Presents a Structurally Specific Parallel to the Gathic Two-Spirits Teaching — Yet It Reaches Only Moderate Confidence Because the Parallel's Directionality Is Still Inferred and the Periodized-Ages Component Rests on Late (Pahlavi) Attestation

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The **cosmic-ethical dualism** of parts of Second Temple Judaism — most strikingly the **"Two Spirits" treatise** in the Qumran Community Rule (1QS III–IV: a Prince of Light and an Angel of Darkness under God, humanity apportioned between them, running to a determined end) — is the **strongest single case** for Zoroastrian influence, because it parallels the **Gathic teaching of two primordial spirits** (Yasna 30.3–5) with unusual structural specificity. **Even so it reaches only Moderate confidence**: the direction of borrowing is still inferred rather than documented, Qumran's dualism is subordinated to strict monotheism (an adaptation, not an adoption), and the *periodized world-history* element (the "ages/metals" scheme) is systematically attested only in the late Pahlavi Zand ī Wahman Yasn (H-d).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Interpretive / causal (directional-influence)**.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (textual attestation + close structural comparison).
- **SRC-0076 (Zoroastrian primary corpus):** the two primordial spirits (Spenta Mainyu / Angra Mainyu) choosing good and evil are **Gathic** (Yasna 30.3–5) — the oldest layer. The *periodized* cosmic history (limited time divided into ages) is developed later; its systematic "ages" form is Pahlavi (Zand ī Wahman Yasn, 9th c. CE).
- **SRC-0070 (Shaked):** dualism is his strongest case for real Iranian influence on Judaism, operating by selective adaptation.
- **SRC-0069 (Cohn):** the periodized-combat cosmology is distinctively Zoroastrian and entered Jewish thought post-conquest.
- **SRC-0067 (Barr):** even a specific parallel needs a demonstrated channel; strict Jewish monotheism reshapes any borrowed dualism, so "influence" cannot mean "adoption."
- **Jewish side:** the Two Spirits treatise, 1QS III–IV (2nd–1st c. BCE) (H-a — datable primary text); the War Scroll's light-vs-darkness cosmic battle.

**Dual-coding (prose only):**
- **Zoroastrian-side H-band for early dating: H-a → H-e** for the *core two-spirits dualism* (Gathic textual presence H-a; absolute date H-e) — the best-dated of the influence-strong cases; **H-d** for the *periodized-ages* component (late Pahlavi, attributing backward).
- **Jewish-side H-band: H-a** (1QS).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 — the two-spirits parallel is textually specific on both sides; Gathic dualism is well-attested-early.
- Relevance: 5 — directly on the dualism question.
- Independence: 3 — poles represented; Shaked (measured) independently rates this the strongest case.
- Quality: 4 — the structural specificity of the 1QS parallel is the highest-quality parallel in the investigation.
- Limitations: establishes a specific parallel with a good dating position; does **not** document the transmission channel, and the periodized-ages sub-component is late-attested.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate–High** for the two-spirits parallel; **Low–Moderate** for a demonstrated channel and for the periodized-ages priority.
- Consensus strength: **Leaning toward influence** here more than in any other doctrine — even cautious scholars (Shaked) grant Iranian dualistic influence at Qumran — but not unanimous, and "adaptation under monotheism" is the agreed framing.

---

# Source Evaluation
This is the case where the pro-influence pole (Cohn) and the measured pole (Shaked) most nearly converge, which is why it is the strongest doctrine case. The skeptical pole (Barr) still bites on the *channel* and on the reshaping by monotheism.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** Gathic dualism dates early (reconstruction).
- **Ontological/cultural:** that a *subordinated* dualism under one God (1QS) and a *primordial* dualism (Gathas) are comparable enough to count as the "same" doctrine — partly true, partly an important disanalogy (Qumran's dualism is created and subordinate, not primordial).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive.** **Risks checked:** *the strongest-parallel trap* (named — a specific parallel is the most seductive; but specificity of *form* is still not a documented *channel*); *monotheist-reshaping* (named — Qumran dualism is subordinated, so "adoption" would over-read it); *late-attestation smuggling* (named — the periodized-ages scheme must not lend its Pahlavi lateness to the strongly-dated two-spirits core, nor vice versa).

---

# Sub-Question Answers (INV-0011 §1)

> Explicit, separable answers to the three sub-questions INV-0011 §1 mandates for every doctrine claim (acceptance criterion #2). Authored at v0.2 from the existing source base (SRC-0067…SRC-0076) only. Items (i) and (ii) restructure analysis already in this record; item (iii) is **newly authored** here — the independent-development rival had the thinnest prior treatment in this claim — and changes no evidence and no confidence level.

## (i) Dating — is the Zoroastrian doctrine datable *before* the Jewish attestation?

**Split answer — the two components must be kept apart (per the late-attestation-smuggling check above).** The **two-spirits core** (Spenta Mainyu / Angra Mainyu choosing good and evil, Yasna 30.3–5; SRC-0076) is **Gathic** — textual presence in the oldest layer (H-a), with absolute pre-Jewish date resting on the ~1000 BCE Gathic reconstruction (H-e). Against the firmly-datable 1QS Two Spirits treatise (2nd–1st c. BCE, H-a), the core's relative priority is well-supported though its attested priority is still reconstruction-dependent. The **periodized-ages component** (the systematic ages/metals world-history) is attested only in the late Pahlavi Zand ī Wahman Yasn (9th c. CE, H-d) — *later* than Daniel's four-kingdoms scheme, so for that component **no priority is established and the documented attestation runs the other way**. **Answer: yes-by-reconstruction for the two-spirits core; no for the periodized-ages component.**

## (ii) Contact pathway — is there an evidenced doctrine-specific transmission channel?

**No. This claim inherits CLM-0057's general verdict (attested opportunity, unattested channel); no doctrine-specific channel evidence exists for dualism.** This negative is especially load-bearing here, because dualism is the strongest parallel in the investigation and therefore the case most tempted by the structural-specificity-as-channel move: even the most specific parallel (1QS ↔ Yasna 30) is form-resemblance, not a documented transmission event (SRC-0067 Barr's channel demand; SRC-0070 Shaked names adaptation as the mode, not a channel). Stating the negative explicitly is what keeps this case at "strongest parallel," not "demonstrated borrowing."

## (iii) Independent development — does the influence thesis survive the strongest rival explanation?

**The strongest rival is independent development from internal monotheist resources, and the influence thesis survives it — narrowly and only for the two-spirits core — because the rival explains the *materials* well but the *specific binary architecture* of 1QS less well.** Steelmanned at full strength: (a) the **two-ways motif** is deeply inner-biblical (Deuteronomy 30:15–19's "life and good, death and evil" choice; the two-ways psalmody of Psalm 1), giving sectarian Judaism a native ethical binary; (b) the **light/darkness polarity is already monotheistically absorbed** in the Persian-period Hebrew Bible itself — Isaiah 45:7 ("forming light and creating darkness") places both poles inside the one God's agency, showing Second Temple religion could generate and control this polarity internally; (c) **Hellenistic-era sectarian polarization** (the community's insider/outsider crisis) supplies a live social engine for hardening a two-ways ethic into a two-camps cosmology; and (d) the Qumran dualism's own shape — **created, subordinated to one God, and eschatologically terminated** (1QS III–IV) — is exactly what an *internal* development would look like, whereas a borrowing would need to explain why the primordial-spirits structure was so thoroughly re-founded. The rival is genuinely strong. **It nevertheless falls short of fully displacing influence on one point: the *specific* two-predestined-spirits-apportioning-humanity-under-a-determined-end architecture of 1QS III–IV has no clean inner-biblical antecedent** — Deuteronomy's two ways are chosen, not apportioned; Isaiah 45:7 absorbs the polarity rather than personifying it — and that residual specificity, paired with the Gathic core's early attestation (item (i)), is why even the measured pole (SRC-0070 Shaked) grants dualism as the strongest influence case. **The influence thesis survives as "plausible, likely as selective adaptation," and the strength of this rival is a principal reason the claim stays at Level 3 (Moderate) rather than higher — while the rival's residual failure on 1QS's specificity is why the claim does not fall lower.**

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** — that Zoroastrian dualism plausibly influenced Second Temple (especially Qumran) dualism; this is the **strongest doctrine case** but still Moderate. **Why not Level 4:** the direction is inferred, not documented (no channel); Qumran subordinates its dualism to monotheism (adaptation); and the periodized-history sub-component is late-attested (H-d). **Why not lower:** the 1QS/Gathas structural parallel is the most specific in the investigation and the two-spirits core is well-dated-early — so this case is bounded *above* Moderate's peers even though it shares their ceiling.

---

# Limitations
- Licenses "strongest influence case, plausible at Moderate," **not** "established," and **not** "Qumran dualism is Zoroastrian."
- The two-spirits core and the periodized-ages component must be kept distinct (they carry different H-bands); no single verdict covers both cleanly.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Internal/other-source development.** Steelmanned: sectarian dualism can grow from inner-Jewish wisdom traditions, the two-ways motif (Deuteronomy 30), and Hellenistic-era sectarian polarization. Weaker here than for judgment, because the *specific* two-spirits structure is hard to derive internally — but not excluded.
2. **Selective adaptation under monotheism (Shaked).** Accepted as the likely mode: real influence, thoroughly reworked. Compatible with Moderate.
3. **Periodized-ages as later convergence.** The metals/ages scheme's systematic Zoroastrian form is later than Daniel's four-kingdoms; for that sub-component, priority is not established and could run the other way. Held open.
