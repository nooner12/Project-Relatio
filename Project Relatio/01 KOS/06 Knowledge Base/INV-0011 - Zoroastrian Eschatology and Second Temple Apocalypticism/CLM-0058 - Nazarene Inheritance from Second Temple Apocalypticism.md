# CLM-0058

# The Earliest Jesus Movement (the Nazarenes) Inherited a Thoroughly Apocalyptic Frame — Resurrection of the Dead, Imminent Final Judgment, Two-Age Periodization, an Angel/Demon Cosmology, and a Coming Eschatological Deliverer — *from Second Temple Jewish Apocalypticism*; This Closes the Chain as a Jewish-Stream-to-Sect Inheritance, and Is Independent of (and Far Better Attested Than) the Contested Persia-to-Judaism Link Upstream

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The apocalyptic frame of the earliest Jesus movement — a Second-Temple Jewish sect — was **inherited wholesale from Second Temple Jewish apocalypticism**: bodily resurrection of the dead, an imminent final judgment, a two-age ("this age / the age to come") periodization, an angel-and-demon cosmology, and a coming eschatological deliverer (the Danielic "Son of Man" horizon). This is a **Jewish-stream→sect inheritance** and is **well-attested and broadly held**. Crucially, this terminus is **logically and evidentially independent of the upstream Persia→Judaism question**: whatever the (contested, at most Moderate) Zoroastrian contribution to the Jewish stream, the sect's inheritance *from that Jewish stream* is direct, datable, and secure — and this claim makes **no** fresh Persia→Christianity assertion and **no** theological claim about Jesus.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — about what the earliest movement's texts demonstrably carry.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (datable primary texts).
- **SRC-0071 (Collins):** the earliest Christian imagination is continuous with the Jewish apocalyptic genre; Collins's subtitle for one edition — "the Jewish matrix of Christianity" — names exactly this inheritance.
- **Jewish-side / earliest-movement primary texts (H-a):** Paul (esp. 1 Thessalonians 4–5; 1 Corinthians 15 — resurrection of the dead, the two ages, imminent judgment), the Synoptic apocalyptic material (Mark 13), and the pervasive Son-of-Man language, all draw directly on Daniel and the Enochic/Second-Temple apocalyptic repertoire. These are datable 1st-c.-CE primary texts.

**Dual-coding (prose only):**
- **Relevant H-band: H-a on both ends of *this* link** — the Second Temple apocalyptic sources (Daniel, Enoch, Qumran) and the earliest-movement texts (Paul, Gospels) are all datable primary manuscripts. This link has **none** of the H-e/H-d dating problem that afflicts the Persia→Judaism links; that is precisely why it is the securest substantive claim in the chain.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 — the apocalyptic character of the earliest movement is mainstream, well-evidenced scholarship (Schweitzer→Sanders→Allison lineage).
- Relevance: 5 — it is the mandated chain terminus.
- Independence: 4 — the primary texts are independent of the Iranian dispute entirely.
- Quality: 4 — strong; the main interpretive variance is over *how* apocalyptic the historical Jesus was, not over the movement's frame.
- Limitations: establishes inheritance *from the Jewish stream*; says nothing about the upstream Persian contribution and nothing theological.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High**.
- Consensus strength: **High** — that the earliest Jesus movement was thoroughly apocalyptic and inherited its frame from Second Temple Judaism is a strong scholarly consensus; debates are internal (degree, emphasis), not about the fact of inheritance.

---

# Source Evaluation
This claim rests on the best-attested, least-contested evidence in the investigation — datable primary texts on both ends of the link — which is why it, with CLM-0056, forms the firm scaffolding around the Moderate/Low doctrine verdicts.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that Paul and the early Gospel material are reliable witnesses to the movement's *frame* (not necessarily to every event) — well supported.
- **Historical:** that "Nazarenes" denotes the earliest Jewish followers as a Second-Temple sect (per §2.3), not a later heresiological category — the term is used in this scoped sense.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive from primary texts.** **Risks checked:** *chain-conflation* (named and central — the temptation to let the *contested* Persia→Judaism link borrow the *secure* Jewish→sect link's confidence, or vice versa; the two are firewalled here); *confessional framing* (named — no theological claim about Jesus is made or implied); *over-reading the historical Jesus* (named — the claim is about the *movement's frame*, sidestepping the narrower historical-Jesus debate).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 4 (High)** — that the earliest Jesus movement inherited a thoroughly apocalyptic frame from Second Temple Jewish apocalypticism. Not Level 5 (reserved; and the degree/emphasis of the movement's apocalypticism carries some interpretive variance). This is the **firm terminus**: it does not depend on, and must not be inflated by, the upstream influence verdicts.

---

# Limitations
- Licenses "the sect inherited its apocalyptic frame from the Jewish stream," **not** any Persia→Christianity claim (that would have to pass through the Moderate/Low upstream links) and **not** any theological claim.
- Does not adjudicate the historical Jesus's own views beyond the movement's inherited frame.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Non-apocalyptic earliest movement (e.g., a sapiential-Jesus reading).** Steelmanned: some scholars foreground a wisdom/sapiential Jesus. Even so, the *movement's* frame (Paul; Mark 13) is apocalyptic on the earliest datable evidence, so the inheritance claim holds at the movement level regardless of the historical-Jesus debate. Not rejected wholesale; bounded to the movement's frame.
2. **Hellenistic/mystery-religion framing.** Steelmanned but weaker for the *earliest* Aramaic-speaking Jewish movement, whose categories are transparently Second-Temple Jewish apocalyptic; Hellenistic reframing is a later-layer question.
