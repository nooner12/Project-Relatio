# 2. Steelman (given before attacking)
This is careful, honest work, and its central architecture is correct. It refuses the two confessional attractors by name and in structure: the perennialist "Judaism borrowed its eschatology from Persia" pipeline and the apologetic "no influence at all" — and it does not let the skeptical pole (Barr) harden into blanket denial (SRC-0067 §3 explicitly forbids that misreading). It does the genuinely hard thing an influence investigation must do: it makes the **evidence-class asymmetry** (late-attested Zoroastrian corpus vs firmly-dated Jewish texts) the load-bearing constraint (CLM-0056) and then actually enforces it — **no doctrine is allowed to reach "established," however seductive the parallel.** The internal-development alternative is steelmanned in every doctrine claim and is explicitly *preferred* where it is stronger (angelology's divine council; the savior's Davidic covenant). The strongest parallel in the set (1QS Two Spirits ↔ Yasna 30, CLM-0053) is held to Moderate despite its specificity, on the correct grounds that structural specificity is not a documented channel and that Qumran's dualism is subordinated to monotheism (adaptation, not adoption) — a distinction I verified is exactly how the mainstream literature frames it ("closest parallel… still monotheized"). The Nazarene terminus (CLM-0058) is firewalled with unusual discipline: it makes no Persia→Christianity claim and no theological claim about Jesus, and it is explicitly barred from lending or borrowing confidence across the firewall. Scale discipline is clean. The flags below are calibration and precision sharpening, not demolition.

# 3. The adversarial pass (the brief's named tests)

**Test A — press CLM-0051 (resurrection) hardest. Verdict: Level 3 is at or above its ceiling; a criterion-grounded case for Level 2 exists. FLAGGED (F-1) — lower or defend.**
This is my primary finding. Following the Barr line the brief named, and grounding it in KOS-0003 §8's own level definitions rather than in disagreement-as-taste:
- **§8 Level 2 (Low)** reads: *"Limited support; **major unresolved issues or significant disagreement**."* **§8 Level 3 (Moderate)** reads: *"Reasonable support; meaningful uncertainty; interpretation may vary."*
- CLM-0051's **own Consensus field** records the field as **"Divided — a genuine, live split,"** and its own analysis concedes (a) the Zoroastrian early-dating priority is **reconstruction only (H-e)**, (b) the **systematic resurrection form post-dates Daniel (H-d)**, and (c) **"the Maccabean-crisis internal pathway is sufficient on its own."** "Significant disagreement" + "major unresolved issue" (undemonstrable priority) is, by the letter of §8, at least as good a fit for **Level 2** as for Level 3.
- **The decisive comparative point:** CLM-0051, CLM-0052 (judgment), and CLM-0053 (dualism) all sit at Level 3, but they are **not evidentially equal**. Judgment has the **best dating** (Gathic, H-a textual presence) and dualism has the **most specific parallel** *and* the least disagreement ("leaning toward influence"). Resurrection is the **only** one of the three that is weakest on **both** axes at once — worst dating (H-e→H-d, post-Daniel systematic form) *and* "Divided" consensus. Under the investigation's own §3.1 rule ("confidence bounded by the weakest load-bearing link… a doctrine whose early Zoroastrian attestation rests only on H-d/H-e cannot support a high-confidence directional claim, however strong the parallel, because the direction of documented attestation may actually run the other way"), resurrection is the claim whose Level-3 parity is least earned. The Specialist uses parallel-distinctiveness to lift resurrection to parity with the better-dated doctrines; that is a coherent move, but it is exactly the move §3.1 cautions against.
- **Independence sub-finding (folded into F-1).** CLM-0051's pro-influence early-dating case is *not* two independent lines. SRC-0069 (Cohn) **explicitly inherits SRC-0068 (Boyce)'s** early-dating and faithful-oral-transmission assumptions (disclosed in SRC-0069 §2). So the pro-influence priority argument for resurrection substantially reduces to **one contested line (Boyce)**, which Barr and de Jong dispute. The Independence score of 3 is therefore mildly generous, and this reinforces the downward pressure.
- **New evidence supporting the pressure (my verification):** even Yasht 19's early date is **only "presumably" Achaemenid**, and I found that *some scholars dispute even that* Yasht 19 uniquely attests resurrection. The Specialist's phrasing ("the *one undeniable* attestation") is appropriately hedged and accurate — but the underlying datum is more fragile than a Level-3 directional claim comfortably carries.
- **Remedy:** F-1. This does not overturn the claim; resurrection is a defensible, textbook influence *candidate*. But I apply downward pressure per ROLE-0004's mandate.

**Test B — CLM-0053 dualism: is the structural specificity overstated? Verdict: NO. Conformant.**
I verified the 1QS III–IV Two Spirits ↔ Yasna 30 parallel against the scholarship. The Two Spirits Treatise is standardly called the **"closest parallel"** to Zoroastrian dualism in the corpus, and the mainstream framing is exactly the one CLM-0053 uses: real structural contact **but monotheized/subordinated** (adaptation, not adoption). The claim does not overstate — it names the disanalogy (Qumran's dualism is created and subordinate, not primordial), keeps the periodized-ages component (late Pahlavi, H-d) analytically separate from the Gathic two-spirits core, and refuses Level 4 on the correct grounds (inferred direction; no channel; monotheist reshaping). This is the best-calibrated of the doctrine claims.

**Test C — the four citation clusters the Specialist could not fully verify.** Spent the bulk of my verification budget here (§1). **Result: every one that I could reach turned out real and, where checkable, precise** — Skjærvø's flagged title is genuine; Cereti's edition is exact; the Encyclopaedia Iranica Yasht 19 phrasing (the item that had returned HTTP 403 for the Specialist) is confirmed verbatim via the article and cross-listings. Boyce's page-level citations remain the one genuinely unpinned item, honestly disclosed. **No fabrication in any cluster.**

**Test D — scale discipline (Appendix A boundary rule).** Checked every frontmatter block and every grading field across INV-0011, FND-0011, CLM-0051–0058, SRC-0067–0076. **Native `Level N` is the only on-record confidence rating; H-bands appear only in prose and in the §4.1 / SRC dual-coding sections; the §5-style 0–5 scores are KOS-0003 evidence scores, not H-bands; no ★-glyph appears anywhere in any object.** Clean. This is a genuine pass, not a formality — the discipline held under a protocol explicitly designed to tempt leakage.

**Test E — scope and firewall.** Exactly 8 claims (CLM-0051–0058); no drift into general comparative religion; no theological adjudication; "strongest/weakest *case*" is repeatedly and explicitly defined as evidential strength of the influence claim, **not** doctrine magnitude (no smuggled ranking). The Nazarene terminus is firewalled with discipline: CLM-0058 makes no Persia→Christianity claim, no historical-Jesus claim, and FND-0011 §4 explicitly bars assembling the doctrine claims with CLM-0058 into a Persia→Christianity chain. **No firewall breach found.**
