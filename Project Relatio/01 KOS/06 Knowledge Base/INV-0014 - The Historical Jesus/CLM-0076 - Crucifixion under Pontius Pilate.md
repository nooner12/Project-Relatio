# CLM-0076

# Jesus Was Crucified — HIGH Confidence, Anchored in the Contemporary Pauline Layer and the Earliest Creed — and the Sources Place the Execution under Pontius Pilate — MODERATE Confidence on the Layer Evidence, with Scholarly Consensus Running Above the Evidence Class (a KOS-0003 §9 Case) — a SPLIT Grade by Component

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **(i) The fact of crucifixion:** Jesus was executed by crucifixion. **(ii) The Pilate specification:** the execution took place under the prefecture of Pontius Pilate (c. 26–36 CE), by Roman sentence. The two components carry **different load-bearing layers and different grades** — the fact rests on the contemporary Pauline layer; the Pilate specification rests on the Gospel narratives and the non-Christian notices — and are graded separately (a split claim, not averaged).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — an event-of-execution claim; the theological interpretations of the death ("for our sins," resurrection) are bracketed and not graded here.

---

# Element (a) — Attestation & Layer (INV-0013 inheritance)
**Component (i), crucifixion:**
- **Undisputed Paul** — [[CLM-0068 - Dating of the Undisputed Pauline Letters|CLM-0068, Level 4 (High)]]: crucifixion is pervasive and load-bearing in the earliest documents (1 Cor 1:23, 2:2, 2:8; Gal 3:1, 3:13; Phil 2:8) — written by a contemporary in contact with the victim's family and followers.
- **The pre-literary creed** — [[CLM-0074 - The Pre-Literary Tradition|CLM-0074, Level 4 (High), inherited]]: "Christ died … and was buried" proclaimed within a few years of the event.
- **Non-Christian notices** — [[CLM-0073 - Non-Christian Attestation|CLM-0073, Level 3 (Moderate)]]: Tacitus (*Ann.* 15.44, the "extreme penalty"); the Testimonium's core (condemned to the cross), partially authentic.
- **Inherited bound (i):** Level 4 — the Pauline/creedal layer.

**Component (ii), under Pilate:**
- **Gospel passion narratives** (all four, independent in detail but sharing the tradition) — dated at [[CLM-0069 - Dating of the Gospels|CLM-0069, Level 3 (Moderate)]].
- **Tacitus** names Pilate explicitly ("suffered the extreme penalty … at the hands of … Pontius Pilatus") and the **Testimonium core** does likewise ("condemned by Pilate") — both at [[CLM-0073 - Non-Christian Attestation|CLM-0073, Level 3 (Moderate)]].
- **Paul does not name Pilate** in the undisputed letters (1 Tim 6:13 does, but sits in the Pastorals — [[CLM-0072 - Pauline Corpus Authorship Gradient|CLM-0072]] grades the Pastorals majority-pseudonymous, Level 3, so it cannot anchor the specification at the H-a layer).
- **Inherited bound (ii):** Level 3 — the strongest load-bearing layers for the Pilate specification (Gospel narratives; external notices) are both Moderate.

---

# Element (b) — Authenticity Criteria
- **Multiple independent attestation** (component i): Paul, the creed, the passion tradition, Tacitus, the Testimonium core — as convergent as ancient-history evidence gets. For (ii): Gospels + Tacitus + Testimonium core; fewer and later lines, but still multiple.
- **Embarrassment** at full strength for (i): crucifixion was the shameful slave's death, a proclamation "stumbling block" (1 Cor 1:23) no movement invents for its founder. It bears less on (ii): naming Pilate carries no embarrassment either way.
- What the criteria fail to establish: the narrative details of the passion accounts (trial scenes, dialogue), which are **not** graded here; the claim is the execution and its administrative frame only.

---

# Element (c) — Rival Explanation (Steelmanned)
**For (i):** the mythicist reading (celestial crucifixion in the sub-lunar realm — Carrier's strongest form) — answered at CLM-0075 element (c) and additionally weakened here: the creed's "died … was buried" and Paul's living contacts with the victim's brother leave no room for a non-terrestrial execution within the tradition's own first years. No serious non-mythicist rival to the fact of crucifixion exists in the field (a swoon/survival reading grades the aftermath, not the execution).
**For (ii):** the strongest rival is **conventionalization** — that "under Pilate" entered the tradition as a chronological anchor because Pilate was the remembered prefect of the period, and Tacitus/Josephus repeat the movement's own dating rather than independent records. Steelmanned: Tacitus plausibly had no archival source for a provincial execution; the Gospel narratives post-date the event by four decades; the specification could be tradition's rounding rather than record.
**Why the claim survives (ii):** Pilate's prefecture is independently documented (Josephus, Philo, the Pilate stone), the execution falls within it on every dating of the movement's origin (the CLM-0074 terminus), and no rival administrative frame is attested anywhere in the tradition. **Cost:** the specification stays at Level 3 — consensus treats it as secure, but the evidence class (reconstructed narratives + secondary notices) is what the layer rule grades.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- **SRC-0103** (primary texts): 1 Cor 1:23, 2:2, 2:8; Gal 3:1, 3:13; Phil 2:8; the four passion narratives; (1 Tim 6:13 noted with its authorship discount).
- **SRC-0099** (testimonia): Tacitus *Ann.* 15.44; the Testimonium core.
- **SRC-0125** (Theissen & Merz): crucifixion under Pilate as the firm terminal datum of mainstream reconstruction.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 (component i — contemporary letters + early creed) / 3 (component ii — reconstructed narratives + secondary notices).
- Relevance: 5 — direct.
- Independence: 4 (i) / 3 (ii — Tacitus's independence uncertain).
- Quality: 4 (i) / 3 (ii).
- Limitations: the passion narratives' details are not graded; only execution + administrative frame.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High** (i); **Moderate** (ii).
- Consensus strength: **Near-universal for both** — Sanders places death by crucifixion on Pilate's order among the "almost indisputable facts." For (ii) this is a **§9 consensus-exceeds-evidence case** (matching the CLM-0069 pattern): consensus High over a Moderate evidence class, recorded separately, not blended into the grade.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- The theological meaning of the death is bracketed (KOS-0003 §3.6) — only the event-of-execution and its frame are graded.
- The Testimonium core's authenticity follows INV-0013's partial-authenticity position (CLM-0073), not re-derived.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive convergence, split by component.** The fact of crucifixion inherits the full strength of the contemporary layer; the Pilate frame inherits only the reconstructed/secondary layers, and the split grade keeps the two from borrowing each other's strength. **Risks checked:** *strength-borrowing* (the named risk this claim is structured against — "crucified under Pilate" as a single graded unit would let the L4 fact carry the L3 frame); *consensus-as-evidence* (§9 separation held for component ii); *narrative creep* (passion-narrative details excluded from the claim).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Component (i) — crucifixion: Level 4 (High).** Bounded by CLM-0068/CLM-0074 (Level 4).
- **Component (ii) — under Pilate: Level 3 (Moderate).** Bounded by CLM-0069/CLM-0073 (Level 3); consensus above the class recorded per §9, not graded into the number.
- No component reaches Level 5.

---

# Limitations
- No passion-narrative detail (trials, dialogue, chronology of the day) inherits either grade.
- The theological interpretation of the death is bracketed entirely.
- The year within Pilate's prefecture (commonly 30 or 33) is not graded — a finer claim than the evidence supports.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Mythicist celestial-execution** — steelmanned at CLM-0075(c); additionally fails here against the creed's burial clause and the living-witness context.
2. **"Pilate as conventional anchor"** — element (c) component (ii); the reason the specification is held at Moderate rather than High.
3. **Jewish-execution primacy** (the Sanhedrin as executing authority) — a minority reading of John 18:31's implication in reverse; crucifixion is a Roman penalty and the Roman sentence is multiply attested; noted, not sustained.
