# CLM-0080

# The Earliest Sources Report, and Jesus' Contemporaries Reputed, That He Performed Exorcisms and Healings — the REPUTE Held at MODERATE Confidence on Multiple Attestation Including a Hostile Concession — While the Events Themselves, as Supernatural Occurrences, Are Strictly BRACKETED in Both Directions and Graded at No Level

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Jesus was **reputed by contemporaries and reported by the earliest sources** to perform exorcisms and healings; this repute belongs to the earliest recoverable layer of the tradition and was shared even by opponents (who attributed the activity to demonic agency rather than denying it). **What is graded is the repute and the report** — that this is what the man was known for. **The events themselves — whether anything supernatural occurred — are bracketed in both directions** (KOS-0003 §3.6): this record neither asserts that miracles happened nor asserts that they did not; that question is outside the historian's tools. Naturalistic redescriptions (psychosomatic response, ancient illness-categories) are likewise **not** graded — they are interpretations of the bracketed events, not part of the claim.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical — a repute-and-report claim.** The bracket is load-bearing: the gradable object is the *reputation*, which is an ordinary historical datum; the *events* are metaphysically contested and are not graded.

---

# Element (a) — Attestation & Layer (INV-0013 inheritance)
- **The exorcism/healing tradition is pervasive across every narrative strand**: Markan miracle cycles, double-tradition material (the Beelzebul controversy; the answer to John), M and L special material, and John's independent sign-tradition — the load-bearing layer is the Gospel strata, [[CLM-0069 - Dating of the Gospels|CLM-0069, Level 3 (Moderate)]], with the strand-count qualification of [[CLM-0070 - Markan Priority and the Synoptic Problem|CLM-0070]] (Q at Level 2).
- **The hostile stratum**: the Beelzebul accusation ("he casts out demons by the ruler of demons" — Mark 3:22 and the double tradition, i.e., attested in two strands *if* Q stands, one visible-rewriting chain if not) preserves an **opponent's concession**: the charge disputes the *source* of the power, not the activity.
- **External echo**: the Testimonium's core calls Jesus a "doer of startling deeds" (*paradoxōn ergōn poiētēs*) — within the partially-authentic portion as INV-0013 grades it, [[CLM-0073 - Non-Christian Attestation|CLM-0073, Level 3 (Moderate)]]; a genuine but interpolation-shadowed external attestation of the *repute*.
- **Later hostile tradition** (the Talmudic sorcery notice, b. Sanh. 43a) is **H-d late polemic** — noted as consistent with an entrenched repute, given no independent weight (it post-dates the period by centuries).
- **Paul is silent** on Jesus' miracles — the H-a layer does not carry this claim; disclosed, and part of why the grade has a ceiling.
- **Inherited bound:** Level 3 — Gospel strata (CLM-0069) and the external echo (CLM-0073) are both Moderate.

---

# Element (b) — Authenticity Criteria
- **Multiple attestation** at strength for the *repute*: every narrative strand and multiple forms (miracle stories, sayings about miracles, controversy dialogues, the summaries) carry it; Meier's judgment (SRC-0127) — the tradition is too broad to be a late accretion in toto.
- **Embarrassment / hostile witness**: the Beelzebul charge is the criterion's cleanest use on this claim — a tradition would not invent an accusation that its founder worked by demonic power; preserving it concedes the accusation existed, and the accusation concedes the activity's reputation.
- **What the criteria fail to establish:** any individual miracle story's historicity (each narrative is a separate, weaker question — not graded here); and, categorically, the **events' supernatural character** — no criterion reaches past the report to the occurrence (Meier's own boundary, matching the bracket).

---

# Element (c) — Rival Explanation (Steelmanned)
**Legendary accretion, at its strongest:** wonder-working was the expected décor of ancient holy men — the topos is documented (Hellenistic wonder-workers, Apollonius of Tyana; charismatic Jewish figures — Honi the Circle-drawer, Hanina ben Dosa); miracle stories demonstrably grow in the tradition (later Gospels heighten; apocryphal Gospels run wild); the Beelzebul controversy could be a church-era polemic exchange retrojected; the Testimonium phrase may be colored even in its "core." On this rival, the repute is the standard posthumous inflation of a revered teacher, telling us about the movement's milieu, not the man's activity.
**Why the claim survives:** the rival explains growth but starts too late — the repute is already load-bearing in the earliest stratum (Mark's structure is unintelligible without the exorcist-healer frame; the controversy tradition presupposes the activity as common ground between Jesus and opponents), and the topos argument cuts both ways (a milieu-only explanation must still say why *this* teacher attracted *this* topos while John the Baptist — more famous by Josephus's measure — attracted none of it: the tradition distinguishes where the topos theory predicts uniformity). The accretion rival rightly claims the *growth* of the tradition and many individual narratives; it does not dislodge the early, hostile-conceded repute.
**Cost to confidence:** real — the repute claim stays at Moderate, and every individual narrative stays ungraded.

---

# The Bracket, Held in Both Directions (load-bearing discipline)
- **Not asserted:** "Jesus performed miracles" (a supernatural event-claim — outside the historian's tools).
- **Not asserted:** "Jesus performed no miracles" / "the reports are false" (the debunking assertion is equally an event-claim on bracketed territory).
- **Graded:** "Jesus was reputed an exorcist and healer, from the earliest recoverable layer, conceded even by opponents" — an attestation-and-repute datum, Level 3.
- The naturalistic redescriptions (psychosomatic response to a charismatic healer; ancient nosology labeling as possession what moderns label otherwise) are *compatible* readings often paired with the repute datum in the literature (Theissen & Merz, SRC-0125), recorded here as interpretive options — **not graded**, because grading them would adjudicate the bracketed events by the back door.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- **SRC-0103** (primary texts): the Markan miracle cycles and summaries; Mark 3:22 parr. (Beelzebul); the double-tradition sayings (Matt 12:28 par.; the answer to John, Matt 11:4–6 par.); John's sign-tradition.
- **SRC-0127** (Meier vol. 2): the exhaustive treatment — repute early and multiply attested; events beyond adjudication.
- **SRC-0125** (Theissen & Merz): Jesus as healer/exorcist in mainstream reconstruction; the in-league-with-the-devil charge.
- **SRC-0099** (testimonia): the Testimonium core's "startling deeds" phrase, at its INV-0013 partial-authenticity grade.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 — narrative strata plus a hostile-concession stratum; the external echo interpolation-shadowed.
- Relevance: 5 — direct for the repute.
- Independence: 3 — cross-strand; hostile stratum is the strongest independence feature; Q-uncertainty carried.
- Quality: 3 — repute-attestation is solid; nothing item-level is usable.
- Limitations: repute only; no individual narrative; events bracketed.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate.**
- Consensus strength: **High** — that Jesus was known to contemporaries as an exorcist/healer is mainstream common ground (Sanders, Meier, Theissen & Merz; including scholars who treat all miracle *events* as non-historical — the repute/event distinction is the field's own). Mild §9 surplus, recorded.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- The repute/event distinction is coherent and the bracket enforceable (KOS-0003 §3.6) — the record's load-bearing assumption, stated openly.
- The Testimonium's partial-authenticity position is consumed from CLM-0073, not re-derived.
- No position on the metaphysics of the reported events is assumed in either direction.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive, on the repute.** Earliest-stratum pervasiveness + hostile concession + external echo → the reputation is historical bedrock even where every narrative is questionable. **Risks checked:** *bracket-slip, direction one* (grading the repute must not become asserting the deeds — the claim text and title carry the bracket explicitly); *bracket-slip, direction two* (invoking the accretion topos must not become asserting the reports false — the rival is steelmanned and bounded, not adopted); *narrative-borrowing* (no individual story inherits the repute's grade); *back-door adjudication* (naturalistic redescriptions left ungraded).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** — for the repute-and-report claim. Bounded by the Gospel-strata and external layers (CLM-0069/CLM-0073, both Level 3); the hostile-concession stratum is why this sits secure within Level 3 rather than lower, and the absence of any H-a attestation is why it cannot go higher.
- **The events: graded at no level, in either direction** (bracketed, KOS-0003 §3.6).

---

# Limitations
- Repute only — no individual healing or exorcism narrative is graded; the events are bracketed.
- The relative proportion of exorcism vs. healing in the earliest layer is not finely graded.
- The Talmudic notice is late polemic — consistency noted, zero independent weight.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Legendary accretion wholesale** — element (c); claims the growth rightly, fails on the earliest-stratum pervasiveness and the hostile concession; bounds the grade rather than defeating the claim.
2. **Confessional reading (the miracles happened as reported)** — bracketed direction one; not asserted, not graded.
3. **Debunking reading (nothing happened; all invention)** — bracketed direction two *as an event-claim*; as a tradition-claim it collapses into (1) and is answered there.
