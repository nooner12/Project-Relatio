# CLM-0078

# Jesus Conducted an Itinerant Ministry in Galilee Whose Central Proclamation Was the "Kingdom of God" — MODERATE Confidence, Bounded by the Gospel-Strata Layer, with Multiple Attestation at Its Broadest and a Dissimilarity Argument from the Early Church's Own Different Idiom — Consensus Again Above the Evidence Class

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Jesus conducted an itinerant public activity centered in Galilee whose **central proclaimed theme was the "Kingdom of God" (basileia tou theou)** — an eschatological announcement of God's coming reign, delivered characteristically in parables and aphorisms. The claim grades the **centrality of the theme and the Galilean itinerant frame**; it does not grade any specific saying's wording (CLM-0079's territory), any particular eschatological timetable, or any theological truth of the proclamation (bracketed).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — what the man characteristically proclaimed and where; the proclamation's truth is not a historical question and is not graded.

---

# Element (a) — Attestation & Layer (INV-0013 inheritance)
- **The kingdom-theme saturates every strand of the sayings tradition**: Mark, the double tradition (Q), special Matthew, special Luke — across forms (parables, aphorisms, the Lord's Prayer petition, beatitudes). The load-bearing layer is the Gospel strata — [[CLM-0069 - Dating of the Gospels|CLM-0069, Level 3 (Moderate)]] — with strand-independence resting on the Synoptic source model ([[CLM-0070 - Markan Priority and the Synoptic Problem|CLM-0070]]: Markan priority Level 4, but the **Q hypothesis only Level 2** — so "Mark + Q" as *two* independent witnesses inherits Q's uncertainty; on the Farrer alternative the double tradition is Luke-used-Matthew and the independent strand count drops).
- **An echo in the H-a layer**: Paul uses "kingdom of God" occasionally and as received vocabulary (1 Cor 4:20, 6:9–10, 15:50; Rom 14:17; Gal 5:21; 1 Thess 2:12) — [[CLM-0068 - Dating of the Undisputed Pauline Letters|CLM-0068, Level 4]] attests the *phrase's currency in the first-generation movement*, though not its centrality to Jesus' own Galilean proclamation.
- **The early-proclamation terminus** — [[CLM-0074 - The Pre-Literary Tradition|CLM-0074, Level 4, inherited]] — anchors *that* the movement's tradition reaches back to the first years; it does not by itself attest the kingdom-theme.
- **Inherited bound:** Level 3 — centrality-to-Jesus rests on the Gospel strata (CLM-0069), with the strand-count qualification from CLM-0070's split.

---

# Element (b) — Authenticity Criteria
- **Multiple attestation at its broadest in the tradition**: the theme (not any one saying) appears in every source strand and nearly every form-category the tradition contains — the criterion's strongest legitimate use (theme-level, per Allison's recurrent-attestation refinement).
- **Dissimilarity (the "proclaimer became the proclaimed" argument)**: the early church's own preferred idiom was christological — Jesus as Christ, Lord, Son — and "kingdom of God" recedes sharply outside the Synoptics (rare in Paul, John, and the second-century fathers). A community retrojection theory must explain why the community saturated Jesus' speech with an idiom it did not itself favor.
- **Coherence**: the kingdom-theme coheres with the baptism origin (CLM-0077 — an eschatological renewal movement) and the exorcism-repute (CLM-0080 — "if I by the finger of God cast out demons, the kingdom of God has come upon you").
- **What the criteria fail to establish:** the kingdom's precise sense (apocalyptic-imminent vs. sapiential-present — a genuine scholarly split), any timetable, and any individual saying.

---

# Element (c) — Rival Explanation (Steelmanned)
**Community retrojection, at its strongest:** the kingdom-idiom entered the tradition from early Palestinian-Christian eschatology (the movement expected the end; its prayer was "your kingdom come"; Aramaic-speaking communities could have generated kingdom-sayings wholesale), and the Synoptic saturation reflects the tradition's formative milieu rather than the founder's voice; Paul's rarity would then reflect Gentile-mission vocabulary shift, not Jesus' distance from the idiom.
**Why the claim survives:** the rival concedes the idiom is early and Palestinian, and it still must explain the **direction of the asymmetry** — the communities that wrote and transmitted the Gospels confessed Christ but wrote kingdom; retrojection paints the founder in the church's own colors, and these are not the church's colors. The dissimilarity argument does not certify any saying, but at theme-level it makes founder-origin the better explanation than community-origin.
**Cost to confidence:** the rival is coherent and the layer is reconstructed narrative; centrality-to-Jesus stays at Moderate. The Galilean itinerant *frame* (village activity around the Sea of Galilee, Capernaum base) is narrative-layer geography — plausible, uncontested, but graded with the same layer bound.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- **SRC-0103** (primary texts): the kingdom-sayings corpus across Mark/Q/M/L; the Pauline kingdom-texts; the Lord's Prayer petition.
- **SRC-0097** (Sanders): kingdom-proclamation among the "almost indisputable facts"; the eschatological-prophet frame.
- **SRC-0127** (Meier vol. 2): the kingdom as the central message — the exhaustive criteria-driven case.
- **SRC-0125** (Theissen & Merz): field-consensus baseline; the sense-split (imminent vs. present) documented.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 — reconstructed narrative strata; the theme-saturation itself is an objective feature of the texts.
- Relevance: 5 — direct.
- Independence: 3 — cross-strand at theme level; strand count qualified by the Q uncertainty (CLM-0070).
- Quality: 4 — theme-level multiple attestation plus the dissimilarity asymmetry is among the stronger argument-structures available for any ministry claim.
- Limitations: theme and frame only; no saying, sense, or timetable.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate.**
- Consensus strength: **Very high** — that Jesus proclaimed the kingdom of God as his central theme is common ground from Sanders through Meier to Theissen & Merz (and to the skeptical wing: Allison's recurrent-attestation method yields it as a primary result). **A §9 consensus-exceeds-evidence case** again; recorded, not graded in.
- The *sense* of the kingdom (imminent-apocalyptic vs. present-sapiential) is genuinely split in the field and no side is graded here.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- Theme-level multiple attestation is a valid criterion use even under the SRC-0124 critique (which targets item-level authentication; theme-level survives as recurrent attestation — SRC-0128).
- The Synoptic source model is consumed from CLM-0070 as graded (priority High, Q Low), not re-argued.
- The proclamation's theological truth is bracketed (KOS-0003 §3.6).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inference to best explanation at theme level.** Saturation + dissimilarity-asymmetry + coherence make founder-origin the best explanation of the kingdom-idiom's distribution; the layer rule keeps the grade at the strata that carry it. **Risks checked:** *item-level slide* (no saying is authenticated by the theme's grade — boundary with CLM-0079 kept explicit); *sense-overreach* (imminent vs. present not adjudicated); *consensus-as-evidence* (§9 separation held); *strand-inflation* (Q's Level 2 uncertainty carried into the independence score, not waved away).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate).** Bounded by the Gospel-strata layer (CLM-0069, Level 3) with the CLM-0070 strand-count qualification; the H-a Pauline echo attests currency, not centrality, and cannot lift the specific claim above its load-bearing layer. Consensus surplus recorded per §9. (Not Level 2: theme-saturation across strands plus the dissimilarity asymmetry is substantially stronger than single-strand attestation.)

---

# Limitations
- Centrality and frame only — no individual saying (CLM-0079), no kingdom-sense adjudication, no timetable, no map of the itinerary.
- "Central" means dominant-in-the-tradition; relative weighting against other themes (Torah interpretation, table-fellowship) is not finely graded.
- The theological truth of the proclamation is bracketed in both directions.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Community retrojection** — element (c); survives as a coherent minority reading; the reason for Moderate rather than High.
2. **Non-eschatological Jesus (kingdom as purely sapiential/present)** — a genuine field minority (Jesus Seminar trajectory); treated as a sense-dispute within the claim, not a rival to the theme's centrality; not adjudicated.
3. **Mythicist reading** (kingdom-sayings as allegorized community theology wholesale) — parasitic on the existence question; answered at CLM-0075(c).
