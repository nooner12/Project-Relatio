# CLM-0079

# The Teaching Tradition Preserves the Characteristic Voice of Jesus at the Level of Recurrent Themes and Forms — MODERATE Confidence — While the Authenticity of Any Specific Saying's Wording Is LOW: the Criteria Cannot Certify Items, Memory Preserves Gist Not Verbatim, and Demonstrable Community Formation Is Visible in the Tradition Itself — a SPLIT Grade, with the Community-Invention Rival Steelmanned at Full Strength

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **(i) General contours:** the sayings tradition preserves genuinely characteristic features of Jesus' teaching — the kingdom-theme (CLM-0078), the parabolic and aphoristic forms, the ethical radicalism (love of enemies, reversal sayings), the distinctive idiom ("amen I say to you," abba) — at the level of **recurrent theme, form, and impression**. **(ii) Specific sayings:** the tradition's preservation of any **individual saying's wording** as Jesus' own is weakly establishable: transmission was oral, gist-preserving, and demonstrably formative; the criteria of authenticity cannot certify items. The two components carry **different grades** (split, not averaged). Bracketed throughout: the teachings' truth or authority.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — about what the transmission process can and cannot be shown to have preserved.

---

# Element (a) — Attestation & Layer (INV-0013 inheritance)
- **The sayings tradition sits in the Gospel strata** — [[CLM-0069 - Dating of the Gospels|CLM-0069, Level 3 (Moderate)]]: written 40–70 years post-event, in Greek (the teaching was mostly delivered in Aramaic — a translation layer intervenes before the earliest text).
- **Strand structure**: Mark, double tradition, M, L — with the double tradition's independence resting on the Q hypothesis, graded **Level 2 (Low)** at [[CLM-0070 - Markan Priority and the Synoptic Problem|CLM-0070]]; Matthew's and Luke's **visible rewriting of Mark** (the same claim's data) is direct, in-the-text evidence that transmitters altered wording freely while preserving substance.
- **A small H-a anchor**: Paul cites teachings *of the Lord* on divorce (1 Cor 7:10–11) and on missionary support (1 Cor 9:14), and transmits the supper tradition (1 Cor 11:23–25) — [[CLM-0068 - Dating of the Undisputed Pauline Letters|CLM-0068, Level 4]]: proof that dominical teaching material circulated and was cited as authoritative within ~25 years; note Paul gives the *substance*, in his own words, not verbatim sayings — the earliest witness already exhibits gist-transmission.
- **The pre-literary layer** — [[CLM-0074 - The Pre-Literary Tradition|CLM-0074, Level 4, inherited]] — establishes that tradition-transmission ("received/delivered") was a first-generation practice.
- **Inherited bound:** component (i) rests on the Gospel strata plus the Pauline anchor → Level 3 ceiling; component (ii) rests on item-level attestation whose independence is thin (Q at Level 2) and whose wording is demonstrably fluid → Level 2.

---

# Element (b) — Authenticity Criteria
- **The criteria apparatus itself is the contested instrument here** (SRC-0124): multiple attestation, dissimilarity, embarrassment, and coherence were built to certify items, and the field's methodological turn (Keith, Le Donne; Allison; converging with Dunn from the orality side) concludes they cannot — dissimilarity presupposes knowing the church and Judaism well enough to subtract them; coherence is circular; attestation-counting inherits the source-model's uncertainty (Q, Level 2).
- **What survives the critique** is theme/form-level recurrent attestation (SRC-0128): the same characteristic features recurring across strands and forms warrant component (i) at Moderate.
- **What the criteria fail to establish** — and this failure is the claim's own content: no criterion certifies a specific saying's wording; even strong candidates (the divorce prohibition with its Pauline anchor; the Lord's Prayer core; "amen" idiom) reach *substance-probably-dominical*, not *wording-certified*.

---

# Element (c) — Rival Explanation (Steelmanned — hardest here, per the investigation brief)
**Community formation, at full strength:** the tradition demonstrably *created* teaching material — form criticism's core observation stands: sayings served post-Easter needs (church discipline in Matt 18; mission instructions adapted between Q-versions; parables allegorized in transmission — Mark 4's interpretation widely judged secondary); early Christian prophets spoke in the risen Lord's name and their oracles could enter the tradition undifferentiated (the mechanics are visible in 1 Cor 14 and Revelation's "thus says" idiom); Matthew and Luke rewrite Mark's wording *before our eyes*, and John's Jesus speaks in the evangelist's own theological voice — proof that communities put extensive discourse in Jesus' mouth within the first century. On this rival, the tradition is a community product shaped by community needs, and item-level authenticity is unrecoverable in principle.
**Why the split verdict, not a rout:** the rival wins component (ii) — that is precisely why specific-wording authenticity is graded **Low**, and the concession is recorded as such. It does not win component (i): the characteristic-impression layer resists it — the dissimilarity asymmetry (CLM-0078: the church wrote an idiom it did not confess in), the retained embarrassments (CLM-0077), the un-invented silences (the tradition never has Jesus address the burning first-generation disputes — circumcision, Gentile table-fellowship, glossolalia — exactly where free invention would have been most useful), and the Pauline anchor showing dominical teaching cited as a distinct, constraining category by the 50s. A wholesale-invention reading must explain why the invented founder-voice was left unarmed on the church's own battles.
**Cost to confidence:** component (i) at Moderate, not High — the formation processes are real and pervasive; component (ii) at Low — the rival substantially succeeds there.

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- **SRC-0103** (primary texts): the sayings corpus and its synoptic variation (the rewriting evidence); 1 Cor 7:10–11, 9:14, 11:23–25; the silence on first-generation disputes.
- **SRC-0126** (Dunn): the oral-transmission model — fixity in gist, variation in wording; the characteristic Jesus.
- **SRC-0128** (Allison): memory frailty; recurrent attestation as the reliable layer; against item-certification.
- **SRC-0124** (Keith & Le Donne): the criteria critique — the instrument-level case for component (ii)'s Low grade.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 (i) / 2 (ii) — the strata are reconstructed; the variation data is objective.
- Relevance: 5 — the claim is about exactly what this evidence shows.
- Independence: 3 (i — cross-strand at theme level) / 2 (ii — item-level counts inherit Q's uncertainty).
- Quality: 4 — the synoptic rewriting evidence is unusually direct (transmission behavior observed, not inferred).
- Limitations: no list of authentic sayings is produced — deliberately; the claim grades the *process*, not an inventory.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** (i) / **Low** (ii).
- Consensus strength: **High and convergent on the split itself** — the field's confident wing (Dunn) and skeptical wing (Allison; Keith–Le Donne) both land on gist-over-item; the older confident position (criteria can certify sayings) has lost its standing, and the wholesale-skeptical position remains a minority. Here consensus and evidence **align** — no §9 surplus.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- Memory-science transfer to first-century oral communities is valid in its deflationary direction (an argued assumption — SRC-0128; its limits disclosed there).
- The synoptic source model is consumed as graded (CLM-0070), not re-argued; conclusions that depend specifically on Q's existence inherit its Level 2.
- The teachings' religious truth/authority is bracketed entirely (KOS-0003 §3.6).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inference from observed transmission behavior.** The tradition's own documents show how it transmitted (rewriting Mark; Paul giving dominical substance in his own words): gist-stable, wording-fluid, need-responsive. Component grades follow that observed behavior. **Risks checked:** *false middle* (the split is argued per component, not an average); *inventory temptation* (producing an "authentic sayings" list would exceed the method — refused); *skeptical overshoot* (the rival's full win would prove too much — it cannot explain the asymmetries and silences; the Moderate grade on (i) is defended, not conceded); *apologetic drift* (the Pauline anchor shows citation-as-authoritative, not verbatim preservation — not over-read).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Component (i) — characteristic themes/forms/impression: Level 3 (Moderate).** Bounded by the Gospel-strata layer (CLM-0069, Level 3); the Pauline anchor (CLM-0068) confirms the practice of dominical citation but covers too few items to lift the general claim.
- **Component (ii) — specific-saying wording authenticity: Level 2 (Low).** The criteria cannot certify items (SRC-0124); memory is gist-preserving (SRC-0126/0128); formation is demonstrable in the text; item-level independence inherits Q's Level 2 (CLM-0070).
- No component reaches Level 4: this claim's subject is the transmission process's *limits*, and the evidence for those limits is itself the ceiling.

---

# Limitations
- No inventory of authentic sayings; individual sayings are graded only as *classes* here.
- The Aramaic-to-Greek translation layer is noted, not independently graded.
- Non-canonical sayings sources (Thomas) are out of scope per INV-0013 §2.2's boundary — their independent-attestation potential is a recorded gap, not silently resolved.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Community formation wholesale** — element (c); wins component (ii), fails on (i)'s asymmetries; the split verdict is its honest accommodation.
2. **Conservative transmission (Gerhardsson's rabbinic-memorization model; verbatim preservation)** — steelmanned as the maximalist pole: if first-generation transmission were memorized Torah-style, wording would be recoverable. Against it: the observed synoptic fluidity is the direct counter-evidence; the model describes a later rabbinic institution, its retrojection to the Jesus movement unevidenced. Not sustained above Low.
3. **Total skepticism (no access to Jesus' voice at all)** — fails against the recurrent-attestation layer and the H-a citation anchor; a minority not sustained.
