# CLM-0077

# Jesus Was Baptized by John the Baptist — MODERATE Confidence Bounded by a Modest Attestation Layer, with the Criterion of Embarrassment at Its Strongest and Scholarly Consensus Running Well Above the Evidence Class — the Investigation's Showcase Case of Authenticity Criteria and Raw Attestation Coming Apart

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Jesus of Nazareth was baptized by John the Baptist at the outset of his public activity. The claim is the **event of baptism and the historical relation it implies** (Jesus began within or adjacent to John's movement); the theophany elements of the narratives (the voice, the dove) are **not** part of the claim, and any theological reading of the scene is bracketed.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — an event claim about the man's biography; the narratives' visionary elements are bracketed.

---

# Element (a) — Attestation & Layer (INV-0013 inheritance)
- **The narrative attestation is modest**: Mark 1:9–11 is the primary account ([[CLM-0069 - Dating of the Gospels|CLM-0069, Level 3 (Moderate)]] for the Gospel layer; [[CLM-0070 - Markan Priority and the Synoptic Problem|CLM-0070]] establishes at Level 4 that Matthew and Luke *depend on Mark* here — so the Synoptic parallels are **not independent attestation** of the event). John's Gospel presupposes contact with the Baptist's circle but omits the baptism itself. Independent multiple attestation for the *event* is therefore thin — essentially one primary narrative strand.
- **What is independently attested** is the frame: John the Baptist himself is documented outside the tradition (Josephus, *Ant.* 18.116–119 — within SRC-0099's testimonia corpus; the notice attests John and his baptizing movement, not the baptism of Jesus), and the Baptist's continuing presence in the tradition (Q material on John's preaching; the embarrassed redactional trajectory) is cross-strand.
- **Paul is silent** — no H-a layer bears on this event at all.
- **Inherited bound:** Level 3 — the Gospel-narrative layer (CLM-0069) is the load-bearing attestation, and Markan dependence (CLM-0070) *reduces* the count of independent witnesses rather than raising it.

---

# Element (b) — Authenticity Criteria
- **Embarrassment at its strongest anywhere in the tradition** (Meier, SRC-0127): Jesus submitting to John's "baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins" (Mark 1:4) implied subordination to John and an apparent need of repentance — and the redactional trajectory visibly wrestles with it: **Mark states it plainly → Matthew inserts John's protest ("I need to be baptized by you," 3:14–15) → Luke displaces the moment into a subordinate clause after John's arrest notice → John's Gospel narrates the Baptist's witness but omits the baptism itself.** A tradition inventing the scene would not then labor to neutralize it; the discomfort is itself evidence the event was fixed in the tradition before the discomfort arose.
- **Coherence**: Jesus' documented proximity to the Baptist movement (disciples transferring, continuing comparisons between the movements) coheres with an origin inside John's orbit.
- **What the criteria fail to establish:** the scene's details (location, wording, the theophany) and John's own understanding of the act — the criteria certify the *event-kernel*, nothing finer. And per the criteria-critique (SRC-0124), embarrassment arguments infer invention-improbability, not documentation — they cannot substitute for attestation the layer does not provide.

---

# Element (c) — Rival Explanation (Steelmanned)
**Community invention, at its strongest:** the scene could have been created to enlist John — a revered, independently famous prophet (Josephus gives him more space than Jesus) — as the authorizing herald of Jesus, exactly the use every Gospel puts him to; the theophany, not the submission, is the scene's narrative point, and the "embarrassment" is a modern projection onto a text whose author saw only christological confirmation.
**Why the claim survives:** the invention reading explains the theophany but not the **kernel it had to be attached to** — a movement free to invent would authorize Jesus *without* placing him under John's repentance-baptism (as John's Gospel in fact does: witness without baptism). The redactional trajectory shows the tradition treating the event as a given to be managed, not a creation to be enjoyed. The rival better explains the scene's *elaborations* than its existence.
**Cost to confidence:** the event-kernel survives, but the rival — plus the thin independent attestation — is why the claim cannot rise above its layer despite the criterion's strength.

---

# The Divergence Flag (INV-0014 brief; recorded per the GB-2026-027 option-b prose protocol)
This claim is the investigation's clearest case of **authenticity criteria and raw attestation coming apart**: the attestation layer is modest (one primary narrative strand, no H-a witness, dependence reducing the parallels), while the criterion of embarrassment is at its strongest and field consensus (Sanders' "almost indisputable" list; Meier's "one of the most certain things we know about Jesus"; Theissen & Merz) treats the event as virtually certain. Under INV-0014's layer-inheritance rule the **layer wins the grade** (Level 3) and the consensus surplus is recorded as a **KOS-0003 §9 consensus-exceeds-evidence case** — the CLM-0069 pattern again. In H-typology prose terms: an H-e-criteria-argued event on an H-e-dated narrative layer, graded below its consensus — evidence-kind and confidence visibly distinct.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- **SRC-0103** (primary texts): Mark 1:4, 1:9–11; Matt 3:13–17; Luke 3:21–22; John 1:29–34; the redactional trajectory across them.
- **SRC-0127** (Meier vol. 2): the embarrassment argument at full strength; "Jesus never was without John."
- **SRC-0125** (Theissen & Merz): the field-consensus baseline (baptism as historically secure).
- Josephus on John (*Ant.* 18.116–119) via **SRC-0099**'s corpus — frame attestation only (attests John, not the baptism of Jesus).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 — one primary narrative strand, four decades post-event; the redactional trajectory is objective and datable evidence of early fixity.
- Relevance: 5 — direct.
- Independence: 2 — Synoptic parallels are Mark-dependent (CLM-0070); John's Gospel is a disputed independent; Josephus attests the frame only.
- Quality: 3 — narrative tradition, but with an unusual internal marker (the embarrassment trajectory) that raises its evidential character above bare narration.
- Limitations: event-kernel only; no detail of the scene inherits the grade.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** (the layer and independence count above).
- Consensus strength: **Very high** — across the field the baptism is treated as among the most secure facts (Sanders, Meier, Theissen & Merz). **Recorded as a §9 consensus-exceeds-evidence case**; the surplus is not graded into the Level.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- The embarrassment criterion's logic (traditions do not invent what they must then neutralize) is sound as an *invention-improbability* argument — while acknowledged (SRC-0124) as incapable of substituting for attestation.
- The theophany elements are bracketed as visionary/theological narrative, graded at no level.
- Markan priority (CLM-0070, Level 4) is consumed, not re-argued.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inference to best explanation on the event-kernel.** The kernel is the explanandum the invention rival cannot cover; the trajectory of neutralization is the strongest single datum. **Risks checked:** *criterion-overreach* (embarrassment treated as probability argument, not proof — the SRC-0124 discount applied); *consensus-as-evidence* (§9 separation held — the field's near-certainty did not move the grade); *detail creep* (scene details and theophany excluded).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate).** Bounded by the layer-inheritance rule at the Gospel-narrative layer (CLM-0069, Level 3) with independence further thinned by Markan dependence (CLM-0070) — the strongest criterion in the tradition cannot lift a claim above its attestation layer. The consensus surplus is recorded per §9, not graded. (Not Level 2: the embarrassment trajectory and frame-attestation are genuine evidence beyond bare narration.) **Reconciliation of the independence-2 dimension score with Level 3** (per the Critical Review recommendation): the 0–5 dimension scores grade the evidence profile's parts, and low independence is *why* the claim sits at its layer bound rather than above it — the grade itself is set by the element-(a) layer (CLM-0069, L3) plus the redactional-trajectory datum, which is evidence *within* the single strand that bare narration would not supply; a 2 on one dimension does not mechanically force Level 2 where the layer bound and the remaining profile hold at Moderate.

---

# Limitations
- Event-kernel only: no location, wording, or scene detail; the theophany bracketed.
- John's own view of Jesus is not graded (the "are you the one" tradition is a separate, weaker question).
- The precise chronological relation (how long Jesus remained in John's orbit) is not graded.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Community invention (herald-enlistment)** — element (c); explains elaborations, fails on the kernel; the reason the grade is bounded, not the reason to reject.
2. **Full narrative historicity (including theophany as event)** — a confessional reading; the visionary elements are bracketed per KOS-0003 §3.6, neither asserted nor denied.
