# CLM-0075

# Jesus of Nazareth Existed as a Historical Figure — a First-Century Galilean Jew Whose Movement and Execution Are Attested by a Contemporary Who Knew His Brother and His Closest Followers, and Corroborated by Non-Christian Notices — Held at HIGH Confidence Against the Steelmanned Mythicist Rival

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> A historical Jesus of Nazareth existed: a first-century Jewish figure from Galilee around whom the movement formed and whose execution the movement itself proclaimed from its earliest recoverable layer. The claim is **existence only** — it asserts nothing about his deeds, teachings, or any theological characterization (each graded separately, and the metaphysical claims bracketed).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — an existence claim about a past person; no theological content.

---

# Element (a) — Attestation & Layer (INV-0013 inheritance)
The earliest attestation, by layer, with the INV-0013 grade each rests on:
- **The undisputed Pauline letters (c. 50–60 CE)** — [[CLM-0068 - Dating of the Undisputed Pauline Letters|CLM-0068, Level 4 (High)]]: a contemporary writing within ~20–30 years, who reports meeting **James "the brother of the Lord"** (Gal 1:19) and Cephas/Peter (Gal 1:18, 2:9), and who treats Jesus as a recently executed man (1 Cor 2:8; Gal 3:1). This is the load-bearing layer: personal acquaintance with the figure's family and inner circle.
- **The pre-literary tradition** — [[CLM-0074 - The Pre-Literary Tradition|CLM-0074, Level 4 (High), inherited by the INV-0014 `depends_on` edge]]: the 1 Cor 15:3–7 creed shows the movement proclaiming a died-and-buried (and, bracketed, raised) Jesus within a few years of the crucifixion — the proclamation terminus sits in the 30s CE.
- **Non-Christian attestation** — [[CLM-0073 - Non-Christian Attestation|CLM-0073, Level 3 (Moderate)]]: Tacitus (*Ann.* 15.44, executed under Pilate), Josephus (*Ant.* 20.200 on James "brother of Jesus called Christ," the more secure notice; *Ant.* 18 Testimonium partially authentic) — corroborates existence and execution from outside the movement, at the H-c near-contemporary-secondary layer.
- The Gospel narratives ([[CLM-0069 - Dating of the Gospels|CLM-0069, Level 3]]) add multiple independent narrative strands but are **not** load-bearing for bare existence — the Pauline layer alone carries it.

**Inherited bound:** the load-bearing layer (undisputed Paul + embedded creed) is graded **Level 4 (High)** in INV-0013; this claim can rise no higher.

---

# Element (b) — Authenticity Criteria
- **Multiple independent attestation** bears at full strength: Paul, the pre-Markan tradition, Q, Josephus, and Tacitus are independent lines converging on an executed founder-figure. It establishes *that the figure existed and was executed*; it does **not** establish any narrative detail.
- **Embarrassment** bears indirectly but strongly: a **crucified** messiah was a proclamation liability (1 Cor 1:23 "a stumbling block"); a movement inventing its founder from scratch has no reason to invent a shamefully executed one.
- The criteria-critique (SRC-0124) bites least here: existence does not rest on authenticating any individual saying or scene, so the demise-of-authenticity argument leaves this claim's basis intact.

---

# Element (c) — Rival Explanation (Steelmanned)
**The mythicist reading (Carrier, SRC-0101), at its strongest:** Christianity began with a celestial Christ known through revelation and scripture (cf. the ambiguous "born of a woman" and the sparse biography in Paul); "brother of the Lord" is fictive-kinship language for baptized Christians generally; the Testimonium is interpolated and Tacitus repeats what Christians themselves said; the Gospels are later allegorization of the celestial figure — so all attestation lines reduce to one Christian tradition, not independent witness.
**Why the claim survives:** (i) Gal 1:19 distinguishes James from the other apostles *by* the kinship term — the fictive-kinship reading must explain why only James (and "the brothers of the Lord," 1 Cor 9:5, distinguished from apostles generally) get it; (ii) the creed (CLM-0074) shows proclamation of a died-and-buried man within years, leaving no gap for a purely celestial phase; (iii) on *Ant.* 20.200, Carrier's strongest form is engaged (he **grants** the text is secure in the manuscripts): the argument is that "who was called Christ" is an **accidental scribal gloss** and the James in view is the brother of **Jesus ben Damneus**, the incoming high priest named later in the same passage. Against it: the abbreviated back-reference ("the brother of Jesus who was called Christ, James by name") presumes a figure the designation identifies for the reader, which the *Ant.* 18 core supplies and ben Damneus does not; the ben-Damneus reading must compound two auxiliary hypotheses (an undetected gloss *and* a mis-identification that makes Josephus introduce the same man twice in one passage under different descriptions); and the majority of Josephan specialists across confessional lines holds "called Christ" original — the passage's non-confessional, incidental register is the opposite of interpolation style; (iv) the near-universal scholarly consensus across confessional and non-confessional scholars (SRC-0100) rests on these evidence lines, not on faith.
**Cost to confidence:** real but bounded — the rival is coherent enough, and the external notices dependent enough (Tacitus plausibly reporting the movement's own account), that Level 5 is out of reach; the claim rests at the grade of its Pauline layer, not above it.

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical.**
- **SRC-0103** (primary texts): Gal 1:18–19, 2:9; 1 Cor 9:5, 15:3–7; 1 Cor 1:23, 2:8.
- **SRC-0099** (non-Christian testimonia): Tacitus *Ann.* 15.44; Josephus *Ant.* 20.200 (secure) and the partially authentic Testimonium.
- **SRC-0100** (Ehrman): the historicity case and the consensus report.
- **SRC-0101** (Carrier): the steelmanned mythicist rival (contrasted, not supporting).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 — the Pauline acquaintance-evidence is contemporaneous and undisputed in its text; the external notices carry known interpolation/dependence issues (disclosed).
- Relevance: 5 — the evidence bears directly on bare existence.
- Independence: 4 — genuinely independent lines (Pauline, pre-Pauline creed, Josephus *Ant.* 20.200); Tacitus's independence is uncertain (may report Christian claims).
- Quality: 4 — strongest available class for an ancient private person (contemporary letters naming family/associates).
- Limitations: establishes existence and execution only; nothing else transfers.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High** (for bare existence).
- Consensus strength: **Near-universal** across confessional, Jewish, agnostic, and atheist scholarship (SRC-0100); mythicism is a fringe position. Consensus recorded separately from evidence class per §9 — here they align.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- That Gal 1:19's kinship term is biological on its natural reading (majority; the fictive reading is the named rival).
- That *Ant.* 20.200 is substantially authentic (strong majority; argued from its non-confessional content and context).
- No directional premise: historicity was tested against the steelmanned rival, not assumed (INV-0014 §6).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive convergence.** Independent attestation lines, anchored by a contemporary's personal acquaintance with the figure's brother and inner circle, converge on existence; the strongest rival must explain away each line separately and multiplies auxiliary hypotheses in doing so. **Risks checked:** *consensus-as-evidence* (the consensus is reported per §9 but the grade rests on the evidence lines themselves); *circular corroboration* (Tacitus's possible dependence on Christian report is disclosed and discounted — *Ant.* 20.200 carries the independent weight); *category slide* (existence ≠ reliability of any narrative about him — firmly separated).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 4 (High).** Bounded by the layer-inheritance rule at the grade of its load-bearing layer (CLM-0068/CLM-0074, both Level 4); the steelmanned rival is survivable but not costless; **not Level 5** (reserved; and no cross-domain historical inference warrants it — INV-0014 protocol).

---

# Limitations
- Existence and execution only; no deed, teaching, or title claim inherits this grade.
- The external corroboration is Moderate-layer (CLM-0073) — supportive, not load-bearing.
- "Jesus of Nazareth existed" is here a minimal historical claim (a Galilean Jew, executed, whose movement proclaimed him) — not the full Gospel portrait.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Mythicism (celestial-Christ)** — steelmanned in element (c); rejected as the minority reading whose auxiliary hypotheses (fictive kinship confined to James; interpolated *Ant.* 20.200; a celestial phase invisible in the creed's timeline) are each weaker than the natural readings they replace.
2. **Agnosticism ("evidence insufficient to decide")** — a live philosophical stance, but it must discount a contemporary's report of meeting the figure's brother; the evidence class here exceeds what suffices for most uncontested ancient figures.
