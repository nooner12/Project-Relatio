# FND-0018

# Islam Origins and Abrahamic Relationship Shape

## Draft Finding Record

---

# 1. Finding (the honest shape)

On the catalogued base, INV-0018 resolves to **hypothesis (b) of the three held open at scaffold: a SEPARATE ROOT WITH INFLUENCE RELATIONSHIPS AND NO DESCENT EDGE.** Neither hypothesis (a) (multi-parent descent) nor (c) (a mix) is supported. Layered, and thin where the evidence or the vocabulary is thin:

- **Islam emerged in the seventh century CE in western Arabia and the adjoining late-antique Near East** (**High**). This is the family's one firm result, and it is firm for a specific reason: **the field's mainstream and revisionist poles converge on it despite opposed method** — the revisionist works relocate the text's stabilisation and the confession's self-definition, not the movement's century. **When a distinctly *Islamic* confessional identity crystallised is a different and genuinely contested question** (**Moderate**), running from the traditional account through Donner's later crystallisation to the strong revisionist re-datings; this circuit bounds it (public confessional expression attested by 691/692 and 696/697 CE) without settling it, and grades its own ability to adjudicate the re-datings **Low**.

- **The relationship to Judaism is INFLUENCE AND ENGAGEMENT, NOT DESCENT** (**Moderate**). Engagement is well attested — Jewish communities in seventh-century Arabia, and extensive Qur'anic engagement with biblical and post-biblical narrative material. The one descent-shaped reading in the base, the *Hagarism* reconstruction of an early Judeo-Arab messianic movement, was **taken seriously and weighed rather than dismissed**, and failed on its own recorded reception: not a consensus position, methodologically objected to on its own terms, and reported to be distanced by both its authors. **The character of seventh-century Arabian Jewish communities is a recorded base gap and is graded Low.**

- **The relationship to Christianity is INFLUENCE AND ENGAGEMENT, NOT DESCENT** (**Moderate**), reasoned **independently** and reaching the same category by a **different route**. Here the base contains **no** descent-shaped thesis at all, and the engagement has a distinctive shape: it is **simultaneously appropriative and repudiative** — dense uptake of Christian figures and narrative alongside explicit rejection of the doctrines that define the tradition, publicly attested by the 691/692 inscription. That is an interlocutor's posture, not a descendant's. **The derivation-shaped Syriac-substratum literature is absent from the base and is disclosed as absent rather than treated as refuted** — the stated reason this component is Moderate and not High.

- **THE EDGE DECISION: NO `branches_from` EDGE IS DRAWN, AND ISLAM RENDERS AS AN UNCONNECTED NODE.** Under the edge-restraint rule this is an **honest rendering requiring no repair**, and the refusal is recorded and reviewer-verified as rigorously as an edge would be. **`disputed` was not used and could not be** — it means *descent is disputed*, whereas here descent is **not established**; hedging influence with `disputed` is expressly forbidden. `heterodox-offshoot` and `syncretic-descent` were each considered and refused with reasons.

- **A SECOND, INDEPENDENT FINDING: NO EXISTING ENTITY WOULD HAVE BEEN AN HONEST TARGET EITHER** (**Moderate**) — and the two gaps have **different shapes**. **ENT-0012 Second Temple Judaism fails the chronological-coherence check outright** (`range_end_year: 70` against a seventh-century relationship). **ENT-0014 Rabbinic Judaism passes chronologically but over-specifies.** **ENT-0013 Christianity passes chronologically but is too coarse** — the documented contact was with particular eastern communities the vault does not model. **Neither gap is the reason no edge is drawn; the evidence is.** Both are stated separately so neither props up the other, and **no entity was created and no edge forced onto an approximate target.**

- **The tradition's own account is RESTORATIONIST** (**Moderate as a reported fact; the terminology graded Low for the translation limit**): not derivation from Judaism or Christianity but restoration of an original Abrahamic monotheism prior to and independent of both — *millat Ibrāhīm*. **It is steelmanned as a fact about the tradition, it does not settle the historical question, and it is not dismissed as error.** The separation is enforced **structurally**: no typed epistemic edge runs between CLM-0098 and the historical claims in either direction, and the two tracks' agreement that Islam does not descend from either tradition is **named and explicitly denied evidential weight**.

**Net:** the world-religions timeline now holds **three families, rendering as TWO connected clusters plus ONE single unconnected node** — the Iranian family (INV-0016), the Judaism–Christianity fork (INV-0017), and Islam, a third family whose entire population is one entity with no edges (remediation, Critical Review – RQ-0018 Flag 3: the earlier "three families and a disconnected node" wording implied a fourth thing). Islam is the program's **first tradition entity with no lineage edge at all** — not because the evidence is thin, but because the evidence, such as it is, points at a relationship the vocabulary cannot express. **The finding the timeline cannot show is the finding that matters most here**, and that is Anchor Fit Part 2's subject.

## PART 1 — MULTI-PARENT (AF-2): **NEGATIVE RESULT, RECORDED AS A RESULT**

**AF-2 does NOT get its second demonstrating case here.** The evidence supports **no** descent from **any** parent, so *a fortiori* it supports no descent from more than one. INV-0016 remains AF-2's only demonstrating case, and the recommendation **stays deferred** on that basis (ADR-GOV-0010 D4). **This is a real result and is recorded as one, not omitted** — the family that looked most likely on its face to produce a multi-parent descent case produced instead the program's first zero-parent case.

**One substantive observation, which is not a hedge on the negative.** The **multi-parent *shape* recurs at the influence layer**: Islam stands in documented influence relationships to **two** traditions at once, assessed independently and landing in the same category by different routes. So while multi-parent *descent* did not fire, **multi-parent *relation* did** — which bears directly on Part 2: **any `influenced_by` type must support multiple targets from birth**, and must not inherit `branches_from`'s de-facto single-parent practice. **Recommendation (routed):** record this as a design constraint on the Part 2 candidate, and record that AF-2's own question (can a lineage edge carry more than one parent?) remains open with one case.

**Specification of what such a type would need — assessed and recommended only. IT IS NOT CREATED.**

Mutual influence is **two explicit edges**, never one symmetric edge — `graph_integrity.py` must treat it as directional (as it does `branches_from` and `derived_from`) and must **not** raise reciprocity advisories on it.

Both demonstrating cases are satisfiable at that domain: INV-0011's relation holds between **ENT-0008 Zoroastrianism** and **ENT-0012 Second Temple Judaism**, both of which exist; INV-0018's would hold between **ENT-0015** and Jewish/Christian entities — **except that INV-0018's two entity-target gaps bite here too.** **This is the load-bearing caveat on the whole recommendation: creating the edge type would not, by itself, let INV-0018's finding be drawn**, because the honest targets do not exist. **Recommendation (routed): the edge-type question and the entity-resolution question must be governed together, not sequentially.**

- **Qualifier set.** A qualifier is warranted, but **the `branches_from` lesson is that freezing a controlled list before use is the error** — INV-0017 hit a gap on the list's first real stress-test and needed ADR-GOV-0010 to repair it. **Recommendation (routed): a MINIMAL required list at first use — `documented` / `contested` — explicitly provisional and expected to be extended by the third case**, rather than a speculative taxonomy of influence kinds (thematic / structural / textual / institutional) invented in advance of a case that needs one. `contested` is the analogue of `disputed` and is what makes a contested influence claim *render* as contested.

## PART 3 — RESTORATIONIST SELF-UNDERSTANDING vs THE VOCABULARY (owner-flagged; OBSERVATION ONLY)

**The structure.** Islam's self-account (CLM-0098) claims **not** descent from Judaism or Christianity but **restoration of *millat Ibrāhīm***, prior to and independent of both. Structurally that is ***claimed descent from something not continuously transmitted*** — a lineage across a gap, where the antecedent is held to have been lost or corrupted rather than handed down.

**None of the six qualifiers names it.** `schism`, `reform`, `continuation`, and `heterodox-offshoot` all presuppose a **continuously existing parent** to depart from, reform, carry forward, or deviate within. `syncretic-descent` presupposes **multiple living parents blending**. `disputed` means the **historical** descent is disputed — a different thing entirely from a **claimed** descent across a discontinuity. The gap is real and it is a different gap from the one ADR-GOV-0010 repaired.

**Kinship with the anticipated REVIVAL case.** The same structure — a tradition developing from the **recovered teachings of a tradition no longer living** — is what reconstructionist and revivalist movements present. Both are "descent claimed across a gap," and a vocabulary that acquired a word for one would very likely serve the other.

**Why this is an OBSERVATION and not a recommendation to act — the distinction that matters most here.** The two cases are **not** the same kind of thing:
- The **revival** case would be a **historical** finding: the discontinuity and the recovery are things the record could establish about what happened.
- **Islam's is a SELF-UNDERSTANDING structure**, and under the dual-track rule a self-account **may never be encoded as a lineage edge**. Encoding it would be the exact violation the investigation exists to prevent — it would let the tradition's account of itself do graph work that only historical evidence may do.

**Therefore:** Islam is a **PRESSURE case, not a demonstrating case.** It shows that the vocabulary has no way to express descent-across-a-gap, and it shows this at the self-understanding layer where the vocabulary must **not** be extended to reach. **The genuinely open question it surfaces is whether the *revival* case — when one arrives as a historical finding — will need a qualifier**, and this record's contribution is to name the shape in advance and to log Islam as a **possible future second-use input**, not as one now. **Recommendation (routed): record the shape and the distinction; take no vocabulary action on a self-understanding case.**

**Grade discipline, stated explicitly:** **CLM-0098's grades did not move on account of anything in this Part, and nothing in this Part rests on CLM-0098's grade.** The `tradition_type` strain is the same shape at the entity layer — the four-value list (`founded`/`emergent`/`reform`/`syncretic`) contains no `restorationist` value either — and is **reported, not force-fit**; `founded` stands as least-wrong for the historical register (CLM-0095).
