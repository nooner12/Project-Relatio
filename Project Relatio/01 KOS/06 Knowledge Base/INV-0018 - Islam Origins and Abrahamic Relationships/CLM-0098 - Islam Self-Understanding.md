# CLM-0098

# Islam Self-Understanding

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **In its own account, Islam does not derive from Judaism or Christianity: it RESTORES an original Abrahamic monotheism that preceded and is independent of both.** The tradition holds that the same message was sent to earlier prophetic communities, that the Qur'an confirms what was revealed before it while correcting what those communities came to hold, that Abraham belonged to neither of the later communities but to the primordial upright monotheism, and that the community is called to *millat Ibrāhīm* — the creed of Abraham. **This is recorded here as a FACT ABOUT THE TRADITION, steelmanned at its strongest in that register.** Under the dual-track rule it **does NOT settle the historical question** addressed by CLM-0095/0096/0097, and it is **NOT dismissed as mere error**; both slides are guarded, and **no grade in this claim moved any grade in those claims, or was moved by them**. **Translation limit, binding:** the terms this account turns on — *ḥanīf*, *millat Ibrāhīm*, *tanzīl*, *ummī* — are **contested renderings**; the base holds **one** English translation (SRC-0151, Abdel Haleem) and **neither a second translation nor the Arabic was consulted this session**. The terminological component is therefore **graded down for that limit** rather than asserted, and **no passage is quoted, paraphrased, or cited at verse level.**

---

# Element (i) — What the Tradition Holds (sourced to primary text, SRC-0151)

Stated at the **thematic level**, as the loci SRC-0151 §4 identified for this claim, and **without quotation, paraphrase, or verse-level citation** (see the Translation Limit section for why):

- **Continuity of a single message across prophetic communities.** The tradition holds that the message given to Muhammad is the same message previously sent to earlier communities through their own prophets, in their own languages, and that its scripture **confirms** what was given before it.
- **Confirmation *and* correction.** Alongside confirmation, the account holds that earlier communities came to hold or transmit what departs from the original message — so the later revelation both attests the earlier and corrects it. **The register of this is the tradition's own; the record does not evaluate it.**
- **Abraham as prior to, and outside, both later communities.** The account presents Abraham as belonging to neither of the two later scriptural communities but to a prior, upright monotheism — the sense carried by ***ḥanīf*** in this material — and calls the community to ***millat Ibrāhīm***, the creed or religion of Abraham. **This is the structural core of the restorationist self-account**: the tradition's stated ancestry runs *past* Judaism and Christianity to something they are held to have descended from and departed from, not *through* them.
- **Revelation as *tanzīl*.** The account frames its scripture as a sending-down from the same source as the earlier revelations — a vertical relation to the source, not a horizontal derivation from the earlier communities. **That structure is what makes the self-account restorationist rather than derivative**, and it is the point of contact with Anchor Fit Part 3.
- **The earlier communities as addressed others.** They are addressed as *People of the Book* — distinct communities in relation, neither absorbed nor denied.

**Register limit, restated from SRC-0151's own record:** this source is definitive evidence of the tradition's self-understanding and carries **no authority on the historical questions**, in either direction.

# Element (ii) — Its Status as a Fact About the Tradition (dual-track, both slides guarded)

- **Slide 1 — it does NOT settle the historical question.** That a tradition holds itself to restore rather than derive is evidence about the tradition's self-understanding. It is **not** evidence about seventh-century historical genealogy, and **no grade in CLM-0095, CLM-0096, or CLM-0097 was raised or lowered on account of anything in this claim.** Concretely: the fact that the self-account denies descent from Judaism and Christianity is **not** among the reasons CLM-0096 and CLM-0097 refuse a `branches_from` edge. Those refusals rest on historical evidence stated there and would stand unchanged if the self-account said the opposite. **This separation is structural, not merely asserted: no typed epistemic edge (`depends_on`/`supports`) runs between this claim and CLM-0095/0096/0097 in either direction** — the cross-references are prose and flat-list only, precisely so the graph cannot be read as letting one track carry the other.
- **Slide 2 — it is NOT dismissed as mere error.** The self-account is not a naive or defective version of the historical question; it is an account **in a different register**, internally coherent and load-bearing for the tradition's own self-definition, and it is reported at its strongest. **A record that treated it as a confused historical hypothesis to be corrected would fail the neutrality requirement exactly as badly as one that let it settle the history.**
- **The bracket.** Whether the restoration account is *true* — whether there was an original monotheism, whether earlier communities departed from it, whether revelation occurred — is a **metaphysical question bracketed entirely** (INV-0018 §2.1). The record neither affirms nor denies it, and takes no confessional or supersessionist position **in any direction**: not Islam over the earlier traditions, and not the earlier traditions over Islam.

# Element (iii) — How It Relates to, and Diverges From, the Scholarly Picture (recorded, not adjudicated)

Two tracks are set side by side. **No arrow is drawn between them.**

| | **Self-understanding track (this claim)** | **Historical track (CLM-0095/0096/0097)** |
|---|---|---|
| Origin | Restoration of a primordial Abrahamic monotheism | Seventh-century CE Arabian emergence (High) |
| Relation to Judaism | Not derivation; the earlier community departed from a shared original | Influence and engagement, **not** descent (Moderate) |
| Relation to Christianity | Not derivation; likewise | Influence and engagement, **not** descent (Moderate) |
| Direction of ancestry | *Past* both traditions, to Abraham | Alongside both, in a shared late-antique milieu |

- **Convergence, and what it does and does not mean.** Both tracks decline to model Islam as descending from Judaism or Christianity. **This convergence is recorded and is explicitly given no evidential weight**: the two reach it for wholly different reasons (a claim about revelation and primordial monotheism; a weighing of seventh-century historical evidence), and a coincidence of conclusion across incommensurable registers is not corroboration. **Treating it as corroboration would be the dual-track violation in its most tempting form**, and it is named here so the reviewer can check that it was refused.
- **Divergences, recorded.** The tracks diverge on the **direction and object of ancestry** (past both traditions, versus alongside them in a shared milieu); on the **status of the shared material** (an original message the earlier communities departed from, versus engagement with and reworking of material circulating in the milieu); and on the **existence and character of the antecedent** itself. **These divergences are recorded, not resolved theologically or historically.**
- **What is *not* claimed here.** That the self-account is late, apologetic, or constructed is **not** asserted — that would be adjudicating it. That it is historically accurate is **not** asserted either.

# Translation Limit (SRC-0151, binding — honoured by grading down)

- **The account above turns on terms of art whose English rendering is contested.** ***ḥanīf*** (rendered variously as "upright," "pure/true in faith," and whose derivation and semantic history are themselves debated in the scholarship); ***millat Ibrāhīm*** ("religion" / "creed" / "faith" of Abraham — the choice carries weight); ***tanzīl*** ("sending-down" / "revelation"); ***ummī*** ("unlettered" / "gentile" / "scriptureless"). **Different translations differ materially on this material** (SRC-0151 §3).
- **What this circuit did.** It **named the limit rather than working around it**: it read no interior, consulted **no second translation and not the Arabic**, and therefore **asserted no reading that turns on a contested rendering**, quoted nothing, paraphrased nothing, and **cited no *sūra*:*āya***. The thematic characterisation in element (i) is pitched at the level the base supports.
- **The consequence, taken rather than avoided.** Per SRC-0151's binding limit — *check a second translation or the Arabic, or **grade down for the limit*** — this circuit **grades down**. `terminological_reading_translation_limit` is **Level 2 (Low)**, and it is the component any future reading of *ḥanīf*, *millat Ibrāhīm*, *tanzīl*, or *ummī* must be measured against. **The Arabic is authoritative; this record rests on one English rendering and says so.**

**Descriptive** — a claim about **what a tradition holds about itself**. Not a historical claim about the seventh century, and not a metaphysical claim. The distinction is the whole point of the record.

- Independence: **none, and that is the point** — it *is* the tradition's voice, which is its evidential value here and precisely why it is confined here.

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Dual-track separation is enforced structurally**, not merely stated: no typed epistemic edge runs between this claim and the historical claims in either direction.
- **Steelmanning in register.** The self-account is presented at its strongest *as a self-account*; strength in that register implies nothing in the historical one.
- **Metaphysical bracket, total.** Revelation, prophethood, and scriptural inspiration are outside what this record adjudicates — for **all three** traditions.
- **Linguistic assumption, disclosed and paid for.** The account is mediated through one English translation; the Arabic is authoritative; the cost is taken as a Low grade.
- **Descriptive fidelity over comprehensiveness.** Internal diversity in how the tradition has articulated this account across its history is **not** surveyed here; the claim characterises the account at the level the scriptural record supports and does not present it as the only articulation.

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Descriptive reporting with an explicit register firewall**, not inference. The one real reasoning risk is **register leakage** — letting a strong self-account do historical work, or letting historical findings function as a verdict on the self-account. It is controlled three ways: (1) the historical claims' reasons are stated in their own records and are demonstrably independent of this one; (2) the convergence between the two tracks is named and explicitly denied evidential weight; (3) the graph carries **no** typed edge between the tracks. Second risk: **over-precision from a single translation** — controlled by refusing verse-level citation and grading the terminological component down.

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **self_account_as_restoration — Level 3 (Moderate):** the restorationist shape of the self-account (continuity of a single message; confirmation-and-correction; Abraham prior to both later communities; *millat Ibrāhīm*) is **not a contested characterisation**. **The grade is capped by verification posture, not by scholarly doubt** — this circuit read no interior, consulted one translation, and cited nothing at verse level. **Not Level 4**, for that reason alone, and the reason is recorded so a future circuit with the Arabic in hand knows exactly what would lift it.
- **terminological_reading_translation_limit — Level 2 (Low):** taken deliberately, under SRC-0151's binding limit, in place of asserting a reading of *ḥanīf* / *millat Ibrāhīm* / *tanzīl* / *ummī* that a single English rendering cannot underwrite.
- **divergence_from_scholarly_picture_recorded — Level 3 (Moderate):** the divergences are recorded accurately at the level both tracks are established; the two-track table is a **record**, not an adjudication.
- **No Level 4 or 5.** R0. **These grades neither moved nor were moved by any grade in CLM-0095/0096/0097.**

# Limitations

Establishes **nothing** historical; adjudicates **nothing** theological; supports **no** reading turning on a contested rendering; surveys **no** internal diversity of articulation across the tradition's history.

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Treat the self-account as evidence for hypothesis (b) (separate root).** Rejected — that is the dual-track violation the investigation exists to prevent. Hypothesis (b) is adopted on historical grounds in CLM-0096/0097 alone.
2. **Treat the self-account as a later apologetic construction.** Rejected — asserting it would be adjudicating the self-account historically, on a base that carries no evidence for the assertion.
3. **Assert a reading of *ḥanīf* or *millat Ibrāhīm* from the single catalogued translation.** Rejected — SRC-0151's binding limit; grading down was taken instead.
4. **Read the two tracks' agreement (neither models descent) as mutual corroboration.** Rejected explicitly, and named in element (iii) so the refusal is checkable.
