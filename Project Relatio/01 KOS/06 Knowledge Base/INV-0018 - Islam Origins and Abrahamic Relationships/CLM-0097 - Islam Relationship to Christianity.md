# CLM-0097

# Islam Relationship to Christianity

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **Islam's historical relationship to Christianity is INFLUENCE AND ENGAGEMENT, NOT DESCENT — reasoned independently of CLM-0096, and reaching the same category by a different route.** The engagement is dense and, distinctively, **simultaneously appropriative and repudiative**: the Qur'an takes up Christian narrative and figures at length while explicitly rejecting central Christian doctrinal claims. **That is not the shape of descent.** No evidence in the catalogued base places the earliest Muslim community *within* a Christian community, and the base contains **no** descent-shaped Christian thesis comparable to *Hagarism*'s on the Jewish side. **Therefore NO `branches_from` edge is drawn from ENT-0015 to ENT-0013.** On the entity-precision requirement the situation is the **inverse** of CLM-0096's: **ENT-0013 Christianity (30 CE → present) PASSES the chronological-coherence check**, but it models **the tradition whole**, whereas the documented contact was with **specific eastern communities — Syriac (East Syrian and miaphysite) and Ethiopic/Aksumite Christianity, and the Christianised Arab tribal confederations** — which the vault does not model. **Attaching an edge to the tradition-whole would over-generalise**; that is a second, differently-shaped entity gap, recorded and routed to Anchor Fit. `disputed` is not used and could not be: descent is not disputed here, it is **not established**.

---

# Element (i) — Documented Contact and Its Evidence Base

- **Communities in and around the formation region.** Syriac Christianity (both the East Syrian tradition and the miaphysite churches) was the dominant Christian presence to Arabia's north and east; the Christianised Arab tribal confederations sat on the Byzantine and Sasanian frontiers; **Ethiopic/Aksumite Christianity** was the presence across the Red Sea, with documented Aksumite involvement in sixth-century South Arabia; and Najrān in southern Arabia held a Christian community. **SRC-0149 Griffith is the base's principal surface** for Christianity in the Arabic-speaking world; SRC-0150 for the wider ecology.
- **Qur'anic engagement with Christian material.** The Qur'an engages Jesus and Mary at length — Mary is the subject of a named sūra — takes up material paralleling apocryphal and infancy-gospel traditions and other para-biblical Christian narrative, and addresses Christian communities directly as *People of the Book*. **SRC-0148 Reynolds is the base's surface** for the biblical-subtext reading.
- **Engagement that is simultaneously repudiative.** The same corpus explicitly rejects divine sonship and the trinitarian formulation, and denies the crucifixion-as-death. **This is characterised here at the thematic level only**: no passage is quoted or paraphrased, and **no verse-level citation is asserted**, because the base's primary text (SRC-0151) has an unread interior and its own record binds any reading that turns on a contested rendering (see CLM-0098). **The verified 691/692 Dome of the Rock inscription** (CLM-0095) is the earliest *public* documentary attestation of this doctrinal positioning that this circuit verified.
- **The mode of transmission, negatively established.** SRC-0149's subject is that a **written Arabic Bible is not demonstrable before Islam**. If so, Christian scriptural and narrative material reached the Qur'anic milieu **not as an Arabic text but through oral, liturgical, homiletic, and para-biblical channels** in Syriac, Greek, and Ethiopic. **That is a real and important finding about channel — and it is inferential about mechanism**, which is why `transmission_mechanism` is graded **Low**.

# Element (ii) — Relationship-Type Assessment (independently reasoned — NOT inherited from CLM-0096)

> **Independence discipline.** CLM-0096's answer is not evidence here. The bar is re-stated, the Christian-side evidence is measured against it on its own, and the reasons below are Christian-side reasons. That the two claims land in the same category is a **result**, not a transfer — and the *routes* differ materially (a weighed-and-failed descent thesis there; **no descent thesis in the base at all** here, plus a repudiation pattern absent there).

## (a) The bar, re-stated

A `branches_from` edge to ENT-0013 would require evidence that **the earliest Muslim community emerged out of a Christian community as its parent** — constituted within it and separating from it. Knowing Christian material, arguing with Christian communities, or sharing narrative and vocabulary with them does not meet that bar.

## (b) The evidence measured against it

- **The base contains no descent-shaped Christian thesis.** This is a substantive asymmetry with CLM-0096 and is stated as such: there, a genuine descent candidate existed and was weighed; here, **none of SRC-0143/0147/0148/0149/0150 proposes that Islam derives from Christianity as a parent tradition.** SRC-0148's own record states that intertextual relation is not historical genealogy; SRC-0149's states that availability of biblical material is not derivation. **The claim does not rest on the base's silence alone** — the counter-indications in (c) are positive.
- **A recorded base gap that cuts the other way, disclosed rather than exploited.** There *are* positions in the wider field proposing a Syriac-Christian lectionary or liturgical substratum beneath the Qur'anic text — a strongly derivation-shaped family of theses. **The existence of that literature is reported here parametrically and was NOT verified this session, and no work in it is named, cited, or characterised beyond this sentence** (remediation, Critical Review – RQ-0018 Flag 1). **It is not represented in the catalogued base** (INV-0018 §2.5 recorded Syriac-language scholarship as a corpus gap), and this circuit therefore **does not weigh it, adopt it, or dismiss it.** They are named here so that the record does not silently benefit from their absence. **This disclosure is a reason `relationship_type_influence_not_descent` is graded Moderate rather than High.**
- **Positive counter-indications.** (1) **The repudiation pattern**: the corpus engages Christian material intensively while rejecting the doctrines that define the tradition — an interlocutor's posture, not a descendant's. (2) **The cultic centre is Arabian**, not a Christian liturgical inheritance. (3) **The community's self-address positions Christians as a distinct addressed community** (*People of the Book*), i.e. as others in relation, not as the community's own antecedent institution. (4) **SRC-0143's porous-boundaries reading**, the base's strongest statement of early monotheist proximity, is about **boundary permeability among neighbouring monotheisms**, not about parentage.
- **Determination: INFLUENCE AND ENGAGEMENT, NOT DESCENT.** Graded **Moderate**, held down by the unread interiors, the disclosed Syriac-scholarship gap, and the inferential character of the transmission mechanism.

## (c) ENTITY-PRECISION REQUIREMENT — worked explicitly (the inverse case)

**(a) The specific existing entity a candidate edge would attach to.** **ENT-0013 Christianity** — the only Christian tradition entity in the vault.

**(b) Chronological-coherence check — PASSES.** ENT-0013's bounds are `range_start_year: 30`, `range_end_year: present`. A seventh-century relationship to it is chronologically coherent. **This is the opposite result from ENT-0012's on the Jewish side, and the difference is recorded rather than smoothed**: the two entity-precision outcomes in this family fail for *different reasons*, which is itself informative about the requirement's value.

**(c) Is the tradition-whole the honest target? NO — over-generality.** ENT-0013 models **Christianity as such**, warranted by CLM-0093 as the first-century movement that separated from Second Temple Judaism. The **documented contact in the Qur'anic milieu was not with "Christianity as such"** but with **particular eastern communities**: East Syrian and miaphysite Syriac Christianity, Ethiopic/Aksumite Christianity, the Christianised Arab confederations, and Najrān — bodies distinguished from one another (and from the Chalcedonian imperial church) precisely on the Christological questions the Qur'anic material engages. **The vault models none of them**, and modelling them is out of scope: they are sub-traditions, and this family creates no sub-tradition entity (INV-0018 §2.4). An edge to ENT-0013 would assert a relation to the whole where the evidence documents relations to parts.

**FINDING — ENTITY GAP, SECOND SHAPE (routed to Anchor Fit).** *The vault's tradition-entity layer has **no resolution below the tradition-whole**, so a documented relationship to specific historic communities within a tradition has nowhere honest to attach. This is a distinct gap from CLM-0096's (**over-generality** here, **chronological incoherence plus character mismatch** there), and it is likely to recur in every family whose contacts are with sub-tradition bodies.* **No entity is created.**

**Independence of the two findings, again.** As in CLM-0096, the entity gap is **not** the reason no edge is drawn. The evidence is. Both are recorded separately.

## (d) Edge decision, and the `disputed` guard

- **NO `branches_from` edge from ENT-0015 to ENT-0013.** Combined with CLM-0096, **Islam has no lineage edge at all** and renders as an **unconnected node** on the timeline — an honest rendering under INV-0018 §3.1, requiring no repair.
- **`disputed` is not used.** Descent is **not established** here, not *disputed*; the qualifier may never hedge for influence.
- The influence finding is recorded in prose and routed to **Anchor Fit Part 2**, where — with INV-0011 as the pre-existing first case — Islam is the **second** case for a possible `influenced_by` edge type.

# Element (iii) — Rival Readings (steelmanned, with cost stated)

- **Rival A — a Syriac-Christian derivation reading.** Steelmanned as far as honesty permits: if the Qur'anic text substantially reworks a Syriac Christian liturgical or lectionary substratum, the relationship would be derivation-shaped and would press toward an edge. **Cost and disposition:** the base does not represent this literature (a recorded §2.5 gap), so this circuit **cannot weigh it** — and says so rather than treating the gap as a refutation. It is neither adopted nor dismissed; it is **the strongest reason this claim's determination is Moderate and not High**, and it is flagged as the highest-value target for any owner-approved base extension.
- **Rival B — Islam as a Christian heresy (the Byzantine and medieval European characterisation).** Steelmanned at its strongest as a historical *reading*: an Abrahamic monotheism sharing figures and eschatology, denying Christ's divinity — a shape the ancient heresiological categories fit to Arianism and similar movements. **Cost:** it is a **confessional and polemical** category, produced by a tradition classifying an outsider, and INV-0018 brackets exactly that register (§2.1). Historically it fails on its own terms: the movement had its own scripture, prophet, cultic centre, and community from the outset, and did not arise within a Christian ecclesial body. **Does not survive** — but it is stated at strength and refused on evidence, **not** waved away as offensive, because refusing it by register alone would be a different failure of neutrality.
- **Rival C — the opposite slide: "the Christian material is superficial or late."** Steelmanned: on a late-crystallisation reading the Christian material belongs to a later milieu; alternatively, the parallels are generic late-antique monotheist commonplaces rather than specifically Christian. **Cost:** the density and specificity of the engagement — named figures, distinctive narrative, and doctrinally targeted repudiation attested publicly by 691/692 — resist a "superficial" reading, and relocation is not deletion. **Does not survive as a reason to lower `documented_engagement_with_christianity` below Moderate.**
- **Net.** Rivals A and C bracket the determination from opposite sides; Rival B is refused on evidence rather than on register. The determination sits at **influence, not descent**, Moderate.
