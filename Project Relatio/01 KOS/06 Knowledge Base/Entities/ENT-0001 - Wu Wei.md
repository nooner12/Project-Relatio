# 1. Identification

**Term:** wu wei (Pinyin *wúwéi*), Chinese 無為.
**Literal gloss:** "non-doing" / "non-action."
**Domain:** early Chinese thought; central to philosophical Daoism (*Daodejing*, *Zhuangzi*), also present in Confucian and other early texts.

---

# 2. Definition

*Wu wei* denotes **effortless, non-coercive action in accord with the natural way (the Dao)** — acting without contrived striving, forcing, or self-conscious interference. It is **not** literal inaction or passivity.

The concept is stated paradoxically in the primary texts:
- 為無為 (*wéi wú wéi*) — "act without acting" / "do non-doing" (*Daodejing* 63).
- 道常無為而無不為 (*Dao chang wu wei er wu bu wei*) — "the Dao does nothing, yet nothing is left undone" (*Daodejing* 37).

The paradox resolves once *wu wei* is read as the absence of *forced, self-conscious striving* rather than the absence of *activity*.
