# ENT-0006

# Co-Regulation

## Draft Entity Record

---

# 1. Identification
**Co-regulation** — the interactive process (Rosanbalm & Murray 2017) through which a warm, responsive, skilled adult (caregiver, teacher, coach, or mentor) supports a child's or young person's development of **self-regulation**: the capacity to manage attention, emotion, and behaviour. It reframes "getting a child to behave" as "building the child's *own* regulation over time through relationship, structure, and coaching."

# 2. Definition
Co-regulation operates through three caregiver supports:
- **Warm, responsive relationship** — care and affection; reading and responding to the child's cues; support in times of stress.
- **Structured, predictable environment** — consistent routines, clear expectations, and defined, logical consequences.
- **Skills instruction / coaching** — modelling, opportunities for practice, prompting, and reinforcement of each step toward independent regulation.

Its active ingredients are **social cues, predictability, and a relationship the child experiences as safe** ("felt safety"). These are the same design features named in domain 5 of INV-0008 (predictability, routine, co-regulation through skilled adult presence, graduated challenge, felt safety).

# 3. Classification (per KOS-0004)
- **Category:** Abstract developmental / psychological **process construct** — relational and dynamic, not a physical object or single event.
- **Ontological status (KOS-0004 humility):** A **theoretical construct with strong developmental-science grounding**, not a directly measured quantity. Its role in self-regulation development is well attested; **effect magnitudes in program (non-caregiving) settings are largely unquantified.** Held as a strong interpretive/design lens, not a measured law.

# 4. Why It Is Load-Bearing (relevance to INV-0008)
Co-regulation is the **construct linking domains 4 and 5** — mentored adult presence and trauma-aware/felt-safety design converge on it. It earns an entity record on the merit test: **more than one line of reasoning depends on it** (CLM-0038 instantiates it; CLM-0037's mentoring-relationship-quality moderators are a co-regulatory mechanism), and it is a **plausibly cross-investigation** construct recurring in child-development, education, clinical/trauma, and coaching literatures. Not created for symmetry.

# 5. Scope, Boundaries, and Cautions
- **Provenance caution:** the strongest evidence base is **caregiver–child (parenting)** research across birth to young adulthood; some strands draw on **at-risk/clinical** populations. The *universal design-feature* framing is well-grounded developmentally but must not silently import clinical-population effect claims (see CLM-0038).
- **Transfer caution:** applying caregiver co-regulation to **non-parental adults in a weekly community program** is a reasonable but **untested extension**; magnitudes are not established in that setting.
- **Not** a synonym for discipline, compliance, or mere routine: it is relational and skill-building, aimed at the child's growing independent regulation.
