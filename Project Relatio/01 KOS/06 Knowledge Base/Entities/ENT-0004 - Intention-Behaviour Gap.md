# ENT-0004

# Intention–Behaviour Gap

## Draft Entity Record

---

# 1. Identification
The **intention–behaviour gap** — the well-replicated discrepancy between what people *intend* to do (form a goal to do) and what they *actually do*. In health/wellness behaviour it is the gap between deciding to exercise, eat differently, meditate, or improve sleep and reliably enacting and sustaining that behaviour.

# 2. Definition
A construct from social/health psychology (action-control and dual-process traditions) denoting that **intention is a necessary but weak and insufficient cause of behaviour**. Empirically, the gap is asymmetric: people who lack intention almost never perform the behaviour, whereas a large share of people *with* positive intention still fail to act (Rhodes & de Bruijn 2013 for physical activity: non-intenders ~97–98% inactive; roughly **half of intenders** fail to translate intention into activity). The gap widens further over time — the move from *initiation* to *maintenance* is a second, distinct failure zone (Kwasnicka et al. 2016).

# 3. Classification (per KOS-0004)
- **Category:** Abstract / theoretical construct (a measured psychological regularity), not a concrete object. It is an explanatory pattern operationalised as the statistical discordance between measured intention and measured behaviour.
- **Ontological status (KOS-0004 humility):** Real as a **robust empirical regularity** across many behaviours and populations; its *magnitude* is domain- and measurement-dependent (the exact intender-failure percentage varies by study and restatement). Held as a well-supported pattern, not a fixed constant.

# 4. Why It Is Load-Bearing (relevance to INV-0006)
The intention–behaviour gap is the **conceptual hinge of the whole investigation**. The owner's two-part question — why people fail to maintain wellness interventions, and what best counteracts that — is, restated, *why the gap exists and what closes it*:
- **The "factors" half** is an anatomy of the gap: weak/controlled motivation and low self-efficacy (why intention is fragile), the fact that maintenance depends on different determinants than initiation (why the gap re-opens over time), and baseline attrition in self-directed formats.
- **The "interventions" half** is a catalogue of gap-closers: self-monitoring and progress feedback, implementation intentions/planning (the most direct gap-closer), autonomy-supportive motivation (making intention robust), and habit formation (making enactment automatic so it no longer needs an intention at all).
- It also **cross-links** to the durability findings of INV-0004 (T2D lifestyle change) and INV-0005 (chronic-stress interventions): all three turn on the same distinction between *starting* a health behaviour and *sustaining* it.
