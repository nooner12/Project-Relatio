# CLM-0059

# Filamentous Fungi Grow by Hyphal-Tip Extension and Forage Adaptively — Directing Growth Toward Resources, Reinforcing Productive Connecting Cords and Regressing Unproductive Mycelium — a Well-Attested, Replicated Whole-Network Behavior (High) Whose "Adaptive" Character Is Established Behaviorally Without Implying Cognition

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Filamentous fungi extend at the **hyphal tip** and grow as **mycelial networks that forage adaptively**: on encountering a new resource, biomass and nutrients are **reallocated** toward it, **productive connecting cords thicken and are reinforced**, and **unproductive mycelium regresses and is recycled** — a selective, resource-directed remodelling repeatedly demonstrated in saprotrophic cord-forming systems. The *descriptive* facts (tip growth, foraging, reallocation) are near-settled; the *causal/functional* term "adaptive" is warranted at the **behavioral** level (growth is contingent on resource quantity, location and decay state) **without** thereby implying learning, intention, or cognition.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** (tip extension, foraging, reallocation) **+ bounded causal/functional** ("adaptive" = resource-contingent remodelling). Not interpretive: no cognition is asserted here.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (repeatable soil-microcosm and field experiments; quantitative network analysis).
- **SRC-0077 (Boddy):** decades of cord-forming-basidiomycete experiments — mycelia reallocate biomass/nutrients on locating new resources depending on relative inoculum/resource size, decay state, prior colonization, and geometry; productive routes thicken, unproductive ones regress.
- **SRC-0078 (Fricker et al. 2017):** the mycelium as a self-organizing adaptive network that explores territory while maintaining transport, adapting by **selective reinforcement of major routes and recycling of redundant material** — quantified with graph-theoretic/flow methods.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 5 — repeatedly and independently demonstrated over decades, multiple species and labs.
- Relevance: 5 — directly answers the "how do they grow/forage" part of the primary question.
- Independence: 3 — Boddy (ecology) and Fricker (network biophysics) are distinct programs converging, **but both anchor sources share Boddy as a (co-)author** (SRC-0077 is Boddy; SRC-0078 is Fricker, Heaton, Jones & Boddy), so they are not fully independent *for this claim's cited sources*. The phenomenon itself is replicated across species and labs well beyond these two records, which is what sustains the Level 4 confidence. *(Lowered 4 → 3 per Critical Review – RQ-0012 F-3.)*
- Quality: 5 — controlled microcosms plus quantitative imaging/network metrics.
- Limitations: establishes **observed adaptive foraging**; does **not** establish that foraging is cognitive, learned, or goal-representing.

---

# Source Evaluation
Both anchors are deflationary/mechanistic in register (no cognition hype), which strengthens the descriptive claim: the phenomenon is agreed even by non-enthusiast experts. This is the firmest ground in the investigation.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** microcosm foraging generalizes to field networks (well-supported for cord-formers; bounded for other guilds).
- **Semantic:** "adaptive/foraging" is used in the ecological sense (fitness-relevant, resource-contingent), not the psychological sense — an assumption made explicit to prevent the datum→interpretation slide.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive** from a large, replicated experimental base. **Risks checked:** *anthropomorphic projection* (named — "foraging"/"decision"/"choice" are ecological shorthands here, not mental states; the claim is deliberately capped at behavioral adaptiveness); *over-generalization* (named — cord-formers are the best-studied guild; claim scoped to filamentous networks generally with that caveat).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 4 (High)** — that fungi grow by tip extension and forage adaptively with cord reinforcement/regression. **Why not Level 5:** Level 5 is reserved; and the single word "adaptive," even bounded behaviorally, carries a whisker of functional interpretation, so High is the honest ceiling. **Why not lower:** the evidence is replicated, independent, mechanistically understood, and uncontested across poles.

---

# Limitations
- Licenses "fungi forage adaptively (behaviorally)," **not** "fungi decide/choose/know."
- Best-established for saprotrophic cord-formers; other guilds (e.g. some mycorrhizal, aquatic fungi) are less exhaustively characterized.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Cognitive reading ("the fungus chooses").** Steelmanned via Money (SRC-0089): the contingency looks decision-like. Rejected *at this claim* as unwarranted surplus — the behavioral data are fully explained by local growth rules + resource gradients; the cognitive reading is graded elsewhere (CLM-0064/0066), not here.
2. **Pure passive diffusion.** Rejected: reallocation is directional and resource-contingent, not mere passive spread — the reinforcement/regression pattern exceeds passive growth.
