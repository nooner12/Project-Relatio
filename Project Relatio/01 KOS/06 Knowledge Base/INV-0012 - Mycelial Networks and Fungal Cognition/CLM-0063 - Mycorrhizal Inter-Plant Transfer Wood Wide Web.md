# CLM-0063

# A Bounded Split Verdict on the "Wood Wide Web": Belowground Carbon/Nutrient MOVEMENT Between Plants via Mycorrhizal Fungi Is Real (Moderate), but the STRONG Claims — That Common Mycorrhizal Networks Are Widespread, Ecologically Significant Routes That "Mother Trees" Use to Preferentially Provision Kin or Send Warning Signals — Are NOT Established (Low), With the Kin-Provisioning Claim Having No Peer-Reviewed Field Support

## Draft Claim Record

> **The split IS the finding for this claim (INV-0012 Non-Negotiable 7).** Both poles are steelmanned; the evidence genuinely divides by *strength of claim*.

---

# Claim
> **(Weak claim, Moderate):** Carbon and nutrients **can move belowground between plants** in association with shared mycorrhizal fungi, demonstrated in lab and some field systems (Simard et al. 1997). **(Strong claims, Low):** the popular "wood-wide web" package — that **common mycorrhizal networks (CMNs) are widespread, ecologically significant conduits**, that **mature "mother" trees preferentially route resources to kin**, and that trees **send defence/warning signals** through them — is **not established**; a systematic review (Karst, Jones & Hoeksema 2023) finds the field evidence limited, the literature positively citation-biased, and the **preferential mother-tree-to-kin provisioning claim without peer-reviewed field support.**

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal / contested** (does the network *cause* ecologically meaningful, directed inter-plant transfer?). The weak existence claim is causal-descriptive; the strong claims are causal and disputed.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (field isotope experiments) **+ systematic critical review**.
- **SRC-0083 (Simard et al. 1997, pro pole):** reciprocal isotope labelling shows **bidirectional C transfer** between birch and Douglas-fir, net to the sink (~6% of recipient's photosynthate), via a hyphal pathway. Establishes *movement occurs*.
- **SRC-0084 (Karst, Jones & Hoeksema 2023, skeptical pole — load-bearing):** of three popular CMN claims — (1) CMNs are widespread; (2) transfer through CMNs benefits seedlings; (3) mother trees preferentially provision kin/signal danger — field support is **limited (1)**, **weaker than cited (2)**, and **absent from the peer-reviewed record (3)**; documents a **positive citation bias.** *Notably, co-author M.D. Jones also co-authored Simard 1997 — an insider correction.*
- **SRC-0085 (Kiers et al. 2011):** where transfer/exchange is regulated, it is explained by **biological-market (reward/source–sink) dynamics**, not by tree "intent" — deflates the directed-provisioning narrative.
- **Rebuttal (steelman, live-verified this session):** a "Facts, not fantasy" response (Klein et al., 2023 — first author **Tamir Klein**; Klein, Rog, Livne-Luzon, van der Heijden & Körner, *Open Research Europe* 3, doi:10.12688/openreseurope.16594.1) concedes citation bias and over-interpretation but maintains that **carbon movement via mycorrhizal pathways is solid and accumulating** and may serve osmoregulation/defence rather than major biomass subsidy.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 for *movement occurs*; 2 for the *strong directed-network* claims.
- Relevance: 5 — directly the CMN question.
- Independence: 5 — the strongest independence signal in the investigation: a Simard-1997 co-author (Jones) co-authors the skeptical review, and a further group rebuts it — a genuine, self-correcting scientific dispute.
- Quality: 4 for the review's evidence-mapping; field experiments on directed transfer are few and confounded (soil pathways vs. hyphal pathways hard to separate).
- Limitations: establishes that *movement occurs* and that *the strong narrative is unproven*; does **not** settle the ecological significance of transfer either way.

---

# Source Evaluation
This is where evidence-quality discipline bites hardest. Simard's 1997 primary result is sound *for what it measured* (transfer between two trees in one system); the popular "wood-wide web" extrapolated far beyond it. Karst et al. is authoritative precisely because it is an **insider** correction, not an outside attack, and it targets *overinterpretation and citation practice*, not the bare existence of transfer. Simard's later public advocacy (mother trees) is a named motivated-reasoning vector on the strong claims.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** isotope appearing in a neighbour reflects transfer — but **through the CMN specifically** vs. via soil/root/microbial pathways is the crux, and is often not cleanly separable.
- **Cultural/narrative:** the "web/mother tree" framing imports intention and altruism that the data do not require — named and bracketed.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive, split.** **Risks checked:** *positive citation bias* (the named, documented failure mode here — Karst et al. quantify it); *conflating "transfer happens" with "network-mediated, significant, and directed"* (the core equivocation — firewalled into weak vs. strong claims); *anthropomorphic/altruism projection* ("mother tree feeds her children" — named and rejected as unsupported); *over-correction* (the skeptics could over-swing to "no transfer at all" — checked by conceding the movement datum and the Klein et al. rebuttal).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Weak claim (belowground inter-plant C/nutrient movement occurs) — Level 3 (Moderate).**
- **Strong claims (widespread, significant, directed CMN "wood-wide web") — Level 2 (Low)**; the specific **kin-provisioning** claim is **Low, leaning negative** (no peer-reviewed field support per Karst et al.).
- **Headline for the claim — Level 2 (Low)**, because the claim's principal work is to *deny* the strong narrative its popular confidence; the Moderate weak-claim is carried but explicitly firewalled from the strong claims. Not lower, because transfer genuinely occurs and a credible rebuttal maintains the evidence is accumulating.

**Headline-selection rule for two-level split claims (stated per Critical Review – RQ-0012 F-2; shared with CLM-0061).** Where a claim carries two sub-levels, **the headline is set at the sub-level that carries the claim's principal assertive burden — i.e. the proposition the claim is *for*.** Applying it:
- **CLM-0061** is a *hold-the-datum* claim: its assertive burden is "fungi really do produce stimulus-sensitive electrical spikes," and it merely *denies* the language reading. So it headlines at the **higher** sub-level (the datum, Moderate).
- **CLM-0063** is a *deny-the-strong-narrative* claim: its assertive burden is "the strong wood-wide-web package is not established," while it merely *concedes* that movement occurs. So it headlines at the **lower** sub-level (the strong claims, Low).
The two directions are **the same rule**, not two rules: each headlines at whatever sub-level the claim is chiefly asserting. The asymmetry is a feature of what each claim is *for*, not an inconsistency. The FND-0012 §2 breakdown always lists **both** sub-levels explicitly, so no downstream reader loses the Moderate movement datum behind the Low headline.

---

# Limitations
- Licenses "C/nutrients can move between plants via mycorrhizal fungi (Moderate)" and "the strong wood-wide-web narrative is unproven (Low)"; does **NOT** license the popular picture of forests as deliberate resource-sharing networks, and does **NOT** claim transfer never matters.
- Ectomycorrhizal (Simard) and arbuscular-mycorrhizal (Kiers) systems differ; conclusions are about the *dispute's shape*, not a single settled magnitude.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Strong wood-wide-web (Simard, enthusiast).** Steelmanned: real transfer + mapped networks + suggestive seedling-establishment patterns. Rejected as *established* (Low): field evidence for significance/direction is thin and citation-biased.
2. **Strong deflation (no meaningful transfer).** Steelmanned via Karst et al. Not fully accepted: movement is demonstrated and a credible rebuttal (Klein et al.) holds it is solid and accumulating — so "Low," not "refuted."
3. **Middle (transfer real, significance/direction unproven).** Accepted — the position the split occupies.
