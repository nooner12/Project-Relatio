# FND-0012

# Fungal Mycelia Are Genuinely Sophisticated Adaptive Biological Networks — They Forage, Translocate, and Integrate as Whole Colonies (High) — but the Evidence Lands at Rungs (a)–(b) of the Cognition Ladder, Reaches Only Operationally Into (c), Warrants "Basal Cognition" (d) Only as a Contested Framing (Low), and Leaves Sentience (e) Undetermined; the "Language" and Strong "Wood-Wide-Web" and "Fungal Brain" Claims Are NOT Supported

# 1. Statement

> **The primary answer.** Filamentous fungi grow by hyphal-tip extension and behave as **genuinely sophisticated, self-organizing adaptive networks**: they forage in a resource-directed way, reinforcing productive cords and regressing unproductive mycelium (**CLM-0059, High**); they **translocate** carbon, nutrients and water bidirectionally under source–sink regulation (**CLM-0060, High**); and the colony behaves as a **physiologically integrated unit** coordinated by chemical/cytoplasmic (and likely ionic) signaling plus mass flow, with global order emerging from **local rules, not a central controller** (**CLM-0062, Moderate**). This is real, impressive biology — and it is **agreed even by deflationary experts.**
>
> **Graded against the §1 cognition ladder, the evidence lands as a map, not a verdict of "intelligent":**
> - **(a) Stimulus–response — High** (uncontested).
> - **(b) Adaptive foraging / information integration — High** (CLM-0059/0060/0062).
> - **(c) Learning & memory — Low.** Fungi show a real but *operational* memory-like carry-over (directional regrowth from prior growth history — **CLM-0064, Level 2 (Low), single-group** — the datum rests on a single unreplicated Boddy-group study), but cognitive learning (habituation/association/anticipation) in fungi is largely unestablished; the dramatic maze/Tokyo-rail "memory" results are ***Physarum*, an acellular slime mold — a protist, NOT a fungus** — and are firewalled out of the fungal evidence base.
> - **(d) Minimal / basal cognition — Low, and only as a contested interpretive *framing*** (**CLM-0066**). Under Lyon's biology-first criteria a case can be *stated*; deflationary critics read the same data as ordinary adaptive physiology. The evidence licenses "**arguably a candidate for basal cognition**," never "**is cognitive.**"
> - **(e) Sentience / consciousness — BRACKETED as undetermined.** Neither asserted nor denied; the strong pole (Baluška & Reber's Cellular Basis of Consciousness) and the strong deflation are both live and neither is settled by evidence.
>
> **Three popular claims are specifically NOT supported:**
> 1. **"Fungi have a language."** Fungi *do* produce stimulus-sensitive electrical spikes (a real datum, **CLM-0061, Moderate**, independently attested since Olsson & Hansson 1995), but the interpretation that these spikes constitute a **language / encode information** (Adamatzky 2022) is **Very Low** — plausibly part-artifact, with no language-like structure (Blatt et al. 2024) and hard-to-replicate measurements (Buffi et al. 2025).
> 2. **The strong "wood-wide web."** Belowground carbon/nutrient *movement* between plants via mycorrhizal fungi is real (**CLM-0063, Moderate**), but the strong package — CMNs as widespread, ecologically significant, *directed* networks in which "mother trees" preferentially feed kin or send warnings — is **Low**; the kin-provisioning claim has **no peer-reviewed field support** (Karst, Jones & Hoeksema 2023, an *insider* correction documenting positive citation bias).
> 3. **The "fungal brain."** The neural-network analogy holds only as a **limited structural metaphor** (Moderate); it **breaks decisively** on dynamics and function — no neurons, no synapses, no action potentials in the neuronal sense, no central integrator, and timescales of **seconds-to-hours vs. neuronal milliseconds** (**CLM-0065, Low** for the strong analogy). Fungal networks resemble *Physarum*/adaptive-transport systems more than brains.
>
> **Motivated-reasoning guard.** Two convenient stories were named and resisted: the enthusiast/marketable "fungi think / talk / mother their young" narrative (checked by the datum≠interpretation discipline, the *Physarum* firewall, and the citation-bias evidence) and the over-corrective "it's mere chemistry, cognition talk is nonsense" (checked by the genuinely High rungs (a)–(b) and the legitimacy of basal cognition as a research program). The honest result sits between them: **remarkable adaptive network biology; cognition only as a contested minimal framing; sentience bracketed.**

# 2. Supporting Claims (native `Level N`, KOS-0003 §8)

- **CLM-0059** (growth & adaptive foraging) — **Level 4 (High)** — *descriptive/causal*. Tip growth, resource-directed reallocation, cord reinforcement/regression.
- **CLM-0060** (resource transport) — **Level 4 (High)** — *descriptive*. Bidirectional, source–sink-regulated translocation.
- **CLM-0061** (electrical signaling) — datum **Level 3 (Moderate)**; "language" interpretation **Level 1 (Very Low)** — *descriptive datum + flagged interpretive over-reach*. Held apart.
- **CLM-0062** (chemical/Ca²⁺ signaling & whole-colony integration) — **Level 3 (Moderate)** — *descriptive/causal*. Integration solid; specific molecular mechanisms less pinned.
- **CLM-0063** (mycorrhizal inter-plant transfer / "wood-wide web") — movement **Level 3 (Moderate)**; strong CMN claims **Level 2 (Low)**; kin-provisioning Low, leaning negative — *causal/contested, split verdict*.
- **CLM-0064** (learning/memory) — carry-over **Level 2 (Low), single-group** *(lowered from Moderate per Critical Review F-1)*; cognitive learning **Level 2 (Low)**; headline **Level 2 (Low)** — *causal/interpretive*. *Physarum* firewalled.
- **CLM-0065** (neural-network analogy) — structural **Level 3 (Moderate)**; strong analogy **Level 2 (Low, leaning negative)** — *interpretive/analogical*. Disanalogies decisive.
- **CLM-0066** (cognition verdict) — rungs (a)–(b) **Level 4 (High)**; (c) **Low**; (d) basal cognition **Level 2 (Low)** as a contested framing; (e) sentience **BRACKETED**; headline **Level 2 (Low)** — *interpretive/metaphysical*.

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**The finding is a synthesis and makes no claim above its weakest necessary link.** The **descriptive network biology** (rungs a–b) is secure at **Level 4 (High)** and carries the affirmative half of the answer. But the **cognition verdict** — the part that answers "what intelligence does the evidence warrant?" — is governed by its weakest necessary interpretive links (learning, basal-cognition framing) and is bounded at **Level 2 (Low)**; likewise the "language" interpretation (Very Low), the strong wood-wide-web claims (Low), and the strong neural analogy (Low). **Rung (e) sentience is bracketed, not graded.** **No element reaches Level 5** (reserved; and interpretive/cross-domain inference cannot warrant it — OPS-0003). The correct overall shape is therefore **asymmetric: High for what fungi demonstrably do, Low for what those doings are claimed to mean cognitively** — and that asymmetry is the finding, not a hedge.

# 4. Scope & Limitations (what is NOT supported)
- **NOT supported:** that fungi have a language, communicate propositional content electrically, or that spike-trains encode information (CLM-0061 interpretation is Very Low).
- **NOT supported:** the strong "wood-wide web" — widespread, ecologically significant, directed CMNs; "mother trees" preferentially provisioning kin or sending warnings (CLM-0063 strong claims Low; kin-provisioning without peer-reviewed field support).
- **NOT supported:** that mycelium is a nervous system or brain (CLM-0065 strong analogy Low).
- **NOT supported:** that fungi learn/remember in the cognitive sense, or any capacity imported from *Physarum* (a non-fungal protist — the load-bearing §2.2 firewall).
- **NOT supported / BRACKETED:** fungal sentience or consciousness (rung e) — **undetermined**, neither asserted nor denied; the hard problem is not adjudicated.
- **NOT a magnitude ranking** of fungi or behaviors by "how intelligent," and **not** an individualized/applied claim (agriculture/medicine/pharmacology excluded by construction).
- **NOT a systematic review** — 14 sources chosen to represent both poles and be live-verifiable; the verdicts are robust for this evidence set and represent the mainstream shape of the disputes, not a meta-analytic settlement.
