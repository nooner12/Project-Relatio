# CLM-0064

# Fungal Mycelia Show a Genuine, Operationally-Defined "Memory-Like" Carry-Over (Directional Regrowth From Prior Growth History) at Moderate — but Evidence for LEARNING in the Cognitive Sense (Habituation, Association, Anticipation) in FUNGI Specifically Is Weak (Low); the Famous Maze/Rail "Memory" Results Are *Physarum* (a Non-Fungal Protist) and Must NOT Be Imported

## Draft Claim Record

> **Scope firewall (INV-0012 §2.2, load-bearing).** The dramatic maze-solving / Tokyo-rail "memory" results belong to *Physarum polycephalum*, an **acellular slime mold (protist), NOT a fungus** (SRC-0090). They are comparanda only and are **excluded from the fungal evidence base** of this claim. Popular coverage routinely mislabels them as fungal; that error is exactly what this claim guards against.

---

# Claim
> Fungal mycelia exhibit a **real, operationally-defined "memory-like" carry-over effect** — e.g. after severance and re-setting, hyphae preferentially regrow from the side that previously faced a resource (Fukasawa, Savoury & Boddy 2020) — a **structural/physiological retention of prior growth history**, not stored representational content. **Evidence for LEARNING in the cognitive sense** (habituation, associative learning, anticipation) **in fungi specifically is weak.** The strong "fungal memory/intelligence" impression in popular culture is largely borrowed from *Physarum*, which is not a fungus.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal / interpretive** — the carry-over datum is causal-descriptive; whether it counts as "memory/learning" is interpretive and depends on the definition (linked to CLM-0066's framework).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical (fungal) + opinion (enthusiast pole) + comparandum (non-fungal, excluded)**.
- **SRC-0086 (Fukasawa, Savoury & Boddy 2020):** directional regrowth from prior resource orientation — a demonstrated fungal carry-over. The authors **explicitly treat "memory" as operational**, offering external / somatic / structural mechanisms and calling it "a first step in the study of non-neuronal intelligence" (i.e. they hedge, not assert cognition).
- **SRC-0089 (Money 2021, steelman only):** frames such spatial recognition / short-term memory as "cellular consciousness" — the enthusiast reading, quarantined from grading (opinion, not evidence).
- **SRC-0090 (Tero et al. 2010, COMPARANDUM — NOT fungal evidence):** *Physarum* network optimization / "memory" — cited **only** to mark the boundary and to explain why popular "fungal memory" claims are inflated; contributes **zero** evidential weight to this claim (typed `related_to`).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 for the carry-over datum (one well-designed experimental line, but a single Boddy-group study — no cross-lab replication); 1 for cognitive "learning" in fungi (largely absent).
- Relevance: 5 — directly the learning/memory rung (c) of the ladder.
- Independence: 2 — the fungal memory datum rests **entirely on one group** (SRC-0086, Fukasawa/Savoury/Boddy); the other two sources contribute zero datum-weight (Money quarantined as opinion; *Physarum* firewalled to zero). Not broadly replicated across labs.
- Quality: 3 for carry-over; the *Physarum* material is high-quality but **inadmissible** here.
- Limitations: establishes a structural/physiological carry-over on a single study; does **not** establish habituation, association, anticipation, or recall in fungi.

---

# Source Evaluation
Fukasawa/Boddy is the right kind of source: a *fungal* experiment whose authors are careful about the word "memory." Money is honestly labelled opinion. The *Physarum* source is included specifically to be excluded — a discipline mechanism, not evidence.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Semantic:** "memory" is used operationally (history-dependence of behavior), not as neural recall — the whole interpretive burden sits on this distinction.
- **Methodological:** the carry-over is a genuine biological retention, not an experimental artifact of severance (the authors argue it is genuine; independent replication is limited).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive + analogical (guarded).** **Risks checked:** *Physarum→fungus import* (the load-bearing named risk — explicitly enforced; no *Physarum* result counts here); *operational→cognitive slide* (named — "memory-like carry-over" must not become "the fungus remembers/learns"); *single-group generalization* (named — the datum needs broader replication); *anthropomorphic projection* (named, via the Money steelman being quarantined).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Memory-like carry-over (operational) — Level 2 (Low).** *(Lowered from Level 3 (Moderate) per Critical Review – RQ-0012 F-1, path (a).)* A real, measured, history-dependent effect — but its **entire** datum-weight rests on a **single unreplicated study from one group** (SRC-0086; Independence 2, Reliability 3), with no independent corroboration in the evidence set (Money quarantined; *Physarum* zero-weight). This fits KOS-0003 §8 Level 2 ("limited support; major unresolved issues") at least as well as Moderate; single-group, unreplicated evidence is the textbook signature of "limited support," so the honest grade is Low.
- **Learning in the cognitive sense (habituation/association/anticipation) in fungi — Level 2 (Low)**, essentially unestablished on fungal evidence.
- **Headline for the claim — Level 2 (Low)** (unchanged): the claim's net assertion is that fungi show *at most* an operational carry-over and that cognitive learning is unsupported; the weakest necessary link (cognitive learning) governs the headline, and the carry-over sub-grade now agrees with it. Rung (e) sentience not addressed here.

---

# Limitations
- Licenses "fungi show an operational memory-like carry-over (Low, single-group)"; **does NOT** license "fungi learn/remember" cognitively, and **explicitly does NOT** import any *Physarum* capacity.
- The carry-over datum needs independent replication beyond the Boddy group.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Genuine cognitive memory/learning.** Steelmanned via Money and the "non-neuronal intelligence" framing. Rejected as *established* (Low): the fungal data show history-dependence, not demonstrated learning; the strong impression is *Physarum*-borrowed.
2. **No memory at all (pure current-state physiology).** Partly rejected: the directional-regrowth result is a real history-dependence that pure current-state physiology does not predict — hence the carry-over sits at Low, not Very Low (a single genuine effect, not nothing; but single-group and unreplicated, so not Moderate).
3. **Structural carry-over (middle).** Accepted as most parsimonious — the position of the claim.
