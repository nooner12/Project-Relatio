# CLM-0073

# The Non-Christian Sources (Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny) Corroborate, at MODERATE Confidence and With Partial Independence, That the Movement and Its Executed Founder Were Real and Known to Outsiders by the Early Second Century — but No More: the Testimonium Flavianum Is Only Partially Authentic (the Confessional Clauses Are Interpolations), and These Notices Corroborate Existence, Not the Gospel Narrative

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Three non-Christian sources bear on Jesus and the earliest movement, and together they establish — at **Moderate confidence** — that the movement and a founder executed under Pilate were **real and known to outside observers by the early second century**, while establishing **nothing** about the truth of the Gospel narrative:
> - **Tacitus, *Annals* 15.44** (c. 116 CE): "Christus... suffered the extreme penalty... at the hands of... Pontius Pilatus" — widely accepted as **authentic** and the strongest independent Roman notice (though possibly relaying what Christians reported).
> - **Josephus, *Antiquities* 18.63–64** (the *Testimonium Flavianum*): **partially authentic** — the majority view is that a neutral Josephan core existed but the confessional clauses ("He was the Christ," the resurrection line) are **later Christian interpolations**; a real minority holds the whole passage interpolated.
> - **Josephus, *Antiquities* 20.200**: "James, the brother of Jesus who was called Christ" — **more textually secure** than the TF, and the stronger Josephan datum.
> - **Pliny, *Epistles* 10.96** (c. 112 CE): Christians in Bithynia "sang a hymn to Christ as to a god" — attests the movement's existence and worship, not the historical Jesus directly.
> What the external record establishes (with partial independence — see Independence = 3 below): existence, an execution context, and early outside awareness. What it does **not** establish: the Gospel narrative, miracles, or resurrection (bracketed).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — an attestation claim about what the external, non-Christian record documents.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical / testimonial** (near-contemporary secondary notices).
- **SRC-0099 (primary testimonia):** the three sources themselves.
- **SRC-0098 (Meier):** the partial-authenticity reconstruction of the TF and the security of *Ant.* 20.200.
- **SRC-0100 (Ehrman, *Did Jesus Exist?*):** the historicity consensus, of which the external notices are one (secondary) leg.
- **SRC-0101 (Carrier):** the steelmanned mythicist reading (that the notices may not be independent of Christian report) — represented and then judged against the majority.

**Dual-coding (prose only):** evidence class **H-c** (near-contemporary secondary accounts — written 2–3 generations after, by non-eyewitnesses). Within the same H-band the *quality varies*: *Ant.* 20.200 and Tacitus are more secure; the TF is textually compromised. The native confidence is **Moderate** — H-c secondary attestation, partly dependent on Christian report, establishing existence but not narrative.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 3 — reputable authors, but non-eyewitnesses; the TF is textually corrupted.
- Relevance: 4 — bears directly on the existence/early-awareness question, less on the texts' internal claims.
- Independence: 3 — Tacitus/Pliny may relay Christian report; *Ant.* 20.200 is the most plausibly independent; the independence is real but partial.
- Quality: 3 — good ancient sources, but the TF requires reconstruction and Pliny/Tacitus are early-2nd-century.
- Limitations: establishes existence and early external awareness; establishes nothing about the Gospel narrative or any theological claim.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** (H-c, partial independence, one compromised text).
- Consensus strength: **High** that these sources attest the movement's existence and an executed founder; **High** that the TF is partially interpolated; **High** (near-universal) that a historical Jesus existed (SRC-0100). Recorded separately per §9 — the *historicity* consensus is far stronger than any single external notice, because it rests chiefly on the earlier Christian sources (Paul, the Gospels, the pre-literary tradition), not primarily on these external notices.

---

# Source Evaluation
Meier (SRC-0098, a Catholic scholar reaching a partial-authenticity — not maximalist — verdict) disciplines the Josephan evidence; Ehrman (SRC-0100, an agnostic defending historicity) checks the confessional-bias charge; Carrier (SRC-0101) supplies the steelmanned skeptical pole. The external notices are treated as *corroboration of existence*, not as the load-bearing evidence for historicity.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that the confessional clauses of the TF are interpolations (near-universal) and that a neutral core plausibly remains (majority).
- **Epistemological:** that near-contemporary hostile/neutral notices are meaningful evidence of a movement's existence even if partly dependent on the movement's own report.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive.** The best explanation of the external notices is a real movement with an executed founder known to outsiders by c. 112–116 CE. **Risks checked:** *overstating the Testimonium* (named explicitly — the fully-authentic reading is rejected; partial authenticity is reported *with* the fully-interpolated minority kept live); *treating the external notices as independent corroboration of the Gospel narrative* (named — they corroborate existence, not narrative); *mythicist over-reach* (named — Carrier's reading that the notices are non-independent is steelmanned but does not overturn the majority, which also rests on the earlier Christian sources).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** — that the non-Christian sources independently attest the movement's existence, an execution under Pilate, and early outside awareness. **Not Level 4:** the H-c evidence is partly dependent on Christian report, the TF is textually compromised, and the notices are early-2nd-century — they add corroboration, not decisive independent weight. (The broader *historicity* of Jesus is held more firmly, but that confidence rests chiefly on the earlier Christian sources graded in CLM-0068/0074, not on these external notices alone.)

---

# Limitations
- Establishes existence and early external awareness; establishes nothing about the truth of the Gospel narrative or any theological claim (bracketed).
- The TF's usable content is a reconstruction; a minority holds it wholly interpolated.
- Pliny's letter was not full-text-verified this session (SRC-0099 verification note); the load-bearing datum (early worship of Christ) is standard.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Maximalist reading (the TF is fully authentic; the notices confirm the Gospels).** Rejected — the confessional clauses are interpolations; the notices confirm existence, not narrative.
2. **Mythicist reading (the notices are late, dependent, and prove nothing — Carrier).** Steelmanned and rejected as the majority view — *Ant.* 20.200 and Tacitus retain independent value, and the historicity consensus rests mainly on the earlier Christian sources; but the reading correctly cautions against overstating the notices' independence, which is why this claim sits at Moderate.
