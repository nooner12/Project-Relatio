# CLM-0068

# The Seven Undisputed Pauline Letters Are the Earliest Christian Documents, Firmly Datable to c. 50–60 CE — Self-Authored, First-Generation Sources Written Within Roughly Two Decades of the Movement's Origin, Anchored by an External Synchronism (the Gallio Inscription)

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The seven undisputed letters of Paul (Romans, 1–2 Corinthians, Galatians, Philippians, 1 Thessalonians, Philemon) are **the earliest surviving Christian documents**, written c. **50–60 CE** — roughly two decades after the movement's origin (crucifixion c. 30–33 CE). They are **self-authored, first-person, occasional letters**, whose dating is unusually secure for an ancient corpus: internal synchronisms (notably the **Gallio inscription**, which anchors Paul's Corinth stay to c. 51–52 CE) fix the chronology within narrow bounds. This makes them **H-a contemporaneous primary texts** and the firmest evidentiary anchor in the whole New Testament source base.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — a dating statement about the documentary evidence.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (documentary chronology).
- **SRC-0103 (primary texts):** the letters are self-identifying, occasional, and internally datable; 1 Thessalonians (c. 50 CE) is generally the earliest.
- **SRC-0097 (Sanders):** the undisputed Pauline letters are the earliest datable stratum and a first-hand witness within ~two decades of the origin.
- **SRC-0093 (Ehrman):** the standard-textbook dating of the undisputed corpus to the 50s.

**Dual-coding (prose only):** evidence class **H-a** (contemporaneous primary text) — Paul is writing within living memory, as a participant. This is the strongest H-band available in the investigation, and the confidence tracks it.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 5 — the 50s dating is uncontested across the critical field.
- Relevance: 5 — directly answers the "when written" layer for the earliest documents.
- Independence: 5 — the Gallio synchronism is an external, non-Christian epigraphic anchor.
- Quality: 5 — self-authored contemporaneous letters with an external chronological fix.
- Limitations: dates the *documents*; Paul says little about Jesus's earthly life, so early dating of the letters is not early dating of Gospel *narrative* material.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High**.
- Consensus strength: **High** — the 50s dating of the undisputed letters is one of the most secure and least contested judgments in New Testament scholarship.

---

# Source Evaluation
The primary texts (SRC-0103) supply the internal evidence; Sanders (SRC-0097) and Ehrman (SRC-0093) supply the mainstream chronology; the Gallio inscription supplies an external anchor. Convergent and independent.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that the Gallio inscription reliably fixes the proconsulship and thus Paul's Corinth stay (mainstream).
- **Epistemological:** that self-authored occasional letters are strong dating evidence — uncontroversial here.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive/deductive.** From internal self-attestation + the external synchronism to a secure 50s dating. **Risks checked:** *over-precision* (named — individual letters are dated within a few years, not to the month; the range is c. 50–60, not a point); *retrojection* (named — early letters do not license reading Gospel narrative back to the 50s; Paul is early, the Gospels are not).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 4 (High)** — that the undisputed letters are the earliest Christian documents, dating to c. 50–60 CE, on H-a contemporaneous evidence with an external anchor. **Not Level 5:** reserved; and the exact dating of individual letters carries minor residual uncertainty (a few years), appropriate to High.

---

# Limitations
- Dates the *letters*, not the events they presuppose; Paul reports little of Jesus's earthly biography.
- "Undisputed" is the operative scope — the disputed/pseudonymous Paulines are handled separately (CLM-0072).

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Minor redating within the 50s–early 60s.** Accepted as within the band — does not affect the claim that these are the earliest documents.
2. **Radical late-dating of Paul (Dutch Radical / mythicist-adjacent).** Rejected — a fringe position without support in the mainstream; the Gallio anchor and the internal coherence of the chronology stand against it.
