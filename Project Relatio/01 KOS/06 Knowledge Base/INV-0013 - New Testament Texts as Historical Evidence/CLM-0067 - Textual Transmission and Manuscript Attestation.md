# CLM-0067

# The Wording of the New Testament Is Recoverable to a High Degree from an Unusually Rich Manuscript Base, but Not "Perfectly Preserved": the Text Is Reconstructed from Thousands of Witnesses Whose Earliest Fragments (P52) Carry Wide Palaeographic Date-Ranges, and a Small Set of Substantive Variants Bounds the Certainty

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The New Testament text is attested by a **manuscript base far richer than that of any other ancient work** (thousands of Greek manuscripts plus early versions and patristic citations), and text-critical method recovers the earliest reachable wording to a **high degree of confidence** for the overwhelming majority of the text. But this is **recoverability, not inerrant preservation**: (a) no autograph survives, so the "original" is itself a reasoned reconstruction; (b) the earliest witnesses are fragmentary and carry wide palaeographic date-ranges (P52, the earliest, dates only to a broad second-century window, not a fixed c. 125 CE); and (c) a small but real set of substantive variants (the longer ending of Mark 16:9–20; the *pericope adulterae* John 7:53–8:11; the Comma Johanneum 1 John 5:7–8) bounds the certainty. The calibrated position is the middle: neither "the text is perfectly preserved" (apologetic) nor "the text is hopelessly corrupt" (over-skeptical).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — a statement about the state and recoverability of the textual evidence, not about the truth of the text's content.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical / material** (papyrology and textual criticism).
- **SRC-0091 (Metzger & Ehrman):** the manuscript base and its density; the famous variant-count framing (c. 300,000–400,000 variant readings across ~138,000 words, the overwhelming majority orthographic/trivial); the conclusion that even the patristic citations alone would nearly suffice to reconstruct the text — a statement of *recoverability*.
- **SRC-0092 (Nongbri):** P52's palaeographic date is a wide second-century window, not a point-date — the earliest physical witness does not fix an early text as precisely as once claimed.
- **SRC-0103 (primary texts):** the material witnesses themselves — P52; P46 (~200 CE); P66/P75 (~200 CE); Codices Sinaiticus and Vaticanus (4th c., earliest near-complete/complete).

**Dual-coding (prose only):** the evidence class is **H-b** (material/papyrological attestation — the manuscripts) resting on **H-a** documents; the *reconstruction of the autograph wording from the witnesses* is itself **H-e** (text-critical reconstruction). The native confidence is **High** because H-b evidence here is abundant and convergent.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 5 — the manuscript facts and the text-critical method are mainstream and secure.
- Relevance: 5 — directly answers the "how well is the wording preserved" layer (§2.3).
- Independence: 5 — thousands of witnesses across regions/versions/citations provide massively cross-checking, independent attestation.
- Quality: 4 — high, but the step from best-attested reading to autograph is reconstruction, and the earliest witnesses are fragmentary.
- Limitations: establishes recoverability of *wording*, nothing about the *truth* of content; a residual set of substantive variants remains genuinely disputed.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High**.
- Consensus strength: **High** — the richness of the manuscript base and the general recoverability of the wording are not seriously contested; the disputes are over specific variant-units and over the theological *significance* of certain variants (Ehrman vs. more conservative critics), not over the basic recoverability.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0091 (Metzger & Ehrman) spans the establishment and skeptical poles, checking bias in either direction; SRC-0092 (Nongbri) disciplines over-early dating; SRC-0103 is the material evidence itself. The convergence of a conservative handbook and a skeptical papyrologist on "rich base, recoverable wording, bounded uncertainty" is why the claim carries High confidence.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that reasoned-eclectic text criticism reliably approximates the earliest recoverable text (mainstream; itself reconstruction but robust).
- **Epistemological:** that "recoverable wording" and "true content" are distinct questions — this claim answers only the first.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** From the density and convergence of independent witnesses to high recoverability. **Risks checked:** *apologetic inflation* (named — "perfectly preserved"/"99% pure" overstates; the raw variant count is not "errors" but neither is the text variant-free); *over-skeptical deflation* (named — Ehrman's popular framing can imply hopeless corruption; the technical consensus is that the substantive uncertainty is small and bounded); *conflating recoverability with truth* (named — this claim does not touch content-truth).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 4 (High)** — that the wording is recoverable to a high degree from a rich, cross-checking manuscript base, with bounded residual uncertainty. **Not Level 5:** Level 5 is reserved; and the non-surviving autograph, the fragmentary earliest witnesses, and the genuine set of substantive disputed variants introduce exactly the residual uncertainty appropriate to High rather than Very High.

---

# Limitations
- Establishes recoverability of *wording*; establishes nothing about the *historical or theological truth* of the content.
- The date of the earliest witnesses (P52) is itself uncertain (H-e palaeography); the earliest *substantial* witnesses are ~200 CE (P46) and the earliest complete ones 4th-century.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Maximalist preservation ("the NT is essentially perfectly preserved").** Steelmanned: the base *is* extraordinarily rich and most variants are trivial. **Partly accepted, but overstated** — it elides the non-surviving autograph and the substantive variant set; recoverability is high, not total.
2. **Radical corruption ("we can't know what the originals said").** Rejected as over-reach — the convergence of thousands of witnesses makes the substantive uncertainty small and localizable, not pervasive.
