# CLM-0069

# The Four Gospels Are Dated by Modern Scholarly Reconstruction (Not by Any Colophon or Contemporary Witness) to c. 65–110 CE — Mark c. 65–75, Matthew and Luke c. 80–90, John c. 90–110 — a Dating That Carries HIGH Consensus on the Broad First-Century Placement and Relative Order but Only MODERATE Confidence at the Level of Precise Decades

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The four canonical Gospels are dated to c. **65–110 CE** — **Mark c. 65–75** (around or just after the Jewish War and the destruction of the Temple in 70 CE), **Matthew and Luke c. 80–90**, and **John c. 90–110**. Critically, **none of these dates is documented**: there is no colophon, no dated manuscript, and no contemporary witness fixing composition. The dating is entirely a **modern scholarly reconstruction (H-e)** from internal evidence (e.g. Mark's apparent awareness of the Temple's fall; the Synoptic literary relationships — CLM-0070) and external attestation (Irenaeus, c. 180 CE). The consensus on the **broad first-century placement and the relative order** is **High**; confidence at the level of **precise decades** is only **Moderate**, with real minority positions (early-daters such as J. A. T. Robinson; later-daters for John).

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — a dating statement, explicitly flagged as reconstruction.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (reconstruction from internal + external evidence).
- **SRC-0093 (Ehrman), SRC-0094 (Brown):** the mainstream ranges (Mark c. 65–73; Matt/Luke c. 80–90; John c. 90–110), reached by two scholars of different confessional backgrounds — a cross-check.
- **SRC-0097 (Sanders):** the Synoptics are post-70 and tradition-dependent.
- **SRC-0092 (Nongbri):** the earliest physical witness to John (P52) carries a wide date-range and does **not** pin John early — the manuscript layer does not resolve the dating layer.

**Dual-coding (prose only):** evidence class **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) — there is no H-a or H-b anchor for Gospel *composition dates*. This is a **KOS-0003 §9 consensus-exceeds-evidence** case: **H-e** evidence and **Moderate** evidence-based confidence on precise decades, yet **High** consensus on the broad placement. *(Axis characterization corrected per Critical Review – RQ-0013 F-1: on the strict band-vs-confidence axis this is **not** a divergence — it is the §9 phenomenon of consensus outrunning evidence class, which is recorded separately from the band.)*

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 — the reconstruction is robust and cross-checked, but it is reconstruction.
- Relevance: 5 — directly answers the "when written" layer for the narrative sources.
- Independence: 3 — the datings are somewhat interlocked (Markan priority and the post-70 argument propagate across the Synoptics); Irenaeus is a single late external anchor.
- Quality: 4 — careful, criteria-based, but without documentary confirmation.
- Limitations: fixes ranges, not years; the post-70 argument for Mark depends on reading the "abomination"/Temple material as *vaticinium ex eventu*, which early-daters contest.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** (H-e reconstruction; no documentary anchor).
- Consensus strength: **High** on the relative order (Mark first) and the broad first-century placement; **Moderate** on the precise decade bands.
- **Relationship:** an instructive case where consensus **exceeds** the evidence class — a strong professional agreement built on reconstruction, not on documents. Recorded separately per KOS-0003 §9.

---

# Source Evaluation
Ehrman (SRC-0093) and Brown (SRC-0094) — different confessional backgrounds, convergent ranges — supply the cross-checked consensus; Nongbri (SRC-0092) shows the manuscript layer cannot rescue precision. The independence of Ehrman/Brown is why the *consensus* is High despite the H-e evidence.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that the Temple-destruction allusion in Mark 13 is best read as post-70 (mainstream; contested by early-daters).
- **Epistemological:** that internal literary evidence + a late external attestation can fix a composition range absent documentary confirmation (the disciplined default).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive.** The ranges are the best explanation of the internal + external evidence. **Risks checked:** *treating consensus as if it were documentary attestation* (named — this is exactly the case where high consensus must not be mis-read as high evidence class; the dates remain H-e); *false precision* (named — decade bands, not years); *early-dating and late-dating minorities steelmanned* (Robinson's pre-70 case is coherent but rests on absence-of-explicit-hindsight arguments the majority finds weaker than the post-70 reading; some push John later, but c. 90–110 remains standard, bounded by P90/P52 and Irenaeus).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** — for the **precise decade dating** of each Gospel, because the evidence is H-e reconstruction without documentary anchor and genuine minority positions exist. The **broad first-century placement and relative order** would rate higher (approaching High) and the **consensus** is High — but the on-record confidence is set by the evidence for the *precise* claim, not by the consensus (KOS-0003 §9). **Not Level 4:** no documentary anchor; the post-70 premise is contestable; decade bands are wide.

---

# Limitations
- Fixes ranges, not years; no Gospel is dated by a document.
- The dating interlocks with Markan priority (CLM-0070); if that were overturned the bands would shift.
- Says nothing about the *historicity* of Gospel content — only when the texts were composed.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Early dating (Robinson: all NT pre-70).** Steelmanned: it is internally coherent and avoids assuming hindsight. **Rejected as the majority reading** — the post-70 awareness in Mark 13 and the Synoptic dependence chain fit the standard ranges better; but it keeps the *precise* dating at Moderate, not High.
2. **Later dating of John (into the 2nd c.).** Partly live — but bounded above by early papyri (P52, P66/P75) and Irenaeus; c. 90–110 remains standard.
