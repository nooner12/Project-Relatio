# CLM-0070

# Two Reconstructions (Both H-e) at Sharply Different Confidences: Markan Priority — That Mark Was Written First and Used by Matthew and Luke — Is a Near-Consensus (High); the Q Hypothesis — a Lost Sayings Source Behind the Double Tradition — Is Genuinely Contested (Low), the Same Evidence Class Carrying Two Very Different Levels

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The Synoptic Problem (the literary relationships among Matthew, Mark, and Luke) yields **two reconstructions of very different evidential strength**, both belonging to the **same evidence class (H-e, modern reconstruction)**:
> - **Markan priority** — that Mark was written first and used as a source by Matthew and Luke — is a **near-consensus** position, resting on convergent internal arguments (Mark's shorter/rougher text, the pattern of agreements, editorial "improvements" by Matthew/Luke). **High confidence.**
> - **The Q hypothesis** — that a lost sayings source ("Q") lies behind the "double tradition" (material common to Matthew and Luke but absent from Mark) — is **genuinely contested**. The Two-Source hypothesis (Mark + Q) is the majority view, but the **Farrer hypothesis** (Luke used Matthew directly; no Q — Goodacre) explains the same data without positing an unattested document. **Low confidence** in Q as a *necessary* entity.
> The point: identical evidence *class* (H-e), radically different warranted *confidence* — the H-band does not fix the Level.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — a claim about literary-source relationships, decomposed into two sub-claims at different confidences.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (literary source-criticism).
- **SRC-0093 (Ehrman), SRC-0094 (Brown):** Markan priority as the standard basis of Synoptic study; the Two-Source (Mark + Q) hypothesis as the majority solution to the double tradition.
- **SRC-0095 (Goodacre):** the Farrer case — Markan priority affirmed, Q rejected as unnecessary; Q is a *hypothetical* document with no manuscript and no patristic citation.

**Dual-coding (prose only):** both sub-claims are **H-e** (reconstruction). Markan priority: H-e at **High**. Q: H-e at **Low/contested**. This is the investigation's cleanest demonstration that **the H-band is orthogonal to the Level** — same evidence kind, two Levels apart.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 4 (Markan priority) / 2 (Q as necessary) — the priority arguments are strong and convergent; the Q inference is one of two viable explanations.
- Relevance: 5 — the source relationships bear directly on how the Gospels function as (non-independent) evidence.
- Independence: 3 — the arguments are internal-literary; Q has no external attestation at all.
- Quality: 4 (priority) / 2 (Q) — priority is a robust reconstruction; Q rests on the disputed premise that Luke did not know Matthew.
- Limitations: all of this is literary inference; no manuscript settles it.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- **Markan priority:** Evidence Moderate-High (reconstruction, but convergent); Consensus **High**.
- **Q hypothesis:** Evidence Low; Consensus **Contested** — the Two-Source view is the majority, but the Farrer/Goodacre challenge is a serious, published minority. Recorded separately per §9.

---

# Source Evaluation
Ehrman/Brown supply the majority (Two-Source) position; Goodacre (SRC-0095) supplies the steelmanned minority. Crucially, Goodacre *affirms* Markan priority — so the two sub-claims are cleanly separable, and the contest is over Q alone.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that literary agreements/disagreements can establish direction of dependence (mainstream).
- **Epistemological (the crux for Q):** whether Luke plausibly knew and used Matthew — if yes, Q is unnecessary; if no, Q is required. This single assumption divides the field.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive.** Markan priority is the best explanation of the agreement patterns; for the double tradition, *two* explanations compete (Q vs. Farrer). **Risks checked:** *conflating Markan priority with Q* (named — the classic error Goodacre isolates; affirming priority does not require Q); *treating the majority view as settled* (named — Q is majority but contested, not established); *false dichotomy* (named — Griesbach/Matthean-priority alternatives exist but are minority; the live contest is Two-Source vs. Farrer, both assuming Markan priority).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Split, by construction:**
- **Markan priority: Level 4 (High)** — near-consensus, convergent internal evidence. Not Level 5: reserved, and it remains reconstruction.
- **Q hypothesis: Level 2 (Low)** — a viable but non-necessary inference, seriously challenged by the Farrer hypothesis; Q is an unattested document.
The single native rating carried for this claim in the confidence summary is **Level 4 (High) for Markan priority / Level 2 (Low) for Q** — the split is the point.

---

# Limitations
- Entirely literary reconstruction; no external attestation for Q.
- Does not bear on Gospel *dating* except that Markan priority underwrites the relative order (CLM-0069).

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Two-Source hypothesis (Mark + Q), majority.** Accepted as the majority solution but held at Moderate-for-the-model / Low-for-Q's-necessity — it works, but Q is not required by the evidence.
2. **Farrer hypothesis (no Q; Luke used Matthew), Goodacre.** Steelmanned and accepted as fully viable — which is precisely why Q sits at Low, not Moderate.
3. **Griesbach (Matthean priority), minority.** Rejected — a minority position; Markan priority is far better supported.
