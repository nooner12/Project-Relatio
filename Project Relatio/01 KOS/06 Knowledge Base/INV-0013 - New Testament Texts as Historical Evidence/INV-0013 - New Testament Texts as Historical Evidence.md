# 1. Research Question

**Primary:**

> What is the nature, dating, authorship, and transmission of the earliest sources for Jesus and the first Christian movement — and, graded claim by claim, how strong an evidential base do they actually provide (with the truth of any theological claim bracketed)?

**Register & the metaphysical bracket (load-bearing).** This is a **descriptive, textual-historical** investigation in the neutral historical-critical register. It grades **what is attested** — the manuscripts, their dates, their authorship, their transmission, and what they and external sources document — **never** the truth of any supernatural or theological claim. Resurrection-as-event, miracles-as-event, and divinity are **bracketed metaphysical claims** (KOS-0003 §3.6), outside the historian's tools; where they appear it is only as *"the earliest sources report/proclaim/believe X,"* which is a descriptive claim about attestation, not an event-claim. No confessional framing in any direction. The **mythicist** ("no historical Jesus") and **maximalist** ("the traditional attributions largely hold") poles are each **steelmanned at their strongest** (OPS-0003 Non-Negotiable 6); where evidence is split, a **split verdict at honest confidence** is the answer (Non-Negotiable 7).

**Why texts-first (the methodological rationale — owner-directed).** Source evaluation is *logically prior* to any claim built on the sources (KOS-0003 §6, and the KOS-0003 pipeline: evaluate sources → then form claims). This investigation establishes the **quality and depth of the evidence base itself**; a follow-on investigation (candidate **INV-0014**, the historical Jesus) can then build historical-reconstruction claims on this graded foundation, rather than burying the source-critical question beneath conclusions that depend on it.

# 2. Scope & Disambiguation

## 2.1 In scope: the earliest sources as evidence
The canonical New Testament sources for Jesus and the first-generation movement — the **undisputed Pauline letters**, the **four canonical Gospels**, **Acts**, and the relevant **non-Christian** notices (Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny) — evaluated as *historical evidence*: their manuscript transmission, dating, authorship, source relationships, and the earlier tradition they embed.

## 2.2 Out of scope by construction
- **The historical reconstruction of Jesus' life/deeds/teachings** — deferred to a candidate follow-on (INV-0014). INV-0013 grades the *evidence base*, not what it lets us conclude about the man. (This is the owner-directed sequencing.)
- **The truth of any theological/metaphysical claim** — resurrection/miracles/divinity as events are bracketed (§1). The investigation does not adjudicate them.
- **The later canon-formation and doctrinal history** (2nd century onward) except where it bears on dating/authorship attribution of the first-century texts.
- **The non-canonical gospels** (Thomas, etc.) except where a source uses them to date or illuminate the canonical texts' tradition.
- **No magnitude ranking** of texts by importance absent a stated metric.

## 2.3 The three attestation layers (kept analytically distinct — the crux for the H-scale)
Following standard method, three questions are held apart because they carry **different evidence classes**:
- **What the text *is* / how well its wording is preserved** — the *textual-transmission* question (manuscripts, papyri): H-a/H-b.
- **When it was written** — the *dating* question: H-e (reconstruction from internal + external evidence), occasionally H-b (a dated papyrus gives a *terminus ante quem*).
- **Who wrote it** — the *authorship* question: internal (H-a, anonymous/self-identifying) vs. the *traditional attribution* (H-d — later tradition attributing backward: Papias, Irenaeus).
Conflating these is the classic error; keeping them apart is what lets the H-band do work (§3.2).

## 2.4 The candidate historical-evidence scale (H-a…H-e) — protocol scale under test
| Band | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **H-a** | Contemporaneous primary text |
| **H-b** | Archaeological / epigraphic / material attestation (incl. dated manuscripts/papyri) |
| **H-c** | Near-contemporary secondary account |
| **H-d** | Later tradition attributing backward |
| **H-e** | Modern scholarly reconstruction |
Coding rule: the H-band takes the **strongest evidence class actually attested** for the claim. H-bands are a **typology of evidence kind**, feeding — never replacing — the native `Level N` confidence assessment.

## 4.2 GB-2026-027 verdict: did the axes diverge on descriptive claims?

**Bottom line (CORRECTED per Critical Review – RQ-0013 F-1; stated plainly, per §3.3): on the STRICT band-vs-confidence axis the instrument actually tests (§3.2 — do the H-band *value* and the native Level *value* come apart, or track?), this descriptive circuit is PREDOMINANTLY COLLINEAR, with ONE clean, review-independent orthogonality case (CLM-0070) and ONE KOS-0003 §9 consensus-exceeds-evidence outlier (CLM-0069). > **Correction note (F-1, reflexive §7.6).** My draft mis-coded CLM-0071 and CLM-0072 as "diverge." In both, the band *tracks* the confidence (H-a→High; H-d/H-e→Low) — I even wrote "the confidence follows the band" in CLM-0071. That co-variation is exactly the **collinear** pattern INV-0011 §4.2 counted as collinear/redundant; labelling it "diverge" here was a cross-investigation inconsistency that **inflated the tally in the flattering direction** (the project's own interest in the H-scale earning STD-0008). What CLM-0071/0072 genuinely show is band **diagnostic/explanatory** value — the band locates and explains *why* confidence is low — **not** that the two values diverge. The §3.2 definitional example ("a H-d attribution at Low confidence") is itself a *collinear* pairing offered as an example of divergence, which was the root of the confusion.

**Honest recount on the band-vs-confidence axis:**
- **Genuine divergence (same evidence class, different confidence — band orthogonal to Level): CLM-0070 only.** Markan priority (Level 4) and Q (Level 2) are both **H-e** reconstruction yet sit two Levels apart. This is the real, clean, valuable result — legible *without* the adversarial circuit. Keep and foreground it.
- **Collinear (higher evidence class → higher confidence): six of eight — CLM-0067, CLM-0068, CLM-0073, CLM-0074, plus CLM-0071 and CLM-0072** (recharacterized from the draft's "diverge": in both the band tracks the confidence).
- **§9 consensus-exceeds-evidence outlier (NOT a band-vs-confidence phenomenon): CLM-0069** — its surprising feature is High *consensus* over an H-e *evidence class*, which KOS-0003 §9 records separately from evidence class; it is not the GB-27 band-vs-confidence question.

**What survives, and what does not.** *Survives:* **the H-typology is a coherent axis conceptually distinct from confidence, and does real diagnostic/explanatory work** — shown most cleanly at **CLM-0070** (same band, two Levels), and in a different mode at **CLM-0071/0072**, where the band explains *why* confidence is discounted for late/reconstructed attribution. *Struck:* the draft's claims that the axes "materially DIVERGED," that this is "materially STRONGER than INV-0011," and that there were "four straightforwardly legible" divergences. On the strict values-diverge test the circuit is predominantly collinear, close to where INV-0011 landed.

**Strengthened counter-weight (the reviewer noted my draft under-stated it).** Three honest deflations: (1) the collinear claims are the *securest* ones — the scale adds least exactly where the evidence is strongest (H-a/H-b), so its value concentrates on the weaker reconstructed/attributed layers. (3) The band never *overrode* a native Level; it explained and located confidence, it did not set it (native `Level N` remains the only on-record rating). Together these support **"diagnostically useful typology" strongly** and **"the values genuinely diverge / a standing coded standard is necessary" weakly**.

**Contrast with INV-0011 (corrected — the pair is the GB-2026-027 evidence base).** INV-0011 (influence claims) ran largely collinear and surfaced one non-redundant discrimination *via the adversarial circuit* (CLM-0052 vs CLM-0051). INV-0013 (descriptive claims) is **also predominantly collinear** on the band-vs-confidence axis, but surfaces one clean orthogonality case (**CLM-0070**) that is legible *without* review, plus genuine diagnostic value in CLM-0071/0072. The gain is real but **modest** — cleaner and self-evident rather than review-dependent — **NOT** a change in the overall collinear-vs-divergent balance.

**Net assessment for the owner (GB-2026-027 second data point; owner-reserved, not a standards enactment).** The pair (INV-0011 + INV-0013) usefully **brackets the scale's domain**: the H-typology earns diagnostic value on descriptive multi-evidence-class claims (**CLM-0070** is the exhibit), and adds least where confidence is already defined by a single strong evidence axis.
