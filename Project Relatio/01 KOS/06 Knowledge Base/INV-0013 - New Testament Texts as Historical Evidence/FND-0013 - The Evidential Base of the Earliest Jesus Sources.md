# FND-0013

# The Earliest Jesus Sources Are a Strong but Layered and Uneven Evidential Base: the Wording Is Recoverably Preserved (High) and the Undisputed Pauline Letters and Early Creedal Tradition Give a First-Generation Anchor (High), While Gospel Dating Is Firm-in-Consensus-but-Reconstructed (Moderate) and the Traditional Gospel Authorship Is Weakly Evidenced (Low) — Strength Tracks the Attestation Layer, Not the Text as a Whole

## Draft Finding Record

# 1. Statement

> **The evidential base is genuinely strong — but its strength is layered, and the single most important result is that the confidence must be assigned by attestation layer, not to "the New Testament" as an undifferentiated whole.** The three layers held distinct throughout (§2.3 of INV-0013 — text / date / authorship) come apart in confidence exactly as the historical-critical method predicts:
>
> - **The wording is recoverably preserved (CLM-0067, Level 4 / High).** The manuscript base is far richer than that of any other ancient work; text-critical method recovers the earliest reachable wording to a high degree. This is **recoverability, not inerrancy**: no autograph survives, the earliest witnesses are fragmentary with wide date-ranges (P52), and a small set of substantive variants bounds the certainty. The calibrated middle, not either pole.
> - **The undisputed Pauline letters give a first-generation anchor (CLM-0068, Level 4 / High).** Seven letters, c. 50–60 CE, self-authored, externally synchronized (Gallio) — **H-a contemporaneous** and the firmest datum in the base.
> - **The early creedal/oral tradition reaches back further still (CLM-0074, Level 4 / High).** The sources embed pre-literary formulae — flagship the 1 Cor 15:3–7 creed — showing the movement **proclaimed** Jesus as raised within a few years of the crucifixion (c. 30s). This is a *descriptive* datum about early proclamation; **the resurrection as an event is bracketed**, not graded.
> - **Gospel dating is firm-in-consensus but reconstructed (CLM-0069, Level 3 / Moderate).** Mark c. 65–75, Matt/Luke c. 80–90, John c. 90–110 — no colophon, no documentary anchor. Consensus on the broad placement and relative order is **High**; confidence on the precise decades is **Moderate**. Here evidence-*class* (H-e) and *consensus* (High) visibly diverge.
> - **The Synoptic relationships split (CLM-0070): Markan priority Level 4 (High); the Q hypothesis Level 2 (Low).** Same evidence class (H-e reconstruction), two Levels apart — the clearest demonstration in the whole investigation that the evidence *kind* does not fix the *confidence*.
> - **The traditional Gospel authorship is weakly evidenced (CLM-0071).** The Gospels are formally **anonymous** (H-a internal, Level 4 / High); the apostolic attributions rest on **later second-century tradition** (H-d: Papias via Eusebius, Irenaeus) and hold **Level 2 (Low)**. The band here does *diagnostic* work — it explains *why* the attribution confidence is low (a collinear pattern where band tracks confidence, not a band-vs-confidence divergence; see INV-0013 §4.2 as corrected per Critical Review F-1).
> - **The Pauline corpus is a gradient, not a block (CLM-0072).** Seven undisputed (Level 4 / High); three disputed (Level 2 / Low, genuinely divided); three Pastorals majority-pseudonymous (Level 3 / Moderate).
> - **The external, non-Christian record corroborates existence, not narrative (CLM-0073, Level 3 / Moderate).** Tacitus, Josephus (Testimonium **partially** authentic — confessional clauses interpolated; *Ant.* 20.200 more secure), and Pliny establish that the movement and an executed founder were real and known to outsiders by the early 2nd century — and no more.
>
> **On the two contested poles, at honest confidence.** The **mythicist** pole ("no historical Jesus," steelmanned at its strongest via Carrier) is represented and does not survive the majority evaluation: the near-universal scholarly consensus for a historical Jesus rests chiefly on the **early Christian sources graded here** (the undisputed Paul, who knew James and Peter; the multiply-attested and early-proclaimed tradition) rather than primarily on the external notices. The **maximalist** pole ("traditional attributions largely hold") is likewise not sustained at high confidence: the Gospel attributions are late and dependent (Low), and the Pastorals are majority-pseudonymous. The honest picture sits between the poles and **varies by layer**.
>
> **Motivated-reasoning guard.** Three convenient stories were named and resisted: the **apologetic** ("the text is perfectly preserved / the early creed proves the resurrection") — checked by the bounded-variant reality and by refusing to slide from *proclamation* to *event*; the **maximalist** ("the traditional authors wrote the Gospels") — checked by the H-d lateness of the attributions; and the **anti-confessional/mythicist** ("it's all late invention") — checked by the H-a Pauline anchor and the very early creed. The evidence survives without any of these priors, and the layered result is what remains.

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**The finding is a synthesis and makes no single overall claim above its strongest component; its value is the layered map, and each layer carries its own confidence.** The **anchors are High** (CLM-0067 wording; CLM-0068 Pauline dating; CLM-0074 early proclamation; CLM-0070 Markan priority; CLM-0071 anonymity; CLM-0072 the seven undisputed). The **reconstructed layers are Moderate** (CLM-0069 precise Gospel dating; CLM-0073 external attestation; CLM-0072 Pastorals). The **weakly-evidenced layers are Low** (CLM-0071 traditional Gospel authorship; CLM-0070 Q; CLM-0072 disputed three). **No element reaches Level 5** (reserved). The synthesis does **not** average these into a single number — a single "how strong is the New Testament as evidence" figure would be a category error; the accurate answer is the by-layer distribution. Where the finding makes a *combined* judgment (that a strong first-generation evidential base exists), it is bounded by its strongest secure components (the H-a Pauline/creedal anchor and the recoverable wording) and is stated as **High for those anchors specifically**, not for the traditional attributions or the precise Gospel dating.

# 4. Scope & Limitations (what is NOT supported)
- **NOT supported:** any single undifferentiated verdict on "the reliability of the New Testament" — confidence is layer-specific by construction.
- **NOT supported:** the maximalist claim that the traditional Gospel authors wrote the Gospels (Low; H-d late attribution).
- **NOT supported:** "perfect preservation" of the text (recoverability with bounded uncertainty, not inerrancy).
- **NOT supported:** the fully-authentic Testimonium Flavianum (only partial authenticity; the confessional clauses are interpolations) — nor its total-interpolation dismissal as certain (a live minority).
- **NOT supported:** the Q hypothesis as an established entity (contested; Farrer viable) — though Markan priority is High.
- **NOT supported:** mythicism (steelmanned, rejected on the majority evaluation) — nor is historicity asserted by fiat; it rests on the early sources graded here.
- **NOT supported / BRACKETED:** the truth of any supernatural or theological claim — resurrection, miracles, divinity as events are outside the historian's tools; only *attestation/proclamation* is graded (CLM-0074).
- **NOT a reconstruction of Jesus' life or teachings** — that is deferred to candidate INV-0014; this finding grades the *evidence base*, not what it lets us conclude about the man.
- **NOT a systematic review** — thirteen sources chosen for spectrum coverage and live-verifiability; the judgments are the mainstream shape of the field, not a meta-analytic settlement.
