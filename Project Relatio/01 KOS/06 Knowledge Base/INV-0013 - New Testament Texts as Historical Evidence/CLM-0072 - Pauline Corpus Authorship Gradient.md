# CLM-0072

# The Pauline Corpus Carries a Confidence GRADIENT Within a Single Body of Texts: Seven Letters Are Securely Pauline (High, H-a Self-Attestation), Three Are Genuinely Disputed (2 Thessalonians, Colossians, Ephesians — Low-to-Moderate), and the Three Pastorals Are Judged Pseudonymous by Most Critical Scholars (Moderate Confidence in Non-Pauline Authorship, on H-e Stylometric Grounds)

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The thirteen letters attributed to Paul do not form a uniform block; they carry a **confidence gradient** in authorship:
> - **Seven undisputed** (Romans, 1–2 Corinthians, Galatians, Philippians, 1 Thessalonians, Philemon) — **securely Pauline**, on internal self-attestation and uncontested scholarship. **High confidence.**
> - **Three disputed** (2 Thessalonians, Colossians, Ephesians) — **genuinely divided**; each is defended and doubted by serious scholars on stylistic/theological grounds. **Low-to-Moderate confidence** either way.
> - **Three Pastorals** (1 Timothy, 2 Timothy, Titus) — judged **pseudonymous** by most critical scholars (developed church offices, distinctive vocabulary, later trajectory). **Moderate confidence that they are NOT by Paul** (a substantial conservative minority still defends them).
> The gradient runs *within one corpus*, on the same kinds of evidence (internal self-attestation + H-e stylometric/theological reconstruction), producing sharply different confidences — a second showcase that evidence-kind does not fix the Level.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** — an authorship claim, decomposed into a three-tier gradient.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical** (internal self-attestation + stylometric/theological reconstruction).
- **SRC-0103 (primary texts):** the seven undisputed letters share a coherent style, theology, and self-identifying occasional character.
- **SRC-0093 (Ehrman), SRC-0094 (Brown):** the standard partition (seven undisputed / three disputed / three pseudonymous); Brown's authorship "scorecard" reaches the same tiers from a Catholic standpoint.
- **SRC-0096 (Ehrman, *Forgery*):** the technical case for pseudonymity of the Pastorals (and disputed Deutero-Paulines).

**Dual-coding (prose only):** the seven undisputed rest on **H-a** (internal self-attestation, uncontested) at **High**; the disputed and pseudonymous judgments rest on **H-e** (stylometric/theological reconstruction) at **Low-to-Moderate**. Same corpus, evidence-kind shifts H-a→H-e as confidence falls — the gradient is legible in the H-band. *(Axis corrected per Critical Review – RQ-0013 F-1: because evidence-kind and confidence move together (H-a→High, H-e→Low/Moderate), this is a **collinear** pattern, not a band-vs-confidence divergence; the band's value is **diagnostic**, locating the gradient. The one genuine divergence in the investigation is CLM-0070.)*

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- Reliability: 5 (seven) / 3 (disputed) / 4 (Pastorals-as-pseudonymous) — the seven are secure; the disputed are genuinely split; the Pastorals' pseudonymity is a strong-majority reconstruction.
- Relevance: 5 — directly answers the "who wrote it" layer for the Pauline corpus.
- Independence: 4 — Ehrman and Brown (different backgrounds) converge on the tiers.
- Quality: 5 (seven) / 3 (disputed) / 4 (Pastorals) — stylometric argument is robust for the Pastorals, weaker/divided for the disputed three.
- Limitations: pseudonymity is inference from internal evidence; it cannot reach the certainty of the seven's self-attestation.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- **Seven undisputed:** Evidence High; Consensus **High** (near-universal).
- **Three disputed:** Evidence Moderate; Consensus **Divided** — 2 Thessalonians and Colossians roughly split; Ephesians leans toward non-Pauline.
- **Pastorals:** Evidence Moderate-High; Consensus **Majority-pseudonymous**, with a real conservative minority. Recorded separately per §9.

---

# Source Evaluation
The convergence of Ehrman and Brown on the same three tiers — from opposite confessional starting points — is the claim's backbone; SRC-0096 supplies the technical pseudonymity apparatus; SRC-0103 grounds the secure seven.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** that style, vocabulary, and presupposed church structures are valid authorship discriminators (mainstream, with known limits on small samples).
- **Epistemological:** that a strong stylometric divergence warrants a pseudonymity judgment at Moderate confidence — not certainty.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Abductive/inductive.** The gradient is the best fit to the internal evidence. **Risks checked:** *treating the corpus as uniform* (named — the error the claim corrects; "Paul says X" must specify which letter tier); *over-reading stylometry* (named — small samples and secretarial/amanuensis hypotheses keep the disputed three genuinely uncertain, so they sit at Low-to-Moderate, not High-pseudonymous); *maximalist uniformity steelmanned* (the case that a single author can vary style/topic is real and keeps the disputed three live — hence not graded as securely pseudonymous).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Graded, by construction:**
- **Seven undisputed as authentically Pauline: Level 4 (High).**
- **Three disputed (2 Thess, Col, Eph): Level 2 (Low)** confidence in *any* single verdict — genuinely divided.
- **Pastorals as pseudonymous: Level 3 (Moderate)** — strong-majority reconstruction, real minority dissent.
The native rating carried in the confidence summary is the gradient: **Level 4 (High) seven authentic / Level 2 (Low) disputed three unresolved / Level 3 (Moderate) Pastorals pseudonymous.** **No Level 5** (reserved).

---

# Limitations
- Authorship verdicts on the disputed/pseudonymous letters are H-e inference; they cannot reach the security of the seven.
- Says nothing about the letters' *value or theology* — only their authorship attestation.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **All thirteen are Pauline (maximalist).** Steelmanned (secretaries, stylistic range, life-stage change). **Rejected as high-confidence** for the Pastorals (strong stylometric/anachronism case) but it keeps the disputed three genuinely open.
2. **All ten non-undisputed are pseudonymous (strong-critical).** Overstated for 2 Thessalonians/Colossians, which remain divided; accepted with high confidence only for the direction, not for each letter.
