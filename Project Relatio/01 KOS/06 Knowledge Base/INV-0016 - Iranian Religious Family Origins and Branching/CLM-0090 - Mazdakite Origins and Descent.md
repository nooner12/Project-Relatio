# CLM-0090

# Mazdakite Origins and Descent

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Mazdakism was a **heterodox religious-and-social movement within Sasanian Zoroastrianism**, associated with Mazdak and flourishing under Kavad I in the **late 5th–early 6th century CE** before its suppression. Its descent from Zoroastrianism as a **`heterodox-offshoot`** is the standard framing — but **everything about it (doctrines, organisation, chronology, even the suppression narrative) is reconstructed from a late, largely hostile Perso-Arabic tradition**, so every component is graded **Low**. This is the family's thinnest, most hostile-source-bound case, exactly as anticipated — the low grade is the honest result, not a failure.

---

# Element (i) — Emergence Dating
- **Floruit under Kavad I, late 5th–early 6th c. CE.** SRC-0123 (Crone 1991) treats Kavad I's association with the movement and its suppression (dated c. 528–531 CE, itself debated). Precursor doctrines may reach into the later 5th c. (a precursor figure, "Zaradusht of Fasa," is **reported in the scholarship on this material but is not itemised in the SRC-0123 record and was not live-verified** — carried as parametric, not as a catalogued datum), but any such earlier dating is not firmly established.
- **The evidential base is late and hostile.** SRC-0123 §3: the narrative rests on Perso-Arabic tradition (al-Ṭabarī, the *Šāhnāme*) written *centuries* after the events; the near-contemporary Procopius attests upheaval in Kavad's reign but **not** the massacre narrative. "No amount of critical skill repairs that."
- **Disposition:** a late-5th/early-6th-c. floruit is the best the base supports; specific dates (including the suppression c. 528–531) are contested. Graded Low — hostile, late, uncorroborated. `display_range` on ENT-0010: "late 5th–early 6th c. CE (fl. under Kavad I)."

# Element (ii) — Descent Relationship
- **Heterodox offshoot of Zoroastrianism.** Mazdakism arose from within the Sasanian Zoroastrian world as a heresy/reform movement (Crone's title: "Kavād's Heresy and Mazdak's Revolt"), combining a dualist-cosmological doctrine with a social program. The program is **standardly reported** as advocating sharing of property (and, in the hostile tradition, of women); those specifics are **parametric — not itemised in the SRC-0123 record and not live-verified — and are treated as illustrative, not load-bearing.** What SRC-0123 §3 *does* document is source-skepticism toward the **massacre narrative and the chronology** of the suppression (the near-contemporary Procopius attests upheaval, not the massacre), which is the deflationary weight applied here.
- **In-family edge:** `branches_from` ENT-0008 (Zoroastrianism), qualifier **`heterodox-offshoot`** — warranted by its origin as a heterodoxy within Zoroastrianism. The scaffold offered `heterodox-offshoot` **or** `reform`; the evidence best supports **`heterodox-offshoot`** (the sources frame it as heresy departing from orthodoxy, not as an accepted internal reform), with `reform` as the near-miss recorded for Anchor Fit. `tradition_type` on ENT-0010 is **`reform`** (the movement self-presented as religious-social reform) — the `reform` tradition_type and the `heterodox-offshoot` qualifier describe *different things* (its own emergence mode vs. the nature of its descent link) and are deliberately not conflated; the near-coincidence is flagged.
- **Distinctness (§2.3):** Mazdakism clears the threshold more securely than Zurvanism — it had an identifiable leader, adherents, a program, and a datable public career (a movement/sect, not merely a doctrinal tendency) — so an entity is warranted, at Low confidence.

# Element (iii) — Rival Reading (steelmanned)
- **Strongest rival — near-total source skepticism (a Crone-adjacent minimalism).** Because the entire narrative is late and hostile, one may hold that almost nothing about Mazdakite *doctrine* or *organisation* can be reconstructed — perhaps not even that "Mazdakism" was a coherent religious tradition rather than a label later applied to a revolt. **Survives, substantially** — it is why every component is Low and why the doctrines are not asserted in detail. **Cost:** the *existence* of a Kavad-era heterodox movement is attested even in the thin near-contemporary layer (Procopius on upheaval), so total dissolution over-reaches; a heterodox offshoot existed, even if its content is largely unrecoverable.
- **Rival on the qualifier — `reform` not `heterodox-offshoot`.** If Mazdak's program is read as an attempted reform of Zoroastrian society rather than a departure from orthodoxy, `reform` would fit. Recorded for Anchor Fit; not adopted, because the sources predominantly frame it as heresy.

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical** — emergence and genealogical position, reconstructed over a hostile late base; metaphysics bracketed.

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical (source-critical over late/hostile tradition).**
- SRC-0123 (Crone 1991): the source-critical re-reading; the late Perso-Arabic tradition as the effective base; the near-contemporary/late gap (live-verified citation; strong bibliographic verification). This is the single catalogued source for Mazdakism in the base — the thinness is structural.

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Low** — a single late/hostile tradition, critically handled but not corroborated.
- Consensus strength: the *existence* of a Kavad-era heterodox movement is accepted (Moderate); its *doctrines, organisation, and the massacre narrative* are debated (Low).

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Hostile-source honesty (§2.2):** the grade reflects the evidence class; the movement is graded down, not worked around.
- **Distinctness threshold (§2.3):** met (identifiable movement), unlike Zurvanism.
- **Metaphysical bracket** held.

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **emergence_dating — Level 2 (Low):** late-5th/early-6th-c. floruit is the best supported; specifics contested; late/hostile base.
- **heterodox_descent — Level 2 (Low):** heterodox offshoot of Zoroastrianism is the standard framing but thinly and hostilely based. **No Level 3+ anywhere** — the honest thin-base outcome. R0.

# Limitations
- Establishes a Kavad-era heterodox offshoot at Low grade; does **not** securely establish Mazdakite doctrine in detail, the suppression narrative, or precise chronology.
