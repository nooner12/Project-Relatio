# FND-0016

# Origins and Branching of the Iranian Religious Family

## Draft Finding Record

---

# 1. Finding (the honest shape)

The Iranian religious family, as the surveyed catalog base supports it, has the following origins-and-branching shape — **layered by tradition, and thin where the evidence is thin**:

- **Zoroastrianism is the family root** (Moderate). It is founder-attributed (Zarathustra) and the earliest member; its **emergence cannot be dated absolutely** — the traditional 6th-c.-BCE date is a demonstrable late construct, the competing c. 1500–1000 BCE dating is indirect linguistic reconstruction, and the whole picture is inherited (not re-derived) from INV-0011 CLM-0056. It carries no `branches_from` edge.

- **Zurvanism is best modelled as a heterodox CURRENT WITHIN Zoroastrianism, not a distinct branching tradition** (Low). Zurvanite *ideas* (Zurvan/Time begetting Ohrmazd and Ahriman) are attested (5th c., Eznik) and broadly accepted; but on the §2.3 distinctness threshold Zurvanism does **not** clear the bar — there is **no attested separate community/sect**, and the sources frame it as a current/tendency *within* Zoroastrianism (with an explicit warning that hostile heresiography inflates currents into sects). **It receives no tradition entity and no lineage edge.** This is the investigation's decisive anchor-stress result — the distinctness threshold *discriminated*, which is the threshold working.

- **Manichaeism is a 3rd-c.-CE founded, deliberately syncretic religion whose descent is multi-parent** (Low for the in-family lineage). Within the family it `branches_from` Zoroastrianism (`syncretic-descent`, incorporating Iranian two-principle dualism) — but its **primary formative matrix (a Jewish-Christian baptist/Elchasaite community) is out-of-family**, so the single-target edge represents one of several parents. The founding date is the family's firmest; the *Zoroastrian-as-parent* weight is the uncertain part.

- **Mazdakism is a late-5th/early-6th-c.-CE heterodox offshoot of Zoroastrianism** (Low). It `branches_from` Zoroastrianism (`heterodox-offshoot`). Its entire picture — doctrine, organisation, chronology, suppression — is reconstructed from a **late, largely hostile Perso-Arabic tradition**; the *existence* of a Kavad-era heterodox movement is attested, but little of its content is recoverable. The family's thinnest case, graded down honestly.

- **Yazidism crystallised in the 12th c. CE around Sheikh ʿAdī / Lalish, and its deeper descent is genuinely DISPUTED** (Moderate for the disputedness). It `branches_from` Zoroastrianism with the qualifier **`disputed`** — a *finding*, not a hedge: the Iranian-substrate account (Kreyenbroek, itself advocacy) and the Sufi-origin account are both live, neither established, and there is no direct textual chain. The strongest single alternative (Sufi origin) is out-of-family.

**Net:** the family resolves into **one root (Zoroastrianism)**, **one internal current (Zurvanism, not a branch)**, and **three branching traditions** whose descent qualifiers are, respectively, `syncretic-descent` (Manichaeism), `heterodox-offshoot` (Mazdakism), and `disputed` (Yazidism). **No verdict rises above Moderate; three of five components are Low** — the expected honest outcome for a family built around extinction, contested dating, and hostile-source-only attestation.

# 3. What this finding does NOT establish (brackets)
- It does **not** fix an absolute emergence date for any tradition (least of all Zoroastrianism).
- It does **not** establish Zoroastrianism as Manichaeism's *primary* source, nor Yazidism's Iranian descent as fact (nor refute it).
- It does **not** reconstruct Mazdakite doctrine in detail, nor settle whether "Zurvanism" was ever an organised sect.
- **Metaphysical bracket:** no tradition's truth claims are affirmed or denied; the register is neutral historical religious studies throughout.
- **No cross-family edges:** Manichaeism's Christian/Buddhist parents and Yazidism's Sufi-Islamic parent are noted in prose, not modelled as edges (those entities do not exist; §2.4).
