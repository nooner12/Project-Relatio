# CLM-0088

# Zurvanism Origins and Descent

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> **Zurvanite ideas** — a cosmogony making Zurvan (Time) the primordial principle who begets both Ohrmazd and Ahriman — are attested in the Sasanian period and broadly accepted as having existed. But **whether "Zurvanism" was a distinct religious tradition, versus a heterodox theological current within Zoroastrianism, is genuinely disputed and cannot be settled on the surveyed base.** On the §2.3 distinctness threshold Zurvanism **does not securely clear the bar** — there is no attested separate community, priesthood, or lineage, and the literature predominantly frames it as a current/tendency *within* Zoroastrianism. It is therefore modelled here as a **heterodox current of Zoroastrianism, NOT as a distinct branching tradition** — it receives **no tradition entity and no `branches_from` edge.** This is the investigation's central anchor-stress finding.

---

# Element (i) — Emergence Dating
- **Attested in the 5th century, via a hostile outsider.** SRC-0120 (Eznik of Kołb, *De Deo*, 5th c. CE) contains the fullest early account of the Zurvanite myth. This fixes a *terminus ante quem* for the ideas' circulation (5th c. CE, Sasanian era) but is near-contemporary Armenian Christian heresiography, not internal attestation.
- **Earlier existence is unknown.** Whether Zurvanite ideas are older (Achaemenid or earlier Sasanian) is not supported by the base — SRC-0120 §3 warns against extrapolating its 5th-c. witness backward. No absolute origin date is establishable.
- **Disposition:** emergence of the *ideas* datable to (at latest) the 5th c. CE; deeper origins unknown; graded Moderate for the ideas' existence, no false precision on antiquity.

# Element (ii) — Descent Relationship
- **Descent (of the ideas): from within Zoroastrianism.** The Zurvan cosmogony is a Zoroastrian-internal solution to the "dilemma" of the common origin of the twin spirits — it presupposes and reworks Zoroastrian dualism. So whatever Zurvanism is, it is *of* Zoroastrianism.
- **But the prior question — is it a distinct tradition at all? — fails the §2.3 threshold.** The working criterion requires (a) an identifiable separate community/lineage, (b) a distinguishing doctrinal core, and (c) recognition as a *named tradition* rather than a *current within* another. Zurvanism clears **(b)** (the distinctive Zurvan cosmogony) but **not (a)** — SRC-0120 §3 records that **"there is no native Zoroastrian primary text attesting a Zurvanite sect"** — and **not (c)**: the sources characterise it as a *current / tendency / heresy within* Sasanian Zoroastrianism (SRC-0121 describes it as "a distinct theological current within Sasanian Zoroastrianism"), and SRC-0120 §3 explicitly warns that hostile heresiography "characteristically systematises and sharpens... turning **currents into sects**, and tendencies into doctrines."
- **Consequence (the honest disposition):** Zurvanism is **modelled as a heterodox current of Zoroastrianism**, recorded on ENT-0008 (Zoroastrianism) in prose, not as its own entity. **No `branches_from` edge is drawn.** Had the threshold been cleared, the qualifier would have been `heterodox-offshoot` (the scaffold's working hypothesis); the prior distinctness question dissolves the edge, so the hypothesis is **not confirmed and not applied**. Routed to the Anchor Fit Assessment as the threshold's decisive test case.

# Element (iii) — Rival Reading (steelmanned)
- **Strongest rival — Zaehner's maximalist reconstruction (SRC-0121).** Zaehner (1955) collected the scattered testimony and argued Zurvanism was a *major, quasi-orthodox current* — substantial enough to count as a distinct religious phenomenon of Sasanian Iran; on this reading it would warrant an entity (qualifier `heterodox-offshoot`, or even a near-orthodox `schism`). **Survives as a live minority/older position** — the Zurvan cosmogony is real and was clearly significant. **Cost:** the reconstruction is contested (SRC-0122 is standardly read as qualifying it, though this record leaves Boyce's specific argument *unestablished* — the article was not read); it is built almost entirely from hostile outsider witnesses over a thin base (SRC-0120, SRC-0121 §3); and hostile heresiography is exactly the source-type that inflates currents into sects. On the disciplined default (attestation over reconstruction, per CLM-0056's method), the maximalist reading does not clear the distinctness bar — it *argues for* clearing it, which is not the same thing.

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / historical + classificatory** — about the emergence of the ideas and the tradition-vs-current status of the phenomenon.

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical (hostile-outsider primary + reconstructive secondary).**
- SRC-0120 (Eznik, *De Deo*): the fullest early witness to the Zurvanite myth; a single hostile source; explicit that no native text attests a Zurvanite sect (parametric; weak verification, disclosed).
- SRC-0121 (Zaehner 1955): the maximalist reconstruction as a distinct current; base thin and mostly hostile (parametric; weak).
- SRC-0122 (Boyce 1957): standardly read as qualifying the maximalist picture, **but its specific argument is left unestablished here** (the article was not read; SRC-0122 §6).

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Low** for the tradition-vs-current question — a single hostile witness plus a contested reconstruction.
- Consensus strength: the *existence of Zurvanite ideas* is broadly accepted (**Moderate**); the *"sect"/distinct-tradition status* is contested (**Low** — no settled view; the disputed status is itself the consensus).

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Distinctness threshold (§2.3) applied as the working criterion**, not as settled law — its strain on this case is the point.
- **Disciplined default:** attestation over reconstruction (CLM-0056); a coherent account built from unsystematic hostile notices risks importing coherence the phenomenon lacked (SRC-0120 §3).
- **Metaphysical bracket** held.

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **zurvanite_ideas_attested — Level 3 (Moderate):** the Zurvan cosmogony existed and was attested by the 5th c. CE.
- **distinct_tradition_status — Level 2 (Low):** whether it was a distinct tradition (vs a current) is disputed and, on §2.3, not securely cleared; thin hostile base. **No Level 4/5.** R0.

# Limitations
- Establishes the ideas' existence and the *disputed* distinct-tradition status; does **not** establish an organised Zurvanite church, nor a firm origin date, nor resolve the maximalist/minimalist dispute.
- The current-not-tradition disposition is a modelling judgment under §2.3, not a demonstrated historical fact — recorded as such, and surfaced for review.
