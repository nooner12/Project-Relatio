# CLM-0091

# Yazidi Origins and Descent

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Yazidism crystallised as a distinct Kurdish religious tradition around the historical figure of **Sheikh ʿAdī ibn Musāfir (12th century CE)** and the cult centre at Lalish, out of a **Sufi-Islamic milieu** (the ʿAdawiyya order). Its **deeper descent is genuinely disputed**: one scholarly account (Kreyenbroek) argues a primary substrate in a **pre-Islamic Western Iranian religion**; a competing account derives it primarily from the **Sufi tradition** around Sheikh ʿAdī. There is **no direct textual chain** to any Iranian antecedent — the substrate case is comparative reconstruction. Its in-family lineage edge therefore carries the qualifier **`disputed`**, which is a *finding*, not a hedge.

---

# Element (i) — Emergence Dating
- **12th-century crystallisation.** SRC-0118 (Kreyenbroek 1995, pp. 27–44) treats Sheikh ʿAdī ibn Musāfir as the historical figure around whom Yazidism formed, with the Lalish cult centre; ʿAdī was a Sufi shaykh of the ʿAdawiyya order (12th c. CE). This is the datable crystallisation point.
- **Earlier substrate undated and disputed.** Any pre-12th-c. Iranian substrate is, on the substrate thesis, *reconstructed comparatively* — there is no dated text or manuscript chain (SRC-0118 §3). The published Yazidi "sacred books" (Kitêba Cilwe, Mishefa Reş) are, per SRC-0118/SRC-0119, **modern (19th–20th c.) compilations of scholarly provenance**, not ancient scripture — so they cannot date the tradition backward.
- **Disposition:** 12th-c. crystallisation reasonably grounded (Moderate); any earlier origin undated and contested. `display_range` on ENT-0011: "12th c. CE crystallisation (earlier substrate disputed)."

# Element (ii) — Descent Relationship
- **Descent DISPUTED — the finding is the disputedness.** Two accounts, neither established:
  - **Iranian-substrate thesis (Kreyenbroek, SRC-0118):** Yazidism descends primarily from a pre-Islamic Western Iranian religion, later overlaid with Sufi-Islamic forms. SRC-0118 §3 flags this as **advocacy, not neutral survey** — "there is no direct textual chain, and the reconstruction is comparative."
  - **Sufi-origin thesis:** Yazidism derives primarily from the Sufi tradition around Sheikh ʿAdī (i.e., an Islamic-mystical origin), with any Iranian colouring secondary. SRC-0118 surveys this as the competing account.
- **In-family edge:** `branches_from` ENT-0008 (Zoroastrianism), qualifier **`disputed`** — warranted by the genuine, live scholarly dispute over Iranian descent. **Two honest strains, both flagged for Anchor Fit:** (1) the "Iranian" substrate in the substrate thesis is *ancient Western Iranian religion broadly*, not specifically Zoroastrianism, so edging to ENT-0008 (Zoroastrianism, the family's root representative) stretches the target; (2) the strongest single alternative (Sufi origin) is **out-of-family** and therefore unedgeable, so the `disputed` qualifier — not the absence of an edge — is what carries the contestation. `tradition_type` on ENT-0011 is **`syncretic`** (an Iranian-substrate/Sufi-Islamic blend under either account).
- **Distinctness (§2.3):** met — Yazidism is a living tradition with its own community, priesthood/caste structure, cult centre, and recognition as a named religion; the dispute is about its *descent*, not its *distinctness*.

# Element (iii) — Rival Reading (steelmanned)
- **Strongest rival to Iranian descent — the Sufi-origin account.** Yazidism is, on this reading, a heterodox development *of Islam* (the ʿAdawiyya Sufi order), and its "Iranian" features are later or superficial; on this account the tradition does not belong in an Iranian religious family at all, and the `branches_from` Zoroastrianism edge is unwarranted. **Survives, fully as a live alternative** — it is precisely why the qualifier is `disputed` and why the descent components are Moderate (grading the *disputedness*), not higher. **Cost:** it must still account for the Iranian-language and non-Islamic mythological elements that the substrate thesis foregrounds; neither account has closed the case.
- **Note on advocacy:** SRC-0118's substrate thesis is the record's strongest Iranian-descent source but is itself advocacy (SRC-0118 §3); it is not treated as the settled view, per the source's own caution.

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Historical (monograph + documentary history; oral-tradition-mediated).**
- SRC-0118 (Kreyenbroek 1995, pp. 27–44): Sheikh ʿAdī and Lalish; the Iranian-substrate thesis (flagged as advocacy) and the competing Sufi-origin account; the modern provenance of the "sacred books" (live-verified citation; strong bibliographic verification).
- SRC-0119 (Guest 1993): the 19th–20th-c. documentary trail and the modern compilation of the published "sacred books" (parametric; weak, disclosed).

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** for the *fact of the dispute* and the 12th-c. crystallisation; **Low** for either specific descent.
- Consensus strength: there is **no consensus on descent** — the disputed status is the accurately reported state; the 12th-c. ʿAdī crystallisation is broadly accepted (Moderate).

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Report the dispute at its actual weight (§2.2 analog):** neither derivation is asserted; the finding is the contestation.
- **Substrate thesis not privileged** despite being the record's Iranian-descent source (SRC-0118's own advocacy caveat).
- **Metaphysical bracket** held.

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **crystallization_dating — Level 3 (Moderate):** 12th-c. ʿAdī/Lalish crystallisation reasonably grounded; earlier substrate undated.
- **descent_disputed — Level 3 (Moderate):** that the descent is genuinely disputed (Iranian-substrate vs Sufi-origin) is well-supported; *neither* specific descent is established (each Low). **No Level 4/5.** R0.

# Limitations
- Establishes a 12th-c. crystallisation and a live descent dispute; does **not** establish an Iranian descent as fact (nor refute it), and does not date any pre-Islamic substrate.
- The `branches_from` target (Zoroastrianism) is a family-root stand-in for "ancient Western Iranian religion"; recorded as an Anchor Fit strain.
