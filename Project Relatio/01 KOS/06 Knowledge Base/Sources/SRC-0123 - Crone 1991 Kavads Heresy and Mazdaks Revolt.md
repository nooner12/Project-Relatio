# SRC-0123

# Crone 1991 — "Kavad's Heresy and Mazdak's Revolt"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Patricia Crone, "Kavād's Heresy and Mazdak's Revolt," *Iran* 29 (1991), 21–42.

A critical re-examination of the Mazdakite episode — Kavad I's association with the movement, its doctrines, and its suppression — working sceptically over the late Perso-Arabic historiographical tradition that is effectively the only source for it.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Crone was a major historian of the late antique and early Islamic Near East, known specifically for source-critical rigour with exactly this kind of late tradition; *Iran* is a peer-reviewed journal of record.
- **Transparency:** High. The argument works openly over the named sources and is explicit about what the late material can and cannot bear.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly work. Its critical stance toward the received narrative is one of its principal virtues.
- **Intent:** Source-critical revision — to test the received account of the Mazdakite episode against the character of the evidence for it, rather than to retell it.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **The underlying source base is late and problematic**, and no amount of critical skill repairs that. The narrative rests on Perso-Arabic tradition (al-Ṭabarī, the *Šāhnāme*) written centuries after the events; Crone's reconstruction is a critical argument *over* that late material, not access to better evidence.
- **The near-contemporary evidence is thinner than the narrative it is used to support.** Procopius attests upheaval in Kavad's reign but does not attest the massacre; the gap between what near-contemporary sources support and what the later tradition asserts is the crux, and it is not closed here.
- **Details remain debated** — including the massacre narrative itself and the chronology (c. 528–531 CE). This article is an influential position, not the settlement of the question.
- It is a **journal article on a specific problem**, not a general treatment of Mazdakism.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The source-critical re-reading of the Mazdakite episode and its suppression.
- The assessment of the late Perso-Arabic tradition as the effective evidential base.
