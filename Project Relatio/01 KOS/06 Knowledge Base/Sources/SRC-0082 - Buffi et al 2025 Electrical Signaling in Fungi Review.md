# SRC-0082

# Buffi et al. 2025 — "Electrical Signaling in Fungi: Past and Present Challenges"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Matteo Buffi, Julia M. Kelliher, Aaron J. Robinson, Diego Gonzalez, Guillaume Cailleau, and colleagues (senior author Pilar Junier), "Electrical signaling in fungi: past and present challenges," *FEMS Microbiology Reviews* (2025), fuaf009. A **recent, balanced peer-reviewed review** of the fungal-electrophysiology field. **In this KB it is the measured-middle anchor**: it treats fungal electrical signaling as a real but under-explored phenomenon while foregrounding **methodological caveats** — exactly the balance CLM-0061/0062 need.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Multi-institution microbiology review team; peer-reviewed in a leading reviews journal.
- **Transparency:** High. Explicitly catalogues the difficulties of replicating prior recordings and the artifact risks of electrode methods on microscopic hyphae.
- **Independence:** Strong — neither an enthusiast nor a pure debunking source; it takes the phenomenon seriously and disciplines the claims.
- **Intent:** To survey what is and is not established about fungal electrical signaling and to flag methodological challenges.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- A review, not new primary data on the language question; it **neither vindicates nor fully refutes** the "language" reading — it counsels caution.
- Confirms the field is **underexplored and methodologically hard**, which is itself a reason to keep the electrical-signaling datum at moderate (not high) confidence and the interpretation low.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Electrical signaling in fungi is **real but poorly characterized**; hyphal dimensions and mycelial modularity make traditional electrophysiology unreliable, so caveats attend prior recordings.
- The review notes caveats identified when **replicating previous studies** — supporting the position that no single, well-defined "fungal spike/language" is yet established.
