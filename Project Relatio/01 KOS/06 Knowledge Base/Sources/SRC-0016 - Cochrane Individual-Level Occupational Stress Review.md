# SRC-0016

# Cochrane (Tamminga et al., 2023) — Individual-Level Interventions for Reducing Occupational Stress in Healthcare Workers

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Cochrane systematic review of **individual-level** interventions to reduce occupational stress in healthcare workers. Categorises interventions by whether they direct attention **toward** the experience of stress (cognitive-behavioural training, mindfulness) or **away** from it (relaxation, exercise, yoga/tai chi, massage, music). This is the largest, most rigorous synthesis bearing directly on the durability question for individual techniques.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — Cochrane Work Review Group; GRADE certainty ratings.
- **Transparency:** Very High — pre-registered protocol, risk-of-bias, GRADE.
- **Independence:** Very High — no product interest.
- **Intent:** Inform.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Population is healthcare workers** — a specific occupational group; generalisation to the general adult population is a limitation.
- Evidence certainty is **low** — the effect exists but its size and durability are uncertain.
- "Up to a year" is the **ceiling of available follow-up**, not a demonstrated durable plateau.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- **117 studies, 11,119 participants.**
- Individual-level interventions (both "toward" and "away" types) **may reduce stress symptoms in the short and medium term, with the effect potentially lasting up to a year** — but on **low-certainty evidence**.
- Named effective individual routes: **cognitive-behaviour training, exercising, listening to music.**
- Historical context (earlier Cochrane occupational-stress work): CBT with/without relaxation was, in some studies, **no better than no intervention at one-month follow-up** — i.e. the durability signal has always been fragile.
