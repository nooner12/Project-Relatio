# SRC-0044

# Polanyi 1966 — The Tacit Dimension

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Polanyi, M. (1966), *The Tacit Dimension*, Doubleday (widely reissued, University of Chicago Press 2009). The origin of the concept of **tacit knowledge** and the maxim **"we can know more than we can tell."** Catalogued to anchor the **context-stripping / tacit-loss** half of CLM-0029 (previously invoked via the Polanyi/Nonaka tradition without a catalogued source).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — Polanyi is the foundational source for tacit knowledge; the work is canonical across philosophy of science and knowledge management.
- **Transparency:** Moderate-to-High — a philosophical monograph; its argument is discursive rather than empirical.
- **Independence:** High — a single-author philosophical work, no commercial interest.
- **Intent:** Inform / philosophise (epistemology of knowing).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It is a **philosophical argument**, not an empirical measurement of how much knowledge is tacit or how much is lost in codification.
- It establishes that **some knowledge resists articulation**; it does not quantify the codification loss for any particular knowledge system.
- The application to KM codification strategy is via the later tradition (Nonaka); Polanyi supplies the *foundational concept*, not the managerial claim.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Central maxim: **"we can know more than we can tell"** — a substrate of embodied, personal, largely inarticulable knowledge underlies explicit knowing.
- Consequence used here: because tacit knowledge resists lossless codification, **over-codification can strip the context/tacit component** that made knowledge useful — the built-in limit on the value of formalizing knowledge (CLM-0029).

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0029 (the tacit-knowledge / context-stripping limit on codification).
