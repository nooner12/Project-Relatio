# SRC-0075

# Silverman 2012 — Persepolis and Jerusalem: Iranian Influence on the Apocalyptic Hermeneutic

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Jason M. Silverman, *Persepolis and Jerusalem: Iranian Influence on the Apocalyptic Hermeneutic* (Library of Hebrew Bible/Old Testament Studies 558; T&T Clark, 2012; ISBN 978-0-567-20551-3). A method-focused monograph asking whether Persian Zoroastrianism inspired the apocalyptic turn among early Second Temple Jewish groups. It devotes chapters to the Achaemenid Empire, the sources for Iranian religion, historical-reconstruction and dating methodology, and the *loci* of Iranian–Judaean interaction, then analyzes pericopes from Ezekiel, Daniel, and 1 Enoch. **In this KB it is the contact-pathway/method anchor (CLM-0057):** Silverman takes seriously *where and how* interaction could have occurred and reframes "influence" as an apocalyptic *hermeneutic* rather than motif-borrowing.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern reconstruction), drawing on **H-b** Achaemenid administrative/material evidence for the contact context.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — a specialist monograph on precisely the contact question (Silverman, Helsinki).
- **Transparency:** High — foregrounds dating methodology and the difficulty of demonstrating a channel; explicit that the argument is about hermeneutic, not proven transfer.
- **Independence:** Moderately pro-interaction but methodologically self-critical; reviewers note it presses a contested thesis carefully.
- **Intent:** To reformulate the methodology of "influence" and locate plausible interaction sites.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Silverman establishes *plausible loci and a reframed method*, not a documented doctrinal-transmission channel — the pathway remains inferential, which is exactly the bound CLM-0057 records.
- The "apocalyptic hermeneutic" reframing is itself contested; it strengthens the plausibility case without proving directional transfer of any specific doctrine.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The Achaemenid administrative context provides sustained, evidenced Persian–Judaean contact (imperial governance of Yehud).
- "Influence" is better modeled as a shared apocalyptic hermeneutic than as motif-borrowing; specific transmission channels remain reconstructed, not documented.
