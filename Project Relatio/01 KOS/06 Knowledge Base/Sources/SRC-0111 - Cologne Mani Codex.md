# SRC-0111

# Cologne Mani Codex

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
The Cologne Mani Codex (*Der Kölner Mani-Kodex*), a miniature Greek parchment codex held at Cologne under the shelfmark P. Colon. inv. 4780 — among the smallest known codices from antiquity. Its text, conventionally titled *On the Origin of His Body*, is a hagiographic compilation of earlier Manichaean accounts concerning Mani's youth, his upbringing in a Jewish-Christian baptist (Elchasaite) community, and his revelations and break with that community.

Catalogued as a **primary text** — an extant ancient artifact and the single most important witness to Manichaean tradition about Mani's origins.

**Corpus rule applied — split, with the judgment recorded.** The Iranian-family source base cites this work in the fused conventional form "*Der Kölner Mani-Kodex*, ed. Koenen & Römer (1988)," which names object and edition in a single citation. I have nonetheless **split it into two records** (this one and SRC-0112). Reasoning: the fused form is a bibliographic convention for citing any edited ancient text, not evidence that the two are inseparable; and the codex has a genuinely separate, live edition history (earlier partial editions preceded the 1988 critical edition), so "is this rendering current?" is a real question with a different answer from "is this codex authentic?" Bundling would have collapsed exactly the distinction the corpus rule exists to preserve.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High as a witness to *Manichaean tradition*. It is a genuine ancient artifact, and it preserves material attributed to named early tradents, placing it within institutional living memory of Mani.
- **Transparency:** Mixed. The codex is extant, published, and physically examinable — but it is **damaged and heavily lacunose**, so what the text says in many places is itself an editorial reconstruction (see SRC-0112). The compilation's own principles of selection are not stated.
- **Independence:** **None.** This is an in-group hagiographic compilation produced by the movement about its founder. It is not independent testimony and must not be treated as such; its value is as the tradition's own best witness to itself.
- **Intent:** Hagiographic and legitimating — to authorise Mani's prophetic mission and to account for his separation from the baptist community. Edificatory, not historiographic.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It establishes **what Manichaean tradition held** about Mani's origins, not the independent truth of the biographical details. Proximity to living memory constrains fabrication somewhat; it does not verify.
- The compilation **postdates Mani**, and the codex postdates the compilation. Attribution of material to named early tradents is a claim made *within* the source.
- Its **lacunose state** means specific readings can be edition-dependent; a claim resting on a damaged passage inherits the editor's reconstruction (SRC-0112).
- It bears on Mani's **early life and self-understanding**, not on the later institutional history, the death chronology (SRC-0114), or the Roman suppression (SRC-0115).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The account of Mani's upbringing within a Jewish-Christian baptist community and his break from it.
- The birth notice placing Mani near Seleucia-Ctesiphon in the early 3rd century.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- **Critical edition used: SRC-0112 (Koenen & Römer 1988)**, recorded in `related_documents`. The typed edge is carried directionally from the edition (`derived_from` this record); no reciprocal edge is asserted, since the codex does not depend on the edition.

# 6. Verification (STD-0006 §5.7 / §7.5)
**Not verified — parametric.** The codex was **not re-located or examined** during the authoring session; this record is written from the author-model's parametric knowledge. The shelfmark, the codex's miniature format, and its general content are recorded as standardly reported in the field, not as checked facts. The **codex's date is given as an approximate palaeographic range and is marked unknown in precision**; the compiler and tradents are anonymous. Verification strength: **weak**. Note that **no verification status is shared with SRC-0112** — neither was checked, and verifying one would not have verified the other.
