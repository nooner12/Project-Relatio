# SRC-0120

# Eznik of Kolb — De Deo

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Eznik of Kołb, *De Deo* (the Armenian treatise conventionally known in English as *Against the Sects* / *Refutation of the Sects*), 5th century CE. Its section on Persian religion contains the fullest early account of the Zurvanite myth — the birth of Ohrmazd and Ahriman from the primordial figure Zurvan.

Catalogued as a **primary text**: a near-contemporary, external witness to what Zurvanism was understood to teach.

**No object/edition split made.** The source base names the author and work only; **no edition or translation is separately cited**. Per the corpus rule's constraint, no edition has been invented to force a split — this is a single record. (This is a judgment call in the sense that an Armenian text of this period is necessarily read through some modern edition; but naming one that the source base does not would be fabrication, so the record stops at the object.)

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Moderate-to-high as a witness to **5th-century Armenian Christian understanding** of Zurvanism. It is near-contemporary with the Sasanian religious situation it describes, which is its principal strength.
- **Transparency:** Good in one specific respect — **the polemical frame is explicit**, not concealed. A reader knows exactly what the author is trying to do, which makes the bias legible and therefore discountable in a way that hidden bias would not be.
- **Independence:** **External to Zoroastrianism, and this cuts both ways.** As outsider attestation it is not shaped by Zoroastrian self-presentation; but it is hostile outsider attestation, with all the distortion risk that carries.
- **Intent:** Christian apologetic and heresiological — to refute and discredit Persian religious teaching. The account exists to be argued against.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **It carries disproportionate weight, and that is a structural weakness of the whole question.** There is **no native Zoroastrian primary text** attesting a Zurvanite sect; Eznik is therefore doing evidential work that no independent source corroborates. A single hostile witness cannot settle what it is nearly alone in reporting.
- Hostile heresiography characteristically **systematises and sharpens** its opponents' positions — turning currents into sects, and tendencies into doctrines. The existence of Zurvanite ideas is broadly accepted; the **extent, organisation, and "sect" status of Zurvanism are contested**, and precisely those are what Eznik's framing would be expected to overstate.
- It attests **what an outsider understood**, not what Zoroastrians held. The gap between the two is exactly the question at issue.
- It bears on the 5th century; extrapolation to earlier Sasanian or Achaemenid religion is not supported.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The account of the Zurvanite myth: Zurvan as primordial figure and the origin of Ohrmazd and Ahriman.
