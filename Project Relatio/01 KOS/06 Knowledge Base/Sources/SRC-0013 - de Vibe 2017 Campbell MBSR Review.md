# SRC-0013

# de Vibe et al. (2017) — MBSR for Health, QoL and Social Functioning (Campbell Systematic Review)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Campbell Collaboration systematic review and meta-analysis of MBSR for improving mental and physical health, quality of life, and social functioning in adults.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — Campbell Collaboration reviews use rigorous, pre-specified systematic-review methodology.
- **Transparency:** High — Campbell protocols, risk-of-bias assessment, and active-vs-inactive control stratification.
- **Independence:** High — collaboration review, not investigator-allegiance driven.
- **Intent:** Inform (rigorous appraisal of the MBSR evidence base).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Follow-up windows reported (range **1–34 months**) are heterogeneous; the review does not establish a durable >1-year plateau in general adults.
- Because it candidly reports the quality problem, it is best read as a **caution** on the MBSR literature, not a durability endorsement.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Effects were generally **maintained at follow-up across a 1–34 month range**, and correlated with increases in self-reported mindfulness.
- **Roughly two-thirds of included studies were at considerable risk of bias**; risk was higher in studies with **inactive** controls, and **higher-quality studies reported lower effects** — the classic small-study / bias signature. *(Verified at abstract/summary level this session.)*
