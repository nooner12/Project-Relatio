# SRC-0102

# Lüdemann — The Resurrection of Jesus (1994): the 1 Cor 15 pre-Pauline creed

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Gerd Lüdemann, *The Resurrection of Jesus: History, Experience, Theology* (Fortress Press, 1994; ET of *Die Auferstehung Jesu*). In this KB it is used **strictly for its source-critical dating of the pre-Pauline creed in 1 Corinthians 15:3–7** — Lüdemann's argument that the formula Paul says he "received" and "passed on" predates his letter and can be dated to within a few years of the movement's origin (he places its formation c. 30–33 CE). Lüdemann is a **non-Christian** NT scholar (he publicly renounced Christian faith), which is why his early-dating of the *proclamation* is evidentially significant: it is a datum about **what was proclaimed how early**, entirely independent of whether the proclaimed event occurred (which is bracketed here as a metaphysical claim).

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) — a source-critical dating argument about material embedded in the H-a Pauline text.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Lüdemann (Göttingen) was a recognized specialist in early Christian origins and the resurrection traditions.
- **Transparency:** High — the criteria for isolating the pre-Pauline formula (non-Pauline vocabulary, formulaic structure, "received/delivered" transmission language, Semitic features) are stated explicitly.
- **Independence:** High — an explicitly non-Christian scholar; his early dating of the *proclamation* cannot be attributed to apologetic motive — indeed he uses it to argue a *naturalistic* account of the resurrection belief.
- **Intent:** To argue a historical-critical (and, on the event itself, skeptical) thesis; used here only for the dating of the tradition, not for his theological conclusions.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It establishes the **early date of the proclamation/creed** — that the movement was proclaiming Jesus "raised" within a few years — **not** that any resurrection event occurred (strictly bracketed; Lüdemann himself denies it).
- The precise date (early 30s) is a reconstruction (H-e); the broad point (the formula is pre-Pauline and very early) is widely held, the exact year less so.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- 1 Cor 15:3–7 is a pre-Pauline formula that Paul received and transmitted; its elements date to the first few years after the crucifixion (Lüdemann: c. 30–33 CE).
- The early dating is corroborated across the spectrum (e.g. Wedderburn, Wright) — the *proclamation* of Jesus as raised is one of the earliest datable data points about the movement.

# 6. Verification (STD-0006 §5.7 / §7.5)
**Live-verified this session (strong for the dating claim; moderate for exact citation).** Lüdemann's dating of the 1 Cor 15:3–7 formation to "between 30 and 33 CE" / "not later than three years" after the crucifixion, and his status as a non-Christian scholar, were confirmed against multiple independent summaries this session; the broad early-creed consensus (c. 30–35 CE) is well attested across confessional and non-confessional scholars. The specific edition (Fortress, 1994, ET) is standard; page-level citation not asserted. No fabrication. The resurrection **event** is not graded — only the dating of the proclamation.
