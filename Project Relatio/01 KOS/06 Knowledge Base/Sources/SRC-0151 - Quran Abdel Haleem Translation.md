# SRC-0151

# The Qur'an — Abdel Haleem English Translation (Oxford World's Classics)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

The **Qur'an**, catalogued in the English translation of **M. A. S. Abdel Haleem** (*The Qur'an: A New Translation*, Oxford World's Classics; Oxford University Press, first published 2004; Oxford World's Classics paperback issue 2008). Abdel Haleem is Professor of Islamic Studies at SOAS, University of London, and the translation is among the most widely used English versions in academic settings.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (f) — primary text for the SELF-UNDERSTANDING track only**, following the **SRC-0135 precedent** (a tradition's own voice catalogued in the self-understanding register only).

**REGISTER LIMIT — binding (INV-0018 §2.1, §3.1 dual-track separation).** This source evidences **what the tradition holds about itself** — its account of restoration and of confirmation-and-correction of earlier revelation, *millat Ibrāhīm* — and is the source for **CLM D (i)**. It is **not** evidence for or against the historical claims (CLM A / B / C), in either direction: it may not be used to establish a historical relationship, and it may not be dismissed as mere error. **Both slides are guarded.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** **High for its own register** — the Qur'an is the tradition's own foundational text and is definitive evidence of the tradition's self-understanding. It carries **no authority on the historical questions**, which are settled (or left open) by historical evidence.
- **Transparency:** High as to the translation apparatus — Abdel Haleem supplies an introduction, explanatory notes on historical and personal allusions, and a topical index; the translator's interpretive choices are stated.
- **Independence:** **None with respect to the tradition** — it *is* the tradition's own scripture, which is precisely its evidential value in the self-understanding register and precisely why it is confined to it.
- **Intent:** Scripture, in its own register; the edition's intent is accessible scholarly translation for an English readership.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **Nothing about the historical questions** (dual-track rule): it evidences self-understanding only, and no CLM A/B/C grade may move on it.
- **TRANSLATION IS INTERPRETIVELY LOADED — disclosed, not neutralised.** The **Arabic text is the authoritative object** in the tradition and in scholarship; every English rendering embeds interpretive decisions (lexical choice, syntactic disambiguation, the handling of terms of art such as *ḥanīf*, *millat Ibrāhīm*, *tanzīl*, *ummī*). **This record names its edition so that any claim citing it names an interpretive choice rather than "the Qur'an" simpliciter.** Where a CLM D reading turns on a contested rendering, the circuit must say so and, if it matters, check against at least one further translation or the Arabic — **or grade the point down for that limit.**
- Different translations differ materially on passages relevant to CLM D; **no claim may rest on a rendering peculiar to this edition without that being disclosed.**
- The text's own compositional history, chronology, and canonisation are **contested historical questions** treated by the secondary literature (SRC-0145 / SRC-0146 / SRC-0147), **not settled by this record's `publication_date` field**, which records the traditional dating as traditional.
- **The interior was not systematically read this session** (§6); this is a catalogue entry, not an exegesis.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- **None consumed at cataloguing.** The passages bearing on the self-understanding track (the Abrahamic-restoration material, the confirmation-and-correction material, the addresses to the People of the Book) are **identified as the relevant loci for CLM D at circuit** and are cited by *sūra*:*āya* with the edition named — **no passage is quoted or paraphrased here.**

**Live-verified bibliographically (strong); interior not systematically read — disclosed.** Translator, title, series (Oxford World's Classics), publisher (Oxford University Press), first publication (2004) and the Oxford World's Classics paperback issue (2008, ISBN 978-0-19-953595-8) confirmed this session against the Oxford University Press academic catalogue listing, retail and bookseller catalogue records (including the 2004 ISBN 978-0-19-283193-4), and Internet Archive copies of the edition. **Reported extents vary (c. 502–512 pp.) across listings and both are recorded rather than one being chosen.** An attempted direct fetch of the OUP product page returned no content this session; the data above rest on the remaining surfaces, and that limit is disclosed. **No passage was quoted or paraphrased at cataloguing.** Language: English translation of an Arabic original (**the Arabic is authoritative**; the Arabic text itself is not separately catalogued at this stage — a recorded limit). Accessibility: in print, inexpensive, widely available; multiple public-domain and open scans of this edition exist online. No fabrication.
