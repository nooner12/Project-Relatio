# SRC-0104

# Shahbazi 1977 — "The 'Traditional Date of Zoroaster' Explained"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
A. Shapur Shahbazi, "The 'Traditional Date of Zoroaster' Explained," *Bulletin of the School of Oriental and African Studies* 40/1 (1977), 25–35.

The standard critical treatment of the so-called traditional date of Zoroaster — the placement of the prophet "258 years before Alexander," i.e. in the 6th century BCE. Shahbazi's argument is that this figure is a late Sasanian-era scholastic back-calculation rather than preserved historical memory.

Catalogued here as part of the Iranian-family source base (Zoroastrianism, Zurvanism, Manichaeism, Mazdakism, Yazidism). No investigation is open against it.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Shahbazi was a specialist Iranologist; *BSOAS* is a peer-reviewed journal of record for the field. This article is the standard reference point for the traditional-date question and is routinely cited as having settled it.
- **Transparency:** High. The argument is philological and chronological, worked openly from the late Zoroastrian and Perso-Arabic chronological sources; the reader can follow how the 258-year figure is reconstructed as an artifact of calculation.
- **Independence:** Independent of the tradition. This is external critical scholarship, not a tradition-internal chronology, and it argues *against* the tradition's own dating.
- **Intent:** Scholarly-critical — to explain the origin of a received date, not to defend or replace it.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It establishes that the 258-years-before-Alexander date is a **late construct**; it does **not** thereby establish any alternative date for Zoroaster. The competing early dating (c. 1500–1000 BCE) rests on wholly separate comparative-linguistic evidence (see SRC-0105) and does not follow from Shahbazi's negative result.
- It concerns a **date**, not the historicity of Zoroaster as a person, and settles nothing about the Gathas' authorship or transmission.
- Published 1977; the specific reconstruction has been discussed since. It is treated here as the standard critical account, not as beyond revision.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The reconstruction of the "258 years before Alexander" figure as a Sasanian scholastic calculation rather than a transmitted historical datum.
- The consequent scholarly rejection of a 6th-century BCE Zoroaster.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- Sits in tension with the tradition-internal dating it analyses; catalogued alongside SRC-0105, which carries the competing comparative-linguistic dating argument. No typed edge is asserted to SRC-0105: the two address different questions (origin of a received date vs. positive linguistic dating) and are not straightforwardly in contradiction.
