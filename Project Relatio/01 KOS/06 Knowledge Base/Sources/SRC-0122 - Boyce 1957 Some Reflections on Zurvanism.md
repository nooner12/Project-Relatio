# 1. Identification
Mary Boyce, "Some Reflections on Zurvanism," *Bulletin of the School of Oriental and African Studies* 19 (1957).

A short article engaging the Zurvanism question in the period immediately following Zaehner's monograph (SRC-0121).

**Scope boundary — deliberate.** This is a **separate work** from Boyce's multi-volume *A History of Zoroastrianism*, which is already catalogued as **SRC-0068** and is **reused, not recreated, by this build**. The two must not be conflated, and SRC-0068's scope must not be extended to cover this article: they differ in genre, date, argument, and evidential weight. SRC-0068 is referenced here in prose only, and no typed edge or frontmatter link is asserted to it, so that this cataloguing does not modify an existing record.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High for the author and venue. Boyce was among the foremost scholars of Zoroastrianism and *BSOAS* is a journal of record. Note that in 1957 this is **early-career work**, preceding the studies on which her authority principally rests.
- **Transparency:** Presumed good for a *BSOAS* article of the period, but **not assessed** — the article was not consulted for this record.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly work, external to the tradition.
- **Intent:** Critical reflection on a question then freshly reopened. Beyond that general characterisation, the article's specific aim is **not established here** (see §6).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **A short article, not a monograph.** It does not carry the evidential weight of a full study and should not be cited as a comprehensive treatment.
- **Its position is not established by this record.** The article is standardly *reported* as qualifying the maximalist Zurvanite reconstruction, but that characterisation is unverified here. **It must be read before being relied on for any specific claim about what Boyce argued** — attributing a position to a scholar on the strength of a general reputation is exactly the failure this limitation exists to prevent.
- Being 1957, it predates most subsequent work on the question.
- **Page range unknown**; the article cannot presently be located precisely within the volume from this record alone.
