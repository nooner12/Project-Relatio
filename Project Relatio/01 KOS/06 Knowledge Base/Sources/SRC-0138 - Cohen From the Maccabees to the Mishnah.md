# Cohen — From the Maccabees to the Mishnah

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Shaye J. D. Cohen, *From the Maccabees to the Mishnah* (Library of Early Christianity; Philadelphia: The Westminster Press, 1987; 2nd ed. Louisville: Westminster John Knox Press, 2006; 3rd ed. 2014). A standard single-author survey of Judaism from the Maccabean period through the redaction of the Mishnah (c. 200 CE) — spanning **both ends of the INV-0017 fork's Jewish side**: the internal diversity and sectarianism of Second Temple Judaism *and* the emergence of rabbinic Judaism after 70 CE. This makes it the base's **bridge source** between the Second Temple hub (Area 1) and Rabbinic formation (Area 3). Catalogued for **INV-0017** (ADR-GOV-0003 pattern); **no claim is asserted at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Cohen is a leading scholar of ancient Judaism (Harvard); the volume is a long-standing set text in the Library of Early Christianity series, now in its third edition.
- **Transparency:** High — an introductory synthesis that shows its evidentiary basis and flags scholarly disagreement.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship.
- **Intent:** A teaching survey of the development of Judaism across the period, including the transition from Temple-centred to rabbinic forms.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **A survey, not a monograph** — it summarises and orients rather than settling contested points (e.g., the pace and cleanness of rabbinic consolidation); its authority is as a standard overview.
- **Edition drift:** three editions (1987 / 2006 / 2014) with revisions; a claim citing it at circuit should name the edition consulted.
- Treats the Jewish side of the fork; the Christian separation is not its subject (SRC-0139/0140/0141 cover that).
- **Interior not read this session** (see §6).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The arc from Maccabean-era through Second Temple sectarian diversity to the post-70 rabbinic movement and the Mishnah's redaction c. 200 CE (recorded from verification surfaces; not consumed by any claim at cataloguing).
