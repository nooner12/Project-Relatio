# SRC-0076

# Zoroastrian Primary Corpus — Avestan (Old + Younger) and Pahlavi

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
The Zoroastrian scriptural/religious corpus, cited here as the primary-text basis for the doctrine claims. It is **stratified by date**, and that stratification is the crux of the whole investigation:
- **Old Avestan / Gathic layer** — the Gathas (Yasna 28–34, 43–51, 53) and the Yasna Haptaŋhāiti. Oldest layer; dated by comparative philology to ~1000 BCE (reconstruction). Attests **cosmic dualism** (the two primordial spirits, Yasna 30.3–5), **individual eschatological judgment** (the Činvat crossing; reward/punishment; the ordeal of molten metal), and **the daevas as rejected/demonized beings** — but *not* a systematic bodily resurrection or a developed savior scheme.
- **Younger Avestan layer** — e.g. **Yasht 19 (Zamyad Yasht)** and **Yasht 13 (Frawardin Yasht)**. Dated (by reconstruction) to roughly the Achaemenid period. Yasht 19.88–96 names the **Saoshyant / Astvat-ereta** and describes the **Frashokereti** (world made perfect, undying, undecaying); Yasht 13.129 names the Saoshyant. Yasht 19 is, per the reference literature, *the one Avestan text that undeniably attests resurrection*.
- **Pahlavi (Middle Persian) books** — **Bundahišn**, **Dēnkard**, **Zand ī Wahman Yasn**. Written in the 9th c. CE (claiming to preserve lost Avestan nasks such as the Dāmdād). These carry the **systematic** eschatology: the full resurrection sequence, the three future Saoshyants, the periodized world-year, and the universal molten-metal ordeal.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Primary — this *is* the tradition's own textual witness. Highest authority for *what the tradition holds*; the question is *when* each element enters.
- **Transparency:** Mixed — the texts do not date themselves; dating is external (philology, script analysis).
- **Independence:** n/a in the modern sense; it is the object of study.
- **Intent:** Liturgical, doctrinal, priestly — not historiographic.
- **H-band layering (the load-bearing point):**
  - *As surviving texts*, the Gathas and Yashts are primary texts (**H-a** for "this doctrine is present in this text").
  - *For early dating* — the question sub-question (i) actually asks — the Younger Avestan dating is **H-e** (linguistic reconstruction; no early manuscript), and the systematic Pahlavi doctrines are **H-d** (later tradition attributing content backward to lost Avestan sources). This split — H-a textual presence but H-e/H-d for *early dating* — is exactly the asymmetry the dual-coding protocol is built to expose.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Textual presence is not early dating.** That a doctrine appears in the corpus does not fix *when* it entered; the Pahlavi systematic form (9th c. CE) is centuries *later* than the Jewish attestations it is compared with, and its claim to preserve archaic Avestan material is a tradition-internal attribution (H-d), not independent evidence.
- No contemporaneous manuscript witness exists for any Avestan layer (oral transmission; Sasanian-era fixation) — see SRC-0073.
- The corpus cannot, by itself, establish *direction* of influence with Judaism; it establishes only what Zoroastrianism held and (via reconstruction) the relative order of its own layers.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Yasna 30.3–5 (two primordial spirits — dualism); Činvat judgment (Gathic).
- Yasht 19.88–96 (Saoshyant, Frashokereti, resurrection); Yasht 13.129 (Saoshyant named).
- Bundahišn / Zand ī Wahman Yasn (systematic resurrection, three Saoshyants, periodized ages) — late (9th c. CE) systematic form.
