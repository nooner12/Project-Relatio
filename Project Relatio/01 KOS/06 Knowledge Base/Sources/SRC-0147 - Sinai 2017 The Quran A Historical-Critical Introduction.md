# SRC-0147

# Sinai (2017) — The Qur'an: A Historical-Critical Introduction

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

Nicolai Sinai, *The Qur'an: A Historical-Critical Introduction* (The New Edinburgh Islamic Surveys; Edinburgh: Edinburgh University Press, 2017). A **current, methodologically explicit introduction to the historical-critical study of the Qur'an** — its literary structure, its chronology and internal development, its relation to the late-antique religious environment, and the evidentiary state of the dating and transmission questions. Sinai (University of Oxford) is a leading contemporary Qur'anic-studies scholar.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (c) — Qur'anic studies (historical-critical)**. It is the base's principal surface for **weighing the revisionist re-datings against the manuscript, linguistic, and literary evidence** (CLM A (iii)) — a work that takes the revisionist challenge seriously on its merits rather than by reputation, which is exactly the register INV-0018 requires. **No claim is asserted and no evidence is graded at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High — Sinai is Professor of Islamic Studies at the University of Oxford and a central figure in current Qur'anic studies; Edinburgh University Press, in an established survey series.
- **Transparency:** High — an introduction that foregrounds method, states where the evidence is thin, and marks its own judgements as judgements.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship, not confessional.
- **Intent:** Orientation and synthesis for a scholarly and student readership; explicitly historical-critical in method.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **An introduction, not a monograph** — it maps positions and evidence rather than settling contested questions; its own judgements are one scholar's, and INV-0018 must not read a survey's framing as the field's verdict.
- It is a source on **the Qur'anic text and its milieu**, not a history of the community's formation (SRC-0143/0144) or of the Arabian political-religious setting (SRC-0150).
- It does **not** evidence the tradition's self-understanding (that is SRC-0151's register only).
- **Interior not read this session** (§6) — no page-level citation may be drawn from it at circuit.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- The historical-critical framing of Qur'anic chronology, textual transmission, and late-antique context (recorded from verification surfaces; **not consumed by any claim at cataloguing**).
