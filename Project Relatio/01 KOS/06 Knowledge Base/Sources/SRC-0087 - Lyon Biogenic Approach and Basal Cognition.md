# SRC-0087

# Lyon — The Biogenic Approach to Cognition / Basal (Minimal) Cognition

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Pamela Lyon (University of Adelaide), "The biogenic approach to cognition," *Cognitive Processing* 7(1):11–29 (2006); and "Of what is 'minimal cognition' the half-baked version?," *Adaptive Behavior* 28(6):407–424 (2020). The **biogenic/basal-cognition framework** starts from biology and works "up" to the human case, treating cognition as a graded set of capacities (sensing, valence, integration, memory, adaptive action) present, in minimal form, well below the neural threshold. **In this KB it supplies the technical grading instrument (rungs c–d of the §1 ladder) for CLM-0066** — the definitional apparatus by which "does this count as cognition?" is asked rigorously rather than rhetorically.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High as a framework source. Lyon is a leading theorist of basal cognition; her definitions are widely cited and comparatively disciplined.
- **Transparency:** High. The framework is explicit about criteria and about *not* claiming sentience.
- **Independence:** Lyon is broadly sympathetic to non-neural cognition — a pro-expansion prior to weigh — but she is notably careful to distinguish "cognition" (technical) from "consciousness/sentience."
- **Intent:** To provide a biology-first, non-anthropocentric definition of cognition applicable across the tree of life.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Provides a **framework/definition**, not fungal data; whether fungi *meet* the criteria is an empirical/interpretive question answered by the other sources.
- "Basal cognition" is **a contested construct**: deflationary critics (cf. SRC-0081 authors; Taiz) hold it over-extends "cognition" to ordinary physiology. The framework licenses grading, not a verdict — and rung (e) sentience is explicitly outside it.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Cognition studied as **graded biological capacities** (goal-directedness, valence, temporal integration, memory-like adjustment), from cells up to and including simple neural systems.
- The **basal-cognition** compromise term and its criteria — the instrument used to locate fungal evidence on rungs (b)–(d) without importing sentience.
