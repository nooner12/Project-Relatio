# SRC-0021

# Nature Exposure & Stress Literature (Cluster)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
A cluster of meta-analyses and scoping reviews on nature/greenspace exposure and stress (self-reported stress, salivary cortisol, physiological arousal), treated as one Source record.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Moderate — several peer-reviewed syntheses; a fast-growing but methodologically uneven field.
- **Transparency:** Mixed — many included studies are small, brief-exposure, lab or single-session designs.
- **Independence:** High.
- **Intent:** Inform.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Dominated by acute / short-exposure designs** (10–30 minute exposures). It does **not** establish durable (>6–12 month) chronic-stress relief.
- Cortisol findings are **mixed** across reviews (some find association, others insufficient evidence).
- Confounding with physical activity, social context, and expectancy.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- **10–30 minutes** outdoors/in nature is associated with **lower cortisol and blood pressure** and reduced negative affect (acute effect).
- Reviews report **mixed** greenspace–cortisol associations and note insufficient evidence on serum/salivary cortisol — i.e. even the acute physiological signal is not clean.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0018 (acute yes, durable unestablished).
