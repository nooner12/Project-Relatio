# SRC-0001

# Canonical Gospels

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

The four canonical Gospels — Matthew, Mark, Luke, John — as the corpus for "the teachings of Jesus" in INV-0001. Cited by book, chapter, and verse.

---

# 2. Source Evaluation (per KOS-0003 §6)

**Authority:** High as *primary witnesses to the teachings as the Christian tradition received them*. Not eyewitness court transcripts; each Gospel is a theologically shaped composition. Authority is for *what the tradition teaches*, not for verbatim historical reconstruction.

**Transparency:** Each Gospel has a discernible theological program (e.g. Matthew's Torah framing; Luke's concern for the poor; John's high Christology). These programs are legible to scholarship and must be read *with*, not around.

**Independence:** Limited. Matthew and Luke draw on Mark (and likely a shared sayings source, "Q"); the Synoptics are therefore not fully independent witnesses. John is largely independent of the Synoptics.

**Intent:** To proclaim and form faith ("that you may believe," John 20:31), not to neutrally report. Intent informs interpretation; it does not invalidate the source.

---

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- It does not establish what the historical Jesus said verbatim (historicity is bracketed in INV-0001).
- It does not present a single systematic "teaching"; sayings are occasional and genre-varied (parable, aphorism, discourse).
- Read only in translation here; Koine Greek nuances (*basileia*, *metanoia*, *agapē*) are flagged, not resolved.

---

# 4. Key Passages Used in INV-0001

- Matt 5:5 (meek); 5:39 (non-resistance); 6:25–34 (anxiety / lilies / birds); 10:34 ("not peace but a sword" — counter-text).
- Mark 4:26–29 (seed growing *of itself*, *automatē*).
- "Father"/*Abba* language and Kingdom of God (*basileia*) sayings throughout (esp. Matt 6:9–10; Luke 11:2).
