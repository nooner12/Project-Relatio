# SRC-0134

# Irons (2017) — "Yiguandao," World Religions and Spirituality Project Profile

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Edward Irons, "Yiguandao," World Religions and Spirituality Project (WRSP), published 2017-12-02. A scholarly encyclopedia-style profile (WRSP publishes academic profiles of religious movements under academic editorship) by a Hong Kong-based specialist on Yiguandao and Chinese new religions (PhD, Graduate Theological Union, 2000; Director, Hong Kong Institute for Culture, Commerce and Religion). **The only source in this base whose full content was read live this session** — it therefore anchors the datable-fact spine: Wang Jueyi (1832–1886?), fifteenth patriarch of Xiantiandao, formed a group c. 1875; Liu Qingxu (sixteenth) gave the name "Yiguandao" in 1905; Lu Zhongyi seventeenth patriarch 1919; Zhang Tianran and Sun Suzhen joint eighteenth patriarchs 1930, with Zhang as "the true founder of the Yiguandao movement" refashioning it from 1934; legalized by Taiwan's Legislative Yuan 1987; ~810,000 Taiwanese members (3.5% of population) by 2005.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Moderate-to-High — a specialist author in an academically edited reference project; reference-work genre (tertiary synthesis) rather than peer-reviewed monograph.
- **Transparency:** High — profile format with historical narrative and references.
- **Independence:** High — academic; no confessional stake.
- **Intent:** Reference documentation of the movement for scholars and students.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Reference-work genre: it synthesizes the monographic literature; where it and a monograph diverged, the monograph would win (no such divergence was encountered on the facts used).
- Its classification vocabulary ("Chinese syncretic religion," new religious movement, salvationist/millenarian dimensions) is one specialist's summary of field usage, not a criteria argument.
- The uncertain death-year of Wang Jueyi ("1886?") is carried as given — uncertainty preserved, not resolved.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The dated lineage spine (all read live): c. 1875 formation under Wang Jueyi out of **Xiantiandao**; 1905 naming by Liu Qingxu; 1919 Lu Zhongyi; 1930 Zhang Tianran/Sun Suzhen; 1934 refashioning; 1987 legalization; 2005 scale figures (CLM-0081).
- Doctrinal composition (read live): Ancient/Eternal Mother cosmology with the **three ages ("Green Yang, Red Yang, and White Yang")**; incorporation of "the three Buddhas (Lamplighter, Sakyamuni, Maitreya), as well as all deities and the various founders of all religions, such as Jesus and Mohammed"; the "obtain first, then cultivate" soteriology (CLM-0082).
- The Daoist-lineage datum (read live): Wang Jueyi "added a strong neo-Confucian interpretation on top of existing teachings **derived from Quanzhen Daoism**" — the profile's statement of a real Daoist-derived strand in the Xiantiandao inheritance (CLM-0084).
- Classification usage (read live): "Chinese syncretic religion" / new religious movement with millenarian-salvationist dimensions; a modernization of earlier sectarian traditions — not classed as a school of Daoism (CLM-0083 census).
