# SRC-0085

# Kiers et al. 2011 — "Reciprocal Rewards Stabilize Cooperation in the Mycorrhizal Symbiosis"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
E. Toby Kiers, Marie Duhamel and colleagues (senior author H. Bücking), "Reciprocal rewards stabilize cooperation in the mycorrhizal symbiosis," *Science* 333(6044):880–882 (2011). Experimental demonstration that **plants preferentially reward fungal partners that deliver more phosphorus, and fungi preferentially deliver nutrients to roots supplying more carbon** — a bidirectional "biological market" enforcing cooperation. **In this KB it anchors the regulated, source–sink/reward character of network resource exchange (CLM-0062/0063)** without invoking cognition.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. *Science* primary paper; Kiers is a leading symbiosis/evolutionary-biology researcher.
- **Transparency:** High. Controlled experiments manipulating partner quality with isotope tracing.
- **Independence:** Strong. Frames results in **evolutionary/economic (market) terms**, explicitly not cognitive — a useful counterweight to anthropomorphic readings.
- **Intent:** To explain how mutualism is stabilized against cheating.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Establishes **reciprocal, discriminating resource exchange** as an emergent market-like regulation; does **NOT** establish cognition, decision-making in a psychological sense, or the strong wood-wide-web narrative. "Discrimination" here is biochemical/selective, not deliberative.
- Arbuscular-mycorrhizal (AM) system; ectomycorrhizal forest CMNs (Simard/Karst) differ.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Plants **detect and reward** higher-quality fungal partners with more carbon; fungi reciprocally **increase nutrient transfer** to more-rewarding roots — bidirectional enforcement.
- Supports **source–sink/reward regulation** of transfer as a mechanistic, non-cognitive account of "who gets what" in the network.
