# SRC-0126

# Dunn — Jesus Remembered (2003)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
James D. G. Dunn, *Jesus Remembered* (Christianity in the Making, vol. 1; Grand Rapids: Eerdmans, 2003). The major statement of the **oral-tradition / memory model** of the Jesus tradition: the Gospels give access not to unmediated events but to the **remembered Jesus** — the impact he made on his first followers, transmitted as performative oral tradition that was **stable in gist and theme while variable in wording**. Dunn's "characteristic Jesus" method grades the *recurring, characteristic features* of the tradition (kingdom-proclamation, parables, aphorisms, table-fellowship, exorcism-repute) as historically grounded, while declining to authenticate individual sayings verbatim. In this KB it is one pole of the transmission question in CLM-0079: **relatively confident about gist**, paired against Allison (SRC-0128), who presses the frailty of memory harder.

**H-band of this source (prose only):** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction — an orality/memory model).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Dunn was among the leading NT scholars of his generation; *Jesus Remembered* is a standard reference for the transmission question.
- **Transparency:** High — the oral-transmission model and its assumptions are stated at length and argued against alternatives.
- **Independence:** Moderate-to-High — a Christian scholar, but the memory model deflates verbatim-authenticity confidence rather than inflating it; the model is adopted across confessional lines.
- **Intent:** Scholarly reconstruction of how the tradition formed and what that implies for historical access.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- The stability-in-gist model is a **model**: the actual first-generation transmission is not directly observable; the model is argued from orality studies and the synoptic data's pattern of fixity-plus-variation.
- "Characteristic Jesus" establishes broad contours, **not** any individual saying's wording.
- Allison (SRC-0128) argues Dunn under-weights the documented frailties of individual and collective memory — the pairing is load-bearing for CLM-0079's grade.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The tradition shows fixity in theme/structure with performance-variation in wording — what oral transmission predicts; verbatim ipsissima verba are generally beyond recovery.
- The "characteristic Jesus": features attested repeatedly across strands and forms (kingdom, parables, aphoristic style, ethical radicality) are the historically secure layer of the teaching tradition.
- Community and apostolic witness gave the tradition a fixed center rooted in Jesus himself (the point Allison contests at the level of memory reliability).
