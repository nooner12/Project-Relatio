# SRC-0079

# Olsson & Hansson 1995 — Action Potential-Like Activity Found in Fungal Mycelia Is Sensitive to Stimulation

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Stefan Olsson & Bill S. Hansson, "Action potential-like activity found in fungal mycelia is sensitive to stimulation," *Naturwissenschaften* 82(1):30–31 (1995). An early electrophysiological demonstration of **spontaneous, propagating electrical potential fluctuations ("action-potential-like" spikes) in fungal hyphae**, and that this activity **changes on external (chemical/physical) stimulation** — e.g. an *Armillaria* cord altering its spiking when its mycelium contacted nearby wood. **In this KB it grounds the descriptive datum of CLM-0061 independently of Adamatzky** — establishing that fungal electrical spiking is a real, decades-attested phenomenon, not an artifact peculiar to one lab.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Solid. Peer-reviewed primary electrophysiology in a reputable journal; Olsson is an established fungal biologist.
- **Transparency:** Good for its era; intracellular-style recording with a reference electrode in agar, quantitative parameters reported.
- **Independence:** High relative to the "fungal language" debate — this is a mechanistic 1995 datum with **no cognition/language framing**; it predates and is independent of Adamatzky's interpretation.
- **Intent:** To report and characterize the electrical phenomenon and its stimulus-sensitivity.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Establishes **the datum** (stimulus-sensitive electrical spiking exists in mycelium); does **not** establish that spikes **encode or transmit information as a "language"** (that is the contested interpretation graded far lower in CLM-0061).
- Reported spike parameters (see §4) are **orders of magnitude faster** than Adamatzky's later hour-scale "spikes," which is itself evidence that "fungal spiking" is not a single well-defined signal class — a caveat CLM-0061 must carry.
- Small-scale early recordings; measurement caveats about extracellular potentials apply (cf. SRC-0081, the FEMS review SRC-0090-class critique).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Spontaneous action-potential-like spikes in *Pleurotus ostreatus* and *Armillaria* hyphae: resting potential −70 to −100 mV; **spike amplitude ~5–50 mV; duration ~20–500 ms; frequency ~0.5–5 Hz** (per secondary summaries citing this paper — see §6 verification note).
- The pattern of electrical activity **changes in response to stimulation** (contact with wood/chemical cues), showing the signal is coupled to environmental events.

# 6. Verification (STD-0006 §5.7 / §7.5)
**Live-verified this session (moderate).** Existence, authorship, journal (*Naturwissenschaften* 82:30–31, 1995) and the core finding (stimulus-sensitive action-potential-like activity) confirmed via multiple independent secondary citations (biorxiv/PMC review papers citing it directly). The **specific numeric parameters (−70 to −100 mV; 5–50 mV; 20–500 ms; 0.5–5 Hz) were read from a secondary review's summary of this paper, not from the 1995 primary PDF this session** — flagged accordingly; they are consistent across the reviews that cite it.
