# SRC-0106

# Humbach 1991 — The Gathas of Zarathushtra

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Helmut Humbach, *The Gāthās of Zarathushtra and the Other Old Avestan Texts* (1991).

A standard critical edition, translation, and commentary of the Old Avestan corpus. Promoted to its own Source Record out of the embedded critical-edition list in SRC-0076 (the Zoroastrian primary corpus), because it does **contested** work rather than merely providing access: it is cited in support of the single-author reading of the Gathas, expressly against the alternative edition of Kellens & Pirart (SRC-0107).

**Corpus rule applied.** This record is the *edition*, not the *object*. The Gathas themselves are catalogued as part of SRC-0076. The two carry different evaluations: the object's trust question is authenticity and contemporaneity; this record's trust question is whether the scholarly rendering is current or superseded.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Humbach was a major Avestan philologist and this is a standard critical edition of an extremely difficult corpus.
- **Transparency:** High for its type. An edition presents text, translation, and commentary together, so its interpretive choices are visible and checkable — which is precisely what allows the disagreement with SRC-0107 to be located.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly work, external to the tradition.
- **Intent:** Philological — to establish and render the Old Avestan text. Not doctrinal, and not a defence of a religious position.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **An edition is a rendering, not the object.** Old Avestan is a small, archaic, and heavily contested corpus; translation involves substantial reconstruction, and Humbach's readings are one scholarly account among several.
- Its bearing on **single Gathic authorship** is interpretive, not demonstrative. The internal unity of dialect, meter, and voice on which such readings rest is evidence compatible with more than one explanation, and Kellens & Pirart (SRC-0107) read the same corpus to different effect. This record must not be used as if the authorship question were settled.
- **Currency:** published 1991. For an edition the live trust question is whether it has been superseded; that has not been assessed here and should be checked before load-bearing use.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Critical text, translation, and commentary of the Gathas (Yasna 28–34, 43–51, 53) and other Old Avestan material.
- The interpretive basis on which the corpus is read as the work of a single poet.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `derived_from` SRC-0076 — this edition is a scholarly rendering produced from the primary Avestan corpus catalogued there. (Directional by design: the object does not depend on the edition.)
- `contrasts_with` SRC-0107 — the two editions reach materially different conclusions on the Old Avestan text, including the single-authorship question. Reciprocated from SRC-0107.
