# SRC-0148

# Reynolds (2010) — The Qur'ān and Its Biblical Subtext

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

Gabriel Said Reynolds, *The Qur'ān and Its Biblical Subtext* (Routledge Studies in the Qur'an 10; London and New York: Routledge, 2010). A study of the Qur'an's **engagement with earlier Jewish and Christian narrative and scriptural material**, arguing — **recorded as the work's position, not as an INV-0018 finding** — that reading the Qur'an against that biblical and post-biblical subtext, rather than exclusively through the medieval exegetical tradition, changes how a series of Qur'anic passages are understood. Reynolds (University of Notre Dame) works at the Qur'anic–biblical intersection.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (d) — the Judeo-Christian context**, and is the base's most direct surface for **CLM B (i)** (shared narrative material with Jewish tradition) and **CLM C (i)** (Qur'anic engagement with Christian material).

**Discipline note.** Documented intertextual engagement is **evidence of engagement**. Whether it evidences **influence**, **descent**, or **neither** is exactly what INV-0018 must argue (CLM B (ii) / CLM C (ii)) under the **edge-restraint rule** — this record makes no such determination.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High — Reynolds is Professor of Islamic Studies and Theology at Notre Dame and edits work in this subfield; Routledge, in a peer-reviewed Qur'anic-studies series.
- **Transparency:** High — an explicit method (thirteen case studies read against identified antecedents) with its comparanda cited.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship.
- **Intent:** Methodological and interpretive — to argue for reading the Qur'an as a text in conversation with biblical literature, and against exclusive reliance on the later *tafsīr* tradition for its meaning.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **Intertextual relation is not a historical genealogy.** The work evidences textual engagement; it does **not** establish institutional or communal descent, and INV-0018 may not convert its findings into a descent edge (edge-restraint rule).
- **A contested method.** Reading the Qur'an "against" biblical antecedents is itself debated, including by scholars who hold that it under-weights the Qur'an's own compositional logic and its Arabian setting; the field's distribution must be reported, not assumed.
- Case-study in structure — its coverage is selective, not a systematic account of every Qur'anic biblical parallel.
- **Interior not read this session** (§6) — no page-level citation may be drawn from it at circuit.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- The biblical-subtext method and the argument about the interpretive weight of the medieval exegetical tradition (recorded from verification surfaces; **not consumed by any claim at cataloguing**).
