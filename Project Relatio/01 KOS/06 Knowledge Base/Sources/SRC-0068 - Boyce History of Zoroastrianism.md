# SRC-0068

# Boyce — A History of Zoroastrianism / Zoroastrians: Their Religious Beliefs and Practices

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Mary Boyce, *A History of Zoroastrianism*, vols. I–III (Leiden: Brill, Handbuch der Orientalistik, 1975–1991), and *Zoroastrians: Their Religious Beliefs and Practices* (Routledge & Kegan Paul, 1979). Boyce was the field's foremost 20th-century historian of Zoroastrianism. **In this KB it is the pro-influence / early-dating anchor:** Boyce argued that the Zoroastrian eschatological complex is genuinely archaic (rooted in the Gathic teaching, transmitted continuously by a conservative oral priesthood) and that post-exilic Judaism's eschatology was substantially shaped by it during the ~two centuries of Achaemenid rule.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction). Boyce's *early-dating* case is a reconstruction — its strength and its exposure are the same fact: it rests on the claim that the Pahlavi-era doctrines faithfully preserve archaic content transmitted orally, which is precisely what the skeptics (SRC-0067 Barr, SRC-0074 de Jong) contest.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high within Iranology — the standard history; command of the Avestan and Pahlavi corpora is unrivalled for its generation.
- **Transparency:** High on the texts; **lower on the inferential leap** — Boyce tends to read late (Pahlavi) systematic doctrine back into the archaic layer on the assumption of faithful oral transmission, and does not always mark where attestation is late.
- **Independence:** Scholarly, non-confessional; but she is the leading *proponent* of the continuity-and-influence reading, so her framing is not a neutral baseline — it is one pole of the dispute.
- **Intent:** To reconstruct a continuous Zoroastrian history and to argue its priority and influence.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Boyce's **retrojection of Pahlavi-era systematic eschatology onto the Gathic/Achaemenid layer is an assumption, not a demonstration** — it is the exact move the dating problem (CLM-0056) flags. Her confidence in continuous faithful oral transmission is contested.
- She establishes the *coherence and antiquity-claim* of the Zoroastrian system; she does not, on her own, close the directional-influence question against internal Jewish development.
- Exact edition/page references for individual eschatological claims were **not pinned this session** (see §6) — the argument is cited at the level of her well-known overall thesis.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The Zoroastrian eschatological complex (judgment, resurrection, renovation) is archaic and continuous, not a late Sasanian invention.
- The Achaemenid period furnished the sustained contact within which post-exilic Jewish eschatology developed under Zoroastrian influence.
