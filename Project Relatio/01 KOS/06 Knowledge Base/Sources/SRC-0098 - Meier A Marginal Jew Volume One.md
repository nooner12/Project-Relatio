# SRC-0098

# Meier — A Marginal Jew, Vol. 1 (1991)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
John P. Meier, *A Marginal Jew: Rethinking the Historical Jesus*, Volume 1: *The Roots of the Problem and the Person*, Anchor Bible Reference Library (Doubleday, 1991). The standard critical treatment of the **Josephan evidence**, and the most-cited modern reconstruction of the authentic core of the *Testimonium Flavianum* (Josephus, *Ant.* 18.63–64). In this KB it is the **secondary scholarly evaluation** of the non-Christian attestation — the discipline on what Josephus, once the Christian interpolations are removed, actually establishes.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) — a critical evaluation of the H-c external testimonia.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high — Meier (a Catholic priest and NT scholar) produced the field-standard multi-volume historical-Jesus study; his TF reconstruction is a reference point across confessions.
- **Transparency:** High — walks through each clause of the TF, marking secure Christian interpolations vs. plausible Josephan core, and states the methodological criteria.
- **Independence:** High — a Catholic scholar reaching a *partial-authenticity* verdict (rejecting the pious "fully authentic" reading) — cross-confessional discipline in action.
- **Intent:** To inform — a careful, criteria-driven reconstruction.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- The TF reconstruction is **itself a reconstruction (H-e)**: partial authenticity is the *majority* view, but the *fully-interpolated* (e.g. Olson, Carrier) and *substantially-authentic* positions both exist and cannot be excluded with certainty. The claim must carry that spread.
- Meier evaluates; he is not himself a first-century witness. What Josephus establishes independently is modest (existence, execution context, a following) — not the Gospel narrative.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The clause "He was the Christ" and the overtly confessional lines are near-universally judged **later Christian interpolations**; a neutral Josephan core plausibly remains.
- *Ant.* 20.200 (the incidental reference to "James, the brother of Jesus who was called Christ") is **more textually secure** than the TF and is the stronger Josephan datum — most scholars accept it as authentic.
- The partial-authenticity consensus is a *majority*, not a unanimity — a genuine minority holds the whole TF to be interpolated.

**Live-verified this session (strong for the consensus positions; moderate for exact citation).** The partial-authenticity consensus on the TF, the near-universal judgment that "he was the Christ" is interpolated, and the greater security of *Ant.* 20.200 were all confirmed against multiple independent scholarly summaries this session (incl. the Wikipedia "Josephus on Jesus" survey and Oxford Academic chapter listings). The specific volume (Vol. 1, ABRL, Doubleday, 1991, Meier) is standard; page-level citation not asserted. No fabrication.
