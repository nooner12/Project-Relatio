# SRC-0024

# Kwasnicka et al. 2016 — Theoretical Explanations for Maintenance of Behaviour Change (Systematic Review)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Systematic review of behaviour theories addressing **maintenance** (rather than initiation) of health-behaviour change: Kwasnicka, Dombrowski, White & Sniehotta (2016), *Health Psychology Review* 10(3):277–296. It synthesises 100+ theories and identifies the recurring theoretical mechanisms proposed to explain why behaviour change is or is not maintained. It is the organising framework for the "factors behind failure to maintain" half of INV-0006.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed systematic review in a leading health-psychology methodology journal, by prominent behaviour-change researchers.
- **Transparency:** High — systematic search and thematic synthesis reported.
- **Independence:** High — academic, non-commercial.
- **Intent:** Inform (theory synthesis to guide research and practice).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It is a synthesis of **theories**, not a meta-analysis of intervention effect sizes; it establishes *what mechanisms are proposed*, not *how large* each effect is.
- Its five-domain framework is a conceptual organisation, not a validated causal model with quantified weights.
- Says little directly about digital/commercial wellness formats specifically.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Behaviour-change interventions frequently produce **temporary** change; **maintenance is rarely attained** — the review's motivating premise (verified this session via journal/repository abstracts).
- Maintenance is theorised to rest on **five interrelated domains**: (1) **maintenance motives**, (2) **self-regulation**, (3) **habits**, (4) **psychological and physical resources**, and (5) **environmental and social influences**. (Verified this session against the Health Psychology Review abstract and the Stirling/Curtin repository records.)

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0021 (maintenance determinants differ from initiation determinants).
- `related_to` ENT-0004 (the intention-behaviour gap; self-regulation and habit are the constructs that bridge it).
