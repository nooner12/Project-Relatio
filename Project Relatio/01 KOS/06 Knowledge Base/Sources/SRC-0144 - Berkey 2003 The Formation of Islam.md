# SRC-0144

# Berkey (2003) — The Formation of Islam: Religion and Society in the Near East, 600–1800

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

Jonathan P. Berkey, *The Formation of Islam: Religion and Society in the Near East, 600–1800* (Themes in Islamic History 2; Cambridge and New York: Cambridge University Press, 2003). A **mainstream scholarly survey of Islam's formation**, distinctive for situating the tradition's emergence within the **continuous religious history of the late-antique Near East** rather than treating it as a discontinuous beginning — the framing that makes it directly useful for **CLM A (ii)** (what the formation context establishes) and for the relationship questions.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (a) — formation and early Islam (mainstream)**, as the survey-level complement to SRC-0143's argued monograph. **No claim is asserted and no evidence is graded at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High — Berkey is a historian of the medieval Islamic Near East; Cambridge University Press, in a commissioned thematic series (Themes in Islamic History). Reported on reference surfaces as the recipient of the Middle East Studies Association's Albert Hourani Book Award for 2003 — **recorded as reported, not independently confirmed.**
- **Transparency:** High — a synthetic survey with its historiographical positions stated and its scholarly literature cited.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship.
- **Intent:** Synthesis and framing — to present Islam's formation as a long process embedded in Near Eastern religious history, addressed to students and non-specialists as well as scholars.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **A survey, not a primary investigation** of Islam's origins; its early chapters compress questions that SRC-0143, SRC-0145, SRC-0146, SRC-0147 and SRC-0150 treat at length.
- Its chronological range (600–1800) extends far beyond INV-0018's scope; only the formation-period material is relevant, and the later material is **out of scope** (INV-0018 §2.6).
- It is **not** a source on the Qur'anic text's history, nor on the tradition's self-understanding (SRC-0151).
- **Interior not read this session** (§6) — no page-level citation may be drawn from it at circuit.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
