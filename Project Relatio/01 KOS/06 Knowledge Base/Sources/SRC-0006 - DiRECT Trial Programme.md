# SRC-0006

# DiRECT Trial Programme (Diabetes Remission Clinical Trial)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

The **DiRECT** (Diabetes Remission Clinical Trial) programme: a cluster-randomised, open-label RCT in UK primary care testing a structured weight-management intervention (total-diet-replacement low-calorie phase, then stepped food reintroduction and weight-loss maintenance) for **remission of type 2 diabetes**.

Verified this session:
- **Primary (12-month) — Lean et al., *The Lancet*, 2018.** DOI 10.1016/S0140-6736(17)33102-1. Cluster RCT, **306 participants** (149 per group, intention-to-treat), adults with T2D diagnosed **within the past 6 years**, not on insulin, BMI 27–45.
  - **Remission at 12 months: 46% (68/149) intervention vs 4% (6/149) control** (OR 19.7, 95% CI 7.8–49.8).
  - Mean weight change **−10.0 kg** (intervention) vs −1.0 kg (control); **24% (36/149)** lost ≥15 kg.
  - **Dose–response of remission by weight lost:** weight gain 0% (0/76); 0–5 kg 7% (6/89); 5–10 kg 34% (19/56); 10–15 kg 57% (16/28); **≥15 kg 86% (31/36)**.
  - Remission defined as **HbA1c <6.5% (<48 mmol/mol) after ≥2 months off all antidiabetic medications**.
- **2-year — Lean et al., *Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*, 2019.** DOI 10.1016/S2213-8587(19)30068-3. Remission sustained in **36%**; mean weight loss **7.6 kg**.
- **5-year extension — Lean et al., *Lancet Diabetes & Endocrinology*, 2024.** DOI 10.1016/S2213-8587(23)00385-6. Remission at 5 years **10% (12/118) intervention vs 5% (5/93) control**; mean weight loss 5.6 vs 4.6 kg; those still in remission had averaged **8.9 kg** loss.
- **Predictors — Thom et al., *Diabetic Medicine*, 2021.** DOI 10.1111/dme.14395. **Weight loss was the strongest predictor** (adjusted OR ≈1.24 per kg at 12 months). Within this trial, **baseline BMI, fasting insulin, fasting C-peptide, and diabetes duration did NOT independently predict remission** — but note the enrolment cap of ≤6 years' duration limits the range over which duration could be tested. Remission depended on retained beta-cell function.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** Very high — landmark randomised controlled trial, *Lancet* family, investigators are leading diabetes researchers (Lean, Taylor).
- **Transparency:** High — pre-registered, protocol published, ITT analysis, funder (Diabetes UK) declared.
- **Independence:** High — publicly/charity funded; not a product-marketing study. The dose–response and 5-year relapse data are reported candidly (including the decline).
- **Intent:** Inform — to test whether primary-care-delivered remission is feasible.

---

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **Does not establish durability beyond ~5 years**, nor that remission is permanent; the 5-year data in fact show substantial relapse tracking weight regain.
- Population was **early-disease (≤6 years), non-insulin-treated, with obesity** — it does **not** directly establish outcomes for someone on metformin "for many years" or with long duration.
- Open-label; behavioural intervention cannot be blinded.
- Does not isolate a single mechanism (see SRC-0007 for mechanism).
- Its within-trial null for duration as a predictor must not be over-read as "duration doesn't matter" — the range tested was narrow (see CLM-0009).
