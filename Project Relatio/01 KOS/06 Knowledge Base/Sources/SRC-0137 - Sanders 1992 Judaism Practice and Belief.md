# SRC-0137

# Sanders (1992) — Judaism: Practice and Belief, 63 BCE–66 CE

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
E. P. Sanders, *Judaism: Practice and Belief, 63 BCE–66 CE* (London: SCM Press / Philadelphia: Trinity Press International, 1992). A standard scholarly treatment of Second Temple Judaism in its late-period bounds, best known for the **"common Judaism"** thesis — the argument that beneath the sectarian plurality (Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, and others) there was a broadly shared practice and belief centred on Temple, Torah, and the God of Israel. Catalogued here as candidate coverage for **INV-0017** on **dating and characterising Second Temple Judaism as a tradition** (the hub of the Judaism–Christianity fork): the work supplies both the periodisation (its titular 63 BCE–66 CE frame) and the internal-plurality picture the investigation's hub claim will need. **No claim is asserted from it at cataloguing** (ADR-GOV-0003 pattern).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Sanders is a foundational figure in late-twentieth-century study of Second Temple Judaism and Christian origins; SCM Press / Trinity Press International are standard academic imprints; the book is a widely cited reference on the period.
- **Transparency:** High — an argued, source-referenced historical synthesis.
- **Independence:** High — outsider critical scholarship, not a confessional treatment.
- **Intent:** Historical description of Jewish practice and belief in the late Second Temple period, with an explicit corrective aim (against portrayals of the period as legalistic or fragmented).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **The "common Judaism" thesis is itself contested** — later scholarship debates how much unity the evidence supports; the source represents one influential position, not a settled consensus, and INV-0017 must weigh it as such.
- Its frame closes at **66 CE** (the eve of the First Revolt); it does not carry the analysis through the post-70 rabbinic transition (SRC-0138 and SRC-0142 cover that).
- Not a source on the Christian side of the fork.
- **Interior not read this session** (see §6) — no page-level citation is asserted.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Periodisation of late Second Temple Judaism (63 BCE–66 CE) and the "common Judaism" characterisation of shared practice beneath sectarian variety (recorded from verification surfaces; not consumed by any claim at cataloguing).
