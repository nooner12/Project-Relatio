# SRC-0014

# Goyal et al. (2014) — Meditation Programs for Psychological Stress and Well-Being (JAMA Internal Medicine)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
AHRQ-commissioned systematic review and meta-analysis, published in *JAMA Internal Medicine*, of meditation programs (mindfulness and mantra) for psychological stress and well-being. **The most methodologically demanding synthesis in this area**, because it restricted attention to RCTs and paid particular attention to **active control comparisons**. Full text (PMC4142584) fetched and read this session.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — AHRQ evidence report, JAMA Intern Med, comprehensive search (17,801 citations screened).
- **Transparency:** Very High — GRADE-style strength-of-evidence ratings; explicit about comparator type.
- **Independence:** Very High — government-commissioned, no product interest; **anti-allegiance** in tone.
- **Intent:** Inform (calibrate the meditation evidence base).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Does **not** establish that meditation is ineffective — it establishes **small-to-moderate** effects vs non-specific controls and **no proven superiority over active controls**.
- Clinical-population focus (diverse adult clinical samples); not a pure healthy-worker stress trial.
- Follow-up to 3–6 months; does not establish >1-year durability.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used (verified against full text this session)
- **47 RCTs, 3,320 participants.**
- **Mindfulness — anxiety:** effect size **0.38 (95% CI 0.12–0.64) at 8 weeks**, **0.22 (0.02–0.43) at 3–6 months**.
- **Mindfulness — depression:** **0.30 (0.00–0.59) at 8 weeks**, **0.23 (0.05–0.42) at 3–6 months**.
- **Stress/distress, positive mood, well-being:** low or insufficient evidence of benefit.
- **Crucial for durability/honesty:** when compared with **active controls (exercise, progressive muscle relaxation, CBT), meditation showed no significant advantage.** *The effect shrinks toward the comparator once you control for expectancy and attention.*

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0013 (the active-control shrinkage and the effect-decay from 8 weeks to 3–6 months are the load-bearing findings).
- `contrasts_with` the pre-post-inflated MBSR literature (SRC-0012); `related_to` SRC-0013.
