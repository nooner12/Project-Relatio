# SRC-0109

# Kent 1953 — Old Persian: Grammar, Texts, Lexicon

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Roland G. Kent, *Old Persian: Grammar, Texts, Lexicon*, 2nd edition (1953).

The long-standard English-language edition, grammar, and lexicon of the Old Persian corpus, including the Behistun inscription (SRC-0108). It is the vehicle through which the Old Persian texts are conventionally cited in Anglophone scholarship.

**Corpus rule applied.** This record is the *edition*; the inscription itself is SRC-0108. The distinct evaluation matters here more than usual — see §3.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High for its period and still substantial. Kent's volume was the standard reference for decades and remains widely cited.
- **Transparency:** High. Grammar, texts, and lexicon are presented together with apparatus, so readings can be traced and checked.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly philology, external to the material's originating context.
- **Intent:** Philological reference — to make the Old Persian corpus accessible and analysable.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Currency is the live trust question, and it is a real one.** This is a 1953 second edition. Old Persian studies have advanced considerably since — later editions, re-readings, and newly published material exist — so Kent should be treated as a *standard but dated* reference rather than the current critical text. This is precisely the edition-side risk that keeping the edition separate from the object is meant to expose: SRC-0108's authenticity is not in question, but the rendering here may be superseded in specific readings.
- An edition **cannot confer more authority than the object has**, and cannot repair the object's limitations: Kent's philological reliability does nothing to make Darius's royal narrative independent or disinterested.
- It is a corpus edition, not a historical study; it does not adjudicate the historical or religious questions the inscriptions raise.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Critical text, transliteration, translation, and lexicon of the Old Persian corpus, including DB (Behistun).

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
