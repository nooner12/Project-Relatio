# SRC-0020

# Physical Activity, Cortisol & Stress Literature (Cluster)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
A **cluster** of systematic reviews and meta-analyses on physical activity/exercise and stress physiology (cortisol regulation, blunted cortisol stress reactivity) and on exercise as a stress-management route, treated as one Source record because no single trial is load-bearing; the *convergence* is. (Precedent: SRC-0011 in INV-0004.)

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Moderate-High — multiple peer-reviewed syntheses; exercise-mental-health is a large, mature literature.
- **Transparency:** Mixed — pooled from several reviews read at abstract/summary level.
- **Independence:** High — non-commercial.
- **Intent:** Inform.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **No single verified pooled effect size for a "chronic stress" outcome was pinned this session** — the cluster establishes *direction and convergence*, not a precise durable stress effect size. Graded down accordingly (KOS-0003 §12.1).
- Cortisol reactivity is a **biomarker**, not lived stress; its clinical meaning is debated.
- Reverse causation (less-stressed people exercise more) is not fully excluded in observational strands.
- Durability of stress-specific benefit rests partly on the collateral-health argument (see CLM-0014), which is an inference, not a direct >1-year stress-outcome RCT.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Physical activity is associated with **blunted (favourable) cortisol stress reactivity**; a majority of studies link higher fitness/activity to attenuated cortisol responses.
- Exercise is a **named effective individual-level route** in the Cochrane review (SRC-0016).
- Exercise's **broad, durable physical-health benefits** (cardiometabolic, sleep) are among the most robust findings in medicine and are the strongest basis for treating it as a *durable* foundation (independent of the stress-specific durability question).
