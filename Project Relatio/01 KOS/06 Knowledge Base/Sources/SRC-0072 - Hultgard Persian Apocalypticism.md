# SRC-0072

# Hultgård — Persian Apocalypticism / Defeating Death

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Anders Hultgård, "Persian Apocalypticism," in J. J. Collins, B. McGinn & S. Stein (eds.), *The Encyclopedia of Apocalypticism*, vol. 1 (Continuum, 1998): 39–83; and "Defeating Death: Eschatology in Zoroastrianism, Judaism and Christianity," in J. Rubanovich & G. Herman (eds.), *Irano-Judaica VII* (Ben-Zvi Institute, 2019): 23–72. Hultgård is a specialist in *Iranian* eschatology working on the comparative question. **In this KB it is the specialist Iranian-eschatology anchor.** His characteristic thesis is nuanced: the Zoroastrian apocalyptic-eschatological traditions in the late Pahlavi texts carry *earlier* material (visible already in the Avesta), and the encounter with Iranian religion supplied the *stimulus* that let ideas already latent in Judaism develop fully — influence as catalyst, not as wholesale transfer.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) by an Iranologist — his reconstruction of the *antiquity* of the Pahlavi material is the load-bearing (and contested) inference.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — a leading specialist on Iranian eschatology and its comparative reach.
- **Transparency:** High — explicit that the systematic corpus is late-attested and that antiquity must be argued; frames influence as stimulus rather than adoption.
- **Independence:** Moderately pro-influence but methodologically careful; occupies a position between Boyce/Cohn and Barr.
- **Intent:** To reconstruct Iranian apocalyptic/eschatology and assess its comparative role.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- His inference that the late Pahlavi eschatology preserves pre-Achaemenid material is a **reconstruction** exposed to the same dating critique — it strengthens but does not close the priority question.
- "Stimulus/catalyst" is deliberately weaker than "source" — it does not license a strong directional claim; it is compatible with substantial internal development.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The Pahlavi eschatological traditions carry earlier (Avestan-rooted) material; the Avesta already shows eschatological elements (e.g., Yasht 19).
- Iranian religion supplied the *stimulus* for the full development of eschatological ideas latent in Judaism, rather than a wholesale handover.
