# SRC-0065

# Moffitt & Caspi 2001 — Childhood Predictors of Life-Course-Persistent and Adolescence-Limited Antisocial Pathways (Males and Females)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Moffitt, T. E., & Caspi, A. (2001), "Childhood predictors differentiate life-course persistent and adolescence-limited antisocial pathways among males and females," *Development and Psychopathology* 13(2):355–375 (PubMed 11393651). Dunedin longitudinal birth cohort. Load-bearing fact for INV-0010: the **sex ratio depends on onset timing** — approximately **10:1 male:female for childhood-onset (life-course-persistent) antisocial behavior** but only about **1.5:1 for adolescence-onset** antisocial behavior. Onset timing is thus itself a locus of sex/gender divergence. Citation and the two ratios verified this session via PubMed 11393651 and the associated Dunedin publications.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Construct-provenance code: `UNRESOLVED`.** Groups are compared by recorded **sex category** on behavioral/delinquency measures; no biological mechanism is isolated and no gender-socialization construct is measured. The large early-onset male preponderance is described, but the paper gives **no basis to determine whether it is neurodevelopmental, socialization-driven, or ascertainment-influenced** (the authors themselves note the taxonomy fits girls less cleanly and that girls' conduct problems may be under-detected). The construct is undetermined from the paper as written — `UNRESOLVED`, not `CONFLATED` (which would presuppose the data is specifically *gendered*). It **can support only a gender-patterned (or unattributed) onset-timing divergence claim** (operative rule §3.1 — `UNRESOLVED` is constrained identically to `CONFLATED`). Justification: sex-labeled behavioral trajectories, mechanism undetermined per the source's own cautions.
- **Authority:** High — one of the most influential developmental-psychopathology cohorts and frameworks (Moffitt's taxonomy).
- **Transparency:** High — reports the childhood-onset vs adolescence-onset sex ratios directly.
- **Independence:** High relative to the other domains — a distinct cohort and program.
- **Intent:** Test whether childhood predictors distinguish the two antisocial pathways in both sexes.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Single cohort** (Dunedin, New Zealand); the exact 10:1 / 1.5:1 figures are cohort-specific, though the *direction* (early-onset far more male) replicates broadly.
- **No mechanism.** Does not isolate whether the early-onset male preponderance is neurodevelopmental, socialization-driven, or ascertainment-influenced (girls' conduct problems may be under-detected).
- **Taxonomy contested for girls.** The authors and later work note the two-trajectory model fits girls less cleanly — a caution against reading the male-derived model as a sex-general law.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Childhood-onset (life-course-persistent) antisocial behavior: **~10:1 male:female**.
- Adolescence-onset antisocial behavior: **~1.5:1 male:female** — the sex gap largely closes when onset is later.
- The sex divergence is therefore concentrated in **early onset**, making onset timing a divergence locus in its own right.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0049 (the conduct/externalizing sex divergence is concentrated in childhood-onset; the gap narrows for adolescent-onset).
- `part_of` INV-0010.
