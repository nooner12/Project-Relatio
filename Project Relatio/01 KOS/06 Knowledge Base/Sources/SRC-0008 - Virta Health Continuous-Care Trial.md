# SRC-0008

# Virta Health Continuous-Care Trial (Nutritional Ketosis)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

A **non-randomised, controlled** open-label study of a continuous remote-care model delivering **carbohydrate-restricted nutrition with nutritional ketosis** (target blood β-hydroxybutyrate 0.5–3.0 mM) plus a remote coach + medical provider, versus usual care, for adults with type 2 diabetes and overweight/obesity.

Verified this session:
- **Design:** **349 adults** — 262 continuous-care intervention (CCI) vs 87 usual care (UC); ~90% on diabetes medication at baseline. **Not randomised** (participants self-selected arm).
- **1-year — Hallberg et al., *Diabetes Therapy*, 2018** (PubMed 29417495). Improved HbA1c, weight, and reduced diabetes-medication use. *(Exact 1-year reversal/remission percentages were NOT re-verified to the primary this session — see Limitations.)*
- **2-year — Athinarayanan et al., *Frontiers in Endocrinology*, 2019** (DOI 10.3389/fendo.2019.00348), verified this session:
  - HbA1c: **−0.9% absolute** in CCI vs +0.4% in UC.
  - Weight: **−11.9 kg (~10%)** in CCI vs no change in UC.
  - **Diabetes "reversal": 53.5%** of CCI; **remission (partial or complete): 17.6%** of CCI vs 2.4% UC.
  - Medication (excluding metformin): use fell from 55.7% → 26.8% of CCI; **insulin dose reduced ~62%** among users; **sulfonylureas eliminated (100%)** among CCI users.

---

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** Moderate–high — peer-reviewed, but authors are affiliated with / the study was conducted by **Virta Health**, a commercial provider of the intervention (a conflict-of-interest and intent flag).
- **Transparency:** High — endpoints, ketosis biomarkers, and medication changes reported in detail; the Bonferroni-corrected p-values are stated.
- **Independence:** **Low** — the study evaluates the sponsor's own commercial care model; participants self-selected (selection bias). This is the key weakness.
- **Intent:** Inform **and** (implicitly) support a commercial product — held against the results per KOS-0003 §6 (intent informs interpretation, does not invalidate).

---

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **Not randomised**; self-selection means motivated participants populate the intervention arm — it does **not** establish causal superiority as cleanly as an RCT.
- **Commercial sponsorship** (Virta) is a material COI.
- "Reversal" (53.5%) is a **weaker endpoint than consensus remission** (17.6%); the two must not be conflated (this is why CLM-0010 separates them).
- **Metformin was excluded** from the medication-reduction figures — directly relevant to the scenario: many "reversal" participants may have *remained on metformin*.
- Long-term (5-year extension exists) durability and adherence attrition are concerns typical of restrictive diets.
