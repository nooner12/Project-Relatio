# SRC-0107

# Kellens and Pirart 1988-91 — Les textes vieil-avestiques

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Jean Kellens & Eric Pirart, *Les textes vieil-avestiques* (1988–1991).

A critical edition of the Old Avestan corpus representing a distinct — and markedly more sceptical — philological school than Humbach's (SRC-0106). It is cited in the Iranian-family source base specifically as the *contra* position on the single-authorship reading of the Gathas.

**Corpus rule applied.** This record is the *edition*, not the *object*; the Old Avestan corpus itself is catalogued in SRC-0076.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Kellens is a leading figure of the Franco-Belgian Avestan school; this is a serious critical edition of the corpus.
- **Transparency:** High for its type — text and analysis are presented such that the school's characteristic readings can be located and contested.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly work, external to the tradition. Notably independent *of the dominant Anglophone reading*, which is part of its evidential value: it is not a restatement of the same interpretive tradition as SRC-0106.
- **Intent:** Philological — to establish the Old Avestan text on the school's own methodological principles.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Like any edition, it is a **rendering, not the object**, and Old Avestan is contested enough that its readings are positions rather than results.
- Its scepticism about single Gathic authorship is a **live position in an open dispute**, not a demonstrated finding. Citing it as *contra* Humbach establishes that the question is contested; it does not establish that the sceptical side is correct.
- It represents a **particular school** with characteristic methodological commitments; those commitments should be made explicit rather than treated as neutral philology.
- **Currency:** published 1988–91; whether it has been superseded has not been assessed here.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Critical text and analysis of the Old Avestan corpus.
- The basis for contesting single authorship of the Gathas.
