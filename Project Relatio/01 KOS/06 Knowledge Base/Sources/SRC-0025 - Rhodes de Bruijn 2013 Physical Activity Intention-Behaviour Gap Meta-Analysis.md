# SRC-0025

# Rhodes & de Bruijn 2013 — How Big Is the Physical-Activity Intention–Behaviour Gap? (Meta-Analysis)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Meta-analysis quantifying the gap between physical-activity **intention** and **behaviour** using the action-control framework: Rhodes & de Bruijn (2013), *British Journal of Health Psychology* 18(2):296–309. 10 studies, N ≈ 3,899. It is the empirical anchor for the "intention alone does not produce behaviour" factor and grounds ENT-0004.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed meta-analysis by a leading physical-activity behaviour researcher.
- **Transparency:** High — meta-analytic method reported; small study pool (k=10) disclosed.
- **Independence:** High — academic.
- **Intent:** Inform.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Domain-specific:** physical activity only; the *magnitude* of the gap may differ for diet, meditation, sleep, etc. (though the qualitative phenomenon generalises).
- Small number of primary studies (k=10) — a real precision limitation.
- It is **cross-sectional/prospective correlational** in structure; it describes the gap, it does not by itself prove a causal remedy.
- The exact percentage split is restated inconsistently across the secondary literature (see §4) — the robust point is directional, not the precise number.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The intention-behaviour discordance is driven **almost entirely by intenders who fail to act**, not by non-intenders who act: **non-intenders were largely inactive (~97–98% correspondence)**, whereas roughly **half of intenders failed to translate intention into activity** (restated across sources as ~46–54% of intenders unsuccessful). *(Verified this session via the Wiley abstract record and multiple secondary restatements; the precise intender-failure percentage varies by restatement — treated as "approximately half," not asserted to a single decimal, per KOS-0003 §12.1.)*
- The overall intention–behaviour gap is commonly summarised as ≈46–48%.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0019 (the intention-behaviour gap is the central failure point).
- `defines` ENT-0004 (the intention-behaviour gap construct).
