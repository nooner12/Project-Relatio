# SRC-0140

# Dunn — The Partings of the Ways

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
James D. G. Dunn, *The Partings of the Ways: Between Christianity and Judaism and Their Significance for the Character of Christianity* (London: SCM Press, 1991; 2nd ed. 2006). The **classic articulation of the "partings" model** — that Christianity and Judaism pulled apart over identifiable first-century (and shortly-after) disputes, organised around "four pillars" of Second Temple Judaism (monotheism, election/land, Torah, Temple). It names the very framework — *partings of the ways* — that the later revisionist literature (SRC-0139 Boyarin; SRC-0141 Becker & Reed) was written to contest, which makes it the **opposite pole** of INV-0017's rival-reading axis. Catalogued for INV-0017 (ADR-GOV-0003 pattern); **no claim asserted at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Dunn was a major New Testament and Christian-origins scholar; SCM Press / Trinity Press International; the work is a standard, widely cited reference on the emergence of Christian distinctiveness.
- **Transparency:** High — a structured historical argument (surveying the question since F. C. Baur, then working through the "four pillars").
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship, though written from within New Testament studies.
- **Intent:** To explain how and when Christianity became distinct from Judaism, and what that separation implies for Christianity's character.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Represents the "partings" pole that is itself contested** — the "ways that never (cleanly) parted" reading (SRC-0141) and Boyarin's "late/constructed" reading (SRC-0139) are direct scholarly rejoinders; INV-0017 must adjudicate, not adopt.
- Written from the New Testament / Christian-origins side; on the internal development of rabbinic Judaism the Area 1/3 sources are better authorities.
- Two editions (1991 / 2006) with revision; the edition should be named at circuit.
- **Interior not read this session** (see §6).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The "partings of the ways" framing and the "four pillars" analysis of Second Temple Judaism against which Christian distinctiveness is measured (recorded from verification surfaces; not consumed by any claim at cataloguing).
