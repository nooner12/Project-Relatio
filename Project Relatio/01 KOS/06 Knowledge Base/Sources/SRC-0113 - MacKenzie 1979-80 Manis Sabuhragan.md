# SRC-0113

# MacKenzie 1979-80 — "Mani's Sabuhragan"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
D. N. MacKenzie, "Mani's *Šābuhragān*," *Bulletin of the School of Oriental and African Studies* 42/3 (1979), 500–534, and 43/2 (1980), 288–310.

A two-part edition, transliteration, and translation of the surviving Middle Persian fragments of the *Šābuhragān* — the work Mani is held to have composed for the Sasanian king Shapur I. The fragments come from the Turfan finds.

This record is a **scholarly edition of primary fragments**, catalogued as Secondary. The fragments themselves are not separately catalogued here: the source base names no distinct object record for them, and inventing one to force an object/edition split would violate the corpus rule's own constraint.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. MacKenzie was a major Middle Iranian philologist, and this is the standard edition of the material, published in a peer-reviewed journal of record.
- **Transparency:** High. Text, transliteration, translation, and notes are presented together, so the reader can see what survives and what is restored.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly philology, external to Manichaeism.
- **Intent:** Philological — to edit and render what survives of the work.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- The Turfan material consists of **fragmentary later copies, not autographs**. That a founder-authored text is directly extant is a real and unusual evidential strength, but "extant" here means fragments of copies, and the edition establishes what survives, not the complete work or its original form.
- It establishes the *Šābuhragān*'s **existence and partial content**; the tradition that Mani composed it *for Shapur I* is a further claim that the fragments alone do not fully carry.
- Being an edition, it settles wording, not historical interpretation.
- Published 1979–80; currency of specific readings has not been assessed here.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The surviving Middle Persian text of the *Šābuhragān* with translation.
- Evidence bearing on Mani's authorship of his own scriptures.
