# SRC-0089

# Money 2021 — "Hyphal and Mycelial Consciousness: The Concept of the Fungal Mind"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Nicholas P. Money (Miami University), "Hyphal and mycelial consciousness: the concept of the fungal mind," *Fungal Biology* 125(4):257–259 (2021). A short opinion piece arguing that hyphal sensitivity, decision-like developmental switching, and spatial recognition/short-term memory are "expressions of cellular consciousness," inviting a graded notion of consciousness "from apes to amoebas." **In this KB it is a provocative enthusiast-pole voice used with care** — valuable for stating the strong reading of the fungal capacities, explicitly flagged as speculative opinion, not evidence.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Money is a respected mycologist and science writer; but this is an **opinion/perspective piece**, not primary research or a systematic review.
- **Transparency:** High that it is a concept piece; low on new evidence.
- **Independence:** Openly on the "fungal mind" enthusiast side; a motivated-reasoning vector to name — it is rhetoric-forward.
- **Intent:** To provoke reconsideration of consciousness as a graded property including fungi.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Establishes **nothing empirically**; it re-describes known sensitivities (exocytosis-driven form change, thigmotropism, wound repair) in consciousness vocabulary. It **must not** be used to lift any confidence level — it is a pole marker, not evidence.
- The "consciousness" usage is exactly the register slide (§3.1) the investigation guards against.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Hyphal environmental sensitivity, decision-like developmental switching, spatial recognition and short-term memory are cast as "cellular consciousness"; consciousness proposed as a continuum "from apes to amoebas."
- Used to state the enthusiast reading fairly (steelman) while keeping it quarantined from the grading.
