# SRC-0030

# Teixeira et al. 2012 — Exercise, Physical Activity, and Self-Determination Theory (Systematic Review)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Systematic review of Self-Determination Theory (SDT) applied to exercise/physical-activity behaviour: Teixeira, Carraça, Markland, Silva & Ryan (2012), *International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity* 9:78. 66 studies (experimental, cross-sectional, prospective). It is the evidence base for **motivation quality** — autonomous vs controlled motivation — as a determinant of sustained activity.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed systematic review co-authored by SDT co-founder Richard Ryan in a leading behavioural-nutrition/activity journal.
- **Transparency:** High — study inclusion and pattern of relations reported.
- **Independence:** Moderate–High — co-authorship by SDT originators is a theoretical-investment consideration; offset by a large multi-lab evidence base and the review's own noting of measurement heterogeneity.
- **Intent:** Inform.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Much of the evidence is **cross-sectional/correlational**; the autonomous-motivation→behaviour link is well-patterned but not uniformly from RCTs.
- It is a **narrative/vote-count style systematic review**, not a pooled meta-analysis with a single effect size.
- Domain is exercise/activity; extension to diet, meditation, sleep is plausible but an inference.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- A **consistent pattern of positive relations between autonomous motivation (identified and intrinsic regulation) and exercise behaviour**; good evidence for the value of SDT — especially autonomy-supportive contexts and need satisfaction — in fostering physical activity. *(Verified this session via PubMed 22726453 / PMC-3441783 abstract.)*
- Implication carried into INV-0006: interventions that build **autonomous** motivation (autonomy support) are associated with better sustained behaviour than those relying on **controlled/external** motivation.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0020 (motivation quality / self-efficacy predict maintenance; autonomy support is the corresponding intervention lever).
