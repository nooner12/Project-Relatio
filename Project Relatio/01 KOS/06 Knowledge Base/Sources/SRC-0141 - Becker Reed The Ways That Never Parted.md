# Becker & Reed (eds.) — The Ways That Never Parted

# 1. Identification
Adam H. Becker & Annette Yoshiko Reed (eds.), *The Ways That Never Parted: Jews and Christians in Late Antiquity and the Early Middle Ages* (Texts and Studies in Ancient Judaism 95; Tübingen: Mohr Siebeck, 2003; paperback reprint Minneapolis: Fortress Press, 2007). An **edited volume of seventeen essays** (with forewords by Martin Goodman, Simon Price, and Peter Schäfer) growing out of a Princeton–Oxford research partnership; its title deliberately answers Dunn's *Partings* (SRC-0140). The collective thesis: Jewish–Christian separation was **later, more gradual, more uneven, and more locally variable** than the "parting of the ways" model implies — with continued interaction from the Bar Kokhba revolt to the rise of Islam. Directly load-bearing for **INV-0017's descent edges and rival-reading element**. Catalogued for INV-0017 (ADR-GOV-0003 pattern); **no claim asserted at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Mohr Siebeck's TSAJ series (peer-reviewed); editors and contributors are established specialists; the volume is a standard reference for the revisionist position.
- **Transparency:** High for an edited collection — an introduction ("Traditional Models and New Directions") states the shared programme; individual essays argue independently.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship, Jewish and Christian contributors.
- **Intent:** Revisionist — to problematise the "parting of the ways" model and foreground continued contact.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **An edited volume, not a single unified argument** — its essays vary in scope and strength; "the book's view" is a shared orientation, not a monograph's thesis. Cite the specific essay at circuit.
- Represents **one pole** of the debate; SRC-0140 (Dunn) is the position it contests, and INV-0017 must weigh both.
- Its centre of gravity is late antiquity and the early middle ages — later than the emergence-dating of Second Temple Judaism (Area 1).
- **Interior not read this session** (see §6).
