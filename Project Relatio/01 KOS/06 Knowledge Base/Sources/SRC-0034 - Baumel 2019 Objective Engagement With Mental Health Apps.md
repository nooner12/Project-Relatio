# SRC-0034

# Baumel et al. 2019 — Objective User Engagement With Mental Health Apps (Panel-Based Usage Analysis)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Panel-based analysis of **objective** real-world engagement with mental-health and well-being apps, using actual usage data rather than self-report or trial adherence: Baumel, Muench, Edan & Kane (2019), *Journal of Medical Internet Research* 21(9):e14567. 93 apps met inclusion criteria (Android apps with ≥10,000 installs targeting anxiety, depression, or emotional well-being). It is the catalogued **modern, empirical** attrition source corroborating CLM-0022 (Eysenbach 2005's "law of attrition" restated with 2019 objective magnitudes).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed in the leading digital-health journal; a widely-cited engagement benchmark.
- **Transparency:** High — objective retention/open-rate metrics with interquartile ranges reported.
- **Independence:** High — academic; objective third-party usage panel (not vendor self-report), which is a strength against adherence over-reporting.
- **Intent:** Inform (benchmark real-world engagement).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Domain is **mental-health / well-being apps**, a subset of the broader wellness-tool ecosystem; magnitudes for fitness/nutrition apps may differ.
- It measures **engagement/retention**, not clinical outcome; low retention does not itself prove the content is ineffective when used.
- Publicly-listed consumer apps, not necessarily evidence-based or trial-grade programmes.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Real-world retention was **very low and front-loaded**: median **15-day retention ≈ 3.9%** (IQR 10.3%) and median **30-day retention ≈ 3.3%** (IQR 6.2%); median daily open rate ≈ 4.0%. *(Verified this session via JMIR/PMC-6785720; reported with IQRs as a single-study benchmark, not a universal rate.)*
- Confirms that steep early disengagement is **characteristic of self-directed digital tools in the current app ecosystem**, consistent with — and updating — Eysenbach 2005. The wide IQRs show magnitude is study/app-specific, so no single universal attrition rate is asserted.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0022 (modern, objective corroboration that early attrition in self-directed/digital wellness tools is the baseline expectation, not the exception).
