# SRC-0055

# Rogoff, Paradise, Mejía-Arauz, Correa-Chávez & Angelillo 2003 — Firsthand Learning Through Intent Participation

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Rogoff, B., Paradise, R., Mejía-Arauz, R., Correa-Chávez, M., & Angelillo, C. (2003), "Firsthand learning through intent participation," *Annual Review of Psychology* 54:175–203. A **synthetic review** contrasting two traditions of organizing learning: **intent participation** (learners actively observe and "listen in" while participating in shared, ongoing endeavors, common in many communities where children are included in mature activity) versus **assembly-line instruction** (learning organized as a separate activity, transmitted by specialists outside productive work). Compares the two on participation structure, roles of more/less experienced people, motivation, sources of learning, and communication. Citation (Annu. Rev. Psychol. 54:175–203) verified this session via PubMed (12499516) and Annual Reviews.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — a peer-reviewed *Annual Review* synthesis by the core research group; heavily cited, foundational to the field.
- **Transparency:** High — explicit about contrasting two ideal-typical traditions and about the cultural embedding of each.
- **Independence:** Moderate — same research program that advances the construct; synthesises many independent primary studies.
- **Intent:** Articulate intent participation as a coherent, evidenced alternative to instruction-as-separate-activity.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- A **review/theoretical synthesis**, not a primary study: it marshals and interprets evidence rather than reporting a controlled test; no pooled effect size.
- The **two-traditions contrast is ideal-typical** — real communities mix both; the sharp dichotomy is a heuristic, and reading it as "instruction vs. participation, pick one" over-reads it (romanticization risk, CLM-0042/0043).
- Evidence is drawn overwhelmingly from communities where intent participation is **embedded in community structure** — it does not isolate participation from that structure.
- **Funding:** not stated/verified this session.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The **intent-participation vs. assembly-line-instruction** contrast — the conceptual backbone of the primary claim (CLM-0041).
- Keen observation and "listening in" as valued, active learning processes (feeds CLM-0042).
- Explicitly frames both as **cultural** traditions, not one as universally superior — bounds against over-generalisation.
