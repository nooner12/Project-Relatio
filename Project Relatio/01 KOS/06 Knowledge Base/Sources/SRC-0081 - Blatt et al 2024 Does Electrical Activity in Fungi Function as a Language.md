# SRC-0081

# Blatt et al. 2024 — "Does Electrical Activity in Fungi Function as a Language?"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Michael R. Blatt, D. Pullum, Andreas Draguhn, D. Bowman, David G. Robinson & Lincoln Taiz, "Does electrical activity in fungi function as a language?," *Fungal Ecology* 68:101326 (2024). A direct **critical response to Adamatzky (SRC-0080)** and to the broader "fungal language" framing. **In this KB it is the deflationary-skeptic anchor on CLM-0061**, and the source that most sharply enforces the datum≠interpretation boundary.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. The authors are senior plant/fungal electrophysiologists and cognition-critics (Taiz and Blatt are prominent skeptics of "plant/fungal cognition" overreach; Draguhn is a neuroscientist).
- **Transparency:** High. A reasoned methodological critique, not merely rhetorical.
- **Independence:** Represents the opposite pole from Adamatzky; the authors have a stated position against anthropomorphic "cognition" attributions, which is itself a vector to weigh (their prior cuts against over-reading, but is a prior).
- **Intent:** To argue that the "language" interpretation is premature and unsupported.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Argues the **interpretation** ("language") is unsupported and that some signals may be **artifact/noise**; it does **not** prove that fungi have *no* meaningful electrical signaling — the underlying datum (electrical activity responsive to stimuli, SRC-0079) is not thereby erased.
- A commentary/critique, not new primary electrophysiology.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The presumption of a fungal **language is premature and unsupported** by the evidence presented.
- Voltage fluctuations are **likely, in part, non-biological noise and experimental artifacts** given the electrode methodology.
- The measured patterns show **no similarity to any properties of human language** ("far less morphological structure").
