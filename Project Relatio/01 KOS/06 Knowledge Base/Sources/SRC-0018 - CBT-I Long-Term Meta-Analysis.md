# SRC-0018

# CBT-I Long-Term Effects (Meta-Analysis + 10-Year Follow-Up RCT)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
(1) A meta-analysis of the **long-term** effects of cognitive-behavioural therapy for insomnia (CBT-I) in controlled studies, reporting effect sizes at 3-, 6-, and 12-month follow-up; and (2) a randomised trial with **1- and 10-year** follow-up showing maintenance. Sleep is a central pathway between chronic stress and health (ENT-0003), and CBT-I is the best-evidenced *durable* skills-based intervention adjacent to chronic stress.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed meta-analysis (Sleep Medicine Reviews is a leading journal) and a long-follow-up RCT.
- **Transparency:** High (meta-analytic reporting).
- **Independence:** High — behavioural-sleep-medicine research, non-commercial.
- **Intent:** Inform.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Outcome is **insomnia severity**, not "chronic stress" per se — the link to stress relief is inferential (sleep improvement reduces one major stressor/amplifier, not the same as a stress-outcome trial).
- Effects **decline over the first year** even though they remain clinically meaningful.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Meta-analysis (30 RCTs vs non-active controls): **Insomnia Severity Index Hedges' g = 0.64 at 3 months, 0.40 at 6 months, 0.25 at 12 months** — declining but sustained and clinically meaningful at one year. *(Figures live-verified against the primary in Critical Review – RQ-0005 §3 — exact match.)*
- Follow-up RCT: improvements **statistically significant and well maintained at 1- and 10-year follow-up.**

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0015 (CBT-I as the strongest durability case among skills interventions).
- `related_to` ENT-0003 (sleep as an allostatic-load pathway).
