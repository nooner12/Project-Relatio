# SRC-0007

# Taylor Twin-Cycle Mechanism Literature

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

The mechanistic literature, associated principally with **Roy Taylor**, explaining *why* weight loss reverses type 2 diabetes — the **twin-cycle hypothesis** (Taylor, originally 2008): chronic caloric excess drives **excess liver fat**, which raises VLDL export of fat to the **pancreas**; ectopic intra-pancreatic fat impairs beta-cell function. The two organ cycles ("twin cycles") reinforce each other and sustain hyperglycaemia.

Verified this session as a *composite source* (a body of work, not one paper); representative verifiable anchors:
- **Counterpoint (Lim et al. 2011, *Diabetologia*)** — a very-low-calorie diet restored normal fasting glucose and improved beta-cell function; proof-of-concept for the hypothesis. *(Cited from the review literature; the primary DOI was not independently opened this session — see Limitations.)*
- **Counterbalance (Steven et al. 2016, *Diabetes Care*)** — showed normalised metabolism could be maintained over 6 months and **identified the effect of diabetes duration** on likelihood of returning to normal glucose control. *(Same verification caveat.)*
- **Reviews — Taylor 2021, *Journal of Internal Medicine*** (DOI 10.1111/joim.13214) and **Al-Mrabeh et al. 2020** (twin-cycle review, PMC7673778), both opened/summarised this session.

Key mechanistic content (verified via the 2020/2021 review material this session):
- Removal of excess liver and pancreas fat via substantial weight loss can **normalise hepatic insulin responsiveness** and, **in the early years after diagnosis, is associated with recovery of beta-cell acute insulin secretion** in many individuals — consistent with beta-cell **dedifferentiation** (a reversible survival state) rather than permanent death.
- **Beta-cell damage is reversible in the early years**; but prolonged prior lipid exposure (on the order of ~a decade before diagnosis) **compromises recovery capacity**, so longer-duration disease is less likely to remit.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed physiology from a leading group; mechanism is broadly accepted and underpins the NHS remission programme.
- **Transparency:** High — imaging (MR fat quantification) and metabolic measures are explicit.
- **Independence:** Convergent with, yet mechanistically distinct from, the clinical-outcome evidence (SRC-0006) — a triangulation of mechanism and outcome.
- **Intent:** Explain/inform (aetiology and reversibility).

---

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **The Counterpoint (2011) and Counterbalance (2016) primary papers were not directly opened this session** — their findings are reported *via* the review literature and secondary summaries. Exact sample sizes and effect sizes from the primaries are therefore **not asserted here** and should be confirmed on the primaries before any figure is quoted.
- A mechanism being *sufficient in principle* does not fix the *probability* for a given individual.
- The "~10 years / ~a decade" window for beta-cell reversibility is an approximate, population-level statement, not a personal cut-off.
- Studies are small and largely in early-disease, obese cohorts.

---

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- Twin-cycle mechanism: liver fat → pancreatic fat → beta-cell dysfunction; removing ectopic fat can reverse it (CLM-0008).
- Beta-cell recovery is possible in early disease and declines with duration (CLM-0008, CLM-0009).
