# SRC-0131

# Billioud (2020) — Reclaiming the Wilderness: Contemporary Dynamics of the Yiguandao

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Sébastien Billioud, *Reclaiming the Wilderness: Contemporary Dynamics of the Yiguandao* (New York: Oxford University Press, 2020; xi + 296 pp.; ISBN 978-0-19-752913-3). The most recent major anglophone monograph on Yiguandao: a near-decade ethnography of a Hong Kong Yiguandao community as a node of circulation between Taiwan, Macao, and the mainland. The publisher's own description opens by identifying the movement's category: **"The Yiguandao (Way of Pervading Unity) was one of the major redemptive societies of Republican China"** — a current-scholarship classification statement independent of the older survey literature. The book also documents the movement's **Confucian self-positioning** (its emphasis on Confucianism as its public face and mainland-relations strategy) — used by CLM-0082 and CLM-0085.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Billioud (Université Paris Cité) is a leading specialist on contemporary Confucian revival and Yiguandao; Oxford University Press; reviewed across the field's journals (International Journal of Asian Studies; The China Journal; Journal of Chinese Religions).
- **Transparency:** High — long-term ethnography with disclosed method.
- **Independence:** High — academic ethnography; sympathetic-critical register, no confessional adoption.
- **Intent:** Scholarly monograph on the movement's expansion dynamics.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Book interior not independently read this session** (see §6): the classification sentence and content outline are verified from the publisher's description and open review metadata; chapter-level content is not asserted.
- An ethnography of expansion dynamics, not a classification treatise: its category statement is field-standard usage, not a criteria argument (that weight belongs to SRC-0129).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The publisher-description classification: Yiguandao as "one of the major redemptive societies of Republican China," expanding from Taiwan worldwide while remaining forbidden on the mainland (used in CLM-0083's consensus census and CLM-0081's trajectory).
- The Confucian orientation: the role of Confucianism in the movement's contemporary identity and its navigation of Chinese authorities (CLM-0082 strand-proportion evidence; CLM-0085 self-positioning evidence — the movement's public self-framing leans Confucian, not Daoist).
