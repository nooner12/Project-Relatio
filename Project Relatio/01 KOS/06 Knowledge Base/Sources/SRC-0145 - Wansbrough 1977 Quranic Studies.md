# SRC-0145

# Wansbrough (1977) — Quranic Studies: Sources and Methods of Scriptural Interpretation

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

John Wansbrough, *Quranic Studies: Sources and Methods of Scriptural Interpretation* (London Oriental Series 31; Oxford: Oxford University Press, 1977; reprinted with Andrew Rippin's translations and expanded notes, Prometheus Books, 2004). The **foundational work of the revisionist current in Qur'anic studies.** Applying form-critical and literary-analytical methods borrowed from biblical studies, Wansbrough argued — **stated here as the work's position, not as an INV-0018 finding** — that the Qur'anic text as received reached stable, canonical form considerably later than the traditional account allows, in a sectarian milieu outside Arabia, and that the traditional Islamic historiographical corpus is a *salvation history* rather than a straightforwardly documentary record.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (b) — revisionist scholarship**, one of the two revisionist poles in this base (the other being SRC-0146). It bears on **CLM A (iii) rival readings** — the revisionist re-datings that must be steelmanned against the traditional chronology.

**Register discipline (INV-0018 §2.1).** This record catalogues the work **as scholarship**: neither adopted as verdict nor waved away. Its **contested standing is recorded in §3 as part of the record**, per the acute neutrality requirement.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High as scholarship — Wansbrough (1928–2002) was Professor of Semitic Studies and Pro-Director of the School of Oriental and African Studies, University of London; published in SOAS's own London Oriental Series by Oxford University Press. **Authority as an institutional and methodological credential; it is not, and is not treated as, evidence that the thesis is correct.**
- **Transparency:** Moderate — the method is explicit, but the prose is notoriously dense and allusive, which is itself a recurring theme in the reception (Rippin's 2004 apparatus exists partly to address it).
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship, not confessional.
- **Intent:** Methodologically revisionist — to apply literary-critical tools to the Qur'an and the exegetical tradition, and to problematise the received account of the text's origin and transmission.

# 3. Limitations and contested standing (what this source does NOT establish)

**Its standing in the field is contested, and that contest is part of what this record carries.** Recorded neutrally, from reception surfaces read this session:

- **A minority position.** The late-crystallisation thesis has not been adopted as consensus; the field's actual distribution is a matter INV-0018 must **report** under the consensus-asymmetry rule (§2.1), not assume in either direction.
- **Substantive criticisms** (recorded, not adopted): that the form-critical apparatus transposed from biblical studies does not straightforwardly fit the Qur'anic material; that the argument turns heavily on absence-of-evidence for the early period; and that manuscript and documentary findings published since 1977 bear on the dating question in ways the book could not address.
- **Countervailing weight since 1977** — including early Qur'anic manuscript evidence — is treated in later historical-critical work (SRC-0147) and must be weighed there, **not adjudicated from this record.**
- Conversely, the record **does not dismiss** the work: it is historically important, it reframed the questions of the field, and its methodological challenge to source-credulity is part of the mainstream's own toolkit today.
- **Interior not read this session** (§6) — no page-level citation may be drawn from it at circuit.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- The late-canonisation thesis, the sectarian-milieu argument, and the "salvation history" reading of the traditional historiographical corpus (recorded from verification and reception surfaces; **not consumed by any claim at cataloguing**).
