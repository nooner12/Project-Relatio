# SRC-0002

# Philosophical Daoist Corpus

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

The two foundational texts of *philosophical* Daoism (道家): the *Daodejing* (道德經) and the *Zhuangzi* (莊子), as the corpus for "the teachings within Taoism" in INV-0001. Explicitly excludes religious Daoism (道教). Cited by chapter (*DDJ*) or chapter/story (*Zhuangzi*).

---

# 2. Source Evaluation (per KOS-0003 §6)

**Authority:** High as the defining texts of the philosophical-Daoist tradition. The *Daodejing* is aphoristic-poetic; the *Zhuangzi* is anecdotal-parabolic. Authoritative for *the tradition's teaching*, not as the utterances of a single historical author.

**Transparency:** Both texts are gnomic and deliberately paradoxical ("the Dao that can be told is not the eternal Dao"). Meaning is contested *within* the tradition and among translators — opacity is partly intentional.

**Independence:** The two texts are related but distinct voices; the *Zhuangzi* is not merely a commentary on the *Daodejing*. Both are composite (multiple hands/strata).

**Intent:** To reorient the reader toward the Dao and away from conventional striving/valuation — persuasive-transformative, not expository.

---

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- No single historical author or date; "Laozi" may be legendary; the *Zhuangzi*'s later chapters are by other hands.
- Read only in translation here; Classical Chinese terms (*Dao*, *wu wei*, *ziran*, *ren*, *de*) resist English equivalents and translators diverge sharply.
- Internal variety: the *DDJ* includes political/strategic ruler-advice readings of *wu wei* alongside mystical-naturalist ones.

---

# 4. Key Passages Used in INV-0001

- *Daodejing* 1 (the ineffable, unnameable Dao); 5 ("Heaven and earth are not *ren*… straw dogs"); 8 (highest good is like water); 43 (softest overcomes hardest); 78 (water overcomes the hard); *wu wei* / *bu zheng* throughout.
- *Zhuangzi*: Cook/Butcher Ding carving the ox (effortless skill in accord with nature); the uselessness-as-use parables.
