# SRC-0133

# Jordan & Overmyer (1986) — The Flying Phoenix: Aspects of Chinese Sectarianism in Taiwan

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
David K. Jordan and Daniel L. Overmyer, *The Flying Phoenix: Aspects of Chinese Sectarianism in Taiwan* (Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1986; xx + 329 pp.). The classic anthropologist-plus-historian study of Taiwanese **sectarian societies practicing spirit-writing** (the "phoenix" planchette; *bailuan* worship) — the milieu in which Yiguandao practices and organizes. In this KB it is the **milieu-and-register source**: the classic documentation of the spirit-writing sectarian family as a religious formation *in its own right* — the analytic register ("Chinese sectarianism") within which subsequent scholarship on Yiguandao stands.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Princeton UP; Jordan (anthropology) and Overmyer (history of Chinese religions) are foundational figures for this literature.
- **Transparency:** High — fieldwork plus textual history, disclosed method.
- **Independence:** High — academic study; no confessional stake.
- **Intent:** Scholarly documentation and analysis of the sectarian tradition.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Interior not independently read this session** (see §6). In particular, **the book's Yiguandao-specific treatment is not asserted from this record** — its use here is confined to what the open review/publisher surfaces verify: the book's subject (spirit-writing sectarianism in Taiwan) and its analytic register. Any Yiguandao-specific content need is routed to SRC-0131/0132/0134.
- 1986: predates legalization (1987) and all subsequent development.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The register: the spirit-writing sectarian societies of Taiwan analyzed as **"Chinese sectarianism"** — a family of lay, syncretic, revelation-practicing groups treated as a formation distinct from institutional Daoism and Buddhism (CLM-0082's practice-context; CLM-0084's milieu framing).
