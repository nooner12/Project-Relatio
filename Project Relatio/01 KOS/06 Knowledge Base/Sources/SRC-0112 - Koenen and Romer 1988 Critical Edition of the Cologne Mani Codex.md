# Koenen and Romer 1988 — Critical Edition of the Cologne Mani Codex

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Ludwig Koenen & Cornelia Römer (eds.), the critical edition of the Cologne Mani Codex (1988) — the standard scholarly text of P. Colon. inv. 4780, cited in the Iranian-family source base as "*Der Kölner Mani-Kodex*, ed. Koenen & Römer (1988)."

**Corpus rule applied — this is the edition record**; the codex itself is SRC-0111. The split judgment (against a fused citation form) is recorded in SRC-0111 §1.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. This is the standard critical edition of the codex, by editors central to its publication.
- **Transparency:** High, and unusually consequential here. A critical edition of a badly damaged text makes its reconstructions visible through the apparatus — which is what allows a reader to see where the text is secure and where it is editorial supplement.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly philology, external to Manichaeism.
- **Intent:** To establish the text — philological, not interpretive or apologetic.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **The codex is lacunose, so the edition necessarily reconstructs.** Readings of damaged passages are editorial judgments, and any claim resting on such a passage rests partly on the editor rather than wholly on the ancient text. This is the specific reason the object/edition split matters for this item.
- **Currency:** published 1988, and it was preceded by earlier partial editions of the text. Whether specific readings have been superseded by later work has **not** been assessed here and should be checked before load-bearing use.
- An edition **cannot raise the object's evidential standing**: however secure the text, the Cologne Mani Codex remains an in-group hagiographic compilation (SRC-0111 §2), and establishing its wording does not make it independent testimony.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The established Greek text of *On the Origin of His Body*, with apparatus.
