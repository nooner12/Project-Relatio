# SRC-0150

# al-Azmeh (2014) — The Emergence of Islam in Late Antiquity: Allāh and His People

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

Aziz al-Azmeh, *The Emergence of Islam in Late Antiquity: Allāh and His People* (Cambridge and New York: Cambridge University Press, 2014). A large-scale study placing Islam's emergence in the **religious, cultic, linguistic, and political ecology of late-antique Arabia and its Near Eastern surroundings** — including the pre-Islamic Arabian cultic landscape and the processes by which a paleo-Islamic religious formation took shape. Al-Azmeh (Central European University) writes in a historicising register that treats the emergence as a late-antique phenomenon rather than a discontinuity.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (e) — the Arabian / late-antique milieu**, and bears principally on **CLM A (ii)** (what the formation context establishes) and on the milieu component of **CLM B (i)** and **CLM C (i)**. **No claim is asserted and no evidence is graded at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High — al-Azmeh is a senior historian of the pre-modern and modern Islamic world; Cambridge University Press.
- **Transparency:** High in apparatus — an extensive documentary and epigraphic citation base; reviewers note the density of both the argument and the prose.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship, not confessional.
- **Intent:** Historicising and revisionist-in-framing — to dissolve the treatment of Islam's origin as *sui generis* by embedding it in late-antique religious history. **A framing position, not a neutral survey**, and recorded as such.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **A strong interpretive framing**, not a consensus statement; its late-antique dissolution of Islamic exceptionalism is a position among others and is weighed, not inherited.
- Milieu is not genealogy: establishing a shared late-antique environment does **not** establish descent from Judaism or Christianity (edge-restraint rule); it is compatible with hypothesis (b) as much as with (a) or (c).
- Its treatment of the Qur'anic text's own chronology is subordinate to its historical argument (SRC-0147 is the base's surface for that).
- **Interior not read this session** (§6) — no page-level citation may be drawn from it at circuit.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- The late-antique framing of Islam's emergence and the treatment of the pre-Islamic Arabian cultic landscape (recorded from verification surfaces; **not consumed by any claim at cataloguing**).
