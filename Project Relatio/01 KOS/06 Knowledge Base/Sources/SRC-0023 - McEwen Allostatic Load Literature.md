# SRC-0023

# McEwen — Allostatic Load Literature (Cluster)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
The body of work originating the **allostatic load** construct (McEwen & Stellar 1993; McEwen 1998) and its subsequent development: chronic stress produces cumulative "wear and tear" across neuroendocrine, cardiovascular, metabolic, and immune systems. Grounds ENT-0003 and the durability framing of the whole investigation.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — McEwen is the foundational figure; the construct is mainstream stress physiology.
- **Transparency:** High for the theory; the *operationalisation* (which biomarkers compose an AL index) varies across studies.
- **Independence:** High.
- **Intent:** Inform / theorise.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Allostatic load is a **model / composite index**, not a single validated clinical measurement; component panels differ between studies.
- It explains *why chronic stress is harmful and why durable load-reduction is the right target*; it does **not** by itself establish that any particular intervention durably lowers allostatic load.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Definition: cumulative physiological dysregulation from repeated/prolonged stress ("wear and tear"); coined by McEwen & Stellar (1993).
- Consequences include cardiovascular, metabolic, immune, and neural harm — establishing that the target of durable relief is *reducing cumulative load*, not merely producing a transient calm state.
