# SRC-0097

# Sanders — The Historical Figure of Jesus (1993)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
E. P. Sanders, *The Historical Figure of Jesus* (Penguin, 1993). A leading, methodologically restrained treatment of the sources for Jesus by one of the most respected historical-Jesus scholars. In this KB it supplies the **source-critical method** — how a historian handles the Gospels and Paul as evidence, what "almost indisputable facts" the sources bear (independent of theological claims), and the sober use of the Pauline letters as the earliest datable stratum. Used here for the sources-as-evidence question, not for reconstructing Jesus (deferred to candidate INV-0014).

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) — a critical-historical synthesis.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high — Sanders (Duke) reshaped the field (*Jesus and Judaism*); measured and non-sensational.
- **Transparency:** High — explicit about what the sources can and cannot bear, and about the gap between attestation and event.
- **Independence:** High — no confessional program; explicitly brackets faith claims from historical ones.
- **Intent:** To inform — a careful historian's account for a general and scholarly readership.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Sanders's own project *is* reconstruction of Jesus — this KB uses only his **source-critical** framing (the evidence base), and brackets his historical conclusions about Jesus to INV-0014.
- His judgments are H-e; the "almost indisputable" list is a historian's confidence estimate, not documentary proof.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Paul's undisputed letters are the earliest datable Christian documents (50s CE) and a first-hand witness to the movement within ~two decades of its origin.
- The Gospels are later (post-70 for the Synoptics), theologically shaped, and dependent on prior tradition — evidence to be handled critically, not transcripts.
- A historian can nonetheless anchor a small set of well-supported facts about the movement's origin from these sources while bracketing the theological claims.
