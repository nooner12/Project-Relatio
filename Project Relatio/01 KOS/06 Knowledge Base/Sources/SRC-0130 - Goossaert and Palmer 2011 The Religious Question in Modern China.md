# SRC-0130

# Goossaert & Palmer (2011) — The Religious Question in Modern China

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Vincent Goossaert and David A. Palmer, *The Religious Question in Modern China* (Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2011). The standard synthetic survey of religion in China from 1898 to the present. Its chapter 4 treats the Republican-era **redemptive societies** — naming the Universal Morality Society, Tongshanshe, Daoyuan, **and Yiguandao** among them — as a wave of movements combining the Chinese "syncretic sects" tradition with philanthropy, social engagement, and universal-civilization aspirations. In this KB it is the **field-survey placement source**: where the standard survey of the whole modern Chinese religious field locates Yiguandao.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — the standard field survey by two leading specialists (Goossaert: Daoism and modern Chinese religion, EPHE; Palmer: sociology of Chinese religion, HKU); University of Chicago Press.
- **Transparency:** High — synthetic scholarship with the field's apparatus.
- **Independence:** High — no confessional stake; notably, Goossaert is a historian *of Daoism*, and the survey still places Yiguandao among the redemptive societies rather than within institutional Daoism.
- **Intent:** Comprehensive scholarly synthesis.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Chapter interior not independently read this session** (see §6): the placement (ch. 4, redemptive societies, Yiguandao named) is verified from publisher/indexing surfaces; fine-grained content beyond that placement is not asserted.
- A survey: its Yiguandao treatment is contextual, not monographic.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The placement itself: in the standard survey's architecture, Yiguandao appears in the redemptive-societies wave (ch. 4), not in the chapters on the institutional Daoist tradition — consumed by CLM-0083 as survey-level classification evidence, and by CLM-0081 for the Republican-era formation context.
- The characterization of the wave: syncretic-sect inheritance + philanthropy + modern universalism (from the publisher's and indexing summaries of ch. 4).
