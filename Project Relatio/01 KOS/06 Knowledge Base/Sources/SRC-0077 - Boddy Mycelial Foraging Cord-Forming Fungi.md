# SRC-0077

# Boddy — Mycelial Foraging and Cord-Forming Saprotrophic Basidiomycetes (research program)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Lynne Boddy (Cardiff University) and collaborators — a multi-decade experimental research program on the **foraging ecology and network architecture of saprotrophic cord-forming basidiomycetes** (e.g. *Phanerochaete velutina*, *Hypholoma fasciculare*, *Resinicium bicolor*). Anchor works include Boddy (1999) *Mycologia* 91(1):13–32, "Saprotrophic cord-forming fungi: meeting the challenge of heterogeneous environments"; the IMC8 review "Mycelial Foraging Strategies…" (Boddy et al. 2006); and the network-analysis collaborations with Mark Fricker. **In this KB it is the primary descriptive anchor for hyphal-tip growth, resource-directed foraging, and adaptive network remodelling** (cord thickening on productive connections, regression of unproductive mycelium).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Boddy is a leading fungal-decomposition ecologist (Fleming Prize); the cord-forming foraging literature is her recognized specialty, experimentally grounded over decades in soil-microcosm and field studies.
- **Transparency:** High. Findings rest on repeatable microcosm experiments (inoculum + wood "bait" arrangements) with measured biomass/nutrient reallocation.
- **Independence:** Strong. This is descriptive mycology, not a pole in the cognition dispute; Boddy uses deflationary, mechanistic language ("foraging," "reallocation") without cognition claims.
- **Intent:** To characterize how wood-decay fungi search heterogeneous environments and redistribute biomass — decomposition ecology, not a philosophical thesis.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Establishes **adaptive foraging and network remodelling as observed behavior**; does **not** establish that this constitutes learning, memory, cognition, or intentionality — those are separate interpretive claims (CLM-0064, CLM-0066).
- Concerns saprotrophic cord-formers specifically; generalization to all filamentous fungi is bounded.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Hyphae extend at the tip; the colony forages outward as a network, then **reallocates biomass and nutrients** toward newly encountered resources depending on relative resource size, decay state, and prior colonization.
- Productive connecting cords **thicken and are reinforced**; unproductive mycelium **regresses/is recycled** — selective reinforcement of major transport routes.
- Networks are "constantly remodelled in response to nutrient discovery and demand" and display a "directional" carry-over (developed further in SRC-0086, the Fukasawa/Savoury/Boddy memory study).
