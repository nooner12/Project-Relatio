# SRC-0094

# Brown — An Introduction to the New Testament (1997)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Raymond E. Brown, *An Introduction to the New Testament*, Anchor Bible Reference Library (Doubleday, 1997). A magisterial, moderate, mainstream-Catholic critical introduction, widely regarded as one of the most balanced reference works on dating and authorship. In this KB it is the **independent cross-check on Ehrman (SRC-0093)** — a scholar of different confessional background (a Catholic priest) reaching very similar critical conclusions, which is why the convergence carries weight against a charge of skeptical bias.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) — a critical reference introduction.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high — Brown (Union Theological Seminary) was among the most respected NT scholars of the 20th century; the volume is a standard reference.
- **Transparency:** High — lays out arguments on each side and gives his own probability estimates explicitly (e.g. his authorship "scorecard" for the Pauline letters).
- **Independence:** High — a Catholic scholar whose critical conclusions (Gospel anonymity, pseudonymous Pastorals, disputed Deutero-Paulines) align with the secular consensus; this cross-confessional convergence is the point.
- **Intent:** To inform/teach; committed to critical method while remaining within the church — neither apologetic minimizing nor polemical maximizing of skepticism.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Reconstruction (H-e), not documented fact; Brown reports his own confidence ranges and notes where scholarship is divided.
- Written 1997; some sub-debates have moved, but the core dating/authorship judgments remain field-standard.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Gospel dating consistent with the consensus: Mark c. 68–73; Matthew and Luke c. 80–90; John c. 90–110.
- The Gospels are anonymous; traditional attributions are second-century.
- Pauline authorship "scorecard": seven certainly by Paul; Colossians/2 Thessalonians disputed (roughly evenly); Ephesians probably not; the Pastorals probably/almost certainly not by Paul.
