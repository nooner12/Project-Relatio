# SRC-0110

# Hoffmann and Narten 1989 — Der Sasanidische Archetypus

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Karl Hoffmann & Johanna Narten, *Der Sasanidische Archetypus* (1989).

The standard technical study reconstructing the Sasanian-period written archetype of the Avesta — the argument that the Avestan corpus was first committed to writing in the Sasanian era (c. 5th–6th century CE) using an alphabet devised for the purpose, phonetically precise and derived from Pahlavi script.

Catalogued as part of the Iranian-family source base. This is a *study about* the corpus, not an edition of it.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high within its specialism. Hoffmann and Narten were major Indo-Iranian philologists, and this is the reference treatment of the Avestan script and archetype question.
- **Transparency:** High. The argument is technical and explicit, worked from the internal evidence of the script itself, so its inferential steps are visible to a reader competent in the field.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly philology, external to the tradition.
- **Intent:** Reconstructive and technical — to establish when and how the Avesta was written down, not to make doctrinal or historical-religious claims.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It is a **reconstruction, not an attestation**. No manuscript of the Sasanian archetype survives; the archetype is inferred from the script's design and from the later manuscript tradition. The argument is strong of its kind, but it produces a reconstructed object, not a witness.
- Its convergence with the Dēnkard's own account of the Avesta's collection is **not independent corroboration** in the strict sense: the Dēnkard is a tradition-internal source attributing the act backward, and agreement between a modern reconstruction and a late tradition-internal claim is weaker than two independent attestations.
- It settles the **date of writing**, not the date of composition. Nothing here bears on how old the Avestan material is as oral tradition, which is a separate question (see SRC-0105, SRC-0106, SRC-0107).
- Highly technical; not usable at second hand without philological competence.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The reconstruction of the Sasanian written archetype and the purpose-built Avestan alphabet derived from Pahlavi.
- The dating of the Avesta's written fixation to the Sasanian period.
