# SRC-0011

# Diet and Exercise Glycemic-Control Literature (Composite)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

A **composite source** covering the broader lifestyle-intervention evidence for glycaemic control and insulin sensitivity in (pre)type-2-diabetes: Mediterranean-style diet and structured exercise.

Directional content encountered this session (via meta-analytic summaries and the PREDIMED/PREDIMED-Plus programme):
- **Mediterranean diet:** RCT meta-analyses report improved glycaemic control vs control diets — one meta-analysis reported an HbA1c reduction on the order of **~0.3% absolute** (−0.307%) with reductions in fasting glucose and BMI; Mediterranean-diet adherence is also associated with lower T2D incidence (PREDIMED / PREDIMED-Plus).
- **Exercise:** meta-analyses find exercise of any type improves glycaemic control vs none; **combined aerobic + resistance** produces greater improvement than aerobic alone; vigorous intensity improves insulin sensitivity (with compensatory reduced insulin secretion).

---

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** Moderate–high in aggregate (RCTs, large cohorts, meta-analyses), but this record is a **synthesis of several works, not a single vetted citation**.
- **Transparency:** Variable across the constituent studies.
- **Independence:** Multiple independent groups — reasonable convergence on direction.
- **Intent:** Inform.

---

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- **The single most important limitation (evidence integrity):** the specific figures above were seen in **meta-analytic summaries whose individual DOIs were NOT all independently pinned this session.** The **~0.3% HbA1c** Mediterranean-diet effect and specific exercise effect sizes are therefore reported as **directional/approximate and to be verified** before any precise quotation. They are graded accordingly (see CLM-0012).
- These interventions **improve control**; on their own, without substantial weight loss, they are **much less likely to produce consensus-defined remission** than the ≥10–15 kg weight-loss route.
- Effect sizes vary with adherence, baseline, and co-intervention.
