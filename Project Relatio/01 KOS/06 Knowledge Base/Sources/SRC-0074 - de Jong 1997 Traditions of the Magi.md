# SRC-0074

# de Jong 1997 — Traditions of the Magi: Zoroastrianism in Greek and Latin Literature

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Albert de Jong, *Traditions of the Magi: Zoroastrianism in Greek and Latin Literature* (Religions in the Graeco-Roman World 133; Leiden: Brill, 1997; xii + 496 pp; ISBN 90-04-10844-0). The first full treatment since Bidez & Cumont of the classical (Greek/Latin) reports of Zoroastrianism, and a source-critical study of what those external witnesses can and cannot establish about the history of the religion. **In this KB it is the source-critical anchor:** de Jong models exactly the disciplined handling of *external, non-contemporaneous* attestation that the H-scale is meant to formalize, and cautions against retrojecting late systematic doctrine into early periods.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern source-critical reconstruction) — built substantially on **H-c** near-contemporary external witnesses (classical authors reporting on the Magi), whose limits de Jong makes explicit.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high — the standard critical work on the Graeco-Roman evidence for Zoroastrianism (Leiden).
- **Transparency:** Very high — the book is *about* the reliability limits of its sources; it is a methodological exemplar.
- **Independence:** High — source-critical, constrains all parties; it is neither pro- nor anti-influence.
- **Intent:** To assess what classical reports can reconstruct of Zoroastrian history and practice.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It treats the *classical/external* evidence, not the Avestan/Pahlavi corpus directly, and does not itself adjudicate the Jewish-influence question.
- Its caution cuts against over-confident early dating but does not amount to a denial of antiquity; it demands that antiquity be argued, not assumed.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- External (Greek/Latin) witnesses to Zoroastrianism are near-contemporary secondary reports (H-c) with real but bounded value; they must not be read as verbatim doctrine.
- Late systematic doctrine should not be retrojected uncritically into earlier periods — the exact discipline the dating problem (CLM-0056) enforces.
