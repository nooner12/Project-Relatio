# SRC-0142

# Strack & Stemberger — Introduction to the Talmud and Midrash

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Hermann L. Strack & Günter Stemberger, *Introduction to the Talmud and Midrash*, trans./ed. Markus Bockmuehl (Edinburgh: T&T Clark, 1991; Fortress Press ed. 1992; rev. 1996). The **standard reference handbook** for the dating, composition, and character of rabbinic literature — the historical framework, hermeneutics, and the Talmudic and Midrashic corpora, including the **redaction of the Mishnah c. 200 CE** under Judah ha-Nasi. Catalogued as candidate coverage for **INV-0017's Rabbinic Judaism dating claim** (Area 3 — the second branch of the fork): it is the base's technical authority on *when* the rabbinic corpus crystallised. Catalogued (ADR-GOV-0003 pattern); **no claim asserted at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — the standard English-language reference introduction to rabbinic literature; Stemberger is a leading authority on the corpus; T&T Clark / Fortress Press.
- **Transparency:** High — a reference handbook that lays out the evidence and the state of scholarship on dating and redaction.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship (the modern work is substantially Stemberger's revision of Strack's older *Einleitung*).
- **Intent:** To provide a comprehensive, current orientation to the Talmudic and Midrashic literature and its dating.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **A handbook on the *literature*, not a narrative history of the rabbinic movement's social emergence** — it dates and describes the texts; the "how the rabbis became the mainline" story draws on the Area 1/bridge sources (SRC-0138) too.
- **Edition complexity:** an English translation (T&T Clark 1991) of a revised German original, with a Fortress edition (1992) and a further revision (1996); the edition/printing should be named at circuit, and the ISBN 978-0-8006-2524-5 corresponds to the Fortress line.
- Does not treat the Christian side of the fork.
- **Interior not read this session** (see §6).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The dating and redaction framework for the Mishnah (c. 200 CE) and the wider Talmudic/Midrashic corpus (recorded from verification surfaces; not consumed by any claim at cataloguing).

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `related_to` SRC-0138 (Cohen) — converging on Mishnah dating / rabbinic formation from the literature-handbook and survey angles respectively; reciprocated.

**Live-verified bibliographically (strong); interior parametric, with a disclosed edition-date caveat.** Authors (Strack & Stemberger), translator/editor (Markus Bockmuehl), title, publishers (T&T Clark 1991 / Fortress Press 1992; rev. 1996), and ISBN 978-0-8006-2524-5 confirmed this session against Fortress Press, Blackwell's, Amazon, Christianbook, Google Books, and Internet Archive records. **The relationship among the 1991/1992/1996 printings is recorded honestly rather than collapsed to a single year** — the English translation is 1991 (T&T Clark), the Fortress edition 1992, with a revision noted at 1996. **The interior was not read this session; no page-level citation is asserted** — the Mishnah-dating framework is recorded from reference/publisher surfaces.
