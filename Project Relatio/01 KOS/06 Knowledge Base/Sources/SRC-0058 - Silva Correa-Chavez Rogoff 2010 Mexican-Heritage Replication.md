# SRC-0058

# Silva, Correa-Chávez & Rogoff 2010 — Mexican-Heritage Children's Attention and Learning

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Silva, K. G., Correa-Chávez, M., & Rogoff, B. (2010), "Mexican-heritage children's attention and learning from interactions directed to others," *Child Development* 81(3):898–912. A **replication and extension** of SRC-0057 in a different population: **U.S. Mexican-heritage** children aged **5–11**. **44** children whose families likely have experience with Indigenous ways attended to and learned from a toy-construction activity **directed to another child** more than **36** children whose mothers had extensive experience with Western schooling (total N ≈ 80). Citation (Child Development 81(3):898–912) verified this session via PubMed (20573112) and ERIC (EJ883383).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed experimental study in a leading developmental journal.
- **Transparency:** High — clear group definition by maternal Western-schooling experience and clear outcome (attention + learning from a demonstration directed to another).
- **Independence:** Moderate — same research program, but a **replication in an independent population** (U.S. Mexican-heritage rather than Guatemalan Mayan), which strengthens the mechanism claim through convergence.
- **Intent:** Test whether the culturally patterned attention/learning effect generalises beyond the original Mayan sample.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Same **comparative-not-randomized** design as SRC-0057: groups differ by **maternal Western-schooling history**, so the effect remains **confounded with the cultural constellation**.
- Same **narrow competence** (toy-construction attention/learning) and same dependence on an **interaction directed to another child** — again absent in a single-child home (CLM-0043).
- Convergence with SRC-0057 raises confidence in the *mechanism* but **not** in the transfer to a solitary learner.
- **Funding:** not stated/verified this session.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Independent-population **replication** of the "learning from interactions directed to others" effect, tracking maternal Western-schooling experience.
- The convergence (Guatemalan Mayan + U.S. Mexican-heritage) is the reason CLM-0042's mechanism grade reaches Moderate rather than Low.
