# Guest 1993 — Survival Among the Kurds

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
John S. Guest, *Survival Among the Kurds* (1993) — a history of the Yazidis, cited in the source base alongside Kreyenbroek (SRC-0118) on the modern compilation history of the published Yazidi "sacred books."

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Moderate, and **narrower than SRC-0118's**. This is a documented historical survey rather than a philological or religious-studies treatment; its author works as a historian of the documentary record rather than as a specialist in Kurdish or Iranian religious texts. It is authoritative on the modern contact history and considerably less so on the tradition's internal religious content.
- **Transparency:** Good. A documented narrative history with its sources visible.
- **Independence:** Outsider scholarly work, independent of the community.
- **Intent:** Historical survey — particularly of Western contact with the Yazidis and the circumstances under which Yazidi material reached European hands.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Strongest on the 19th–20th century documentary trail**, including the provenance of the manuscripts published as Yazidi scripture — which is precisely the use it is put to here, and a good fit. It should not be extended beyond that.
- **Not a critical edition** and not a text-critical study; on the language and internal character of the texts, SRC-0118 is the better authority.
- **Not authoritative on pre-modern Yazidi religious history** or on the contested origins question, where its treatment is derivative.
- Published 1993; the field has developed since, particularly given the scholarly attention Yazidi communities have received in recent decades.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The documentary history of the manuscripts published as the Yazidi "sacred books" (Kitêba Cilwe, Mishefa Reş) and the circumstances of their appearance.
