# Goodacre — The Case Against Q (2002)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Mark Goodacre, *The Case Against Q: Studies in Markan Priority and the Synoptic Problem* (Trinity Press International, 2002; ISBN 1-56338-334-9). The most accessible modern defense of the **Farrer hypothesis** (Markan priority *without* Q; Luke used both Mark and Matthew), against the dominant **Two-Source hypothesis** (Mark + a hypothetical sayings source Q). In this KB it is the **steelman of the anti-Q pole** — the source that keeps the Q hypothesis honestly marked as *contested*, and that isolates Markan priority (which it shares) from Q (which it rejects).

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction) — a critical argument about literary dependence.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Goodacre (Duke) is a leading Synoptic-problem specialist; the book is the standard reference for the Farrer position.
- **Transparency:** High — states the fallacies it alleges in the Q case and the positive Farrer alternative explicitly.
- **Independence:** High — argues against the field's majority; no confessional stake in the outcome (Q's existence or non-existence is theologically neutral).
- **Intent:** To persuade within scholarship — but by argument laid open to rebuttal, not by assertion.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It does **not** overturn **Markan priority** — it affirms it; the two questions must be kept apart (its central methodological point: Markan priority does not require Q).
- The Farrer hypothesis remains a **minority** position; citing Goodacre establishes that Q is *contested*, not that Q is *disproven*.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Markan priority (Mark written first, used by Matthew and Luke) is broadly held and is logically independent of Q.
- The double tradition (material common to Matthew and Luke but absent from Mark) can be explained by Luke's use of Matthew (Farrer), so Q is not a necessary inference.
- Q is a *hypothetical* document — no manuscript, no patristic citation; its existence is an inference of differing strength from Markan priority.
