# SRC-0124

# Keith & Le Donne (eds.) — Jesus, Criteria, and the Demise of Authenticity (2012)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Chris Keith and Anthony Le Donne (eds.), *Jesus, Criteria, and the Demise of Authenticity* (London: T&T Clark, 2012; xvii + 230 pp.; ISBN 978-0-567-37723-4). The flagship collection of the **criticism of the criteria-of-authenticity approach**: essays by scholars who argue the traditional criteria (multiple attestation, embarrassment, dissimilarity, coherence) either should be abandoned or significantly recast. Contributors address the general use of the criteria in historical-Jesus research, analyze specific criteria, and discuss future prospects for the field (memory-based and social-memory approaches among them). In this KB it is the **methodological counter-weight**: any claim in INV-0014 whose element (b) leans on an authenticity criterion must be graded knowing this literature exists and has traction.

**H-band of this source (prose only):** **H-e** (modern scholarly methodological critique).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Keith and Le Donne are recognized specialists in Jesus-tradition and social-memory research; the contributor list spans the field's mainstream.
- **Transparency:** High — the essays argue openly from the history and logic of the criteria.
- **Independence:** High — the collection critiques the field's own dominant method from within; not a confessional or anti-confessional project.
- **Intent:** Methodological reform — to move historical-Jesus research off a criteria-mechanics footing; argumentative but scholarly in register.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It establishes that the criteria are **contested as a method**, not that every result reached with them is wrong — several contributors accept many standard conclusions on other grounds.
- It does not itself adjudicate any specific claim about Jesus; it bears on **how much weight element (b) can carry**, not on the claims' content.
- An edited collection: positions vary by contributor; the shared core is skepticism about criteria-as-mechanical-proof, not a single alternative method.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The criteria of authenticity emerged from form criticism and inherit its assumptions; used mechanically they promise a false precision ("demise of authenticity" as a diagnosis of the method).
- Memory-based approaches (Keith, Le Donne, with Allison's recurrent-attestation approach adjacent) grade the *general shape* of the tradition rather than authenticating individual sayings.
- Consequence used in INV-0014: element (b) findings are stated as **what the criteria establish and fail to establish**, never as mechanical proof; criteria-based confidence is discounted where this critique bites hardest (individual-saying authenticity, CLM-0079).
