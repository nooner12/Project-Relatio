# SRC-0043

# Merton 1940 — Bureaucratic Structure and Personality

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Merton, R. K. (1940), "Bureaucratic Structure and Personality," *Social Forces* 18(4):560–568. The classic statement of the **dysfunctions of bureaucracy** — in particular **goal displacement** (rule-following becomes an end in itself, displacing the organization's actual objectives) and **trained incapacity** (skills/routines that succeeded before become blind spots under changed conditions). Catalogued to anchor the **goal-displacement** half of CLM-0031 (previously an uncatalogued invocation).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — Merton is a foundational sociologist; this is a canonical, heavily-cited paper.
- **Transparency:** High for a theory paper — the mechanisms (over-conformity → goal displacement; trained incapacity) are stated clearly.
- **Independence:** High — academic.
- **Intent:** Inform / theorise.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- A **theoretical account**, not a measurement of how often goal displacement occurs or how large its effects are.
- Draws on Weber/Veblen traditions; it describes a **tendency** of rule-bound organizations, not an inevitability.
- Concerns **bureaucratic personality/structure**; application to knowledge-system metrics and rules is an analogical extension.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- **Goal displacement:** "an instrumental value becomes a terminal value" — adherence to rules/procedures overshadows the objectives the rules were meant to serve; officials "do things by the book" even when it defeats the purpose.
- **Trained incapacity** (borrowed from Veblen): disciplined competence in stable conditions becomes inflexibility and blindness under changed conditions — a companion to the ossification risk (CLM-0032).

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0031 (goal displacement — the companion to Campbell's Law's gaming mechanism).
