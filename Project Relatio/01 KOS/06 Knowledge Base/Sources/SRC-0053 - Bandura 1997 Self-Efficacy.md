# SRC-0053

# Bandura 1997 — Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control (with Bandura 1977)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Bandura, A. (1997), *Self-Efficacy: The Exercise of Control* (New York: W. H. Freeman) — the definitive statement of self-efficacy theory; and the originating paper, Bandura, A. (1977), "Self-efficacy: Toward a unifying theory of behavioral change," *Psychological Review* 84(2):191–215. The **theoretical anchor** for the load-bearing half of CLM-0036: that self-efficacy beliefs are built from **four sources — enactive mastery experiences, vicarious experience, verbal/social persuasion, and physiological/affective states — of which enactive mastery experience is the strongest.** Catalogued this session in RQ-0008 remediation to close an uncatalogued-companion flag (Critical Review – RQ-0008 §3.1, §8): the "mastery is the strongest source" proposition rests on Bandura, which the domain's empirical anchor (SRC-0050 Multon 1991) does not itself establish.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very High — Bandura's social-cognitive theory and the four-sources model are foundational, textbook-level psychology; among the most cited work in the discipline.
- **Transparency:** High — the theoretical framework and the ordering of the four sources are explicitly argued.
- **Independence:** High — theoretical/academic, independent of any program.
- **Intent:** Establish and systematise the theory of perceived self-efficacy.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It is **theoretical framework**, not an effect-size measurement: it establishes that mastery is the **strongest source** of efficacy, not the **magnitude** by which a given program raises efficacy or downstream outcomes (that empirical link, correlational and reciprocal, is SRC-0050's domain).
- The "strongest source" ordering is best-attested in achievement, clinical, and sport/motor domains; its transfer to **children's heritage/craft mastery in a community program** is analogical (CLM-0036 application grade).
- **Verification this session:** the **four-source hierarchy with enactive mastery as the strongest source** was independently confirmed via WebSearch against multiple secondary summaries of the 1997 text; the primary book was **not** opened to the page this session, and the 1977 *Psychological Review* pagination (84(2):191–215) is the standard citation, not re-derived from the article PDF. No figure is asserted from this source.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Four sources of self-efficacy: **enactive mastery experiences, vicarious experiences, verbal persuasion, physiological/affective states.**
- **Enactive mastery experience is the most influential source** — authentic successes provide the strongest evidence of capability. This is the mechanism a skills-mastery activity targets.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0036 (anchors the "mastery = strongest efficacy source" half).
- `related_to` SRC-0050 (Multon 1991 — the empirical self-efficacy↔achievement anchor that stands on this theory).
