# SRC-0135

# World I-Kuan Tao Headquarters — English Self-Presentation ("Introduction to I-Kuan Tao")

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
"Introduction to I-Kuan Tao," English-language self-presentation published by the **World I-Kuan Tao Headquarters** (the umbrella body established in Alhambra, California, 1996; page copyright 2003) at with.org. A **primary source for the self-understanding track only** (INV-0015 §2.1 dual-track rule): it evidences how the movement presents *itself* in English — it is not evidence about the academic classification. Key self-descriptions read live this session: the name glossed as **"The Tao that unifies all with the one"**; a patriarchal lineage anchored in remote antiquity (**Fuxi** as the earliest reference point) running through **Huang Dehui** (ninth patriarch, credited with establishing Xiantiandao), **Liu Qingxu**, **Lu Zhongyi**, to **Zhang Tianran and Sun Huiming** as the eighteenth-patriarch pair; and the characterization of I-Kuan Tao as "the definitive and authentic Chinese form of spirituality."

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High *for its own register* — an official organizational voice for its own self-understanding; no authority on the scholarly question.
- **Transparency:** Moderate — a public self-presentation; sources and dates of its lineage claims are not given.
- **Independence:** None with respect to the movement (it *is* the movement) — which is precisely its evidential value in the self-understanding register.
- **Intent:** Self-presentation/outreach — confessional in register; consumed as a fact about the movement, per the dual-track rule.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Nothing about the correctness of any classification** (dual-track rule): it evidences self-understanding only.
- One organizational voice: Yiguandao is divisionally plural; this page speaks for the World Headquarters umbrella, not necessarily for every division's self-account (grade bound on CLM-0085).
- Its lineage dates diverge in places from the scholarly spine (e.g., it associates Liu Qingxu with 1886 and Lu Zhongyi with 1905, where SRC-0134 has 1905 naming by Liu and 1919 for Lu; "Sun Huiming" vs. SRC-0134's "Sun Suzhen" for Zhang's co-leader — the same person under variant names). The divergences are **recorded, not resolved**.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The self-gloss "The Tao that unifies all with the one" and the "definitive and authentic Chinese form of spirituality" characterization (read live).
- The lineage self-account: Fuxi as the earliest anchor; Huang Dehui (ninth patriarch) establishing Xiantiandao; succession to the Zhang Tianran/Sun pair (read live).
- **The negative datum (load-bearing for CLM-0085):** the page does **not** describe I-Kuan Tao as a school or branch of Daoism; its claim is to the primordial **Dao itself**, transmitted through a trans-religion patriarchal line — a claim *above* the institutional religions rather than membership in one of them.
