# SRC-0070

# Shaked 1984 — Iranian Influence on Judaism (Cambridge History of Judaism, vol. 1)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Shaul Shaked, "Iranian Influence on Judaism: First Century B.C.E. to Second Century C.E.," in W. D. Davies & L. Finkelstein (eds.), *The Cambridge History of Judaism*, vol. 1 (Cambridge University Press, 1984): 308–325 (notes 441–442). Shaked was a leading authority on Judaeo-Iranian contact (Hebrew University; editor of *Irano-Judaica*). **In this KB it is the measured/middle anchor**: Shaked accepts that Iranian influence on Judaism is real and detectable — especially in dualism and the structuring of the eschatological/angelological imagination — but insists it operated by *selective adaptation and reinterpretation*, not wholesale borrowing, and that the mechanism was diffuse cultural contact rather than a documented doctrinal handover.

**H-band of this source:** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction), by a specialist in *both* corpora — which is exactly the philological competence Barr (SRC-0067) said the field usually lacks.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** Very high — command of Iranian *and* Jewish sources; the reference-work treatment.
- **Transparency:** High — explicit that influence is real but hard to pin to specific channels, and that adaptation reshapes what is borrowed.
- **Independence:** High — neither maximalist (Boyce/Cohn) nor eliminativist (a hard reading of Barr); occupies the evidentially cautious middle.
- **Intent:** To assess, corpus-competently, where Iranian influence on Second Temple / early rabbinic Judaism can and cannot be sustained.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Shaked establishes *plausibility and selectivity*, not a documented transmission channel — the pathway (CLM-0057) remains inferred.
- His chapter's stated window is 1st c. BCE–2nd c. CE; extrapolating his conclusions to the earlier Achaemenid contact is an inference the reader (not Shaked) makes.
- Being measured, he under-determines the doctrine-by-doctrine verdict — he supports "some real influence, selectively," which is compatible with the split verdict but does not by itself grade any single doctrine.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Iranian influence on Second Temple Judaism is real and detectable, concentrated in dualism and the eschatological/angelological imagination.
- It operated by selective adaptation, not wholesale transfer; the specific transmission mechanism is diffuse and not documented.
