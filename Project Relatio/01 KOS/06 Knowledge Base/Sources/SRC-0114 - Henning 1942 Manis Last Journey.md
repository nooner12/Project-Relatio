# SRC-0114

# Henning 1942 — "Mani's Last Journey"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
W. B. Henning, "Mani's Last Journey," *Bulletin of the School of Oriental and African Studies* 10/4 (1942), 941–953.

A foundational study reconstructing the circumstances and chronology of Mani's final journey, imprisonment, and death under Bahram I, working from the early Manichaean church accounts preserved in Middle Iranian sources.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Henning was among the foundational figures of Middle Iranian studies; the article is a standard reference point, published in a peer-reviewed journal of record.
- **Transparency:** High. The reconstruction is argued explicitly from the cited Manichaean material.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly work, external to the tradition — though it is necessarily reconstructing *from* in-group church accounts, which bounds what independence can achieve here.
- **Intent:** Historical-philological reconstruction.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **The death year remains contested** between competing reconstructions (274 and 277 CE are both defended). This article is one position in that dispute, not its resolution; it must not be cited as settling the chronology.
- Its underlying evidence is **early Manichaean church accounts** — well attested within the movement, but in-group and hagiographically shaped. The event of Mani's death in imprisonment is far better supported than any precise date.
- Published **1942**. It is foundational rather than current, and eight decades of subsequent Manichaean studies — including material published since — bear on the question. Age here is a genuine limitation, not merely a formality.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The reconstruction of Mani's final journey, imprisonment, and death under Bahram I.
- The chronological argument bearing on the contested death year.
