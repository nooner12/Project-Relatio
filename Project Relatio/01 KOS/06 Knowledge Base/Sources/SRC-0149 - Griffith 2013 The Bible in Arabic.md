# SRC-0149

# Griffith (2013) — The Bible in Arabic: The Scriptures of the "People of the Book" in the Language of Islam

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification

Sidney H. Griffith, *The Bible in Arabic: The Scriptures of the "People of the Book" in the Language of Islam* (Jews, Christians, and Muslims from the Ancient to the Modern World; Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2013; paperback 2015). A study of **how Jewish and Christian scripture circulated in the Arabic-speaking world before and after the rise of Islam**, and of what the state of that circulation implies about the form in which biblical material was available in the Qur'an's environment. Griffith (The Catholic University of America) is a leading authority on **Syriac and Arabic Christianity** and on Christian–Muslim relations in the early Islamic period.

Catalogued for **INV-0018** under the **ADR-GOV-0003 pattern** (catalog only, grade nothing). Serves **coverage area (d) — the Judeo-Christian context**, complementing SRC-0148 from the scriptures-in-Arabic side, and is the base's principal surface for **CLM C (i)** on **Syriac Christian contact** and the mode in which Christian material was present in the milieu. **No claim is asserted and no evidence is graded at cataloguing.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)

- **Authority:** High — Griffith is a senior scholar of Syriac and Christian Arabic; Princeton University Press, in a peer-reviewed series.
- **Transparency:** High — the manuscript and philological evidence for the (non-)existence of a pre-Islamic written Arabic Bible is laid out explicitly.
- **Independence:** High — critical academic scholarship. Griffith writes from within a Christian institutional setting; **the work's register is historical-philological, not apologetic**, and this record does not treat institutional affiliation as either credential or discount.
- **Intent:** Historical and philological — to establish what Arabic-speaking Jews and Christians actually had, in what language and form, and when.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)

- Its central subject is the **Arabic Bible's transmission history**, much of it **post-formation**; only the pre- and early-Islamic material bears directly on INV-0018's questions.
- **Availability of material is not derivation.** Establishing how biblical material circulated does not establish that Islam descends from Judaism or Christianity; the relationship-type determination is INV-0018's to argue (edge-restraint rule).
- Not a source on Islamic formation as such (SRC-0143/0144), nor on the Qur'anic text's chronology (SRC-0147).
- **Interior not read this session** (§6) — no page-level citation may be drawn from it at circuit.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used

- The question of whether a written Arabic Bible existed before Islam, and the oral/liturgical modes in which biblical material circulated in the Qur'an's environment (recorded from verification surfaces; **not consumed by any claim at cataloguing**).
