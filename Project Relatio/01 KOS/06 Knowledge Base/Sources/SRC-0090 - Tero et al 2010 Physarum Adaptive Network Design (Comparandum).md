# SRC-0090

# Tero et al. 2010 — "Rules for Biologically Inspired Adaptive Network Design" (Physarum — COMPARANDUM ONLY)

## Draft Source Record

> **SCOPE FLAG (INV-0012 §2.2, load-bearing).** *Physarum polycephalum* is an **acellular slime mold — a protist (Amoebozoa), NOT a fungus.** This source is admissible in INV-0012 **only as a clearly-labelled comparandum** for network-level adaptive behavior. **No result in this source is fungal evidence.** Its relationships to claims are typed `related_to` (not `derived_from` / `supports`) precisely to keep it out of the fungal evidential base.

---

# 1. Identification
Atsushi Tero, Seiji Takagi, Tetsu Saigusa, Kentaro Ito, Dan P. Bebber, Mark D. Fricker, Kenji Yumiki, Ryo Kobayashi & Toshiyuki Nakagaki, "Rules for biologically inspired adaptive network design," *Science* 327(5964):439–442 (2010). *Physarum* grown on a map of Tokyo (food at city locations) produced a network with **efficiency, fault-tolerance and cost comparable to the real Tokyo rail system**; the authors abstracted the behavior into a mathematical adaptive-network model. **In this KB it is the famous "slime-mold intelligence" result that popular coverage constantly mislabels as fungal** — held here as the boundary case CLM-0064/0065 must not import.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High as science (*Science*, well-cited); note Fricker & Bebber overlap with the fungal-network group (SRC-0078), which is why the analogy is tempting — and why the boundary must be stated.
- **Transparency:** High; explicit model and physical experiment.
- **Independence:** Not a cognition-hype source; frames results as **adaptive network optimization**, not "thought."
- **Intent:** To derive transferable rules for robust, efficient network design from a biological optimizer.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- Establishes **network-optimization behavior in a protist**; establishes **nothing about fungi**. Any use of it as fungal evidence is the §2.2 scope error the investigation exists to flag.
- Even for *Physarum*, "intelligence" is an emergent flow-optimization, not demonstrated cognition/sentience.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- *Physarum* forms transport networks approximating the cost/efficiency/resilience trade-offs of an engineered rail network; captured by a simple tube-adaptation model.
- Used **only** to illustrate that network-level "smart" behavior can emerge from non-neural flow rules — and to mark the line CLM-0064/0065 do not cross.
