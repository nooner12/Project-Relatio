# SRC-0064

# Allen et al. 2013 — Eating Disorder Symptom Trajectories in Adolescence

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Allen, K. L., Crosby, R. D., Oddy, W. H., & Byrne, S. M. (2013), "Eating disorder symptom trajectories in adolescence: effects of time, participant sex, and early adolescent depressive symptoms," *Journal of Eating Disorders* 1:32, DOI 10.1186/2050-2974-1-32. Longitudinal cohort (Raine Study lineage) tracking eating-disorder symptoms from **age 14 to 20** by participant sex. Finds a **divergence in symptom trajectory**: in females, fasting/global symptoms, purging, and hard exercise **rise from age 14 to a peak around 17** and stay elevated; in males, symptoms are **stable or declining** across the same window. Citation and the sex-differentiated trajectory verified this session via the PMC full text (PMC4081731).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Construct-provenance code: `UNRESOLVED`.** Participants' **sex was recorded at enrollment** and used as a grouping variable; the study assesses no biological mechanism and measures no gender-socialization construct (body-image internalization, thin-ideal pressure, gender role). Recording a sex category is not measuring a construct — and, critically, the paper gives **no basis to determine whether the underlying data is biologically or socially patterned**: the mechanism (biology vs sociocultural body-image pressure vs male under-detection) is undetermined from the paper as written. That is the definition of `UNRESOLVED`, not `CONFLATED` (which would presuppose the data is specifically *gendered*). It **can support only a gender-patterned (or unattributed) divergence claim** (operative rule §3.1 — `UNRESOLVED` is constrained identically to `CONFLATED`: neither can carry a sex-attribution). Justification: sex tag on symptom-scale trajectories, construct undetermined per the source's own limitation notes.
- **Authority:** Moderate–High — established longitudinal cohort, peer-reviewed specialty journal.
- **Transparency:** High — reports symptom-specific trajectories by sex and age.
- **Independence:** High relative to the other domains' sources — separate cohort and team.
- **Intent:** Characterize how eating-disorder symptoms evolve across adolescence and whether the course differs by sex.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **No mechanism.** Does not test whether the female rise reflects sociocultural body-image pressure, pubertal biology, or differential reporting — the very question that would distinguish sex from gender goes unmeasured.
- **Symptom scales, not diagnoses.** Trajectories are of self-reported symptoms, not incident clinical eating-disorder onset.
- **Window starts at 14.** The lower band (5–13) is not directly observed here, so the *emergence* age is inferred from where the trajectories are already diverging, not dated from equality.
- **Single cohort**, generalization is an assumption.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Female eating-disorder symptoms **rise across 14–17 and remain elevated**; male symptoms are stable/declining — a clear sex-categorized trajectory divergence consolidating in mid-adolescence.
- Early-adolescent depressive symptoms predict later eating-disorder symptoms (comorbidity signal, not used for the timing claim).
