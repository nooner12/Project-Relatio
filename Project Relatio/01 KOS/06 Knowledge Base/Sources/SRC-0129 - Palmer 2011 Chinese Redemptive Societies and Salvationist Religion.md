# SRC-0129

# Palmer (2011) — Chinese Redemptive Societies and Salvationist Religion: Historical Phenomenon or Sociological Category?

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
David A. Palmer, "Chinese Redemptive Societies and Salvationist Religion: Historical Phenomenon or Sociological Category?", *Journal of Chinese Theatre, Ritual and Folklore / Minsu Quyi* 172 (2011): 21–72. The article that gives the "Chinese salvationist religion" category its most explicit conceptual statement in the anglophone literature: it outlines a framework distinguishing **salvationist movements as a recurring sociological category in Chinese religious history** from **redemptive societies as the specific Republican-era wave** of such movements (the term "redemptive societies" being Prasenjit Duara's coinage for that wave — Tongshanshe, Daoyuan, Yiguandao, and others). In this KB it is the **load-bearing category source for CLM-0083**: it is where the criteria for the category are articulated.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Palmer (University of Hong Kong) is a leading sociologist/anthropologist of modern Chinese religion; co-author of the standard field survey (SRC-0130).
- **Transparency:** High by genre — an explicit category-construction argument in a peer-reviewed venue.
- **Independence:** High — academic category-building; no confessional stake in Yiguandao's identity in either direction.
- **Intent:** Analytical — to clarify whether "redemptive societies"/"salvationist religion" names a historical phenomenon or a sociological category; the article is itself reflexive about the category's construction (which CLM-0086 uses for the dissent track).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Its detailed argument and criteria list could not be independently read this session** (see §6) — this record establishes the article's existence, venue, framing, and Yiguandao's placement among the Republican-era exemplars, not the fine texture of the criteria; claims leaning on that texture carry a grade ceiling (CLM-0083 element (a)).
- A category article: it does not provide a monographic account of Yiguandao itself.
- Reflexivity cuts both ways: the article's own question ("historical phenomenon or sociological category?") means the category's boundaries are debated *within* the field — the classification of Yiguandao under it is not thereby weakened, but the category's ontological status is a live scholarly question (used at honest weight in CLM-0086).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The distinction: salvationist movements as a recurring sociological type in Chinese religious history vs. redemptive societies as the named Republican-era wave (Duara's coinage).
- Yiguandao listed among the central examples of the Republican-era redemptive societies (with Tongshanshe, Daoyuan, and others).
- The category is constructed **against** the alternative framings "school of Daoism"/"school of Buddhism": salvationist groups are analyzed as a distinct religious family, not as sub-schools of the institutional traditions — the placement itself, not any strand-count, is what CLM-0083 consumes.
