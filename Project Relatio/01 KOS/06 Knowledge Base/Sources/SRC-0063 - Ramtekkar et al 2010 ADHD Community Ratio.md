# SRC-0063

# Ramtekkar et al. 2010 — Sex and Age Differences in ADHD Symptoms and Diagnoses (DSM-V/ICD-11 Implications)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Ramtekkar, U. P., Reiersen, A. M., Todorov, A. A., & Todd, R. D. (2010), "Sex and age differences in Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder symptoms and diagnoses: Implications for DSM-V and ICD-11," *Journal of the American Academy of Child & Adolescent Psychiatry* 49(3):217–228 (PMC3101894). Large community/population-based sample using **parent-report SWAN ratings** and screener interviews. Found an overall **male:female ratio of ~2.28:1** — far below the ~4:1 (and higher) figures from clinical samples — with the authors attributing the difference to reduced referral bias and concluding **females are under-diagnosed** when identification runs through clinical settings. Citation and the 2.28:1 figure verified this session via PMC3101894.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Construct-provenance code: `CONFLATED`.** Rates are compared by recorded **sex category** on **parent-report rating** data; no biological mechanism is assessed and no gender-socialization variable is measured. The paper is again *about* the ascertainment artifact — the community ratio is far below the clinical one. It **can support only a gender/ascertainment-patterned claim** (operative rule §3.1). Justification: sex-labeled parent-report ratings; the finding is that the label's ratio depends on who does the counting.
- **Authority:** High — large community sample, top child-psychiatry journal, DSM-V-informing.
- **Transparency:** High — reports ratios by age and setting, and names the single-informant limitation.
- **Independence:** High — population-based, independent of the Gaub & Carlson pooled studies (convergent, not circular).
- **Intent:** Estimate ADHD sex/age patterns free of clinical referral bias, to inform diagnostic criteria.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Single-informant (parent) ratings** — no direct clinical assessment or biological testing; parent perception is itself gender-influenced.
- **Does not establish biological causation** of the residual (~2.28:1) community difference; it narrows, but does not eliminate, the sex-labeled gap and offers no mechanism for what remains.
- **Not onset-dating.** Bears on identification/diagnosis divergence, not incident neurobiological onset timing.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Community M:F ratio ~2.28:1 vs the ~4:1+ clinical figure — the ratio shrinks markedly when referral is bypassed.
- Explicit conclusion that **females are under-diagnosed through clinical channels**.
- Inattentive presentation most common in both sexes — undercutting a purely "girls have a different disorder" reading.
