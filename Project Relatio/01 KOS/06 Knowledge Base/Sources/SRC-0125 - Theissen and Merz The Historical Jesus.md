# SRC-0125

# Theissen & Merz — The Historical Jesus: A Comprehensive Guide (1998)

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Gerd Theissen and Annette Merz, *The Historical Jesus: A Comprehensive Guide* (Minneapolis: Fortress Press, 1998; trans. of *Der historische Jesus: Ein Lehrbuch*, 1996; ISBN 978-0-8006-3122-2). The standard mainstream **handbook** of historical-Jesus research: it works systematically through the sources, the historical and social context, and the reconstructible profile of Jesus as charismatic teacher, prophet, healer/exorcist, parable-teller, and ethical teacher, stating for each element the evidence and the state of scholarship. In this KB it supplies the **field-consensus baseline** for the deeds-and-ministry claims (crucifixion context, baptism by John, Kingdom-of-God proclamation, healer/exorcist repute) — a non-confessional, methodologically explicit reference point against which stronger and weaker readings are measured.

**H-band of this source (prose only):** **H-e** (modern scholarly reconstruction/synthesis).

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Theissen is among the most influential NT social-historians; the *Guide* is a standard graduate textbook across confessional and secular faculties.
- **Transparency:** High — a textbook by design: sources, criteria, and counter-positions are laid out per topic.
- **Independence:** High — critical-historical register; routinely reports where evidence is weak (it is not an apologetic work).
- **Intent:** Comprehensive instruction — to state the field's evidence and range of positions, not to advocate a novel thesis.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- A synthesis: its judgments are the *mainstream shape of the field* circa the late 1990s, not primary evidence; primary attestation must still be graded on its own layer (INV-0013).
- Predates the criteria-critique wave (SRC-0124) and the memory-research turn (SRC-0126, SRC-0128); its criteria usage is the classical form.
- Reports scholarly consensus, which KOS-0003 §9 records **separately** from evidence strength.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Jesus was baptized by John the Baptist — treated as historically secure, with the embarrassment logic stated.
- The Kingdom of God as the center of Jesus' proclamation — pervasive across the sayings tradition.
- Jesus was reputed a healer and exorcist; the tradition includes the hostile charge that he acted "in league with the devil" (the Beelzebul accusation) — the repute is secure even where individual narratives are not.
- Crucifixion under Pilate as the firm terminal datum of the ministry.
