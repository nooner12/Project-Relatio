# SRC-0136

# ter Haar (1992) — The White Lotus Teachings in Chinese Religious History

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Barend J. ter Haar, *The White Lotus Teachings in Chinese Religious History* (Leiden: E. J. Brill, 1992; Sinica Leidensia 26; ix + 343 pp.; ISBN 90-04-09414-8). The field's foundational **category-critique**: ter Haar argues that "White Lotus Teachings" conflates two phenomena — a real lay Buddhist movement (c. 1130–1400) and, after c. 1525, a **derogatory label applied by officials and literati rather than a name used by believers** — and that scholarship must distinguish **positive autonyms from disparaging labels**, on pain of reproducing the persecutors' taxonomy. In this KB it is the **dissent-track methodological anchor for CLM-0086**: the standing demonstration that category labels in this literature can be artifacts of outside observers, which is the strongest available form of the challenge to any classification of Yiguandao — including the salvationist/redemptive-society one.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — Brill/Sinica Leidensia; ter Haar is a leading historian of Chinese religion; the book is a standard reference, its critique judged "more carefully presented and more painstakingly defended than any study that has appeared before it" in review.
- **Transparency:** High — a source-critical historical argument.
- **Independence:** High — no stake in Yiguandao (which post-dates its period focus).
- **Intent:** Historiographical correction — to expose label-driven distortion in the study of Chinese sectarianism.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **It does not discuss Yiguandao's classification** (the book's focus predates the movement): its bearing on CLM-0086 is methodological (labels vs. autonyms), and that transfer is this investigation's inference, not ter Haar's claim — disclosed as such.
- It does not assert that the salvationist/redemptive-society category is a distorting label; if anything the modern category was constructed by scholars *aware of* this critique (SRC-0129's reflexive title). The contrast edge records the tension of method, not a head-to-head verdict.
- **Interior not independently read this session** (see §6).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The autonym/label distinction and the two-phenomena thesis (verified from open review and publisher surfaces): scholarly categories in Chinese sectarian studies have historically encoded persecutors' vocabulary; post-1525 "White Lotus" was an outsiders' label.
- Consumed by CLM-0086 as the steelmanned form of category-skepticism: the strongest located "dissent" in this literature is dissent about **category construction**, not a positive classification of Yiguandao as a school of Daoism.
