# SRC-0103

# New Testament Primary Texts — the Pauline Letters and the Gospels

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
The **primary documents** themselves, as physical/textual evidence:
- The **seven undisputed Pauline letters** (Romans, 1–2 Corinthians, Galatians, Philippians, 1 Thessalonians, Philemon), composed c. 50–60 CE — the earliest Christian documents and self-authored, first-person witnesses.
- The **four canonical Gospels** (Mark, Matthew, Luke, John), composed c. 65–110 CE, formally **anonymous** narrative sources.
Cited in this KB from the standard critical text (Nestle-Aland 28). This record is the **H-a / H-b anchor** — the documents and their manuscript witnesses — as distinct from the scholarly evaluations of them (SRC-0091, 0093, 0094, etc.). Earliest physical witnesses: **P52** (John fragment, palaeographic date now held to be a wide 2nd-century window — see SRC-0092); **P46** (Pauline collection, c. 200 CE); **P66/P75** (John/Luke, c. 200 CE); and the great 4th-century uncials **Codex Sinaiticus** and **Codex Vaticanus**, the earliest near-complete/complete witnesses.

**H-band of this record:** **H-a** (contemporaneous primary text) for the Pauline letters as first-generation documents; **H-b** (material/papyrological attestation) for the manuscript witnesses. The Gospels are H-a *as documents* but stand at further narrative remove from the events they report.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** As primary evidence, foundational — the undisputed Pauline letters are uncontestedly first-generation; their authorship (Paul) is internally self-attested and uncontested.
- **Transparency:** The Pauline letters are occasional, self-identifying, and internally datable by synchronisms (e.g. the Gallio inscription anchors Paul in Corinth c. 51–52 CE); the Gospels are anonymous and theologically shaped.
- **Independence:** The Gospels are **not** fully independent of one another (Synoptic literary dependence — CLM-0070); Paul is independent of the Gospels and earlier than all of them.
- **Intent:** The Pauline letters aim to instruct/persuade specific communities; the Gospels aim to proclaim — intent that shapes, without invalidating, their evidentiary use.

# 3. Limitations (what this record does NOT establish)
- The documents attest **what was written and believed**, and (for Paul) a first-hand first-generation voice; they do **not**, by their existence, establish the truth of the events or theology they report (bracketed).
- The Gospels' anonymity and mutual literary dependence bound their independence; the traditional authorial names are **not** internal to the texts (CLM-0071).
- No autograph survives; the wording is known through the manuscript tradition and text-critical reconstruction (CLM-0067; SRC-0091).

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The undisputed Pauline letters (c. 50–60 CE) as the earliest datable stratum (CLM-0068).
- The Gospels' formal anonymity and the embedded pre-literary formulae (1 Cor 15:3–7; Phil 2:6–11; Rom 1:3–4) (CLM-0071, CLM-0074).
- The manuscript witnesses (P52, P46, P66, P75, Sinaiticus, Vaticanus) as the material base for the transmission claim (CLM-0067).

# 6. Verification (STD-0006 §5.7 / §7.5)
**Standard, live-verified for the load-bearing facts (strong).** The composition ranges (undisputed Paul c. 50–60; Gospels c. 65–110) and the manuscript landmarks (P52 as earliest fragment with a now-widened date, SRC-0092; P46 ~200; Sinaiticus/Vaticanus 4th c. as earliest near-complete/complete) are well-established and were confirmed against the dating and P52 searches conducted this session. The **Gallio-inscription synchronism** (Delphi; Claudius's letter; Gallio proconsul of Achaia mid-51 to mid-52 CE; the standard anchor for Paul's Corinth stay) — originally self-graded parametric — was **independently re-located during Critical Review – RQ-0013** and confirmed: **upgraded parametric → live-verified**. No fabrication; no manuscript is invented.
