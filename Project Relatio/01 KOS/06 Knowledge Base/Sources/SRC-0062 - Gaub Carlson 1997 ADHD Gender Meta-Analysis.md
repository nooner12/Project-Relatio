# SRC-0062

# Gaub & Carlson 1997 — Gender Differences in ADHD: A Meta-Analysis and Critical Review

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Gaub, M., & Carlson, C. L. (1997), "Gender differences in ADHD: A meta-analysis and critical review," *Journal of the American Academy of Child & Adolescent Psychiatry* 36(8):1036–1045, DOI 10.1097/00004583-199708000-00023. Meta-analysis of 18 studies. Central load-bearing fact for INV-0010: the male:female ADHD ratio is far higher in **clinic-referred** samples (~10:1) than in **community** samples (~3:1), and clinic-referred girls are more severely affected — evidence that **referral/ascertainment**, not just underlying disorder rate, shapes the apparent sex difference. Citation verified this session via PubMed 9256583 and the JAACAP record.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Construct-provenance code: `CONFLATED`.** The meta-analysis pools studies that compare boys and girls by the recorded **sex category** on rating-scale/diagnostic data; it assesses no biological mechanism and measures no gender-socialization construct. Critically, it is itself *about* the artifact — it shows that the sex-labeled ratio is contaminated by gendered referral pathways (boys' externalizing draws referral; girls' inattentive presentation is missed). So it applies sex labels to ascertainment-shaped data and, to its credit, says so. It **can support only a gender/ascertainment-patterned claim** (operative rule §3.1). Justification: sex-labeled clinic/community samples; the paper's own point is that the label mismeasures the construct.
- **Authority:** High — the canonical meta-analysis on ADHD sex differences.
- **Transparency:** High — separates clinic from community ratios; reports domain-by-domain differences.
- **Independence:** High — aggregates independent samples; explicitly critical rather than advocacy.
- **Intent:** Determine whether ADHD sex differences are real disorder differences or artifacts of referral/measurement.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Does not establish a biological sex difference** in ADHD liability; if anything it argues the apparent difference is inflated by ascertainment.
- **Older diagnostic criteria** (DSM-III-R era studies); subtype/ criteria evolution limits direct mapping to current nosology.
- **Not an onset-timing dating study.** It bears on divergence in *identification* and *clinical presentation*, which is the relevant divergence for ADHD, but it does not date incident neurobiological onset.
- **Heterogeneous instruments** across pooled studies.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Clinic-referred M:F ratio ~10:1 vs community ~3:1 — a large ascertainment gap.
- Referred girls show **greater intellectual impairment and lower hyperactivity/externalizing** than referred boys — consistent with a higher (gendered) threshold for girls to be referred.
- Few gender differences in core impulsivity, academic, or social measures once ascertainment is accounted for.
