# SRC-0105

# Hintze 2015 — "Zarathustra's Time and Homeland"

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Almut Hintze, "Zarathustra's Time and Homeland," in Michael Stausberg & Yuhan Sohrab-Dinshaw Vevaina (eds.), *The Wiley Blackwell Companion to Zoroastrianism* (2015).

A reference-handbook chapter surveying the evidence and argument for dating Zoroaster and locating his milieu, including the comparative-linguistic case that places the Old Avestan (Gathic) material in roughly the second millennium BCE on grounds of its archaism and closeness to Rigvedic Sanskrit.

Catalogued as part of the Iranian-family source base. No investigation is open against it.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High. Hintze is a leading Avestan philologist; the *Wiley Blackwell Companion* is a peer-reviewed reference handbook and a standard orientation point for the field.
- **Transparency:** Moderate-to-high for a handbook. The chapter reports and weighs the dating arguments, but the compressed handbook format means the underlying philological demonstration is summarised rather than performed — the reader is directed to the argument rather than shown all of it.
- **Independence:** Independent scholarly treatment, external to the tradition.
- **Intent:** Synthesis and orientation for a scholarly readership — to present the state of a contested question, not to advance a novel thesis.

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- The early dating it reports is **indirect**: it rests on the relative archaism of Old Avestan against Vedic, not on any dated text, manuscript, or external attestation. Linguistic archaism gives a relative, not an absolute, chronology, and the conversion to calendar dates carries wide margins.
- It is a **handbook chapter**, not the primary philological study; for load-bearing use the underlying literature should be consulted directly.
- It does not resolve the dispute — the wide date range (commonly given as c. 1500–1000 BCE) reflects genuine unsettledness, and a majority scholarly view is not the same as a demonstrated result.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- The comparative-linguistic dating of the Old Avestan corpus and its bearing on Zoroaster's floruit.
- Survey of the homeland question (Central Asian / eastern Iranian milieu).
