# Fiese et al. 2002 — 50 Years of Research on Family Routines and Rituals

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Fiese, B. H., Tomcho, T. J., Douglas, M., Josephs, K., Poltrock, S., & Baker, T. (2002), "A Review of 50 Years of Research on Naturally Occurring Family Routines and Rituals: Cause for Celebration?," *Journal of Family Psychology* 16(4). A **narrative (qualitative) review of ~32 studies** since Bossard & Boll's 1950 seminal work. Anchor for domain 6 (family routines/rituals). Citation, journal/volume/issue, review type, and the 32-study count verified this session via the APA press release, the APA journal PDF, and ResearchGate. **Page range not confirmed to the page.**

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High for the construct — Fiese is the leading scholar of family routines/rituals; peer-reviewed in a flagship family-psychology journal.
- **Transparency:** High — the routine/ritual distinction and reviewed evidence are clearly laid out.
- **Independence:** High — academic.
- **Intent:** Synthesise and conceptually organise a scattered literature (the title's "cause for celebration?" is deliberately cautious).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- It is a **narrative review of predominantly cross-sectional, correlational** studies — **not** a meta-analysis and **not** causal. Associations of routines/rituals with child adjustment, health, and academic outcomes are **confounded**: well-functioning families both ritualize *and* raise well-adjusted children.
- The review itself is **cautious** ("cause for celebration?"), signalling the evidence is suggestive, not settled.
- It concerns **naturally occurring family** routines/rituals; transfer to a **community program's** routines/rituals or to *intergenerational skill transmission* specifically is analogical and thinner still.
- Provides **no effect sizes**; magnitude is unquantified.
- **Funding:** not applicable/not stated.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- **Routines** = instrumental ("this is what needs to be done," momentary); **rituals** = symbolic ("this is who we are," continuity of meaning across generations).
- Routines/rituals **associated with** parenting competence, child adjustment, adolescent identity, child health, academic achievement, and marital satisfaction.

# 5. Relationships (STD-0004)
- `supports` CLM-0039 (family routines/rituals) — the weakest evidence strand in the investigation.
