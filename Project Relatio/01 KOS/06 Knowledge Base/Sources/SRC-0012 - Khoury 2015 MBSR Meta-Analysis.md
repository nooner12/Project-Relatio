# SRC-0012

# Khoury et al. (2015) — MBSR for Healthy Individuals: A Meta-Analysis

## Draft Source Record

---

# 1. Identification
Meta-analysis of mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) in **healthy** (non-clinical) adult populations, published in the *Journal of Psychosomatic Research* (2015). Pools pre-post and between-group controlled comparisons on stress, anxiety, depression, distress, quality of life, and burnout.

# 2. Source Evaluation (KOS-0003 §6)
- **Authority:** High — peer-reviewed quantitative synthesis by an established mindfulness-research group.
- **Transparency:** Moderate-High — meta-analytic methods and effect-size conventions reported; verified this session at abstract/secondary-summary level, not full text.
- **Independence:** Moderate — mindfulness-research authorship; not a product study, but a field with known allegiance effects.
- **Intent:** Inform (quantify MBSR effects in healthy people).

# 3. Limitations (what this source does NOT establish)
- **Does not establish long-term (>1 year) durability.** Reported maintenance is at short follow-up (on the order of ~19 weeks average in the pooled data); this is short-to-medium term, not durable relief as defined by INV-0005.
- Pre-post effect sizes overstate efficacy (no control for regression to the mean, expectancy, natural remission).
- Publication bias and allegiance effects are plausible and not fully excluded.
- Healthy-population focus; does not speak to clinical stress-disorders.

# 4. Key Content / Passages Used
- Effect sizes reported as **Hedges' g ≈ 0.66 (pre-post)** and **≈ 0.53 (between-group)** — moderate — with larger effects on stress and smaller on burnout. *(Reconciled per Critical Review – RQ-0005 §3/§6.4: the between-group g 0.53 matches the primary; the pre-post figure is corrected from the record's earlier "≈0.55" to the reviewer's live-verified primary value **≈0.66**. Non-load-bearing figure — durability grade on CLM-0013 unaffected.)*
