# CLM-0005

# Wu Wei as Effortless Action

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim

> *Wu wei* (無為) is more accurately rendered **"effortless, non-forcing action in accord with the natural way"** than as literal "inaction" or "doing nothing." The literal gloss is misleading; the concept concerns *how* one acts (without contrived striving), not *whether* one acts.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)

**Interpretive** (primary) — a claim about the correct meaning/translation of a concept. Requires framework identification, assumptions, and competing interpretations (KOS-0003 §3.3).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)

Type: **Historical/textual** + **philosophical** (scholarship).

- The paradoxical formulations require it: 為無為 "act without acting" (*DDJ* 63) and 無為而無不為 "does nothing, yet nothing is left undone" (*DDJ* 37) are incoherent under literal "inaction" but sensible under "non-forcing action." (SRC-0002)
- The *Zhuangzi*'s exemplars of *wu wei* (Cook Ding carving the ox, the skilled swimmer) are highly *active* — skilled, absorbed action. (SRC-0002)
- Slingerland (SRC-0005) analyzes *wu wei* precisely as "effortless action," a spiritual/ethical ideal of unselfconscious skilled activity.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)

- Reliability: 5 — the textual formulations are canonical and explicit.
- Relevance: 5 — directly on the meaning of the term.
- Independence: 4 — primary-text logic and independent scholarship agree.
- Quality: 4 — strong; translation of a gnomic term is inherently interpretive.
- Limitations: establishes that "inaction" is wrong and "effortless/non-forcing action" is apt; the precise best English phrase remains debatable.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)

- Evidence strength: High. Consensus strength: High among specialists — the "effortless/non-forcing action" reading is mainstream; literal "inaction" is a lay misreading.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)

- That the paradoxical formulations are meant coherently (not as mere mysticism-for-its-own-sake) — a standard scholarly assumption.
- That the *Zhuangzi* skill-stories exemplify the same *wu wei* the *Daodejing* names (broadly accepted).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)

**Abductive.** "Non-forcing action" is the reading that best makes the paradoxical texts and the active exemplars coherent together. Risk checked: imposing a modern "flow" concept anachronistically — mitigated by grounding in the texts and period scholarship rather than only in modern psychology.

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 4 — High.** Strong textual and scholarly support; residual uncertainty is only about the best English wording.

---

# Limitations

- A translation/interpretation claim, not a claim that any one English phrase is definitive.
- Concerns meaning; the *scope* of application (which schools, which contexts) is treated in CLM-0006.

---

# Alternative Interpretations

1. **Literal inaction/quietism:** *wu wei* = doing nothing. (Rejected — contradicted by the active exemplars and the "nothing left undone" formulations.)
2. **Purely political non-interference:** *wu wei* is only about governance. (Too narrow — see CLM-0006; it is one register, not the whole.)
