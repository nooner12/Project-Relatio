# FND-0003

# Wu Wei as Non-Forcing Action

## Draft Finding Record

---

# 1. Statement

> *Wu wei* is not passivity but **effortless, non-coercive action in accord with *ziran* (spontaneous naturalness)**. Its "action through non-action" paradox dissolves once *wu wei* is read as the absence of *forced, self-conscious striving* rather than the absence of *activity*. This single ideal is expressed across three complementary registers — political (non-interfering governance), self-cultivational (effortless skill), and metaphysical (alignment with the Dao) — rather than being several unrelated concepts.

---

# 2. Supporting Claims

- **CLM-0005** — *wu wei* means effortless/non-forcing action, not literal inaction (resolves the paradox).
- **CLM-0006** — the concept spans three complementary registers (fixes its scope).

---

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 3–4.** The core reading (CLM-0005) is Level 4 with strong scholarly consensus; the unification of registers (CLM-0006) is Level 3 (interpretive, with some dissent). The finding as a whole is held at **Level 3** — no more confident than its interpretive component.

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations

- An interpretation of the concept in early Chinese texts; not a claim that any single English rendering is definitive.
- The "one unified ideal" framing is defensible but contested; a tension-emphasizing reading is legitimate.
- Cross-linguistic (Classical Chinese via English) — lossy.
