# CLM-0006

# Three Interpretive Registers

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim

> *Wu wei* is expressed across at least **three distinct but complementary registers** — **political** (non-interfering governance), **self-cultivational** (effortless skill/mastery), and **metaphysical** (alignment with the Dao's spontaneous operation) — which are facets of one ideal rather than rival definitions.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)

**Interpretive** — a claim about how the concept's uses relate. Requires competing-interpretation analysis.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)

Type: **Historical/textual** + **philosophical**.

- **Political:** the *Daodejing*'s ruler-advice — govern by *wu wei*; the people flourish "of themselves" (*DDJ* 57). (SRC-0002)
- **Self-cultivational:** the *Zhuangzi*'s skill-exemplars — Cook Ding, the swimmer, the woodcarver — effortless mastery. (SRC-0002)
- **Metaphysical:** the Dao itself "does nothing, yet nothing is left undone" (*DDJ* 37); the sage models the cosmos. (SRC-0002)
- Slingerland (SRC-0005) shows the effortless-action ideal spanning schools and contexts, supporting a multi-register rather than single-domain reading.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)

- Reliability: 4 — the three uses are clearly textually attested.
- Relevance: 5 — directly on the scope question.
- Independence: 4 — multiple text-layers plus scholarship.
- Quality: 3 — the "complementary, not rival" framing is an interpretive synthesis.
- Limitations: establishes the three uses exist; whether they are "one ideal" is interpretive.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)

- Evidence strength: High (the three uses). Consensus strength: Moderate — that they form *one* unified ideal is a common but not universal scholarly reading; some emphasize tensions between the political and cultivational strands.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)

- That the *Daodejing* and *Zhuangzi* share enough of a concept to speak of one *wu wei* (broadly but not universally held).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)

**Abductive/synthetic.** A single underlying ideal (non-forcing action in accord with *ziran*) best explains why the same term covers governance, skill, and cosmology. Risk checked: over-harmonizing genuinely divergent texts — mitigated by flagging the political/cultivational tension explicitly.

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 3 — Moderate.** The three registers are well-attested (nearer 4); their unification into "one ideal" is a defensible interpretation with real dissent, so overall Level 3.

---

# Limitations

- The "complementary, not rival" thesis is interpretive; a tension-emphasizing reading is legitimate.

---

# Alternative Interpretations

1. **Fragmented:** *wu wei* is really several different concepts sharing a name. (Partly defensible; rejected as overstated given the shared *ziran*-alignment core.)
2. **Single-register:** *wu wei* is essentially political (or essentially mystical). (Rejected as too narrow.)
