# CLM-0014

# Regular Exercise Reduces Stress and Confers Durable Health Benefit

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Regular physical activity produces **moderate short-term reductions in perceived stress and distress** and favourably shifts stress-response physiology (blunted cortisol reactivity), and — uniquely among the interventions considered — it carries **large, well-established, durable collateral benefits to physical and mental health**, which makes it a strong *foundation* for durable stress resilience even though direct >1-year stress-outcome RCT evidence is only moderate.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal** (exercise reduces stress and improves stress physiology) with a supporting **descriptive** claim about the robustness of exercise's general health benefits.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (meta-analyses, RCTs, and a very large observational base).
- **SRC-0020 (physical-activity/cortisol cluster):** physical activity associated with **blunted, favourable cortisol stress reactivity**; a majority of studies link higher fitness/activity to attenuated cortisol responses.
- **SRC-0016 (Cochrane, Tamminga 2023):** **exercise is a named effective individual-level route** for reducing occupational stress (low-certainty, up to a year).
- **General-health evidence (SRC-0020, background):** exercise's cardiometabolic, sleep, and mood benefits are among the most robust and durable findings in medicine — the basis of the "durable foundation" argument via allostatic load (ENT-0003).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 4** for the general direction; **3** for a *precise* durable stress-specific effect size (not pinned this session).
- **Relevance: 4** — cortisol reactivity is a physiological proxy; perceived-stress outcomes are directly relevant.
- **Independence: 4** — physiological, self-report, and occupational-trial strands converge.
- **Quality: 3–4** — strong for general health; more variable for stress-specific durability RCTs.
- **Limitations:** **No single verified pooled effect size for a durable "chronic stress" outcome was located** (SRC-0020 flagged). Cortisol reactivity is a biomarker, not lived stress. The durability argument leans on *collateral* health durability, which is an inference.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate-High** (direction), **Moderate** (durable stress-specific magnitude).
- Consensus strength: **High** that exercise benefits stress and health broadly.
- Relationship: robustly supported in direction; less precise on durable stress-specific magnitude.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0020 is a cluster read at summary level (no single load-bearing trial); weighted for convergence, not precision. SRC-0016 (Cochrane) supplies the occupational-stress endorsement at low certainty.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** blunted cortisol reactivity indexes reduced allostatic load (ENT-0003) — plausible but a biomarker inference.
- **Causal:** the exercise→stress link is not wholly reverse-causal (less-stressed people exercise more); RCT and occupational-trial strands partly address this.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive + abductive.** Convergent physiological, occupational-trial, and general-health evidence supports a genuine benefit (inductive); the best explanation for exercise's durable value is that it *raises buffering capacity and lowers cumulative load* (abductive, via ENT-0003) rather than only damping an acute episode. **Risks checked:** *reverse causation* (named); *overclaiming a precise durable stress effect* (guarded — magnitude graded down for lack of a verified figure); *commercial convenience* (exercise is not central to the owner's framing, so low bias pressure here — noted for symmetry).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Short-term (stress reduction): Level 3–4** — moderate, well-supported in direction.
- **Long-term, stress-specific durable outcome: Level 2 (Low)** — held down per **Critical Review – RQ-0005 §6.1**: the earlier Level-3 grade leaned on collateral-health durability plus cortisol-reactivity inference, which is a **category transfer** (answering durable-*stress* relief partly via durable-*health* benefit). The direct >1-year stress-outcome RCT base is thin, so the stress-specific durable claim is held at Low.
- **Long-term collateral-health durability (separate, better-grounded proposition): Level 4 (High)** — exercise's cardiometabolic, sleep, and mood benefits are among the most robust and durable findings in medicine. This is stated as a *distinct* proposition from durable stress-specific relief, not merged into it. It is what makes exercise a strong *foundation* for resilience (via allostatic load, ENT-0003) even though the stress-specific durable RCT evidence is only Low.

---

# Limitations
- Direct long-term stress-outcome RCTs are limited; the durable **stress-specific** case is partly inferential and is therefore graded **Level 2 (Low)** per Critical Review – RQ-0005 §6.1. The **collateral-health** durability is a separate, better-grounded proposition and must not be read as evidence for durable stress-specific relief (the category-transfer hazard the reviewer flagged).
- Adherence is the real-world bottleneck — efficacy ≠ sustained practice.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"Exercise durably cures chronic stress."** Overstated — rejected; it reduces stress and builds resilience but does not remove an ongoing stressor.
2. **"The cortisol data are just fitness confounds."** Partly true (reverse causation), but occupational RCTs and the mechanistic story make a genuine effect the better reading — rejected as a full explanation.
3. **"Exercise is only as good as any other active control."** Consistent with Goyal (SRC-0014, CLM-0013) — but exercise's *unique* collateral health durability breaks the tie in its favour as a foundation. Partially accepted.
