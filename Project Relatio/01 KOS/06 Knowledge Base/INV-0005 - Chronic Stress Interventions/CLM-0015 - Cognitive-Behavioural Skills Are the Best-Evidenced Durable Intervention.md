# CLM-0015

# Cognitive-Behavioural Skills Are the Best-Evidenced Durable Intervention

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Among skills-based (individual) interventions, **cognitive-behavioural approaches show the strongest evidence of durability** — and **CBT for insomnia (CBT-I) in particular produces clinically meaningful benefits that persist at 12 months and, in follow-up studies, for years** — whereas generic CBT-based *workplace-stress* programmes have **weaker and more mixed** durability evidence.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal** (CBT-based skills durably improve the target outcome) with a **comparative descriptive** element (CBT-I's durability exceeds that of the other skills interventions considered).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (meta-analysis of RCTs; long-follow-up RCT).
- **SRC-0018 (CBT-I long-term):** meta-analysis (≈30 RCTs vs non-active controls) — Insomnia Severity Index **Hedges' g ≈ 0.64 (3 mo), 0.40 (6 mo), 0.25 (12 mo)**: declining but sustained and clinically meaningful at one year; a follow-up RCT shows maintenance at **1 and 10 years.**
- **SRC-0016 (Cochrane, Tamminga 2023):** **cognitive-behaviour training** named among effective individual routes, effect potentially **up to a year** (low certainty). But note the historical Cochrane finding that CBT ± relaxation was, in some studies, **no better than no intervention at one month** — durability of *generic* work-stress CBT is fragile.
- **SRC-0022 (coping theory):** CBT operationalises cognitive reappraisal and problem-solving, linking it to adaptive coping.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 4** — CBT-I long-term meta-analysis + long-follow-up RCT is a genuinely durable signal; **3** for generic work-stress CBT.
- **Relevance: 4** — CBT-I targets insomnia (a major stress amplifier/consequence), not "stress" directly; the stress link is one inferential step.
- **Independence: 4** — sleep-medicine and occupational-stress literatures converge on CBT durability.
- **Quality: 4** for CBT-I; **3** for work-stress CBT.
- **Limitations:** CBT-I evidence is about **insomnia severity**, not a stress scale — treating it as durable *stress* relief is an inference (sleep is a load pathway, ENT-0003). The CBT-I meta-analysis attribution and effect sizes are now **live-verified** (SRC-0018: van der Zweerde et al. 2019; g 0.64/0.40/0.25 — Critical Review – RQ-0005 §3); the earlier unconfirmed-attribution caveat no longer applies.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **High** (CBT-I durability), **Low-Moderate** (generic work-stress CBT durability).
- Consensus strength: **High** that CBT-I is durably effective for insomnia; it is a guideline first-line treatment.
- Relationship: CBT-I is the single best durability case in this investigation; generalising it to "stress" is where the caution lies.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0018 is high-authority and its attribution/effect sizes are now **live-verified** (van der Zweerde et al. 2019; Critical Review – RQ-0005 §3). SRC-0016 supplies the occupational-CBT signal and its fragility. Weighted toward CBT-I's robust, replicated long-follow-up evidence.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** durable improvement in insomnia meaningfully reduces chronic stress/allostatic load — plausible (sleep is a central pathway) but inferential.
- **Epistemological:** guideline status and long-follow-up RCTs are strong durability evidence.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** CBT-I shows the *declining-but-sustained* pattern (0.64→0.25 at 12 mo) plus multi-year maintenance — the clearest durability profile among the skills interventions. **Risks checked:** *overgeneralisation* (guarded — CBT-I ≠ generic stress-CBT; the claim is explicit that work-stress CBT is weaker); *citation reliability* (SRC-0018 attribution now live-verified, Critical Review – RQ-0005 §3); *category error* (insomnia outcome vs stress outcome — flagged as an inferential step, not equated).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Short-term: Level 4 (High)** — CBT-I and CBT skills reduce their target outcomes short-term.
- **Long-term: Level 4 (High) for CBT-I specifically; Level 2 (Low) for generic work-stress CBT.** The split confidence is deliberate — CBT-I is the strongest durable case in the whole investigation, but that strength does not transfer wholesale to "stress."

---

# Limitations
- CBT-I's durability is for *insomnia*; its chronic-stress benefit is inferred via the sleep pathway.
- Generic stress-management CBT durability is weak/mixed.
- Access and cost limit real-world uptake.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"CBT durably fixes chronic stress."** Overstated for *generic* stress-CBT — rejected; accepted only for CBT-I on its own (insomnia) endpoint.
2. **"CBT-I is irrelevant to stress."** Rejected — sleep disruption is both a driver and a consequence of chronic stress (ENT-0003); durably fixing it plausibly lowers load.
3. **"The 10-year maintenance is selection/survivorship."** Possible for the single follow-up RCT, but the meta-analytic 12-month data are more robust; partially conceded, does not overturn the claim.
