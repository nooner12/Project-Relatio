# CLM-0043

# Some LOPI Mechanisms Can Be Partially Reproduced in a Single-Child Home; Others Depend on Community Structures a Single Home Lacks and Degrade Without Them — and the Best-Evidenced Mechanism Is Among Those That Degrade

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Translating LOPI to a **single-child home-education setting** (one child, no multi-age peer cohort, limited genuinely shared productive work) is only **partially** possible. **Reproducible at reduced strength:** a child observing and pitching in to a parent's *real, purposeful* activity (cooking, repair, gardening, caregiving) instantiates the observation and contribution facets. **Largely absent by construction:** the **community-organization** and **mixed-age collaborative** facets, and — critically — **learning from interactions directed to *other* learners**, which is the mechanism with the *strongest* experimental support (SRC-0057/0058). No located study evaluates LOPI in a single-child home; this transfer is **entirely analogical**.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Transfer / external-validity** claim — descriptive about substrate availability, plus a **normative/methodological** implication about how weakly the compound can be asserted for a solitary learner. **No direct evidence exists**; the claim is reasoned from the mechanisms' own requirements.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Meta-evidential / analogical** — reasoned from the mechanism evidence (CLM-0042) and the construct's structure (ENT-0007), not from a study of home settings.
- **SRC-0057 / SRC-0058:** the best-evidenced mechanism *requires an interaction directed to another child* — a substrate a single-child home **lacks by definition**. (These sources `contrast_with` this claim: their strength is precisely the transfer's weakness.)
- **SRC-0054 (Rogoff 2014):** LOPI is a **constellation** of interdependent, **community-organized** facets — facet 1 (community organization) and facet 3 (mixed-age collaboration) have **no substrate** in a single home; the framework itself forbids treating a facet as a portable ingredient.
- **No located study** tests LOPI, or any facet, in a single-child home-education setting.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 2** — the claim rests on the *structure* of the mechanism evidence, not on any home-setting study; robust as reasoning, absent as direct evidence.
- **Relevance: 5** — this is the consequential question the investigation exists to answer.
- **Independence: 3** — the substrate gap is visible across all sources independently.
- **Quality: 2** — no direct evidence; an analogical/deductive transfer claim, appropriately bounded.
- **Limitations:** establishes that transfer is **partial and unproven**, and that the **strongest** mechanism degrades most; it does **not** establish that home LOPI *fails* (absence of evidence), nor quantify how much of the partial transfer survives.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Low** — no direct evidence; strong *a priori* reasoning about substrate.
- Consensus strength: n/a (a structural/transfer observation, not a contested empirical claim).
- Relationship: this claim **caps** how far CLM-0041/0042 may be carried toward a solitary learner.

---

# Source Evaluation
Rests on the *requirements* of SRC-0057/0058 and the *structure* of SRC-0054, plus the demonstrable absence of any single-child-home study. The reasoning is strong; the evidence tier is low because nothing directly measures the target setting.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Epistemological:** a community-embedded constellation does not transfer facet-by-facet to a non-community setting — well supported by the construct's own framing.
- **Methodological:** the search is broad enough to say "no located study" — probable, not certain (stated as *located*, not *existent*).
- **Substantive:** a single home *can* supply "real, purposeful activity" for a child to observe and join — plausible, and the one genuinely transferable piece.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Deductive-from-requirements + abductive.** **Risks checked:** *absence-of-evidence fallacy* (named — unproven ≠ fails; a thoughtful home could still realise the transferable facets); *composition/portability fallacy* (named — LOPI's own constellation framing forbids facet-extraction); *motivated transfer* (named — the culturally attractive reading "just include your kid in real life and skip instruction" over-reads both the transfer *and* the base finding, which never said instruction is unnecessary).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 1 (Very Low)** for any positive claim that "LOPI reproduced in a single-child home yields the documented competence benefits" — no direct evidence, entirely analogical, and the best-evidenced mechanism lacks substrate. **Reaching toward Level 2 for the *observation* facet specifically** — a child keenly observing a parent's genuine purposeful activity (cooking, repair, gardening, caregiving) is a real, if reduced, instance of LOPI's observation facet, and this is the most transferable piece. **The *contribution-to-a-shared-endeavor* facet is weaker still** (below the observation facet, not level with it): genuinely shared, consequential productive work is **scarce in a modern single-child home** — most household tasks are brief and individual, unlike the subsistence/community endeavors the source communities offer — so a child's authentic *contribution to a shared endeavor* has thinner substrate than mere observation (Critical Review – RQ-0009 §3, F-1). **This claim is deliberately not inflated by the Moderate grades on CLM-0041/0042** — the base findings' strength does **not** transfer to the solitary-learner setting.

---

# Limitations
- No direct evidence in the target setting; analogical only.
- Establishes partial-and-unproven transfer, **not** failure.
- The one transferable piece (observe/join a parent's real work) is the least community-dependent facet and the least experimentally quantified.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"A rich home with real shared work and a present, skilled parent reproduces enough of LOPI to matter."** The strongest steelman for transfer — plausible for the observation/contribution facets, but it cannot supply the community, mixed-age, or learning-from-others facets, and no study tests it; it holds the grade at Very Low, reaching Low.
2. **"Because the community facets are absent, home LOPI is worthless."** Rejected symmetrically — absence of the community facets is not evidence the transferable facets fail; the honest position is *partial and undemonstrated*, not *disproven*.
