# CLM-0042

# The Learning Is Carried by Specific Mechanisms — Keen Observation of Purposeful Activity, Contribution to a Shared Endeavor, and Coordinated/Shared Attention — of Which the Shared-Attention Mechanism Has the Strongest (Experimental) Support

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The competence-building in CLM-0041 is carried by identifiable mechanisms: **(i) keen observation of purposeful, ongoing activity; (ii) purposeful contribution to a shared endeavor; and (iii) coordinated/shared attention** — including learning from interactions **directed to others**. Of these, the **shared-attention mechanism is the best-evidenced** (two controlled experiments); the **contribution/initiative mechanism is supported ethnographically** but not experimentally isolated.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal (mechanism)** for the attention component (experimentally supported, though via comparative designs); **descriptive/mechanistic** for the contribution component (ethnographic).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** — two comparative experiments + ethnographic synthesis.
- **SRC-0057 (Correa-Chávez & Rogoff 2009):** non-addressed Guatemalan Mayan-traditional children (n=40) showed **more sustained attention and one-week-later learning** of a toy-construction demonstration directed to a sibling than Mayan-schooled (n=40) or European American (n=40) children.
- **SRC-0058 (Silva et al. 2010):** **replicated** in U.S. Mexican-heritage children (n=44 vs 36), the effect tracking maternal Western-schooling experience.
- **SRC-0056 (Paradise & Rogoff 2009):** ethnographically documents the **contribution/initiative** and **keen-observation** mechanisms, and that communication (verbal + nonverbal) is coordinated through shared reference.
- **Design/population:** children 5–11; comparative-experimental (attention) + ethnographic (contribution).

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — two converging controlled experiments (strong for the attention mechanism); the contribution mechanism rests on ethnography.
- **Relevance: 4** — mechanisms are exactly what a transfer question needs.
- **Independence: 3** — replication across two populations (Mayan, Mexican-heritage) is genuine convergence, but within one research program.
- **Quality: 3** — experiments are well-designed but **comparative, not randomized** (cultural confound); the measured outcome is narrow (toy-construction attention/recall).
- **Limitations:** establishes that the **shared-attention mechanism is real and culturally cultivated**; does **not** cleanly isolate it from the cultural constellation, and does **not** quantify the **contribution** mechanism.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** — strongest for shared attention; weaker for contribution/initiative.
- Consensus strength: **Moderate–High** that the attention style is culturally patterned and consequential for learning.
- Relationship: mechanism supported; magnitude and isolation bounded.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0057/0058: the investigation's strongest empirical anchors — genuine controlled comparisons with out-groups, replicated across populations. Their shared design feature (**learning from an interaction directed to another child**) is both their strength and the pivot for the transfer limit (CLM-0043).

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** the toy-construction attention task **indexes** everyday observational learning — a reasonable analogue, but narrow.
- **Causal:** comparative group differences reflect the attention *mechanism* rather than only the confound — partly; held at Moderate for this reason.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive + experimental corroboration.** **Risks checked:** *cultural confound* (named — groups differ by background, not random assignment); *narrow-outcome generalisation* (named — toy-construction recall ≠ broad competence); *mechanism conflation* (named — attention is well-evidenced; contribution is ethnographic, not to be graded as if experimentally shown).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** for the mechanism set as a whole — driven by the **shared-attention** mechanism (two converging experiments, reaching toward Level 4 on its own) and held down by the **contribution/initiative** mechanism (ethnographic only, ~Level 2) and by the comparative confound. **Not higher:** comparative designs, narrow measured competence, single research program.

---

# Limitations
- Attention mechanism: strong but comparative (confounded) and narrowly measured.
- Contribution mechanism: ethnographic, not experimentally isolated.
- The best-evidenced mechanism specifically involves **other learners present** — see CLM-0043.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"The attention effect is just the cultural confound (schooling, language), not a transferable mechanism."** Partly conceded — it is why the grade is Moderate; but the replication across two populations and the out-group contrast make a real attention mechanism the better explanation.
2. **"Contribution/initiative is the real engine, not attention."** Plausible ethnographically, but **not experimentally shown** — so it is carried at lower weight, not elevated above the attention evidence.
