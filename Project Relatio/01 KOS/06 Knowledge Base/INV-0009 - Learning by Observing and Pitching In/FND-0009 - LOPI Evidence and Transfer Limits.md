# FND-0009

# Children in the Studied Communities Develop Real Competence Through Observing and Pitching In (Best-Evidenced for Shared Attention); Transfer of This to a Single-Child Home Is Partial and Undemonstrated — and the Mechanism with the Strongest Science Is the One That Travels Worst

## Draft Finding Record

> **Gate (STD-0006 §7.5).** Wellness Model research input only (individual↔family scale). **Not** guidance to any family, **not** a curriculum, **not** client-facing; does not reach a participant, prospect, or the public, and is not handed to anyone as instruction, without independent re-verification outside Project Relatio's circuit (and, for any LLC use, clinical-research-precision + safety routing). Confirm against the newest Standing Context on the claude.ai side.
>

# 1. Statement
> **(a) The base phenomenon is real and reasonably well-documented — in its communities.** In the Indigenous-heritage American and comparative communities studied by the LOPI/intent-participation program, children develop genuine, valued competence **substantially through keenly observing and contributing to ongoing family and community activity**, rather than primarily through instruction organized as a separate activity (**CLM-0041, Level 3**). The learning is carried by identifiable mechanisms — observation of purposeful activity, contribution to a shared endeavor, and coordinated/**shared attention** — of which **shared attention is the best-evidenced**, by two converging controlled experiments across two populations (**CLM-0042, Level 3**).
>
> **(b) The evidence has two honest limits.** The strongest quantitative evidence is **comparative** (groups defined by cultural background / maternal Western-schooling history), so "participation" is **entangled with the whole community structure** — language, kinship, subsistence, multi-age cohorts — and is not isolated as a lever. And the **measured competence is narrow** (attention to, and one-week reproduction of, a specific toy-construction demonstration), a laboratory analogue of everyday observational learning, not broad or academic competence. LOPI is a **constellation** its own authors say is community-organized and not decomposable into portable ingredients.
>
> **(c) Transfer to a single-child home is partial and undemonstrated (CLM-0043, Level 1 → 2).** A child observing and pitching in to a **parent's real, purposeful activity** instantiates two facets at reduced strength (Level 2 for that piece). But the **community-organization** and **mixed-age collaborative** facets, and — decisively — **learning from interactions directed to *other* learners** (the mechanism with the *strongest* experimental support), have **no substrate** in a single-child home. **No located study tests LOPI in that setting**; the transfer is entirely analogical. The blunt asymmetry: **the part of LOPI with the best science is exactly the part a solitary learner cannot get.**
>
> **(d) What this is NOT.** Not a finding that instruction is unnecessary (LOPI includes guidance and judicious speech). Not a validation of any home-education method. Not a claim about any specific child. Not a universal claim about all children.

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Base phenomenon: Level 3 (Moderate).** Convergent ethnographic + review + two-population experimental evidence, bounded by the comparative (culturally confounded) design, the narrow measured outcome, and a largely single research program (checked by out-group comparisons).

**Transfer to a single-child home: Level 1 (Very Low), reaching Level 2** only for the observation-of / contribution-to a parent's genuine activity facets. This is **capped independently of** the base grade: the strength of CLM-0041/0042 does **not** propagate to the solitary-learner setting, because the mechanisms that carry the base finding (especially learning from interactions directed to *others*) are the ones that lack substrate at home, and **no study tests the setting.**

**No claim reaches Level 5.** The synthesis is a convergence of separately-designed evidence plus a reasoned transfer limit — genuine, but interpretive for the base and analogical for the transfer.

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations (what is NOT supported)
- **NOT supported:** that single-child home LOPI produces the documented competence benefits — undemonstrated (CLM-0043).
- **NOT supported:** that instruction is unnecessary — LOPI explicitly includes guidance (SRC-0056).
- **NOT supported:** a large or academic-competence effect — the measured outcome is narrow observational learning.
- **NOT supported:** isolation of "participation" from community structure — the evidence is comparative/confounded.
- **NOT addressed:** any specific child, any curriculum, and the other four candidate investigations.
- **Provenance limit:** four of five sources are from one research program; the framework is proponent-authored — the out-group experimental comparisons are the main independent check.
- **Motivated-reasoning guard:** both the anti-instruction romantic reading and the transfer-flattering "include your kid in real life = LOPI" reading were named and held down.

# 5. Implication (offered — not guidance, and gated)
At the literature level, the evidence supports a **narrow, honest** transfer: a single-child home can realistically offer **observation of, and genuine contribution to, a parent's real purposeful activity**, which aligns with two LOPI facets — while it **cannot** supply the community, mixed-age, or learning-from-other-learners facets that carry the strongest evidence. Any home-education inference is therefore a **weak, analogical design orientation, not a demonstrated method**, is **gated** (above), and would need its own evaluation. Whether any particular home arrangement delivers even the transferable facets, and with what effect, is an **empirical question outside this finding's scope.**
