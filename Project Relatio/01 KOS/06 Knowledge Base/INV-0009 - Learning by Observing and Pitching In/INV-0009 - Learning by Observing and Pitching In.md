# 1. Research Question
**Original:** What is actually demonstrated about children developing competence through participation in real family/community activity (the LOPI research program), and how far do those findings survive translation to a single-child home-education setting?

**Refined:** Retained as posed, decomposed into a **primary claim** (participation-based competence in the studied communities) and two **sub-claims** — **(A)** the mechanisms that carry the learning, and **(B)** transfer to a single-child home. Rationale: the question explicitly asks both "what is demonstrated" and "how far does it transfer," so the primary/mechanism/transfer split is a **clarifying decomposition, not a redirection** (ROLE-0002 §4.2a). Each claim is rated **separately**; sub-claim B is expected to be the weakest and is deliberately **not** inflated by A.

---

# 2. Scope & Disambiguation
**In scope:**
- The **descriptive core of LOPI** (keen observation, contribution to shared/ongoing activity, flexible/shared attention, learning organized around real endeavor) as documented in the Rogoff-and-colleagues program on Indigenous-heritage American and comparative communities.
- The **transfer question**: which LOPI mechanisms plausibly hold for a single home-educated child absent the multi-age community and shared productive work the source communities have.

**Out of scope / bracketed:**
- Any **homeschool curriculum design or prescription**, and any recommendation aimed at a **specific child** (gated; ADR-GOV-0002 / the gate above).
- The **other four candidate investigations** (attention/EF, readiness, mixed-age, play/movement) — separate investigations (one-at-a-time rule).
- **Universal claims** about all children, and any claim that **instruction is unnecessary** (LOPI includes guidance — SRC-0056).
- **Causal magnitude** — the evidence is descriptive/comparative; no effect sizes are invented (KOS-0003 §12.1).

**Disambiguation:** "LOPI" / "intent (community) participation" are the same construct (ENT-0007). "Competence" here is competence **as valued in the studied communities**, measured in the experiments as narrow toy-construction attention/learning.

# 4. Findings / Synthesis
See **[[FND-0009 - LOPI Evidence and Transfer Limits]]**. In brief: in the studied communities, children **do** develop real competence substantially through observing and contributing to ongoing activity (CLM-0041, **Level 3**), carried by mechanisms of which **shared attention is best-evidenced** by two converging experiments (CLM-0042, **Level 3**). But the evidence is **comparative** (entangled with community structure), the measured competence is **narrow**, and — decisively for the transfer question — the **best-evidenced mechanism requires other learners present.** Transfer to a **single-child home** is therefore **partial and unproven** (CLM-0043, **Level 1**, reaching Level 2 only for a child observing/joining a parent's real work): the community, mixed-age, and learning-from-others facets have no substrate in a solitary setting, and **no study tests LOPI there.** The honest headline: **LOPI is a well-documented community phenomenon; it is not demonstrated to reproduce in a single-child home, and the part with the strongest science is the part that travels worst.**

# 6. Assumptions & Bracketing (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Motivated-reasoning guard (named).** Two convenient conclusions were read *against*: (i) the romantic "children learn everything by participating, so instruction is unnecessary" — rejected (LOPI includes guidance; SRC-0056 corrects the nonverbal/no-teaching misreading); and (ii) the transfer-flattering "just include your kid in real life and you get LOPI" — held to Very Low, because the community and other-learner facets are absent in a single home.
- **Cultural-confound assumption (the key limit on the base finding).** The quantitative evidence is **comparative** (groups by cultural background / maternal Western-schooling), so "participation" is entangled with language, kinship, subsistence, and multi-age structure. The finding tracks the sources' own framing (a community-embedded constellation), not an isolated lever.
- **Constellation/portability assumption (the key limit on transfer).** LOPI's own model (SRC-0054) forbids facet-by-facet extraction; the transfer claim respects this.
- **Search-completeness.** "No located study" of single-child-home LOPI is bounded by this session's search — stated as *located*, not *existent*.
- **Bracketed:** curriculum/individual-child advice; effect-size magnitude; the other four candidate investigations; durability.
