# CLM-0012

# Exercise and Mediterranean Diet Improve Control but Rarely Alone Remit

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Structured exercise and a Mediterranean-style diet **reliably improve insulin sensitivity and glycaemic control** and lower diabetes risk, but **on their own — absent substantial weight loss — they less often produce consensus-defined remission** than the ≥10–15 kg weight-loss route.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal** — these interventions improve metabolic outcomes — with a comparative element (weaker remission signal than large weight loss).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (RCTs, cohorts, meta-analyses — reported here at directional strength).
- **SRC-0011:** Mediterranean-diet RCT meta-analysis reported an HbA1c reduction on the order of **~0.3% absolute** with fasting-glucose and BMI improvements; PREDIMED/PREDIMED-Plus link adherence to lower T2D incidence. Exercise meta-analyses: any exercise beats none for glycaemic control; **combined aerobic+resistance > aerobic alone**; improved insulin sensitivity.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — large evidence base and consistent direction, but the specific figures were **not pinned to individual verified citations this session** (SRC-0011 §3).
- **Relevance: 4** — addresses "interventions that improve glycaemic control / insulin sensitivity" (RQ part a); less central to remission (part b).
- **Independence: 4** — many independent studies converge on direction.
- **Quality: 3** — heterogeneous; adherence-dependent; the ~0.3% HbA1c figure is directional/approximate.
- **Limitations:** Establishes **improvement in control and insulin sensitivity**, not that diet/exercise **alone** reliably remit established diabetes. Specific effect sizes are approximate and **to be verified** before precise quotation.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** (direction strong; this record's specific numbers under-verified).
- Consensus strength: **High** that exercise and Mediterranean-style eating improve glycaemic control and reduce diabetes risk.
- Relationship: direction consensual; the remission magnitude is modest without weight loss.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0011 is a **deliberately-graded composite**: high aggregate authority, but specific DOIs unpinned — hence the reliability discount and the "verify before quoting" flag, rather than a fabricated precise citation.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** meta-analytic averages generalise across diet/exercise heterogeneity.
- **Behavioural:** effects are contingent on sustained adherence.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** Consistent multi-study direction supports the control/insulin-sensitivity benefit; the comparatively modest remission signal follows from the weight-loss dose–response (CLM-0007). Risks checked: **false precision** — the ~0.3% figure is flagged approximate; **overclaiming** — remission is not attributed to diet/exercise alone.

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 3 — Moderate.** High confidence in the *qualitative* benefit (improved control and insulin sensitivity); moderate/low confidence in the *specific effect sizes* (under-verified) and appropriately low confidence in "diet/exercise alone → remission."

---

# Limitations
- Under-verified specific figures (this session).
- Benefit ≠ remission; weight loss remains the stronger remission lever.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **Exercise/Mediterranean diet can remit diabetes by themselves.** *Bounded/mostly rejected* — they improve control and, where they drive substantial weight loss, contribute to remission; alone, without that weight loss, the remission signal is weak.
2. **These effects are trivial.** *Rejected* — a ~0.3% HbA1c improvement plus insulin-sensitivity gains are clinically meaningful, even if not remission-inducing.
