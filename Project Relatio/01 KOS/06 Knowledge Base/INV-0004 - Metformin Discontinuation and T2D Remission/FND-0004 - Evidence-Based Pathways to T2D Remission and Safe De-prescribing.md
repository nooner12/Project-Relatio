# FND-0004

# Evidence-Based Pathways to T2D Remission and Safe De-prescribing

## Draft Finding Record

> **Educational evidence review — NOT individualized medical advice.** This finding describes what the general evidence supports. It does not assess any specific person's suitability for any intervention or for stopping medication. That is a clinical decision requiring a treating physician (see INV-0004 §2, §6).

---

# 1. Statement

> **The evidence supports a real but conditional conclusion.** Substantial, sustained weight loss (≈10–15 kg) can induce genuine remission of type 2 diabetes — HbA1c <6.5% off all glucose-lowering medication — in a meaningful share of people, principally by removing excess liver and pancreas fat and allowing beta-cell function to recover (CLM-0007, CLM-0008). The most-evidenced pathways, in descending intensity, are **metabolic/bariatric surgery** (most durable), **structured low-calorie diet** (e.g. total diet replacement, as in DiRECT: ~46% remission at 1 year, dose-dependent on weight lost), and **carbohydrate-restricted continuous care** (improves control and cuts medication, with more modest true remission) (CLM-0007, CLM-0010). Exercise and a Mediterranean-style diet reliably improve glycaemic control and insulin sensitivity and are valuable adjuncts, but alone — without substantial weight loss — rarely produce remission (CLM-0012).
>
> **Three honest caveats govern the conclusion.** (1) **Likelihood is not uniform and declines with longer diabetes duration and lost beta-cell reserve** — the key caveat for anyone diabetic for many years (CLM-0009). (2) **Remission is not cure:** it requires at least yearly monitoring and it relapses in a substantial majority over years (DiRECT: ~46% → ~10% remission from year 1 to year 5), tracking weight regain (CLM-0011). (3) **Metformin must never be stopped unilaterally:** de-prescribing is a physician-supervised, monitored process (CLM-0011).

---

# 2. Supporting Claims

- **CLM-0007** — substantial weight loss can induce remission; dose-dependent (the core "yes, it's possible" claim). *Level 4.*
- **CLM-0008** — mechanism: ectopic-fat removal restores insulin sensitivity and beta-cell function (why it works). *Level 4.*
- **CLM-0009** — likelihood declines with duration / beta-cell loss (who it works for; the scenario's key caveat). *Level 3.*
- **CLM-0010** — carbohydrate restriction improves control and reduces medication; modest true remission (an alternative route). *Level 3.*
- **CLM-0011** — de-prescribing must be supervised; remission relapses (the safety frame). *Level 4.*
- **CLM-0012** — exercise / Mediterranean diet improve control but rarely remit alone (adjunct pathways). *Level 3.*

---

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)

**Level 3–4 — Moderate-to-High.** The *existence and mechanism* of weight-loss-induced remission are High (Level 4, randomised + mechanistic + consensus). The *overall practical answer* is held at the level of its weakest necessary links — the **duration/likelihood** claim (CLM-0009, Level 3) and **durability** (CLM-0011) — so the integrated finding does not exceed **Level 3–4** and never reaches Level 5. A synthesis is never more confident than its weakest necessary claim (KOS-0003; ROLE-0002 §7).

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations

- **Not medical advice; the individual case is bracketed.** Whether the woman in the motivating scenario can achieve remission or reduce metformin is *undetermined here*.
- **Population mismatch:** the strongest trial evidence (DiRECT) is in people diagnosed ≤6 years, obese, not on insulin — it generalises imperfectly to long-duration, long-medicated patients.
- **Durability beyond ~5 years is unknown.**
- **Endpoint discipline:** "remission" here always means the 2021 consensus definition (SRC-0010); trial terms like "reversal" or "medication reduction" are weaker and not equated with it.
- **Evidence-integrity flags carried up from the claims:** some secondary figures (exact STAMPEDE 5-year percentages, Virta 1-year rates, the Mediterranean-diet meta-analytic effect size) were **not independently re-verified this session** and are flagged on their Source/Claim records rather than asserted as precise — see INV-0004 §6.
- **Motivated-reasoning bracket:** the finding is deliberately stated against the "cure without drugs" narrative; it survives only in conditional, safety-gated form.
