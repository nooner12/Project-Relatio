# FND-0006

# Sustained Wellness Behaviour Is Best Supported by Self-Regulation, Autonomous Motivation, and Habit — Not Willpower or Incentives

## Draft Finding Record

---

# 1. Statement
> **(a) Why people fail to maintain wellness interventions.** The dominant failure is not lack of desire but the **intention–behaviour gap**: intention is a weak, insufficient cause of action, and roughly half of genuine intenders fail to act (CLM-0019). Three factors widen and re-open that gap: **low-quality motivation** (behaviour driven by external pressure/guilt/reward rather than autonomous value, plus low self-efficacy) predicts dropout (CLM-0020); **maintenance is governed by different determinants than initiation** — habit, self-regulation, adequate resources, and a supportive environment — so behaviour relapses when these are absent, effortful, depleted, or disrupted by life events (CLM-0021); and in **self-directed/digital** formats, **high early attrition is the baseline expectation** regardless of content quality (CLM-0022).
>
> **(b) What has the strongest evidence for counteracting these failures.** The best-evidenced levers target *translation and automatization* of behaviour, not exhortation. **Self-monitoring combined with other self-regulation techniques** (goal-setting, feedback, review — ideally recorded and made accountable) is the most consistently supported ingredient, with **small-to-moderate** effects (CLM-0023). **Implementation intentions / if-then action planning** are the most direct gap-closer for people who already intend to act (medium-to-large *proximal* effect; CLM-0024). **Autonomy-supportive** approaches that build internal, valued motivation and self-efficacy are associated with better maintenance than external control (CLM-0020). **Habit formation** through consistent context-cued repetition makes behaviour self-sustaining, though it takes longer and varies more than the popular "21-day" myth (median ≈66 days, wide range; CLM-0025). **Financial incentives** reliably work *while in place* but fade after removal — a useful initiator, a poor maintainer, and a caution that buying behaviour with external reward does not build durability (CLM-0026).
>
> **Bottom line.** There is **no reliable formula that makes people stick** with a wellness practice. The honest, evidence-based strategy is to **combine** self-regulation skills (self-monitoring + if-then planning), **autonomous** (valued, not pressured) motivation, and **deliberate habit-building around stable cues** — while accepting that even the best-evidenced techniques have **modest effects** and that long-term (>12-month) **maintenance evidence is thin** across the board. These are the **best-evidenced levers**, not a guaranteed recipe.

---

# 2. Supporting Claims
- **CLM-0019** — The intention-behaviour gap is the central failure point (**Level 4**). *Locates the problem: enactment, not desire.*
- **CLM-0020** — Motivation quality / self-efficacy predict maintenance; autonomy support is the corresponding lever (**Level 3**). *Spans factor and remedy.*
- **CLM-0021** — Maintenance depends on different determinants than initiation — habit, self-regulation, resources, environment (**Level 3**). *Why initial success doesn't predict maintenance.*
- **CLM-0022** — Early attrition is the norm in self-directed/digital formats (**Level 3**). *The delivery-mode failure a wellness business most needs to plan for.*
- **CLM-0023** — Self-monitoring + self-regulation is among the best-evidenced ingredients, small-to-moderate effect (**Level 4**). *Strongest single technique cluster.*
- **CLM-0024** — Implementation intentions narrow the gap, medium-to-large proximal effect (**Level 4**). *Most direct gap-closer, but a translation tool only.*
- **CLM-0025** — Habit via context-repetition supports maintenance, slower than the myth (**Level 3**). *The self-sustaining mechanism.*
- **CLM-0026** — Incentives work while present, fade after removal (**Level 4**). *What does NOT durably work — the anti-shortcut.*

---

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 3 (Moderate).** Capped at its **weakest necessary links**. The finding's structural core — *maintenance is best supported by self-regulation + autonomous motivation + habit, and external shortcuts don't endure* — depends necessarily on CLM-0020, CLM-0021, and CLM-0025 (each **Level 3 / Moderate**, resting on correlational, theory-synthesis, or single-study evidence). Two legs are firmer (Level 4: the intention-behaviour gap CLM-0019, the self-monitoring and implementation-intention evidence CLM-0023/0024, and the incentive-fade CLM-0026), and the *diagnostic* half of the finding is better supported than the *prescriptive* half. The synthesis therefore cannot exceed **Moderate**, and explicitly **does not** reach High. Accordingly the finding is framed as the **best-evidenced levers**, not a sufficiency claim or a guaranteed build.

**A load-bearing honesty caveat:** the strongest evidence across this field concerns **initiation and short-to-medium-term** outcomes; **long-term (>12-month) maintenance evidence is thin for every technique here** — consistent with the durability findings of INV-0004 (T2D lifestyle change) and INV-0005 (chronic-stress interventions). No technique reliably guarantees durable maintenance.

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations
- **Educational evidence review, NOT individualized medical, psychological, or behavioural advice.** It describes what the general evidence supports, not what any specific person or client should do.
- **Professional help is warranted** where non-adherence reflects clinical depression, anxiety, eating disorders, or where a wellness change interacts with a medical condition or medication; this finding does not substitute for a clinician.
- **Effect sizes are modest.** The best-evidenced techniques produce small-to-moderate improvements; they improve the odds, they do not guarantee adherence.
- **Long-term maintenance evidence is thin** across the field; "up to ~12 months" is often the ceiling of follow-up, not a demonstrated plateau. Absence of long-term evidence is treated as **uncertainty**, not proof of failure.
- **Domain and population limits:** the strongest anchors are physical-activity and eating behaviour (Michie, Rhodes, Teixeira, Ashford); extension to meditation, breathwork, and sleep practices is partly inferential. Much evidence is from general/occupational adults.
- **Motivated-reasoning disclosure (see §6 of INV-0006):** an owner who runs a wellness business would find a confident "here is how to make people stick" conclusion commercially convenient. The evidence was read *against* that prior, and the convenient conclusion **did not fully survive**: there is no reliable adherence formula, effects are modest, durability evidence is thin, and the technique most adjacent to a "sell motivation" model — **external incentives — is precisely the one that fails after removal (CLM-0026)**. Symmetrically, autonomy-supportive motivation (CLM-0020), which *is* congenial to an "align, don't force" brand, was graded down to Moderate for its correlational evidence base rather than inflated. The finding is stated only in its qualified form.
