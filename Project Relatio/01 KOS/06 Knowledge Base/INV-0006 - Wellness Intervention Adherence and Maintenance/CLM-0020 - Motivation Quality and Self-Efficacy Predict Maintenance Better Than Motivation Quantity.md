# CLM-0020

# Motivation Quality and Self-Efficacy Predict Maintenance Better Than Motivation Quantity

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> A common driver of non-maintenance is **low-quality motivation**: behaviour undertaken for **controlled/external** reasons (guilt, pressure, appearance, short-term reward) is sustained less well than behaviour driven by **autonomous** motivation (identified value and intrinsic enjoyment). Low **self-efficacy** is an additional predictor of dropout. Correspondingly, **autonomy-supportive** approaches that build autonomous motivation and self-efficacy are associated with better sustained wellness behaviour than approaches relying on external pressure or incentive.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Causal** (motivation quality influences maintenance) with a **descriptive** element (autonomous motivation correlates consistently with sustained behaviour). This claim spans **both halves** of the RQ: motivation quality is a *factor* in failure and autonomy support is an *intervention*.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (systematic review of experimental, prospective, and cross-sectional studies).
- **SRC-0030 (Teixeira et al. 2012):** systematic review of 66 studies applying Self-Determination Theory to exercise. **Consistent positive relations between autonomous motivation (identified + intrinsic regulation) and exercise behaviour**; good evidence that autonomy-supportive contexts and need satisfaction foster physical activity.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — a large, consistent evidence base, but much of it is **cross-sectional/prospective (correlational)**, not RCT; and it is a narrative systematic review, not a pooled effect size.
- **Relevance: 4** — directly about *sustained* activity motivation, the core of the maintenance question.
- **Independence: 3** — co-authored by SDT originators (theoretical investment); offset by a multi-lab base. Self-efficacy evidence (Bandura tradition) is invoked conceptually, not separately catalogued here.
- **Quality: 3** — good for a narrative systematic review; the correlational majority caps causal strength.
- **Limitations:** Establishes a **consistent association** between autonomous motivation and sustained activity; it does **not** cleanly prove that *manipulating* motivation quality causes durable maintenance in RCTs, nor quantify a single effect size.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate.**
- Consensus strength: **Moderate–High** — SDT's autonomy/quality-of-motivation account is mainstream in health-behaviour science; the self-efficacy predictor is very well established.
- Relationship: association is robust; the *causal* and *durability* claims are less firmly proven.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0030: high authority, transparent, with a noted theoretical-investment consideration (SDT co-founders) and a correlational-majority limitation. Autonomous-motivation pattern verified this session via PubMed/PMC abstract.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** exercise findings extend to other wellness behaviours (diet, meditation, sleep) — plausible extrapolation.
- **Ontological:** "autonomous vs controlled motivation" is a real, measurable distinction (granted the SDT framework).

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** A consistent cross-design association supports the quality-of-motivation account. **Risks checked:** *confirmation bias / motivated reasoning* — flagged explicitly: an "align, don't force" wellness framing finds "autonomous motivation matters" congenial. Guarded by (a) grading the evidence down for its correlational majority and (b) not asserting proven durable causation; the claim survives only as a **moderately-supported association**, not a proven lever. *Correlational-to-causal leap* is named and bounded.

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 3 (Moderate).** The association between motivation quality/self-efficacy and sustained behaviour is consistent and mainstream, but the evidence is substantially correlational and the causal/durability step is not cleanly proven. Not higher because of the correlational majority and single-domain (exercise) anchor.

---

# Limitations
- Correlational majority limits causal inference.
- Anchored in exercise; extension to other wellness behaviours is inferred.
- "Autonomy support works" as an intervention is less strongly evidenced for *long-term* maintenance than for proximal motivation.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"Any motivation will do if strong enough."** Contradicted by the quality distinction — controlled motivation predicts poorer persistence even when initially strong; rejected.
2. **"Autonomous people were going to succeed anyway (reverse causation)."** A real confound in correlational data — conceded; it is why confidence is held at Moderate, not High.
3. **"Incentives (external motivation) can substitute."** Rejected for durability — see CLM-0026 (incentive effects fade on removal); the two claims `contrast`.
