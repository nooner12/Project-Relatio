# CLM-0019

# The Intention-Behaviour Gap Is the Central Point of Failure

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The most fundamental reason wellness interventions are not adopted or maintained is the **intention–behaviour gap**: forming an intention is a **weak and insufficient** predictor of behaviour. The gap is asymmetric — people who lack intention almost never act, while a large fraction of those who *do* intend still fail to enact the behaviour (in physical activity, roughly **half of intenders**). Non-adherence is therefore primarily a failure of *translation and enactment*, not (usually) a failure to want the goal.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** (a measured empirical regularity) with an **explanatory** function (it locates *where* in the causal chain wellness behaviour fails).

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** (meta-analysis).
- **SRC-0025 (Rhodes & de Bruijn 2013):** meta-analysis (k=10, N≈3,899) of physical-activity intention vs behaviour. **The large majority of non-intenders remained inactive** (in the abstract's cell breakdown only ~2% of the sample were non-intenders who became active), whereas roughly **half of intenders failed to become active** (restated across sources as ~46–54% of intenders unsuccessful). The gap is driven almost entirely by **non-acting intenders**. *(An earlier draft asserted a "~97–98% correspondence" figure for non-intenders; that precise figure could not be confirmed to the paywalled full text this session and an abstract-level reconstruction implies ~91%, so it is softened here to avoid false precision — KOS-0003 §12.1.)*

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 4** — peer-reviewed meta-analysis; the *directional* finding (gap driven by non-acting intenders) is robust and widely replicated.
- **Relevance: 4** — physical activity is a core wellness behaviour; the phenomenon generalises across health behaviours conceptually, though this meta-analysis is activity-specific.
- **Independence: 3** — a single meta-analysis with a small primary-study pool (k=10); the broader intention-behaviour-gap literature corroborates it but is not separately catalogued here.
- **Quality: 3** — sound method but small k and correlational structure limit precision.
- **Limitations:** Establishes the *existence and shape* of the gap for physical activity; it does **not** by itself quantify the gap for diet/meditation/sleep, nor prove which remedy closes it.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate–High** for the phenomenon.
- Consensus strength: **High** — the intention-behaviour gap is a mainstream, foundational finding in health psychology.
- Relationship: strongly supported as a phenomenon; debate concerns *how much* of the gap each moderator explains, not whether it exists.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0025: high authority, academic, transparent about its small primary-study pool. The exact intender-failure percentage is variably restated in the secondary literature; reported here as "approximately half," not a single decimal (KOS-0003 §12.1). The non-intender correspondence figure is stated qualitatively ("large majority inactive") because the specific "~97–98%" figure could not be confirmed to the paywalled full text and an abstract reconstruction implies ~91%. Full text was paywalled this session (Wiley 403); verification is abstract/secondary-restatement level.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** the physical-activity gap is representative of wellness behaviours generally — plausible but an extrapolation.
- **Epistemological:** self-reported intention validly measures genuine intention.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive.** The asymmetric correspondence pattern (non-intenders ~inactive; intenders ~50/50) locates the failure at *enactment of existing intention*. **Risks checked:** *overgeneralisation* (guarded — domain-specific evidence flagged; claim states the phenomenon, not a universal percentage); *false precision* (the ~half figure is reported as approximate per §12.1); *confirmation bias* (this claim is neutral to the owner's commercial interest — it names a problem, not a product).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
**Level 4 (High)** for the existence and central importance of the gap. Not Level 5: the precise magnitude is domain-specific and variably restated, and the evidence anchor here is a single small-k meta-analysis (with a large corroborating literature not individually catalogued).

---

# Limitations
- Quantified here for physical activity only; magnitude for other wellness behaviours is inferred.
- Describes the failure point; does not prescribe the fix (that is CLM-0023/0024/0025).

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"People fail because they don't really want it (weak intention)."** Partly true — intention *strength* matters (see CLM-0020) — but the asymmetry shows many genuine intenders still fail; rejected as the *whole* story.
2. **"The gap is a measurement artefact of self-reported intention."** Possible contributor, but the effect is robust across measures and designs; does not overturn the phenomenon.
