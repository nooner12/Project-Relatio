# 5. FND-0006 confidence cap (structural check)

**The Level 3 (Moderate) cap is correct and honestly constructed.** The finding's constructive core — *maintenance is built by self-regulation + autonomous motivation + habit, and external shortcuts don't endure* — necessarily depends on CLM-0020, CLM-0021, and CLM-0025, each Level 3 (correlational / theory-synthesis / single-study). A synthesis cannot exceed its weakest necessary leg, so the cap tracks those three; the firmer Level-4 legs (0019/0023/0024/0026) do not lift it. The body's "**no reliable formula**," "modest effects," and "long-term maintenance evidence is thin across the board" are properly deflationary and consistent with sibling INV-0004/INV-0005. This is well-calibrated.

**One calibration Flag — the title outruns the cap.** "Sustained Wellness Behaviour **Is Built by** Self-Regulation, Autonomous Motivation, and Habit" is a sufficiency/recipe claim; the Level-3 body explicitly says there is *no reliable formula* and effects are *modest*. This is the same class of defect flagged as "decisively" in the RQ-0005 review — false precision by rhetoric (§12.1 rule 3). Recommend softening to a "best-evidenced levers, not a guaranteed build" formulation (e.g. "Sustained Wellness Behaviour Is Best Supported by …"). The finding's *contrastive* half ("**not** willpower or incentives") is well supported and may stand.

# 6. Figure and evidentiary-gap findings

**6.1 CLM-0019 / ENT-0004 — the "~97–98%" non-intender figure (Flag; §12.1 rule 3/4).** The load-bearing statistic ("roughly half of intenders fail") is **verified** (≈46%). But both records also assert non-intenders were inactive at "**~97–98% correspondence**." I could not confirm this at that precision; the abstract's four-cell breakdown (successful non-intenders ≈2%, inactive non-intenders ≈21% of the sample) implies **~91%** of non-intenders remained inactive. The qualitative point ("non-intenders rarely become active") holds either way and is **not load-bearing**, so this is a Flag, not a Block. **Remediation:** reconcile against the full text, or soften to "the large majority of non-intenders remain inactive." Fix in **both** CLM-0019 §Evidence and ENT-0004 §2.

**6.2 CLM-0022 — no numeric attrition rate is the correct call (confirmed).** Eysenbach 2005 is genuinely a viewpoint that argues *for* a "science of attrition" and distinguishes non-usage from dropout attrition; it reports **no pooled rate**. Declining to assert a number is the epistemically correct move, not an evasion — asserting one would be false precision. **Residual Flag (minor):** the strong universal wording ("the norm," "regardless of content quality," current-ecosystem applicability) is carried by a dated source plus **un-catalogued** later literature; catalogue one modern empirical attrition source or soften the universal wording.

**6.3 CLM-0020 — the self-efficacy leg is under-evidenced (Flag; KOS-0003 §12/§5).** The claim's title and statement foreground "**Self-Efficacy**" co-equally with motivation quality, but the record concedes self-efficacy is "invoked conceptually, not separately catalogued." A claim may not give equal billing to a construct it supplies **no source** for. **Remediation:** either catalogue a self-efficacy source (Bandura-tradition or a maintenance-specific self-efficacy review) or demote self-efficacy in the claim/title to a clearly secondary, un-catalogued invocation. Separately, "**better than motivation quantity**" and the maintenance-specificity are stronger than Teixeira's cited content (consistent *associations* with exercise behaviour, correlational majority); align the wording or add corroboration.

**6.4 Independence grades — spot check.** CLM-0023 Independence **4** is defensible: Michie (cross-trial meta-regression) and Harkin (randomised meta-analysis) are genuinely different methods converging — though note they measure *related-but-not-identical* constructs (self-monitoring-as-BCT vs progress-monitoring manipulation), so the convergence is slightly looser than "same conclusion" implies; Level 4 still holds. CLM-0020 Independence **3** sits at the generous edge (sole source is the theory's founders' own review; self-efficacy un-catalogued) — acceptable **only** because the correlational grade already caps confidence at Moderate.

# 7. CLM-0020 ruling — the primary brief (both directions)

**Was Level 3 over-deflated to look unbiased? No.** The Moderate grade is driven by **real evidentiary limits** — correlational majority, single narrative review, single (exercise) domain, and an un-proven causal/durability step — not by bias-optics. Indeed my independent read finds the claim, if anything, **slightly over-scoped** (self-efficacy + "better than quantity" + maintenance-specificity outrun the one correlational source), which argues the number is right and the *scope* is the problem. So the deflation is genuine, not performative self-flagellation.

**Was it under-graded (should it be Level 4)? No.** One could argue the bare autonomous-motivation→activity *association* is Level-4-robust. But the claim is not the bare association — it is the causal + maintenance + comparative + self-efficacy bundle, and a claim is only as strong as its weakest necessary element. Holding the bundle at Level 3 is correct in both directions.

**Verdict on the guard for this claim:** operated correctly. The defect is the un-catalogued self-efficacy leg (§6.3), which is independent of the calibration.

---

# 8. Two-directional motivated-reasoning finding (owner runs a wellness business)

**(a) Did any commercially convenient conclusion survive unchallenged? No.** The three most convenient conclusions were all denied: (i) "here is how to make clients stick" → **no reliable formula**; (ii) "the techniques deliver" → **modest effects, thin long-term durability**; (iii) the lever closest to a "sell engagement / reward" model — **financial incentives (CLM-0026) — is precisely the one shown to fail after removal**, an *inconvenient* conclusion retained on the evidence ("new habits do not appear to be formed"). This is the clearest signal the guard is real.

**(b) Was the congenial finding inflated? No.** Autonomy-supportive motivation (CLM-0020), congenial to an "align, don't force" brand, was held at **Moderate** for its correlational base rather than promoted — and §7 confirms that Moderate is honestly earned, not theatrically deflated.

**Conclusion: the guard operated correctly in both directions** — subject to the §9 shared-frame caveat, which a same-model reviewer cannot clear.

# 9. Shared-frame caveat (same-model limit)

Both the Specialist and I may over-value the very lenses this investigation is built on: the SDT autonomous/controlled-motivation dichotomy, and the self-regulation/habit "active-ingredient" account of maintenance. These are mainstream but **contested** framings (dual-process and habit constructs are debated; SDT's causal claims are argued). ENT-0004 helpfully concedes magnitude-variability, but the *framework choice itself* is a place where a shared blind spot could survive into review. Genuine independent review (different model / human evidence-synthesis specialist) is the only cure.
