# 1. Research Question

**Primary (authoritative wording — do not improve it):**

> Within the developmental band **ages 5–17**, where does the evidence document divergence **by sex or gender** in the **onset timing** of recognized psychosocial stressors — and for each documented divergence, **what construct was actually measured: sex, gender, or unresolved?**

**Secondary (conditional — only engages where a timing divergence is established):**

> Where onset timing diverges, does the **modal expression** of the stressor diverge as well, and on what evidence?

The primary question is a **mapping** question, not a ranking question (see §2 exclusions). The construct-provenance clause is not an afterthought — it is half the question, and the source-coding scheme in §3 is its instrument.

---

# 2. Scope & Disambiguation

## 2.1 Age band: 5–17 — a bound matched to a decision (settled; do not reopen)

The consuming decision surface is **Hearth & Field Pillar II, which is age-banded 5–17**. An age-banded program's live question is *when* divergence occurs — if divergence timing does not align with band boundaries, the bands are wrong. That is a finding that can move a design.

Lifespan scope belongs to the Wellness Model, which has **no dated decision attached** and is already served by INV-0009 (research input only). Lifespan scope would multiply the source set without a decision to serve.

**Ages 0–4 and 18+ are therefore out of scope by construction. Widening the band is a scope change requiring a recorded decision, not a thoroughness improvement.**

## 2.2 Excluded by construction

- **No ranking by magnitude. No "biggest," "greatest," "most significant," or any superlative.** A magnitude claim requires a stated metric (prevalence / distress intensity / long-term outcome impact / mortality — these rank differently and do not reduce to one another) and a source that measured *that* metric. Absent both, **the investigation maps; it does not rank.**
- **The term "critical period" is not used** unless a source establishes critical-period criteria explicitly (required input; permanent loss or degradation of capacity). Default vocabulary is **"age band."** "Sensitive period" only where a source earns it. Rationale: "critical period" presupposes that psychosocial hardship operates on critical-period logic, which is a finding and not a premise.
- **No individualized guidance of any kind** (ADR-GOV-0002; the §7.5 gate above).

## 2.3 Disambiguation: sex vs. gender as measured constructs

"Sex" here means biological sex as an assessed variable with a stated mechanism of action; "gender" means a socially patterned variable (self-report, role, socialization). The investigation does **not** adjudicate the constructs philosophically; it records **which construct each source actually measured**. Sources that apply sex labels to gender data without drawing the distinction are coded as such (§3) and constrained accordingly.

# 3. Method / Protocol

Execution follows the KOS-0003 pipeline (Question → Claims → Assumptions → Evidence → Confidence) through the full OPS-0003 circuit. Sources via TPL-0002, Claims via TPL-0001, Findings via TPL-0004, all registered in the Identifier Registry.

## 3.1 Source-coding scheme: construct provenance (the spine of this investigation)

Every source entering INV-0010 carries a **construct-provenance code** alongside its normal Relatio confidence treatment:

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `SEX-MEASURED` | Biological sex assessed; divergence attributed to it on a stated mechanism |
| `GENDER-MEASURED` | Socially patterned variable measured — self-report, role, socialization |
| `CONFLATED` | Sex labels applied to gender data; no distinction drawn |
| `UNRESOLVED` | Cannot be determined from the paper as written |

**Operative rule for this investigation:** a `CONFLATED` or `UNRESOLVED` source **cannot support a sex-attributed claim at any confidence level.** It may support a gender-patterned claim only.

## 3.2 Prediction to test, not to assume

The majority of sources are predicted to code `CONFLATED`. If that prediction holds, **it is itself a result and is recorded as one** in the finding. If it fails, that is recorded too. Either way the prediction is evaluated explicitly at closing — it is a hypothesis under test, not a premise the coding may lean on.

## 3.3 Governance classification of this scheme (settled; do not relitigate)

- **Within INV-0010, as investigation protocol:** *content addition.* Normal work.
- **As a standing crosswalk rule ("no construct gained," sibling to Appendix A's "no tier gained"):** *standards change*, requiring an explicit recorded Governance Backlog decision.

The scheme is therefore **not** added to STD-0006, the crosswalk, or Appendix A by this investigation. If it proves out in use, it becomes a GB proposal *after* the investigation closes, authored separately. Classified upward per governance tier rule.

# 6. Assumptions & Bracketing (KOS-0003 §10)

- **No critical-period premise.** The investigation does not assume psychosocial hardship operates on critical-period logic (§2.2); vocabulary discipline enforces this.
- **The `CONFLATED`-majority prediction is bracketed as a hypothesis** (§3.2) — it must not steer coding decisions toward its own confirmation.
- **Magnitude is bracketed.** Onset timing and modal expression are in scope; "how big" is not, absent a stated metric and a source measuring it.
- **The band bound is bracketed as a scope decision, not an empirical claim** — nothing in this investigation asserts that divergence outside 5–17 is absent or unimportant; it is simply not asked here.
- **Bracketed entirely:** individualized guidance; program design; the INV-0008 gate status (untouched and unaffected).
