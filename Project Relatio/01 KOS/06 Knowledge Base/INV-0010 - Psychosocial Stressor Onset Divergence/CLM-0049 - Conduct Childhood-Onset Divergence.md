# CLM-0049

# The Male-Preponderant Divergence in Antisocial/Conduct Problems Is Concentrated in Childhood-Onset (Approx. 10:1 Male:Female) and Largely Narrows for Adolescence-Onset (Approx. 1.5:1) — Making Onset Timing Itself a Divergence Locus; a Gender-Patterned/Unattributed Claim, Not Sex-Attributed

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The sex divergence in antisocial/conduct problems is **onset-timing-dependent**: the male preponderance is large for **childhood-onset (life-course-persistent)** antisocial behavior (~10:1 male:female in the Dunedin cohort) but **much smaller for adolescence-onset** (~1.5:1). Early onset is thus far more male-skewed than later onset. This is a **gender-patterned / unattributed** pattern; no biological-sex mechanism is isolated, and girls' conduct problems may additionally be under-detected.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** (onset-timing-conditional sex ratio). Directionally robust; cohort-specific in exact magnitude; unattributed as to construct.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** — longitudinal birth cohort.
- **SRC-0065 (Moffitt & Caspi 2001, *Development and Psychopathology*):** childhood-onset ~10:1 male:female; adolescence-onset ~1.5:1 — the sex gap is concentrated in early onset.

**Construct-provenance code (governing this claim): `UNRESOLVED`** (recoded from `CONFLATED` per Critical Review – RQ-0010 F-1). Sex is a recorded category on behavioral/delinquency measures; no mechanism is isolated, no gender-socialization construct measured, and the paper gives no basis to determine whether the early-onset male preponderance is neurodevelopmental, socialization-driven, or ascertainment-influenced — construct undetermined, hence `UNRESOLVED`. **Operative rule (§3.1): `UNRESOLVED` is constrained identically to `CONFLATED` — gender-patterned / unattributed claim only, no sex-attribution.** Honored; confidence unchanged.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — landmark cohort; exact ratios cohort-specific, direction replicates broadly.
- **Relevance: 4** — makes onset timing itself a divergence locus, central to the primary question.
- **Independence: 3** — single influential cohort/program (Dunedin).
- **Quality: 3** — strong prospective design; taxonomy fits girls less cleanly (authors' own caution).
- **Limitations:** shows the sex gap is **concentrated in childhood-onset**; does **not** isolate a mechanism and does not resolve possible under-detection of girls' conduct problems.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** for the onset-conditional ratio; the *direction* (early-onset far more male) is broadly supported.
- Consensus strength: **Moderate–High** that childhood-onset antisocial behavior is markedly male-skewed; the two-trajectory model's fit for girls is contested.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0065 is high-authority and prospective; its binding limits are single-cohort magnitude and its own noted weaker fit for girls. `CONFLATED` — sex label on behavior, mechanism not isolated.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** the childhood/adolescence-onset distinction validly partitions antisocial pathways — reasonably warranted (and foundational to the taxonomy).
- **Inferential:** the large early-onset male skew is described, not attributed to biological sex.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive, onset-stratified.** **Risks checked:** *construct conflation* (named and enforced — gender-patterned only); *male-derived-model over-generalization* (named — the taxonomy was built on males and fits girls less well, per the authors; the claim is stated conditionally); *under-detection of girls* (named — girls' relational/covert conduct problems may be missed, which could inflate the apparent male skew); *single-cohort magnitude* (named — exact ratios are Dunedin-specific).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** — that the male preponderance in conduct/antisocial problems is concentrated in childhood-onset and narrows substantially for adolescence-onset. Held at Moderate: single-cohort exact ratios, contested taxonomy fit for girls, possible under-detection. **Not higher.** **Not sex-attributed** (CONFLATED).

---

# Limitations
- Exact ratios cohort-specific; direction is the defensible content.
- Possible under-detection of girls' conduct problems.
- No mechanism; no magnitude ranking against other stressors.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"Girls' conduct problems are under-detected (more relational/covert), inflating the male skew."** Steelmanned and partly conceded — a detection artifact akin to ADHD; it would attenuate but likely not erase the early-onset male concentration.
2. **"The two-trajectory taxonomy is wrong for girls."** Steelmanned; the authors themselves flag weaker fit. The claim is stated as an onset-conditional *ratio pattern*, which survives even if the causal taxonomy is revised.
