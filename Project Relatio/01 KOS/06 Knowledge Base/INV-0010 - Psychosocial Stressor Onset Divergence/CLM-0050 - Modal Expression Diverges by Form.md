# CLM-0050

# Where a Stressor Diverges by Sex/Gender, Its Modal Expression (Form) Also Tends to Diverge Along Gender Lines — e.g., Physical vs Relational Peer Aggression/Victimization — a Modest, Gender-Patterned/Unattributed Divergence (Secondary Question)

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Addressing the **secondary (modal-expression) question**: where a psychosocial stressor diverges by sex/gender, its **form** tends to diverge too, along gender-patterned lines. For peer aggression/victimization specifically, boys are overrepresented in **physical** (and high-across-forms) profiles, while a **primarily relational** profile emerges by late childhood and grows into adolescence among mostly girls. The **magnitude of the form difference is modest**, and it is **gender-patterned / unattributed** — no biological-sex mechanism is claimed.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / comparative** (form-of-expression divergence). Directionally documented; modest in magnitude; construct-unattributed.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** — longitudinal study of aggression/victimization forms.
- **SRC-0066 (Ettekal & Ladd 2017, *Developmental Psychology*):** boys overrepresented in physical/high-across-forms profiles; a mostly-girls, primarily-relational profile emerges by late childhood and increases into adolescence.
- The broader literature notes the physical-vs-relational sex difference is **directionally consistent but modest** in mean-level magnitude — bounding this claim.

**Construct-provenance code (governing this claim): `CONFLATED`.** Sex is a covariate; no gender construct or mechanism is measured (despite this form divergence being *strongly* theorized as socialization-driven). **Operative rule (§3.1): gender-patterned / unattributed claim only.** Honored — and this is a case where the gender-socialization reading is theoretically dominant but still not *measured* by the source.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — solid longitudinal design; form differences replicate directionally.
- **Relevance: 3** — serves the secondary question, not the primary onset-timing one.
- **Independence: 3** — single study for the profile trajectories; the modest-magnitude caveat draws on wider literature.
- **Quality: 3** — good design; mean-level effects are small, limiting strength.
- **Limitations:** documents a **form/modal-expression divergence** that consolidates developmentally; the effect is **modest**, and no mechanism is measured.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Low–Moderate** given modest effect sizes.
- Consensus strength: **Moderate** that boys skew physical and girls relatively more relational; the magnitude is debated and often called small.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0066 is a credible developmental study; its limits for this claim are modest effect sizes and no gender measurement. `CONFLATED`.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** peer-nomination/rating profiles validly index aggression/victimization form — reasonably warranted.
- **Comparative:** "modal expression diverges where the stressor diverges" is offered as a *tendency*, evidenced here for one stressor (peer victimization), not proven across all stressors.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive, single-domain, generalized cautiously.** **Risks checked:** *over-generalization* (named — the secondary claim is evidenced for peer victimization; the internalizing-vs-externalizing channeling seen across the primary claims is a *supporting pattern*, not independently proven here, so the claim is scoped as a tendency); *magnitude creep* (named — the form difference is explicitly called modest, no superlative); *construct conflation* (named and enforced — gender-patterned only, even though socialization is the leading theory).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 2 (Low)** — that modal expression diverges by form along gender lines as a general tendency. **Reaching Level 3 (Moderate)** for the specific, well-documented case of physical (boys) vs relational (girls) peer aggression/victimization, bounded by modest effect sizes. **Not higher:** single-domain evidence, small magnitudes, no mechanism. **Not sex-attributed** (CONFLATED).

---

# Limitations
- Evidenced directly for peer victimization; generalized only as a tendency.
- Modest magnitude.
- No mechanism; gender-patterned/unattributed only.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"The physical-vs-relational sex difference is trivial/overstated."** Steelmanned and partly conceded — hence Low–Moderate confidence; the direction holds but the size is small.
2. **"Form divergence is fully explained by gender socialization."** Steelmanned and theoretically dominant — but unmeasured here, so it cannot upgrade the code past CONFLATED.
