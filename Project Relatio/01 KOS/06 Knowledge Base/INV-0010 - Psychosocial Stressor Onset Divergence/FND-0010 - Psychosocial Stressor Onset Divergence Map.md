# FND-0010

# A Map of Where the 5–17 Evidence Documents Onset-Timing Divergence by Sex or Gender — and, for Each, What Construct Was Actually Measured: The Headline Result Is That Neither Construct Is Typically Measured At All (7 of 8 Sources Do Not Cleanly Measure Either Sex or Gender — 5 CONFLATED, 2 UNRESOLVED), With One Bounded Biological-Timing Exception (Puberty–Depression)

# 1. Statement

> **(a) The map — where the 5–17 evidence documents onset-timing divergence by sex/gender.** Five loci are documented, each with a *distinct* timing signature (which is the point of a map rather than a ranking):
> - **Anxiety** — female-preponderant divergence appears **early / pre-pubertally** (retrospectively ~age 6), the earliest signature (**CLM-0046, Level 2 → 3 for direction**).
> - **Conduct / antisocial problems** — male-preponderant divergence concentrated in **childhood-onset** (~10:1), narrowing sharply for adolescence-onset (~1.5:1): onset timing is itself the divergence locus (**CLM-0049, Level 3**).
> - **ADHD** — the divergence that is actually documented is in **identification/ascertainment** (clinic ~10:1 vs community ~2–3:1; girls under-identified), i.e., *when and whether* a child is recognized, not a demonstrated difference in underlying rate (**CLM-0047, Level 3**).
> - **Depression** — female-preponderant divergence **emerges at the pubertal transition (~ages 12–13)** and consolidates across mid-adolescence (**CLM-0044, Level 4**).
> - **Eating-disorder symptoms** — female-preponderant divergence **consolidates in early-to-mid adolescence** (girls rise ~14–17; boys stable/declining) (**CLM-0048, Level 3**).
>
> **(b) The construct answer — the spine of the investigation, and the finding's headline.** For **every** documented divergence except one, the sources applied a **female/male label to data that measured no biological mechanism and no gender-socialization construct** — the divergence is real as a *category pattern* but **not attributable to biological sex** on the evidence. Within that group there are two distinct failure modes: **`CONFLATED` (5 sources)** — sex labels applied where the data is plausibly *gendered* (e.g., ADHD referral/ascertainment, depression and anxiety epidemiology, peer-victimization form) — and **`UNRESOLVED` (2 sources: eating-disorder and conduct)** — where the paper gives *no basis at all* to determine whether the pattern is biological or social (mechanism undetermined per the sources' own limitation notes). Both groups are constrained identically by the operative rule: they support **gender-patterned / unattributed** claims only, never a sex-attribution. **The single exception is the puberty–depression link (CLM-0045, `SEX-MEASURED`, Level 3):** Angold et al. *physically assessed* pubertal status (Tanner), and the divergence timing tracks pubertal status over chronological age — a **bounded biological-timing** claim. Even there, *why* pubertal status matters (hormonal vs socially-mediated) is **unresolved**; and, decisively, **puberty occurs in both sexes**, so measuring pubertal stage does not establish that the *between-sex* divergence is biologically caused — the sex × puberty interaction is unmeasured. The claim stops at "timing co-varies with a measured maturational variable," not "hormonally caused" and not "biological-sex difference."
>
> **(c) The prediction verdict — HELD, with the corollary now the headline.** INV-0010 predicted (§3.2) that the **majority of sources would fail to cleanly measure a construct** (its shorthand: "code CONFLATED"). Result: **7 of 8 sources do not cleanly measure either construct** — the prediction **HELD**. But the finer split corrects the shorthand: most are **not** demonstrably "sex-label-on-gender-data" (`CONFLATED`, 5) — two are **construct-undetermined** (`UNRESOLVED`, 2), and there are **0 `GENDER-MEASURED`**. The robust, promoted result is therefore the **corollary, not the raw CONFLATED count: neither construct — sex *or* gender — is typically measured at all.** The field does not measure gender-as-socialization any more than it measures a biological sex mechanism; it defaults to imposing a female/male tag on data that assessed neither pole. "Sex differences," in this corpus, is largely a **labeling convention over construct-unresolved data** — and that, not any biological story, is the finding's central claim.
>
> **(d) Secondary (modal-expression) question.** Where a stressor diverges, its **form** tends to diverge too, along gender-patterned lines (physical vs relational peer aggression/victimization; and, across the map, a broad internalizing-skews-female / externalizing-skews-male channeling). This is **modest in magnitude** and **gender-patterned/unattributed** (**CLM-0050, Level 2 → 3** for the peer-victimization case). It is offered as a tendency, evidenced directly for one stressor.

# 3. Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
The **map as a whole** is a synthesis of separately-designed evidence across five stressor domains; it is only as strong as its parts, and it makes **no claim above the level of its strongest component**. The best-supported single element is the **depression timing pattern (Level 4)**; the one biology-linked attribution (**CLM-0045**) is held at **Level 3** and explicitly bounded; the weakest elements (anxiety exact age, modal expression) sit at **Level 2**. **No element reaches Level 5** (reserved; and cross-domain interpretive mapping does not warrant it). The **construct verdict** — that in this corpus neither sex nor gender is typically measured (5 `CONFLATED`, 2 `UNRESOLVED`, 1 `SEX-MEASURED`, 0 `GENDER-MEASURED`) — is itself well-supported (**it is a direct census of what the eight verified sources measured**, not an interpretation): the *census* is high-confidence; the *generalization* to "the field at large" is bounded by an eight-source sample chosen for coverage, not systematically sampled.

---

# 4. Scope & Limitations (what is NOT supported)
- **NOT supported:** that any of these divergences is *caused by biological sex* — including depression's, where the timing tracks a measured maturational variable (pubertal status) but the **sex × puberty mechanism is unresolved and substantially socially mediated**, and puberty occurs in both sexes so pubertal-stage measurement cannot establish a between-sex biological cause. Every non-`SEX-MEASURED` source (5 `CONFLATED` + 2 `UNRESOLVED`) is forbidden from carrying any sex-attribution.
- **NOT supported:** any **ranking** of stressors by magnitude — no "biggest / most significant" divergence; no superlative appears, and no magnitude metric was measured to license one.
- **NOT supported:** a **hormonal** cause even for depression — CLM-0045 licenses only "tracks measured pubertal status," with the pathway unresolved and substantial social mediation noted.
- **NOT supported:** that ADHD's sex difference reflects a real rate difference — what is documented is an **ascertainment** divergence; the residual community gap is left open.
- **NOT supported:** precise **onset ages** as constants — the anxiety age (~6) is retrospective; the eating-disorder emergence below 14 is inferred; the conduct ratios are cohort-specific.
- **NOT a systematic review** — eight sources chosen for domain coverage and live-verifiability; the CONFLATED-majority census is robust *for this set* and directionally indicative beyond it, not a meta-analytic population estimate.
- **NOT addressed:** ages 0–4 and 18+ (out of scope by construction); individualized guidance (gated); program design (INV-0008 untouched).
- **Motivated-reasoning guard:** the convenient tidy stories were named and resisted — the "it's just puberty/biology" reading (bounded at CLM-0045 with peer-stress mediation flagged); the "it's all referral bias, no real difference" over-correction (CLM-0047 leaves the residual open); and any perennialist "sex and gender are the same thing" collapse (the entire investigation is built to keep them apart).

---

# 5. Implication (offered — not guidance, and gated)
At the literature level, the map says two things a program designer would want to know, both **gated and non-individualized**: (1) **onset-timing divergence is real but domain-specific** — different stressors diverge at different developmental points (pre-pubertal anxiety; pubertal depression; childhood-onset conduct; adolescent eating pathology), so a single age boundary cannot be "the" divergence point; and (2) **most "sex difference" findings in this area are construct-unresolved** — they should be read as *gender-patterned or unattributed* divergences, not biological facts, when informing any design. Whether these patterns hold in any specific population or program is an **empirical question outside this finding's scope**, and any application requires the independent re-verification the gate mandates.
