# CLM-0048

# The Female-Preponderant Divergence in Eating-Disorder Symptoms Consolidates Across Early-to-Mid Adolescence (Female Symptoms Rise ~14–17, Male Symptoms Stable/Declining) — a Gender-Patterned/Unattributed Divergence, Not a Sex-Attributed One

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> Eating-disorder symptom trajectories diverge by recorded sex across adolescence: in females, fasting/global symptoms, purging, and excessive exercise **rise from about age 14 to a peak near 17 and remain elevated**, while in males symptoms are **stable or declining** over the same window. The divergence **consolidates in early-to-mid adolescence**. This is a **gender-patterned / unattributed** timing pattern; no biological-sex mechanism is claimed, and the dominant sociocultural (body-image/thin-ideal) explanation was not measured by the source.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive** (sex-categorized symptom-trajectory divergence over adolescence). Robust as trajectory description; unattributed as to construct.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** — longitudinal cohort, symptom trajectories by sex.
- **SRC-0064 (Allen et al. 2013, *Journal of Eating Disorders*):** female symptoms rise 14→17 and stay elevated; male symptoms stable/declining; observation window ages 14–20.

**Construct-provenance code (governing this claim): `UNRESOLVED`** (recoded from `CONFLATED` per Critical Review – RQ-0010 F-1). Sex is a recorded grouping category; no biological mechanism and no gender/body-image construct is measured, and the paper gives no basis to determine whether the data is biologically or socially patterned — construct undetermined, hence `UNRESOLVED`. **Operative rule (§3.1): `UNRESOLVED` is constrained identically to `CONFLATED` — gender-patterned / unattributed claim only, no sex-attribution.** Honored; confidence unchanged.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 3** — single well-run cohort; symptom scales rather than diagnoses.
- **Relevance: 4** — addresses onset-timing divergence for eating pathology within band.
- **Independence: 3** — one cohort/team.
- **Quality: 3** — solid longitudinal design; window begins at 14, so *emergence* below 14 is inferred, not observed.
- **Limitations:** shows the **trajectory divergence consolidating in mid-adolescence**; does **not** date onset below 14, is symptom- not diagnosis-based, and measures no mechanism.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Moderate** for the sex-categorized trajectory divergence.
- Consensus strength: **High** in the field that eating pathology is female-preponderant and consolidates in adolescence; the *driver* is widely theorized as sociocultural/gendered but is unmeasured here.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0064 is a credible cohort; its binding limit for this claim is that it recorded a sex tag without measuring the body-image/gender constructs that dominate the explanatory literature — so it is `CONFLATED` and supports only a gender-patterned reading.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** self-reported symptom trajectories index developing eating pathology — reasonably warranted.
- **Temporal:** trajectories already diverging at 14 imply earlier emergence — an inference, not an observation, flagged as such.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive, trajectory-based.** **Risks checked:** *construct conflation* (named and enforced — gender-patterned only; the strong sociocultural literature is noted precisely because the source did not measure it); *emergence over-dating* (named — the source starts at 14, so "consolidates in early-to-mid adolescence" is stated, not "onset at 14"); *magnitude creep* (named — no ranking against other stressors, no superlative).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 3 (Moderate)** — that the female-preponderant eating-disorder symptom divergence consolidates across early-to-mid adolescence (rising in girls 14–17, stable/declining in boys). Held at Moderate: single cohort, symptom-not-diagnosis, emergence below 14 inferred. **Not sex-attributed** (CONFLATED).

---

# Limitations
- Symptom scales, not incident diagnoses.
- Emergence below age 14 is inferred, not observed.
- No mechanism; gender-patterned/unattributed only; no magnitude ranking.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"The divergence is sociocultural (thin-ideal internalization, gendered body pressure)."** Steelmanned and likely a large part of the story — but unmeasured here, which is exactly why the code is CONFLATED and the claim stays gender-patterned/unattributed.
2. **"Male eating pathology is under-detected (different presentation: muscularity-oriented)."** Steelmanned; plausible partial detection artifact akin to ADHD. It would attenuate but not erase the female-ward symptom rise this cohort shows.
