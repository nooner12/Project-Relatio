# CLM-0046

# The Female-Preponderant Divergence in Anxiety Appears Earlier Than Depression's — Present in Early Childhood (Retrospectively ~Age 6), a Pre-Pubertal Pattern — as a Gender-Patterned/Unattributed Divergence, Not a Sex-Attributed One

## Draft Claim Record

---

# Claim
> The female preponderance in anxiety **emerges earlier in development than depression's** — retrospective data place girls at roughly twice the odds of a prior anxiety disorder by about age 6, i.e., **before the pubertal window** in which the depression ratio diverges. This is a **gender-patterned / unattributed** timing contrast; the specific age is a soft retrospective anchor, and no biological-sex mechanism is claimed.

---

# Claim Type (KOS-0003 §3)
**Descriptive / comparative** (relative timing: anxiety divergence earlier than depression divergence). The direction is the robust part; the exact age is soft.

---

# Evidence (KOS-0003 §4)
Type: **Empirical** — epidemiological cohort with retrospective onset dating.
- **SRC-0061 (Lewinsohn et al. 1998, *Journal of Abnormal Psychology*):** on retrospective data, females were ~2× as likely as males to have had an anxiety disorder **by age 6**; the female skew spans anxiety subtypes.
- **The direction rests on a catalogued within-investigation contrast, not on any uncatalogued review:** the anxiety female skew is already present by ~age 6 (SRC-0061), whereas the depression female skew does not emerge until ~ages 12–13 (SRC-0059 / CLM-0044). Even discounting the exact anxiety onset age for retrospective error, the anxiety skew is documented *pre-pubertally* while the depression skew is documented *at* the pubertal transition — so the *ordering* (anxiety divergence earlier than depression's) is carried by comparing two catalogued INV-0010 sources, which is what supports the direction more than the single age figure does.

**Construct-provenance code (governing this claim): `CONFLATED`.** SRC-0061 applies a female/male label to retrospective self-report onset data with no mechanism and no gender-socialization measure. **Operative rule (§3.1): supports only a gender-patterned / unattributed claim — never a sex-attributed one.** The claim honors this.

---

# Evidence Evaluation (KOS-0003 §5, 0–5)
- **Reliability: 2** — single cohort; the age-6 figure rests on retrospective recall (telescoping, gendered reporting).
- **Relevance: 4** — directly serves the primary question by contrasting anxiety's timing with depression's.
- **Independence: 2** — one sample/region for the specific figure; the *direction* rests on the catalogued SRC-0061 vs SRC-0059 contrast within this investigation (two independent samples/literatures), not on any uncatalogued source.
- **Quality: 3** — sound cohort, but retrospective onset dating is the weak link.
- **Limitations:** establishes a **direction** (anxiety divergence earlier / pre-pubertal); the **precise age is not a firm constant** and no mechanism is shown.

---

# Consensus (KOS-0003 §9)
- Evidence strength: **Low–Moderate** for the exact age; **Moderate** for the direction.
- Consensus strength: **Moderate** that anxiety's female skew appears earlier than depression's; the mechanism is unresolved.

---

# Source Evaluation
SRC-0061 is a credible cohort but leans on retrospective recall for the onset age — the reason this claim sits low. It is `CONFLATED`, so it cannot carry any sex-attribution.

---

# Assumptions (KOS-0003 §10)
- **Methodological:** retrospective onset recall approximates true onset order — weakly warranted; adequate for *relative* ordering (anxiety before depression), poor for absolute dating.
- **Comparative:** comparing across two literatures (anxiety here, depression in CLM-0044) is licensed for direction, not for precise age alignment.

---

# Reasoning (KOS-0003 §7)
**Inductive, comparative.** **Risks checked:** *retrospective-recall inflation* (named — capped the claim's confidence and framed the age as soft); *construct conflation* (named and enforced — gender-patterned only); *over-reading a single figure* (named — the load-bearing content is the *direction/ordering*, not "age 6" as a constant); *cross-literature comparison risk* (named — the anxiety-vs-depression timing contrast is asserted at the level of ordering, not exact ages).

---

# Confidence (KOS-0003 §8)
- **Level 2 (Low)** for the specific "≈age 6" onset figure. **Reaching Level 3 (Moderate)** for the *direction* — that anxiety's female-preponderant divergence appears earlier (pre-pubertally) than depression's. **Not higher:** retrospective dating, single principal source for the figure, no mechanism. **Not sex-attributed at any level** (CONFLATED).

---

# Limitations
- Exact onset age is a soft retrospective anchor.
- Direction is the defensible content; magnitude is not ranked.
- No mechanism; gender-patterned/unattributed only.

---

# Alternative Interpretations
1. **"The age-6 figure is a recall artifact and onset is actually later."** Steelmanned and partly conceded — hence Level 2 for the figure; but even with later true onset, anxiety's divergence still predates depression's pubertal emergence, preserving the direction.
2. **"Anxiety and depression share a divergence and the 'earlier' distinction is spurious."** Steelmanned; the shared internalizing liability is real. But the *timing* signatures differ (anxiety early/pre-pubertal; depression at mid-puberty), which is the claim's point and is what makes the onset-timing map non-trivial.
