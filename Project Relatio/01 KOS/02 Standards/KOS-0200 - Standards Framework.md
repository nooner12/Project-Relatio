# KOS-0200

# Standards Framework

## Version 0.1

---

# 1. Purpose

The Standards Framework defines the operational requirements for all activities performed within the Knowledge Operating System.

The purpose of standards is to ensure that knowledge production remains:

- rigorous,
- transparent,
- reproducible,
- intellectually honest,
- appropriately uncertain.

---

# 2. Relationship to Kernel Principles

All standards must remain consistent with the Kernel.

The hierarchy is:

```
Reality

↓

Ontology

↓

Epistemology

↓

Standards

↓

Roles

↓

Research Activities

↓

Knowledge Outputs
```

Standards translate philosophical commitments into operational requirements.

---

# 3. Universal Standards

Every KOS activity must follow these principles.

---

## 3.1 Intellectual Honesty Standard

The system must:

- represent evidence accurately,
- acknowledge uncertainty,
- avoid selective evidence use,
- identify conflicting information.

The goal is understanding, not confirmation.

---

## 3.2 Transparency Standard

Every significant conclusion must allow review of:

- sources,
- evidence,
- assumptions,
- reasoning,
- limitations.

A conclusion without traceability is incomplete.

---

## 3.3 Evidence-Interpretation Separation Standard

Every analysis must distinguish:

## Observation

What information is available?

from:

## Interpretation

What meaning is assigned to that information?

---

## 3.4 Proportional Confidence Standard

Confidence must match evidence strength.

Strong claims require strong support.

Weak evidence requires qualified conclusions.

---

## 3.5 Uncertainty Preservation Standard

Unknowns must remain visible.

The system must distinguish between:

- known,
- probable,
- possible,
- disputed,
- unknown.

---

# 4. Standards Hierarchy

The Standards layer contains six major categories.

---

# 01 Research Standards

Defines:

How investigations are performed.

Includes:

- question formation,
- scope definition,
- research workflow,
- documentation.

---

# 02 Evidence Standards

Defines:

How evidence is classified and evaluated.

Includes:

- evidence categories,
- strength ratings,
- limitations,
- confidence assessment.

---

# 03 Source Evaluation Standards

Defines:

How sources are assessed.

Includes:

- reliability,
- authority,
- independence,
- bias,
- transparency.

---

# 04 Reasoning Standards

Defines:

How conclusions are developed.

Includes:

- logical validity,
- assumptions,
- alternative explanations,
- argument evaluation.

---

# 05 Writing & Communication Standards

Defines:

How findings are communicated.

Includes:

- clarity,
- neutrality,
- precision,
- uncertainty communication.

---

# 06 Quality Assurance Standards

Defines:

How outputs are reviewed.

Includes:

- completeness,
- consistency,
- revision,
- error correction.

---

# 5. Required Research Output Components

Any formal research product must contain:

## Research Question

What is being investigated?

---

## Scope

What is included and excluded?

---

## Claims

What conclusions are being evaluated?

---

## Evidence

What supports or challenges the claims?

---

## Interpretation

What meaning is derived?

---

## Limitations

Where does uncertainty remain?

---

## Confidence Assessment

How strong is the conclusion?

---

# 6. Standard Review Principle

Standards are not permanent assumptions.

They must themselves be evaluated.

The KOS shall periodically review:

- effectiveness,
- bias,
- applicability,
- unintended consequences.

---

# 7. Standards Governance

Changes to standards require:

- documented reasoning,
- impact assessment,
- version tracking.
