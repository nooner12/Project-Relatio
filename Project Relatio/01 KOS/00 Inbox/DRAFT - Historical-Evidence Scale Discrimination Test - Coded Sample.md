## 1. The three scales

**S1 — House ★-scale** (external work-stream vocabulary; *not* defined in this vault):
★★★ meta-analysis / systematic review · ★★☆ RCT / prospective cohort · ★☆☆ observational / small trial · ◇◇◇ theoretical / mechanistic.

**S2 — Proposed historical-evidence scale** (the hypothesis under test; draft typology, not adopted):
- **H-a** — contemporaneous primary text
- **H-b** — archaeological / epigraphic / material attestation
- **H-c** — near-contemporary secondary account
- **H-d** — later tradition attributing backward
- **H-e** — modern scholarly reconstruction

**S3 — Native canonical confidence scale** (KOS-0003 §8; the vault's only authorized confidence vocabulary): Level 0 (Unsupported) → Level 5 (Very High).

Coding rules used: house-scale codes are applied honestly, without improvised equivalences (Appendix A.4 forbids inventing mappings) — a claim whose evidence is not a study design of any listed kind takes the floor, ◇◇◇. Historical-scale codes take the **strongest evidence class actually attested** for the claim. Native Levels are coded per KOS-0003 §8 definitions, conservatively.

## 2. Coded sample — 15 claims, Iranian family

One family only (Zoroastrianism, Zurvanism, Manichaeism, Mazdakism, Yazidism). Discrete datable/attributable assertions, not whole traditions.

| 1 | Zoroaster lived in the 6th century BCE (the "traditional date," 258 years before Alexander) | ◇◇◇ | **H-d** | **Level 2 (Low)** | The date is a Sasanian-era backward calculation, argued to be scholastic fabrication; no contemporary attestation; most current scholarship rejects it | Shahbazi, "The 'Traditional Date of Zoroaster' Explained," *BSOAS* 40/1 (1977), 25–35 ✓ |

- **S1 (house):** total collapse — 15/15 in one band. Strictly, most claims are *not codable at all* on a study-design typology; ◇◇◇ is the honest floor, and the floor is everything.
- **S2 (historical):** full discrimination — all five bands used, no band holding more than a third of the sample.
- **S3 (native):** discriminates — four bands used (spread 1 / 7 / 5 / 2). The vault's canonical scale does **not** collapse on this domain.

**Orthogonality check (S2 vs S3):** the historical band does not predict native confidence. H-e (modern reconstruction) spans Level 2 (#12) to Level 4 (#5, #13); H-a (contemporaneous primary text) yields only Level 3–4 (#3, #7), *below* the Level 5 earned by an H-b inscription (#4). Evidence *kind* and warranted *confidence* are different axes — the proposed scale is a typology that feeds confidence assessment, not a rival confidence scale.
