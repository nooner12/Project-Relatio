# KOS-0008

# Project Relatio Research Methodology & Investigation Framework

## Version 1.2
## Adopted Kernel Document

---

# 1. Purpose

Project Relatio requires a consistent methodology for transforming questions into reliable knowledge.

The Research Methodology Framework establishes the process for:

- defining research questions,
- collecting evidence,
- evaluating sources,
- analyzing claims,
- synthesizing findings,
- integrating knowledge.

This framework ensures that research remains aligned with Project Relatio’s foundational commitments:

- intellectual honesty,
- transparency,
- evidence alignment,
- non-advocacy,
- preservation of uncertainty.

---

# 2. Central Research Principle

Project Relatio adopts:

> Understanding should be built through disciplined investigation rather than assumption, preference, or conclusion-seeking.

---

# 3. Research Methodology Philosophy

Project Relatio recognizes that different questions require different methods.

No single research approach is sufficient for all domains.

Method selection should correspond to:

- research question,
- claim type,
- available evidence,
- domain requirements.

The Research OS integrates:

- empirical methods,
- historical methods,
- philosophical analysis,
- theological analysis,
- systems analysis,
- comparative methodology.

---

# 5. Research Phases

---

# RP-001 — Question Formation

Every research project begins with a clearly defined question.

A strong research question identifies:

- subject,
- scope,
- assumptions,
- desired outcome.

Example:

Weak:

> Is Buddhism true?

Improved:

> How does Buddhist philosophy conceptualize suffering, consciousness, and liberation, and what evidence exists regarding psychological outcomes associated with Buddhist practices?

---

# RP-002 — Scope Definition

Every project must define:

## Included Scope

What will be investigated?

## Excluded Scope

What will not be investigated?

## Limitations

What boundaries affect conclusions?

---

# RP-003 — Research Design

Before evidence collection begins, researchers must establish:

- research method,
- evidence requirements,
- evaluation criteria,
- expected output format.

---

# RP-004 — Evidence Collection

Sources should be collected systematically.

Source categories include:

---

## Primary Sources

Original materials:

- texts,
- documents,
- observations,
- historical records.

---

## Secondary Sources

Interpretive materials:

- scholarly publications,
- academic analysis,
- expert commentary.

---

## Tertiary Sources

Reference materials:

- summaries,
- educational resources,
- general explanations.

---

# RP-005 — Source Evaluation

Every source should be evaluated according to:

---

## Authority

Who produced the information?

---

## Expertise

Does the source possess relevant knowledge?

---

## Methodology

How was the information generated?

---

## Bias

What assumptions or incentives may influence conclusions?

---

## Transparency

Are methods and evidence visible?

---

## Corroboration

Are claims supported by independent sources?

---

# RP-006 — Search Strategy Framework

Research should document:

- search terms,
- databases,
- source selection criteria,
- inclusion criteria,
- exclusion criteria,
- stopping criteria.

A research process should be reproducible.

---

# 6. Evidence Classification System

Project Relatio distinguishes evidence types.

---

# EC-001 — Empirical Evidence

Evidence derived from:

- observation,
- measurement,
- experimentation.

Examples:

- psychological studies,
- behavioral research,
- scientific data.

---

# EC-002 — Historical Evidence

Evidence derived from:

- documents,
- archaeology,
- historical analysis,
- textual study.

---

# EC-003 — Philosophical Evidence

Evidence evaluated through:

- logical reasoning,
- argument structure,
- conceptual coherence.

---

# EC-004 — Experiential Evidence

Evidence derived from:

- reported experience,
- personal transformation,
- phenomenological study.

---

# EC-005 — Theological Evidence

Evidence based upon:

- revelation claims,
- sacred texts,
- theological reasoning.

Theological evidence must be identified according to its own category and not confused with empirical evidence.

---

# 8. Confidence Rating System

Project Relatio requires explicit confidence assessment.

> **Canonical scale defined in KOS-0003 §8.** Confidence uses a single hybrid **Level + label** scale, written as e.g. "Level 4 (High)." The table below *is* that scale — not a second vocabulary.

|Level|Rating|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|Level 5|Very High|Strong evidence with broad agreement|
|Level 4|High|Strong evidence with limited uncertainty|
|Level 3|Moderate|Evidence exists but interpretation varies|
|Level 2|Low|Limited evidence or significant disagreement|
|Level 1|Very Low|Speculative or weakly supported|
|Level 0|Unsupported|Insufficient justification; not admissible to the permanent Knowledge Base (KOS-0003 §12)|

# 9. Research Integrity Standards

---

# RI-001 — Evidence Before Conclusion

Research must not begin with predetermined answers.

---

# RI-002 — Uncertainty Preservation

Unknown information must remain identified as unknown.

---

# RI-003 — Interpretation Separation

Research outputs must distinguish:

- evidence,
- interpretation,
- speculation.

---

# RI-004 — Alternative Explanation Consideration

Competing explanations should be considered where appropriate.

---

# RI-005 — Revision Readiness

Research conclusions remain open to modification when stronger evidence appears.

---

# RI-006 — Researcher Reflexivity

Researchers must acknowledge:

- assumptions,
- expectations,
- worldview influences,
- interpretive limitations.

The researcher is part of the knowledge process.

---

# RI-007 — Method Transparency

Research outputs must document:

- methods used,
- sources considered,
- selection criteria,
- reasoning process.

---

# RI-008 — Contradiction Mapping

Conflicting evidence must be identified and analyzed.

Contradictions should not automatically be eliminated.

They may reveal:

- different assumptions,
- incomplete evidence,
- unresolved questions.

---

# RI-009 — Knowledge Integration Protocol

Research outputs should connect to:

- existing knowledge structures,
- related documents,
- unresolved questions,
- future research pathways.

---

# 11. Specialist Role Integration

KOS-0008 provides the operating framework for future specialist roles.

---

## Research Specialist

- information gathering,

- synthesis preparation.

- contextual expertise,
- specialized interpretation,

---

## Research Function

"What information exists?"

from:

## Interpretation Function

"What does this information mean?"

# 12. Application Example

Research Question:

> What are the psychological effects of contemplative practices across religious traditions?

Process:

1. Define contemplative practice.
2. Identify traditions.
3. Establish evidence requirements.
4. Collect psychological research.
5. Evaluate sources.
6. Compare findings.
7. Identify limitations.
8. Assess confidence.
9. Integrate results.
