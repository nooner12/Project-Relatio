# CON-0004

# Project Relatio Knowledge & Epistemic Integrity Charter

## Version 1.0

## Adopted Constitutional Instrument

---

# 1. Purpose

Project Relatio exists to improve humanity’s ability to understand reality through disciplined inquiry, transparent reasoning, and relationship-centered knowledge development.

A system dedicated to understanding must establish standards governing how knowledge is approached.

CON-0004 defines the foundational epistemic commitments of Project Relatio.

It establishes principles governing:

- knowledge,
- evidence,
- claims,
- interpretation,
- uncertainty,
- reasoning,
- evaluation.

This document does not define every operational method.

Those belong to the Knowledge Operating System and supporting standards.

---

# 2. Epistemic Philosophy

Project Relatio adopts a **critical realist orientation**.

This means:

1. Reality exists independently of human perception.
2. Human beings access reality through imperfect models.
3. Understanding improves through continual refinement.

Project Relatio rejects both:

## Absolute Relativism

The idea that all claims are equally valid regardless of evidence.

---

## Absolute Human Certainty

The idea that human understanding perfectly represents reality.

---

# 3. Foundational Epistemic Commitment

Project Relatio adopts the following principle:

> Truth is independent of human understanding. Knowledge is humanity’s continually improving relationship with reality.

Therefore:

- models must be distinguished from reality,
- confidence must be distinguished from certainty,
- conclusions must remain open to revision.

---

# 4. Epistemic Categories

Project Relatio shall distinguish the following categories.

---

# EC-001 — Reality

Reality refers to the underlying nature of existence independent of human interpretation.

---

# EC-002 — Observation

Observation refers to information obtained through:

- perception,
- measurement,
- experience,
- documentation,
- recorded events.

Observations require interpretation before becoming understanding.

---

# EC-003 — Evidence

Evidence consists of observations relevant to evaluating a claim.

Evidence should be evaluated according to:

- relevance,
- reliability,
- quality,
- context.

---

# EC-004 — Interpretation

Interpretation is the process of assigning meaning to observations and evidence.

Interpretation is necessary.

Interpretation is not identical to fact.

---

# EC-005 — Claim

A claim is a proposition asserting something about reality.

Claims require evaluation appropriate to their nature.

---

# EC-006 — Belief

Belief is a proposition accepted as meaningful or true by an individual or community.

Belief may contain:

- knowledge,
- interpretation,
- values,
- assumptions,
- personal meaning.

Belief does not automatically constitute validated knowledge.

---

# EC-007 — Knowledge

Knowledge is an increasingly reliable model of reality supported by appropriate justification, evidence, and evaluation methods.

Knowledge remains open to refinement.

---

# 5. Core Epistemic Principles

---

# EP-001 — Reality Independence

Reality exists independently from human belief.

Agreement does not create truth.

---

# EP-002 — Epistemic Humility

Human understanding is limited.

Confidence shall not be confused with certainty.

---

# EP-003 — Evidence Before Conclusion

Claims shall be evaluated through evidence and reasoning rather than preference, expectation, or desired outcomes.

---

# EP-004 — Claim Classification

Significant claims shall be classified according to their nature.

Examples include:

- empirical claims,
- historical claims,
- philosophical claims,
- theological claims,
- experiential claims,
- interpretive claims.

Different claim types require appropriate evaluation methods.

---

# EP-005 — Evidence Evaluation

Evidence shall be evaluated according to:

- source reliability,
- methodological quality,
- relevance,
- consistency,
- limitations,
- alternative explanations.

---

# EP-006 — Uncertainty Transparency

Uncertainty shall remain visible.

Project Relatio shall identify:

- unknowns,
- disagreements,
- limitations,
- confidence levels.

---

# EP-007 — Strongest Interpretation Principle

Ideas, traditions, arguments, and models shall receive their strongest reasonable interpretation before evaluation.

This prevents:

- strawman arguments,
- unfair comparisons,
- ideological distortion.

---

# EP-008 — Evidence and Meaning Separation

Project Relatio shall distinguish:

"What is supported?"

from:

"What does it mean?"

Evidence and interpretation shall not be improperly combined.

---

# EP-009 — Revision Principle

New evidence, improved reasoning, or stronger models may require revision of previous conclusions.

Revision represents progress.

---

# EP-010 — Multiple Knowledge Domains

Project Relatio recognizes that different domains of inquiry require different methods.

Examples:

## Science

May emphasize:

- measurement,
- prediction,
- repeatability.

## History

May emphasize:

- documents,
- context,
- probability.

## Philosophy

May emphasize:

- logic,
- coherence,
- conceptual analysis.

## Theology

May emphasize:

- revelation,
- tradition,
- metaphysical reasoning.

Each domain requires appropriate evaluation.

---

# EP-011 — Accessibility of Reasoning

Significant conclusions should be understandable and reviewable by others capable of evaluating the relevant evidence and reasoning.

Knowledge should not depend solely upon inaccessible authority.

---

# EP-012 — Comparative Model Evaluation

When multiple explanations exist, Project Relatio shall evaluate competing models according to:

- explanatory power,
- evidence,
- coherence,
- limitations,
- predictive or interpretive usefulness.

The existence of multiple models does not imply that all models are equally supported.

---

# EP-013 — Methodological Appropriateness

Claims shall be evaluated using methods appropriate to their nature.

No domain shall be judged exclusively by methods unsuitable for its type of inquiry.

---

# 6. Knowledge Quality Assessment

Project Relatio shall evaluate knowledge claims using multiple dimensions.

A claim may be assessed according to:

## Evidence Strength

- Strong
- Moderate
- Limited
- Unsupported

---

## Reasoning Quality

- Strong
- Moderate
- Weak

---

## Source Reliability

- High
- Moderate
- Low

---

## Interpretive Confidence

- High
- Moderate
- Low

---

Knowledge quality is multidimensional.

A claim may have:

- strong historical evidence,
- weak interpretation,
- high cultural significance.

These dimensions must remain separate.

---

# 7. Epistemic Failure Prevention

Project Relatio shall actively guard against:

---

## Confirmation Bias

Seeking only supporting evidence.

---

## Argument From Authority

Accepting claims solely because of the source.

---

## False Balance

Treating unsupported claims as equivalent to well-supported claims.

---

## Category Errors

Applying inappropriate evaluation methods.

---

## Reductionism

Reducing complex realities to a single explanatory framework.

---

## Premature Closure

Ending inquiry before sufficient evaluation.

---

# 9. Epistemic Integrity Statement

Project Relatio adopts:

> Knowledge is not the possession of conclusions. Knowledge is the disciplined relationship between reality, evidence, interpretation, and understanding.
