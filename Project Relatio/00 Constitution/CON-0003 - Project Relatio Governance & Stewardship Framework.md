# CON-0003

# Project Relatio Governance & Stewardship Framework

## Version 1.0

## Adopted Constitutional Instrument
# 1. Purpose

Project Relatio requires governance structures that preserve:

- mission alignment,
- intellectual integrity,
- accountability,
- adaptability,
- responsible evolution.

Governance exists to protect the purpose and integrity of Project Relatio.

Governance does not exist to establish ownership over knowledge, determine truth claims, or replace inquiry with authority.

---

# 2. Governance Philosophy

Project Relatio adopts a stewardship model.

> Authority within Project Relatio exists to preserve purpose, maintain integrity, and enable responsible development.

Stewardship differs from ownership.

Ownership implies control.

Stewardship implies responsibility.

Those entrusted with governance responsibilities serve the continued integrity of the system rather than personal preference or authority.

---

# 3. Foundational Governance Principles

## GP-001 — Purpose Before Authority

Governance exists to preserve the mission, principles, and integrity of Project Relatio.

Authority is a responsibility granted for a defined purpose.

---

## GP-002 — Evidence Over Preference

Personal preference, expectation, or desired outcomes shall not override supported evidence.

---

## GP-003 — No Authority Over Truth

Governance may establish:

- processes,
- responsibilities,
- standards,
- review procedures.

Governance may not determine reality or establish factual conclusions through authority alone.

---

## GP-004 — Preserve Disagreement

Reasonable disagreement shall remain visible and available for evaluation.

Consensus does not equal truth.

A minority position may contain valid insight and should not be discarded solely due to lack of agreement.

---

## GP-005 — Governance Reflexivity

Project Relatio governance structures shall remain subject to evaluation and improvement using the same standards applied to other system components.

Governance must be capable of examining itself.

---

## GP-006 — Stewardship Continuity

Stewardship responsibilities shall be transferable across individuals, teams, or future systems while preserving Project Relatio’s foundational purpose and integrity.

The project shall not depend upon a single person for continued existence.

---

## GP-007 — Governance Inquiry Separation

Governance shall protect the process of inquiry but shall not determine the conclusions produced by inquiry.

Governance may establish how research occurs.

Governance may not dictate what research must conclude.

---

# 4. Stewardship Functions

Project Relatio recognizes functional stewardship responsibilities.

These functions may be performed by individuals, teams, institutions, or future systems.

The function is primary.

The person or system performing the function is secondary.

---

# 4.1 Vision Stewardship Function

## Purpose

Protect the identity, mission, and foundational principles of Project Relatio.

## Responsibilities

The Vision Stewardship Function is responsible for:

- preserving alignment with CON-0001,
- evaluating major directional changes,
- reviewing constitutional amendments,
- protecting foundational principles.

## Limitations

The Vision Stewardship Function:

- does not determine factual conclusions,
- does not override evidence,
- does not establish preferred beliefs.

---

# 4.2 Systems Architecture Function

## Purpose

Maintain structural coherence and integrity.

## Responsibilities

The Systems Architecture Function is responsible for:

- evaluating architectural decisions,
- identifying contradictions,
- maintaining system coherence,
- proposing improvements,
- protecting scalability.

## Limitations

The Systems Architecture Function:

- recommends structural changes,
- does not define truth,
- does not override research findings without justification.

---

# 4.3 Research Integrity Function

## Purpose

Protect the quality and reliability of inquiry.

## Responsibilities

The Research Integrity Function is responsible for:

- evaluating methodology,
- maintaining research standards,
- identifying bias,
- assessing evidence quality,
- protecting epistemic integrity.

---

# 4.4 Knowledge Management Function

## Purpose

Maintain the organization, preservation, and accessibility of knowledge.

## Responsibilities

The Knowledge Management Function is responsible for:

- information architecture,
- documentation standards,
- archival integrity,
- knowledge accessibility.

---

# 4.5 External Review Function

## Purpose

Introduce independent evaluation and challenge assumptions.

Potential contributors include:

- subject matter experts,
- researchers,
- reviewers,
- future AI-assisted evaluation systems.

External review exists to improve integrity, not to transfer responsibility.

---

# 5. Decision Architecture

Project Relatio recognizes four categories of decisions.

---

# 5.1 Constitutional Decisions

Examples:

- changing mission,
- changing foundational principles,
- redefining project identity.

Requirements:

Highest review standard.

---

# 5.2 Architectural Decisions

Examples:

- changing system structure,
- introducing major components,
- modifying foundational workflows.

Requirements:

Documented rationale and impact assessment.

---

# 5.3 Operational Decisions

Examples:

- research workflows,
- implementation choices,
- project execution.

Requirements:

Must follow established standards.

---

# 5.4 Exploratory Decisions

Examples:

- experiments,
- prototypes,
- temporary approaches.

Requirements:

Must be clearly identified as exploratory.

---

# 6. Decision Lifecycle

Significant decisions shall follow a documented lifecycle:

```
Proposal

↓

Context Analysis

↓

Alternatives Considered

↓

Impact Assessment

↓

Decision

↓

Documentation

↓

Review
```

The purpose of this process is not unnecessary bureaucracy.

The purpose is preserving reasoning and institutional memory.

---

# 7. Architectural Decision Records (ADR)

Project Relatio shall preserve significant decisions through Architectural Decision Records.

ADRs exist to document:

- why decisions were made,
- what alternatives existed,
- what consequences were accepted.

---

## ADR Minimum Structure

```
ADR Identifier

Title

Date

Status

Context

Decision

Alternatives Considered

Reasoning

Consequences

Review Date
```

---

# 8. Conflict Resolution Framework

Project Relatio recognizes that disagreement is a natural component of complex inquiry.

Conflict shall be treated as information requiring analysis.

The preferred resolution process:

```
Identify Conflict

↓

Clarify Claims

↓

Separate Evidence From Interpretation

↓

Separate Values From Evidence

↓

Evaluate Alternatives

↓

Seek Resolution

↓

Document Outcome
```

---

# 9. Governance Safeguards

## 9.1 Transparency

Significant governance decisions should be documented and reviewable.

---

## 9.2 Accountability

Responsibilities should be identifiable.

---

## 9.3 Reversibility

Major decisions should consider whether future correction is possible.

---

## 9.4 Historical Preservation

Previous decisions, versions, and reasoning shall remain accessible.

The history of development is part of understanding the system.

---

# 10. Amendment Governance

Governance structures may evolve.

However, significant changes require:

- documented rationale,
- impact assessment,
- review,
- recorded approval.

Change is necessary for growth.

Uncontrolled drift is not.
