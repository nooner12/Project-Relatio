# CLAUDE.md — Project Relatio

This file is Relatio Reference Implementation (RRI) tooling. It is not a Knowledge Object and is not governed by KOS lifecycle rules. It exists to orient Claude Code sessions working inside this vault.

## What this vault is

This vault is the **Relatio Reference Implementation (RRI)** — the Obsidian implementation of the **Relatio Knowledge Architecture (RKA)**. Maintain this distinction at all times:

- **RKA** = the abstract, platform-independent architecture (principles, standards, governance, object types, relationships).
- **RRI** = this vault: folders, Markdown, YAML, templates, workflows, plugins.
- Folder structure is an *implementation expression*, not the architecture. Folders represent classification only; the knowledge graph lives in semantic relationships, not the file tree.

Central principle: **Knowledge is a structured network of meaningful relationships, not a collection of stored information.**

## Purpose and scope guardrail (owner, 2026-07-09; reframed by ADR-GOV-0002, 2026-07-11)

**Project Relatio's purpose is to conduct thorough and disciplined research on Brian's research questions — and to become an excellent knowledge architecture in the process.** Both are legitimate aims; the research is primary, and building toward the architecture Brian has named ("eventually the world's best") is a genuine concurrent goal, not a deferred one.

The operative discipline is a **merit principle, not a brake**: **every addition — from any contributor — earns its place on its merits, by improving a real answer or the system's ability to produce one.** Additions are justified by *use*, not by symmetry or completeness; a standard, object type, role, or workflow step that catches no real failure and improves no real answer is removed. When in doubt, run a research question through the system and see what breaks. Apply this test to contributions **by their merit, not their source** (including your own).

The original guardrail was a defence against a specific collaborator (ChatGPT) that over-generated architecture; it was never a description of the owner, who does not apply it to himself. With that framing removed, the point is discipline-by-use, not reflexive refusal — do not suppress genuinely warranted work to be "safe." See ADR-GOV-0002.*

## Health / clinical-evidence scope (ADR-GOV-0002)

The system may **investigate any question to full depth and rigour** — the scope is not a limit on inquiry. What it does **not** do is give **individualized** medical/clinical advice: it lacks the person's history, labs, and exam, so it reports *general evidence* and brackets the individual case. That is epistemic honesty, not a restriction. Health findings are framed for **expert application** and are **gated from third-party external reliance** (STD-0006 §7.5) pending genuine independent and clinician review. **The system informs expert judgment — including Brian's own clinical background — it does not replace a clinician.** The boundary is on outward-facing individualized claims, not on what may be researched.

## Attribution and AI-assistance disclosure (STD-0002 §6/§6.1 v1.12 — ADR-GOV-0011)

**Every formal Knowledge Object carries a REQUIRED `attribution` field.** It is **provenance** — who made *this record*, in what role, when, and at what degree of AI assistance. `validate.py` enforces presence and shape at **error level**; the full corpus (335 objects) was backfilled 2026-07-22 and TPL-0001…0006 emit the field, so **new records are born conformant — do not hand-author one without it.**

```
attribution:                 # ALWAYS a list; exactly one entry at Stage 1
  - actor: Brian Noon        # a named human — an AI is NEVER the actor
    role: Vision Steward     # free string; ROLE-#### where one applies (no enum)
    event: created           # the only Stage 1 value
    date: 2026-07-22         # YYYY-MM-DD
    ai_degree: ai-delegated  # unassisted | ai-assisted | ai-delegated
    ai_model_family: Claude  # free string; `none` iff unassisted
```

- **Never a scalar.** The list shape is what makes ADR-GOV-0011's deferred **Stage 2** (per-event attribution) purely additive. The validator deliberately does **not** cap the list at one entry or enum-check `event` — do not "tighten" it.
- **`attribution` ≠ `owner` ≠ `source_author`.** Provenance vs. stewardship vs. the external source's own author. On a Source Record the first and third are both present and mean opposite things.
- **Circuit-produced records attribute to the ratifying human at `ai-delegated`.** The OPS-0003 circuit gets no exemption; that is what makes the backfill honest.
- **INDEPENDENCE IS ASSESSED AT FAMILY LEVEL (binding).** Same model family is same-kind **regardless of version or operator** — a Claude-family review of Claude-family work is **not** independence of kind, and same-family-assisted work **does not sum toward independence**. This binds reliance-tier computation (ADR-GOV-0006), reflexive-gate review eligibility (STD-0006 §7.6), and any future R2 determination.
- **SELECTIVE VISIBILITY (binding).** Attribution is durable but **withholdable at review time** when a review's independence depends on it, and **no tool or surface used for blinded review may render it unconditionally.** Concretely: **neither the tree view nor the timeline view renders attribution, and neither may be changed to** — `build_view.py` builds an explicit known-field dict for exactly this reason, so never replace it with a `**meta` passthrough. Honesty limit: at small n, withholding is *procedural* blindness only; a review may not be recorded as blinded in the §7.6 sense on field-withholding alone.
- **METRIC PROHIBITION (Decision E).** No computed contributor standing, gating, ranking, or metric may be introduced by tooling or accreted by practice — activating computed gating requires **its own governance ADR**. **Any validator, script, or tool found computing contributor scores absent that ADR is nonconformant by definition.** Do not build one.

## Standard version-citation convention (owner-approved, 2026-07-23)

**Cite a standard WITHOUT a version unless the version is load-bearing.** Write "STD-0004" normally; write "STD-0004 v1.2 (which added `branches_from`)" only when *which version* is the point.

**Why this rule exists, and why you must NOT sweep-fix the existing citations.** The ADR-GOV-0011 attribution backfill bumped **every** standard, so prose citations that pin a version (e.g. "STD-0004 v1.3", "STD-0002 v1.11") now read **one behind**. That is cosmetic drift, not error. **Do not sweep-edit them:** fixing a citation edits the citing record, which bumps *its* version and requires *its* history row — and every record that cited *that* record is now one behind in turn. A tidy-up sweep manufactures the very drift it is trying to remove, at corpus scale.

**Read every existing pinned citation as "the version at time of writing."** It records what the author was working against, which is usually the more useful fact. Where a pinned version genuinely misleads, fix **that one citation** as part of a change you were already making to that record — never as a standalone sweep.
